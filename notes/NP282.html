Jones was engaged with the regiment in 1862 during the Peninsula Campaign, including the siege of Yorktown. He was discharged due to disability on March 6, 1863 at Fort Monroe, VA.

He was reported to have died of wounds at Magnolia, FL on Sept. 19, 1864, however a letter from his Captain to the widow Elizabeth Jones states Jones died of "inflammation and ulceration of the bowels." Charles George JORDAN

[I072620]
Death claimed another of Swisher County's pioneer citizens when Charlie George Jordan passed away at his home in Tulia at 11:30 o'clock Friday night, July 2, just two days before his 87th birthday. He had been in ill health for several years.
Funeral services were conducted at the First Methodist Church in Tulia Sunday afternoon at 4 o'clock by Rev. P.E. Yarborough, pastor, with Rev. C.C. Armstrong of the Canyon Methodist Church assisting.
Pallbearers were sons of the deceased: James Albert Jordan and William Archie Jordan of Tulia; Charlie Walker Jordan, Morton, Texas; Gordon Brewer Jordan, Amarillo; Henry Leander Jordan, Pampa; and Edward Lee Jordan, Electra.
Interment was in Rose Hill Cemetery under the direction of Wallace Funeral Home.
Besides his six sons, Mr. Jordan leaves his widow, two daughters, Mrs. Clara May Thompson, Canyon; and Mrs. Mattie Anna Axtell, Spring Lake; one sister and one brother, Mrs. Sally Walker and John Jordan of Rockwall; 14 grandchildren, and four great grandchildren, all boys.
Mr. Jordan was born July 5, 1856 in Rusk County, Texas, and moved to Rockwall, Texas, in 1868. On January 22, 1880, at Rockwall, he was married to Cora May Walker, who was born in Virginia on the same date as his birthday, although a later year, July 5, 1865, To this union were born ten children, two of whom preceded their father in death. Irene Lewis Jordan passed away in May 1903 at Tulia; and Howard Gatchell Jordan died in Arizona in 1917.
On January 19, 1901, Mr. Jordan moved with his family to the Plains and Swisher County, locating eight miles west of Tulia on the ranch where his son, William Archie Jordan, now lives. Except for two years spent at Plainview, 1915 and 1916, he has made his home at Tulia since that time.
For 75 years Mr. Jordan had been a member of the Methodist Church, having been converted and united with the church at the age of about 12 years. He came from Methodist stock and took great pride in his church. Not only did he faithfully attend all services of his local church but also he made it a practice to attend annual and district conferences of the church. He was a constant reader of the Texas Christian Advocate and had been a subscriber of this magazine for 74 years.
Mr. Jordan helped to build the first Methodist Church in Tulia, and also assisted with the construction of many of the early day houses in this area.
The Tulia Herald, 8 July 1943

She was raised in Dubuque, baptized and confirmed at Nativity Catholic Church, and attended parochial and public school. Brodersen on June 23, 1951 at Nativity Church. After marriage they remained in Dubuque until moving to Northfield in 1953. Over the years in Northfield, along with being a mother, homemaker and partner with Bill in his architectural business, Peg worked as an administrative assistant at Dr. Heibergʼs dental office and at St. Olaf College for almost 20 years - retiring in 1994. During those years Peg was always an active volunteer - sharing her time with the Hospital Auxiliary, the Arts Guild and St. Dominic's Church and School.
Peg was a member of the Church of Annunciation in Hazelwood and a former member of the Church of St. Dominic. She enjoyed playing Bridge, traveling with Bill (including trips to Ireland and Germany), and cooking - she was an excellent cook! She will be remembered for her devotion to her family, the faith filled life she lead, and her pumpkin bread.
Survivors include her husband of 61 years, Bill; 6 children and their spouses: Stephen and Mary of Lansing, Kansas, Tom and Mary of Shakopee, Anne Varley of Northfield, Mark and Gail of Minneapolis, Michael & Teil of Champlin, Greg and Nicole of Minnetonka; 16 grandchildren: Elizabeth (Erik) Jorgensen, Catherine Brodersen, Brian (Pamela), Brent and Christine Brodersen, Lori Grant, Brittany (Justin) Hirschuber, Teresa Rae and Andrew Brodersen, Tyler and Nicholas Brodersen, Lynnea and Kendra Waydula, Tyler, William and Mason Brodersen; 5 great-grandchildren: Dalton, Sawyer, Zachary, Makenzie and Emma; her sister, Nike Dreier of Santa Barbara, California; her brother, Robert (Beverly) Jordan of Dubuque; nieces, nephews and other relatives and friends.
