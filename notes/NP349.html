LESLIE R. MASON
SOUTH LONDONDERRY - Leslie Reed Mason, 53, son of Mrs. Flora Hovey of East Arlington and Bernis Mason of East Dorset, died Monday at his home after a long illness.
Born in Manchester Feb 12, 1912, he had resided most of his life in this area and for 20 years had been employed by Smith Inc. of South Londonderry, wood working plant.
Survivors besides his parents are his wife, Elaine, a daughter, Diane, a student at Springfield College; two sons, Richard, serving in the Navy aboard the USS Keppler, and Roy Mason of South Londonderry; a brother, Howard Mason of East Arlington; a sister, Mrs. William Brownell of Hoosick Falls; aunts, uncles, cousins, nieces and nephews.
The funeral will be held Wednesday at 2 pm in the First Baptist Church here by the pastor, the Rev. Elliott Fair. Burial will be in Glebe View Cemetery. Brewster Funeral Service of Manchester Center is handling the arrangements.
Donations may be made to the Vermont Cancer Fund in care of Louise Howe, South Londonderry. There will be no visiting hours.
Bennington Banner, 28 September 1965

[I100859]
Lois L. DeVore, Caledonia, left us to be with her heavenly family on March 21, 2009. She is survived by her husband, Ray W. DeVore; son and daughter-in-law, Fred C. and Martha DeVore; daughter and son-in-law, Debra Ann and Gary McIntyre; grandchildren, John (Dawn) DeVore, Jason DeVore and Holly Ann McIntyre; great-grandchildren, Emily and Madison; her sister, Doris Lawrence; brother, Howard (Shirley) Lawrence; sisters-in-law, Ellen and Bernice Lawrence; several nieces and nephews. Predeceased by her mother and father, Charles Mason and Lulu Lawrence; 5 brothers and 1 sister.
Lois was born to Charles Mason and Lulu Lawrence on October 13, 1925 in Clarion, PA. She attended Clarion schools and graduated High School in 1943 after high school she attended Hoffs Business School and later was a Secretary to management at Pennsylvania Furnace and Iron. On October 7, 1946 she was married to Ray W. DeVore. They moved to Oneida, NY, where Ray worked for GLF (Grange League Federation). They transferred to Caledonia in 1948. They joined the Caledonia United Presbyterian Stone Church in 1955 when Rev. Donald MacLeod was Pastor. Lois was very active in church work including choir for years. She was a quilter most of her life and at one time was recognized by the State of New York as a top notch quilter. For many years she never sat down to rest without her hands making mittens or something. Many children's organizations received a box of mittens every year.
A Celebration of Lois' life will be held 1 PM Saturday (March 28, 2009) with Rev. Ron Sallade officiating at the United Presbyterian Stone Church, 3177 Church St., Caledonia, 14423. Memorials may be made to the United Presbyterian Stone Church. Arrangements Alhart Funeral Home, Caledonia.

[I083557]
LEOMINSTER - Barbara H. (Maston) Naughton, 92 years old, died Thursday, November 7, 2013, in the Life Care Center of Leominster. Her husband of 58 years James J. Naughton, passed away in 2000.
She was born in Leominster, daughter of Roy and Ruth (Short) Maston. She leaves a brother, Roy Maston, Jr., of Manchester, NH; and several nieces and nephews.
She was predeceased by her sisters, Charlene Siren in 2002, and Agnes Kirouac in 2012.
Barbara graduated from Leominster High School and Leominster Hospital School of Nursing, and worked many years in doctor's offices. Then she and her husband established the AF&L Telephone Service which handled telephone answering, two-way radios, pocket pages and direct mailing services.
In 1982, the Naughtons retired to travel about the country in their travel trailer. They wintered in their home in Arizona and summered in their home in Ashburnham. In 2004, Barbara moved to Fort Myers, Florida and returned to this area in 2007. She enjoyed traveling, good books, cards and cross-word puzzles.

David S. MASTRACCI

[I043585]
David S. Mastracci, of Upper Arlington, passed away May 11, 2011 at Kobacker House, surrounded by his loving family. Born March 22, 1933 in Columbus, Ohio. A graduate of Upper Arlington High School, Class of 1951 and later attended the Ohio State University, where he majored in Fine Arts. David proudly served in the United States Army from 1954-1956. Following his discharge, he began working as a graphic illustrator for North American Aviation. After 22 years service, he began working for his good friend, John Mead, at Little Turtle Golf Club. He returned to Rockwell International in 1981, and remained there until the company left Columbus, in 1988. David continued as a graphic artist for Battelle Memorial Institute, until his retirement in 1998. An avid golfer, David was involved in league play in his early years. He also coached little league football and baseball teams, for Upper Arlington Athletic Association in the 1970's. David belonged to Our Lady of Victory Catholic Church and was a lifetime member of the Guild Athletic Club, where he served on the Board of Directors. He is preceded in death by his parents, Tony and Yolanda Mastracci, granddaughter, Sunne Mastracci and beloved aunt, Josephine Mastracci. Dave is survived by his loving wife of 51 years, Lenore, children, Maria (Eric) Olson, of Clemmons, North Carolina, Michael Mastracci, of Columbus and Tony (Sharon) Mastracci of Chicago, grandchildren, Nicolas, Bella and Enzo Mastracci, and Molly and Brett Olson. He is also survived by his cousins Sandy and Lou Menduni of Columbus and Alice and Phil Serra of Detroit. A special thanks to Tina Husted of Homecare Plus for her kind and loving assistance. We would like to thank all the staff of HomeReach Hospice for their care and attention at the time of David's death. Friends may call Sunday, May 15th 2011 at the John Quint Treboni Funeral Home, 1177 W. 5th Avenue, from 1:00-4:00 PM. GAC Prayer Service at 3:30 PM followed by a Catholic Prayer Service 3:45 PM. Funeral Mass will be held 10:30 AM on MONDAY, May 16th at Our Lady of Victory Catholic Church, Rev. Msgr. Romano Ciotola, Presider. Entombment will follow at the St. Joseph Cemetery Mausoleum.
