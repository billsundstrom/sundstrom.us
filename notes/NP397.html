[I051456]
Maxine Gutzler went to be with the Lord Friday, Dec. 5, 2008, at the age of 86. She was surrounded by family who loved her.
Maxine was born Nov. 18, 1922, in Algona, Iowa, to John and Anna Nordstrom. She moved to Portland in 1938 at the age of 16. She started working at Jantzen Knitting Mills in 1942, where she would remain for 43 years.

Edna Alberta NORDSTROM

[I051457]
Funeral services for Edna A. Potrats, 93, of Whittemore were held Saturday, March 15, 2014, at St. Paul's Lutheran Church in Whittemore with Rev. Jason Peterson officiating. Interment followed at St. Paul's Lutheran Cemetery in Whittemore.
Edna A. Potratz was born Nov. 23, 1920, at Algona, to John and Anna (Isakson) Nordstrom. She attended Algona schools and was married to Donald Teeter. The couple lived in Algona and Portland, Ore., before Don died in 1951. After his death, Edna worked at the Johnson House in Algona and began a side business of baking and decorating wedding cakes.
On Oct. 30, 1959, Edna married Martin 'Butch' Potratz in Denison, and the couple lived in Whittemore. Edna continued her cake business for nearly 50 years. She was a long time member of St. Paul's Lutheran Church in Whittemore, a past president of Fun and Fancy Cake Club, a member of the Hospital Auxiliary in Algona and a chair of the Cancer Society in Whittemore. She was an avid spoon collector and traveled often.
Butch died March 17, 2004. In 2008, Edna's daughter Barb retired and returned to Whittemore to live with her mother.
Edna died at her home in Whittemore on Tuesday evening, March 11, 2014, at the age of 93.
Survivors include her children: Kevin Potratz and wife, Lia, of Norwalk; Marijean Carlson of Whittemore; Thomas Teeter and wife, Patricia Hanauer of Farmington, Minn.; Barbara Teeter of Whittemore; and Dale Teeter and wife, Barb, of Algona; as well as eight grandchildren: Randy Carlson; Brett Carlson; Pam Carlson-Gugel; Donald Teeter; Jason Teeter; Louise Teeter; Jacob Potratz and Kylie Potratz ; 12 great-grandchildren: Carrie Carlson, John Carlson, Alec Carlson, Ross Carlson, Collin Carlson, Mattias Gugel, Gillian Gugel, Drew Teeter, Noah Teeter, Quentin Teeter, Ronan Teeter and Silas Teeter; and four great-great-grandchildren: Kiwi Carlson, Raelyn Carlson, Geneva Carlson and Eva Carlson.
Also surviving is one brother: Paul Nordstrom and wife, Pat, of Palm Springs, Calif. Edna was preceded in death by her parents, five brothers, seven sisters and two husbands.
Oakcrest Funeral Services was in charge of arrangements. Online condolences may be left at www.oakcrestfuneralservices.com.
The Algona Upper Des Moines, 25 March 2014

Jack's nick name was Nick, which he got when he was born from hospital staff.
After his parents' separation, he was raised by a number of family members.
During WWII, he enlisted in the Army Air Corps and became a co-pilot. During his last wartime mission, he was flying tank parts to General Patton when his planed was shot down. He was interred in a German POW camp in France until he escaped.
He became a civil engineer after graduating from WSU and worked as the county engineer for Skamania and Klickitat Counties. He retired in 1982 and spent a number of years traveling.
