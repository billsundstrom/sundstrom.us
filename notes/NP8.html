Willard Arlen Amick, 81, of Willits passed away on January 15, 2016. Willard was born January 3, 1935 to Willard Amick and Caroline Amick in Portersville, California.
Willard married Mary Jordan on March 13, 1960 in Reno. They were married 56 years. Willard worked at both Willits Redwood and the Harwood mill for a combined 40 years before retiring. His co-workers always called him "cockroach."
Willard enjoyed hunting, fishing and camping with his family. He also enjoyed tinkering on cars and doing woodworking at his home. Willard was preceded in death by both parents, Willard and Caroline; his two brothers, Oz and Louie; as well as his three daughters: Sally Ann Amick, Mary Sue Marsh, and Vicky Schmidt.

Beverly Diane ANDERSON

Beverly Diane Blake (nee Anderson) passed away peacefully on June 13, 2015 in Tacoma, WA, at the age of 72.
Beverly is survived by her children, Dean Pollock of Tacoma, WA, Jody Schumacher (Gary) of Vallejo, CA, and Paul Blake (Buffi), of Olympia, WA. She had 5 grandchildren; Amanda Hanifan (Ryan), Savannah Pollock, Marshall Blake, Natalie Blake, Carlie Blake, and 2 great grandchildren; Teagan Hanifan and Aedan Hanifan.
Beverly is preceded in death by her parents, Ray and Muriel Anderson of Tacoma, WA, her son Steven Pollock, of Tacoma, WA, son, John Pollock III, of Tacoma, WA, and her grandsons Steven and John Blake, of Olympia, WA.
Beverly was born on September 23, 1942 in Bremerton, WA to Ray and Muriel Anderson. She graduated Lincoln High School in 1960. Beverly gave 24 years of service to Boeing, where she was a Lead Machinist.
Beverly was a very talented seamstress and quilt maker. She quilted, embroidered, and enjoyed donating her creations to various organizations who were able to spread her love to much of Tacoma. Beverly created several thousand unique Christmas stockings for Boeing, who used them to provide Christmas cheer to thousands of disadvantaged youth. Her love for the art of sewing and sewing for charity was nurtured and passed along to subsequent generations.
A memorial service is scheduled for Saturday, June 20, at 7:30 PM at Fergie's on the Ave, McKinley Ave E, Tacoma, WA 98404. All are welcome to attend and celebrate Beverly's life.

FORESTVILLE - Jean Skelly, age 67, of King Road, Forestville, died Saturday, September 27th, 2008 at TLC/HN-Lake Shore Hospital, Irving, following a short illness. She was born in Jersey Shore, PA on July 1, 1941, the daughter of the late Arthur James and Sarah (Soloma) Anderson.
Mrs. Skelly was a Cafeteria Worker for Forestville Central School for 9 years. At one time, she had worked seasonally for the former Red Wing Co., Fredonia, had worked for the former Lake Shore Hospital as a volunteer and was an Amway distributor since the 1970's.
Mrs. Skelly was a member of Forestville United Methodist Church, where she was a Lay Leader and provided both the congregation and the community with many of her homemade "Love Pies". She was a past member of the former Forestville Fire Department Ladies Auxiliary, was a former avid bowler, bingo & card player and reader. Mrs. Skelly also enjoyed crossword puzzles and spending time at the family camp in Pennsylvania.
Survivors include her husband Charles J. Skelly of Forestville, whom she married on June 25, 1960 in the South Dayton Free Methodist Church, two daughters, Debra Skinner and Romona Skelly, both of Forestville, three sons Sam (Tammy) Skelly of Sheridan, Charlie (Kim) Skelly of Brocton, Michael Paul Skelly of Forestville, three sisters, Barb McGinnis of Beaufort, NC, Jennie Lou Wertz of Manassas, VA, Mrs. James (Kathy) Coffee of Stock-bridge, GA, a brother, Jim Anderson of Port Matilda, PA and a half brother, Ken McCollough of Haneyville, PA. Also surviving are 6 grandchildren, 4 great grandchildren and several nieces & nephews.
Besides her parents she was predeceased by a sister Shirley Anderson, by a grandson Chadd Skelly, by a great grandson Dylan Skinner and by a son-in-law Kenneth Skinner.
