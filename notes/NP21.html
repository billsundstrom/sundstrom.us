Willard Baldus, of rural Montevideo, MN died on Tuesday, September 8, 2009 at his home seven days short of his 90th birthday. He moved with his family at the age of 8 to Oakland, MN and later graduated from high school in Austin, MN. On October 5, 1941 he was united in marriage to Genevieve Dalen at Immanuel Lutheran Church near Watson, MN. He was a member of the Watson Lions Club and while in Owatonna was a member of the Eagles, Elks, was involved with Civil Defense and the Owatonna Diving Club. He enjoyed building, even doing his own electrical and plumbing. Bill finished the houses they lived in. Much of his life was spent as a truck driver and maintenance man. His favorite hobby as a young man and father was projectionist at local theaters. Family camping was his favorite summer pastime while his children were growing up. The highlight of his 25 year career as a Watkins, Inc. Representative was a trip to Portugal with his wife in 1990. He is survived by his wife, Genevieve: four children: Caryle(and Bruce) Clifford of Watson, MN; Donna (and Lee) Dale of Colorado; Rodney Baldus (and best friend Dawn Christie) and Gayle (and Kenneth) Snow Jr., all of Owatonna, MN; nine grandchildren; fourteen great grandchildren; a brother, Orville Baldus of Owatonna, MN; as well as by a number of nieces and nephews. Looked upon as extended family are Gary and Carolyn Mens, Les and Heidi Hoover and several others Bill and Gen took under their wings while living in Owatonna. He was preceded in death by his parents; brothers: Irwin, Russell, Arnold and Howard Baldus and by sisters: Elizabeth Coffman, Inez Doggett and Naomi Baldus

Sarah BALDWIN

[I006885]
According to "Field Genealogy," Sarah (Baldwin) (Searle) Edwards was a daughter of Sylvester Baldwin who died on the ship "Martin" while travelling to New England. His widow settled at New Haven then Milford, CT with her children. However, Savage and the "Register," discussing the will of Benjamin Fenn, indicates that Sarah Baldwin, daughter of Sylvester Baldwin, married, as his first wife, Benjamin Fenn. Given that John Searle was of Springfield, MA and Alexander Edwards was of Northampton, it seems less likely that this Sarah Baldwin, residentof CT, was daughter of Sylvester.

Perhaps this Sarah was sister of Joseph Baldwin, an early settler of Milford, CT. He and his wife were admitted as members of the church there 23 June 1644 and, in about 1663, Joseph and his wife removed to Hadley.

According to website "Baldwin Family History" by Stephen Chinn and A Supplement to the Baldwin Genealogy by Charles Candee Baldwin provided by Judy Burritt: [burritt@@cfw.com]: Sarah, daughter of Richard Baldwin & IsabelHarding b. 25 Jun 1621 in Aston Clinton, Buckinghamshire, England; m. (1) JohnSearle on 19 Mar 1639 in Springfield, MA; d.3 Oct 1690 in Northampton, MA.

Richard, son of Richard Baldwin & Isabel Chase b.15 Jun 1576 in Aston Clinton; m. Isabel Harding on 15 May 1598 in Buckinghamshire, England; d. bef. 16 May 1633 in Aston Clinton.

Richard, son of Richard Edward Baldwin & Ellen Apuke b.1540 in Aston Clinton; m. Isabel Chase abt 1575 in Aston Clinton; d. there on18Feb 1632.

FORT EDWARD - Wilfred "Grumpy" Baldwin , 83, passed away following a courageous battle with cancer on Sunday, June 28, 2009, at the Indian River Nursing Facility in Granville.

"Bill" served our country in the U.S. Army during World War II. He was a member of the 25th Division and saw action in the Philippines. After the war, he enjoyed his time stationed in Japan as part of the American occupation forces.
He retired from Ciba-Geigy in 1987, following more than thirty years as an employee.
When he wasn't tinkering around in his garage, "Bill" loved to ride his scooter around town, especially enjoying his social trips to Stewarts. "Bill" also liked going to garage sales and, he was forever purchasing "As seen on TV" items since he always seemed to "need one of those."

Stanley Crittenden BALL

[I070237]
Stanley Crittenden Ball (b. November 19, 1885; d. August 9, 1956) was born in Shelburne Falls, Massachusetts, and educated at Yale University (Ph.B., Sheffield Scientific School, 1911; Ph.D., 1915). After brief appointments at the Massachussetts Agricultural College in Amherst, Springfield (Massachusetts) College, and the Bernice Pauahi Bishop Museum in Honolulu, Hawaii, in 1926 he returned permanently to Yale where he taught biology courses - especially in entomology and ornithology - and curated the zoology collections until his retirement in 1954.
An all-round zoologist, Ball is now best known for his studies of the spadefoot toad (Scaphiopus holbrookii) and autumn bird migration on the Gaspé peninsula, Quebec. Not so well known is his primary role in planning and supervising the construction of all vertebrate and most of the invertebrate exhibits mounted in the Yale Peabody Museum during his tenure, most notably the exquisite wildlife dioramas illustrating Connecticut environments that are permanently on view in the Hall of Southern New England.

tears went into that place Teddie loved so much. She and Wally worked long hours
to completely develop the property for a home site by bringing in water, power
and a road.
Teddie worked many different jobs. She drove a pilot car, worked as a teller
and loan officer in a couple of different banks and was a ticket agent for an
airline. Her most accomplished and satisfying work that she did was work as a
case worker with the Department of Health and Welfare in Bonners Ferry, Idaho.

fairness that made her a success in this field. Teddie never longed much for
material things she always said a home is where you can hang your hat. She was

fishing, picking huckleberries, and long drives around her favorite mountain,
Queen Mtn. Teddie made many accomplishments in her life, but one that she was so
