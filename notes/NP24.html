Edith Mabry BARNETT

[I024464]
SENECA FALLS - Edith Barnett Delavan, 81, passed away April 30, 2011 after fighting declining health at Geneva General Hospital. Family and friends may call from 5 to 7 p.m. Thursday (May 5) at the Sanderson-Moore Funeral Home, Inc., 32 State St., Seneca Falls. Memorial services and Interment in Restvale Cemetery will be at the convenience of the Family.
In lieu of flowers kindly consider contributions to the Fingerlakes Medical Associates, 200 North St. Geneva, N.Y. 14456.
Edith was the only child born to Edith Mabry and Edward in Birmingham, Alabama. She resided during her high school years in New York City, graduating from the Dalton School and then from Vassar College with honors in 1949. She and Nelson were married in 1950, and lived for a few years in Berea, Kentucky. In 1956, they made their home in the Finger Lakes area. After teaching in Interlaken Central School, Edith taught high school English at Ithaca High School from 1963 to 1983. Additionally, she taught some classes at Cornell University from 1985 to 1988. Edith and Nelson moved to their Cayuga Lake home outside of Seneca Falls in 1986, becoming involved with many different activities in retirement. She was an avid photographer and fiber artist, exhibiting her work at many shows and galleries.
She enjoyed traveling and also wrote books of local interest such as "Landmarks of Seneca County." Edith and Carol Serling collaborated on a book called "Grace in the Afternoon," and Edith and Nelson combined her photographs and his poems in a book called "Reflections." Edith joined her husband in community involvement by exploring, sharing and preserving the history of the county and the Finger lakes area. Together they worked on the Delavan Foundation, established by Elizabeth Garnsey Delavan, in honor of Nelson's father. Edith organized ESCAPE by the Lake, a group that presented performances of cultural worth and entertainment value. To highlight the history of the Finger Lakes area, Edith and Nelson established the Seneca Museum of Waterways and Industry.
She is survived by her husband, Nelson B. Delavan; daughters Anne LaFitte of Cortland, Deborah Chatham, Rochester, Edie (George) Steele, Rochester, and Catherine Claire Hartfield, Florida; six grandchildren: Scott, Matthew Chatham, a Sarah, Rachel LaFitte, Benjamin, Richard Steele.
The family wishes to express gratitude to the Lipker family, who were deeply involved in Edith's care, and provided it with compassion and grace. Kathy Lankford ably assisted Edith with many of her projects over the years. There are also many devoted caregivers to whom the family is most indebted for their cheerful flexibility and dedication. Edith's doctors and nurses at Geneva General Hospital are also thanked for their many efforts on her behalf.
Finger Lakes Times, 2 May 2011

Floyd BARQUIST

[I002004]
YAKIMA - Floyd Barquist , 90 of Yakima died here in Yakima April 17, 2010. He was born October 25, 1919 in Osakis, Minnesota to Andrew Peter and Everine (Johnson) Barquist . He came with his family to Enumclaw, WA in 1937.
He attended Western Washington College of Education for two years. He was married to Ruby Larson in 1940 and together they raised four children. He started working as a Mold-Lofts man with a shipbuilding company during WWII. Later he worked as a meat cutter and went on to co-own the Auburn, Washington Super Market in 1946. He and Ruby moved to Yakima, WA in 1953, here he was a partner in a cattle feed lot and a grocery store meat market. In 1956 he assumed management of the meat division and frozen food department for Yakima Grocery. This later became the Continental Foods where Floyd was the meat merchandising manager for Super Duper and the Lucky dollar Stores. In 1964 they moved to Los Angeles, CA where he worked as a meat buyer for certified grocers of California for two years. They returned to Yakima and opened Barquist 's County Meats specializing in custom cutting and retail meat sales, and managed for National Grocery that was later bought by Noel Foods and Floyd retired from the Noel Food Corporation in 1980. Six months later he went back to work as a sales representative for the Kelly-Clark food brokers and retired from there in 1987. He and Ruby also owned and operated 12 apartments from 1968 to 1983.
When he was not working, he loved to fish, golf, play the accordion, sing with the Camerata Club and travel with Ruby. But most of all, he loved spending time with his children and extended family.
Surviving family include his wife Ruby Barquist of Yakima, WA, son Norman Barquist of Bainbridge Island, WA, daughters; Carol Hixson, of Yakima, WA; Lisa (Michael) Miller of Clearwater, FL; Beth (Gary) Mack of Battle Ground, WA. 7 grandchildren and 17 great grandchildren.
He was preceded in death by his parents, siblings; Carl, Bill, Arthur, Ernie, Viola, Esther and one great grandson Matthew Murray.
A celebration of life will be held on Sunday, May 16, 2010 from 2:00 pm to 4:00 PM in the Orchard Park Activity room, located at 620 N. 34th Avenue, Yakima, WA. Cremation arrangements are under the direction of Keith & Keith Funeral Home.
Yakima Herald-Republic, 25 April 2010

George S. BARR

[I068693]
George S. Barr, 90, died Wednesday, December 26, 2012 at Keystone Nursing Care Center in Keystone. Funeral Services will be held at 10:30 AM, Thursday, January 3, 2012 at The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints in Belle Plaine with President Rick L. Sparks officiating. Interment will be in Pleasant Hill Cemetery in Blairstown with graveside military rites conducted by the M.J. Kirby American Legion Post #170 of Blairstown. Visitation will be from 4 7 PM, Wednesday, January 2, 2012 at Phillips Funeral Home in Blairstown. In lieu of flowers a memorial fund has been established. George was born December 3, 1922 at San Antonio, Texas to George S. and Violet Elliott Barr. At a very early age his parents died and he was raised by his grandparents in Southern California. In 1939 George was united in marriage to Erma Coffelt at Lopez Island, Washington. George and Erma had two children, Roger and George and were later divorced in 1944. George served his country in the United States Navy for three years on board the aircraft carrier USS Saratoga in the South Pacific and was honorably discharged in 1946. Between 1946 and 1951 he lived in the Southern California, primarily in the Whittier area. He was united in marriage to Leota Pearson in 1949. Leota preceded George in death in January of 1999. Between 1949 and 1951 George worked at various jobs including as a cab driver, fry cook, waiter, maitred, insurance sales and as a bartender. In 1951 he moved to Blairstown and worked for five years as a tank wagon driver selling and delivering gas and fuel oil. In 1956 he moved back to California and worked nine years as an insurance claims adjuster, primarily in claims involving bodily injury. The last four years as a claims adjuster he worked only with claims that were in litigation. During those nine years he received his GED from Whittier High School and then took the College Equivalency Test given by the California State Bar Association. George was then allowed to enroll in the Southwestern Law School. Upon graduation he took and passed the California State Bar Exam. In 1968 he went to work for the Los Angeles County Public Defenders Office as a Deputy Public Defender, and continued working there for seventeen years retiring in 1984. Following his retirement, George was asked to return to work as a Municipal Court Judge in the Los Angeles area for several years. In 1989 George moved to Blairstown and established a Self-Storage business in Blairstown. In 2002 George was united in marriage to Mary Root in Blairstown. George was a member of the M.J. Kirby American Legion Post #170 in Blairstown, where he held several offices, the Blairstown Public Library Board, the Blairstown Community Foundation and the Blairstown Community Development Corporation. He also founded the Blairstown Investment Club in 1995 and has been the Treasurer of it since its inception. George is survived by his wife, Mary of Keystone; sons, George S. Barr of Lopez Island, Washington and Roger C. (Kerry) Barr of Hermiston, Oregon; eight grandchildren, twelve great grandchildren and one great grandchild and several great neighbors. In addition to his parents, first wife, Erma, second wife, Leota, George was preceded in death by three sisters.

In the family there were four children, Agnes, James, Thomas J. and Anna. After the death of Thomas Barrie, his wife married James Nichols, and this union resulted in one son, John. Mr. and Mrs. Nicholas brought the five children to the United States and located in Union Grove, Meeker county, this state. Later they moved to Paynesville, and still later to California. Thomas J. Barrie devoted his early years to farming. At about the age of thirty he became a barber. First he worked at Bird Island, Renville county, this state, and later at Paynesville. He has done good service as a member of the school board, and belongs to the Masonic Order, the Woodmen and the Modern Brotherhood. Mr. Barrie married Nellie Kingsley, daughter of Quartus Kingsley, and their children are Fred, Nellie, Alice, Howard and Etta.
