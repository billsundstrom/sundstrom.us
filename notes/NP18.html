Clarence R. BABB

[I070606]
HADLEY - Clarence R. "Chuck" Babb , 79, died Monday, Nov. 11, 2013, at Cooley Dickinson Hospital.
He was born Oct. 14, 1934, in Hadley, son of the late Walter R. and Gladys E. (Murray) Babb .
Chuck was a 1953 graduate of Hopkins Academy; he served in the United States Army in Germany and served on the Amherst Fire call force. He joined the Amherst Police Department in 1956, and served the force until 1988, when he retired as a Lieutenant.
Chuck took great pride in the fact that he served his country and his local community. Chuck was a master mason at the Pacific Lodge of Masons in Amherst, a lifetime member of Amherst VFW Post 754, and a member of the Hadley Young Men's Club. He was also an avid sports fan, dedicated to all of the local teams.
He is survived by two sons, Raymond and his wife Melanie of Hadley, and Richard and his wife Connie of Louisiana, and their mother Marjorie Babb . His true passion was the love and pride he had for his four grandchildren, Marisa, Mackenzie, Richard and Andrew.
His "sister", Eleanor Davis Wolff predeceased him.
The family would like to offer a special thanks to Dr. Mohamed Kamel for his many years of excellent care and personal attention.
Funeral Services will be held Monday, Nov. 18, at 10 a.m. at the Douglass Funeral Home in Amherst, followed by burial in Plainville Cemetery in Hadley.
Calling hours at the funeral home will be Sunday, Nov. 17, from 3 to 6 p.m.
In lieu of flowers, memorial gifts may be made to either The Jimmy Fund, the Shriners Hospital in Springfield, or The Amherst Police Relief Association, c/o APD, 111 Main St., Amherst, MA 01002.

Arthur James BACON

[I083002]
Arthur J. Bacon Jr., 78, died March 15, 2002, at Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center in Lebanon, N.H.
He was born Dec. 23, 1923, in St. Albans, the son of Arthur J. and Bessie J. (LaPorte) Bacon. He received his schooling in Randolph.
He married Elizabeth R. Benson on Oct. 15, 1945, in Brattleboro and moved to Windsor. Mrs. Bacon died in 1992.
He was employed by Miller Construction Co. in Windsor for several years. He later owned and operated Arthur J. Bacon Excavating in Windsor for many years until his retirement.
Following his retirement he volunteered at the Springfield Recycling Center, and later did maintenance work at Okemo Mountain and Ascutney Mountain ski areas. At the time of his death he was employed by Vermont Transit Bus Lines in White River Junction.
He had been a member of the Untied Brotherhood of Carpenters and Joiners of America Union 1996 since 1962.
Survivors include a son, Lloyd G. Bacon of Pinetop, Ariz.; a daughter, Ruth Bacon McCabe of Windsor; a stepson, Herbert Osgood of Las Vegas, Nev.; two brothers, William C. Bacon of Windsor and Robert E. Bacon of West Lebanon, N.H.; a sister, Alice Lee of Florida; five grandchildren; seven great-grandchildren; and several nieces and nephews.
He was predeceased by a brother, Edward R. Bacon; and by four sisters, Arnita Lewis, Della Day, Thelma Page and Mona Erskine.

Roger Bacon b. EST 1165, m. Unknown. Roger, son of George sued by his sister-in-law Agnes, widow of his brother Thomas, for distaining her tenants in Baconsthorpe and Lodue and breaking her park. He raised arms with the barons against the King and had his estates confiscated. His lands were returned by favor of Henry III in 1216. Roger's direct line extends to Robert, Reginald, Richard, Sir Robert, Sir Thomas to John, alias Roger (Friar Bacon) where it ends in 1546. Roger was the progenitor of Bacons of Drinkston and Hessett, Suffolk England.

Stephen J. Bagadinski , Jr., 69, of Jobs Pond Road, Portland, passed away peacefully after a long illness, with his family by his side on Sunday, (December 21, 2008) at Middlesex Health Care Center in Middletown. Born in Middletown on July 13, 1939 to the late Stephen J. and Harriet LeMark Bagadinski , ''John'' was a lifelong resident of Portland, where he and his wife Kathleen Gibbs Bagadinski raised their three children. John served in the army stationed in Germany before marrying Kathie in 1963. He received his Associates Degree from Central Connecticut State University, and worked as a Systems Cost Analyst for Pratt and Whitney in East Hartford for 28 years. After retiring from Pratt, John kept busy by working part time for a medical supply company in Middletown. John leaves behind three children, Stephenie MacKeen and her husband Clint of Glastonbury; Jennifer Reinsch and her husband Christopher of Portland; and Stephen Bagadinski and his wife Alison of Singapore. ''Papa'' also leaves behind his beloved grandchildren, Connor and Morgan Reinsch, and Collin and Caleb MacKeen who will miss him dearly. John also leaves three sisters, Marilyn Rinker and her late husband Robert formerly of Vermont, Mary Louise Pini and her husband Nicholas of Portland; and Natalie Pucci and her husband Eugene of Kensington; several nieces, nephews, great-nieces and great-nephews; as well as many friends he has had since childhood. John loved watching the Yankees, NY Giants, and UCONN games religiously. He had a gift for wood working and made many beautiful things for his children, grandchildren and friends. John also loved the beach and enjoyed many summers on the Cape for family vacations, as well as trips to Hammonassett. He also loved making ''Golden Oldies'' music compilations for his many friends. The family would like to thank the Middlesex Health Care Center for their loving care and support provided to the family.
