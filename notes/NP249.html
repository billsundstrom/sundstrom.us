Thomas Martin HOADLEY

[I033537]
FLEMING - Thomas M. Hoadley, 58, of 5914 Petrie Drive, Fleming, passed into eternal life Tuesday, Dec. 16, 2003, at his home.
Born in Louisville, Ky., he was the son of the late Martin "Jack" and Helen Freay Hoadley. He was a 1963 Mount Carmel High School graduate. In 1973, Tom became founder and owner of Hoadley Plumbing, serving the community for 30 years.
Tom was a former member and 1981-1982, past president of Owasco-Fleming Kiwanis. He was the recipient of the Key Man Award in 1974 and 1977, and the Kiwanis of the Year in 1980. Tom was involved in the Great Race Committee and was an avid snowmobiler and boater.
He is survived by his wife, Margaret "Maggie" West Hoadley; his two sons, Daniel Hoadley of Auburn and Matthew Hoadley of Auburn and his companion Kathleen Casler; a brother, James C. Hoadley of Auburn; a sister, Susan C. Hoadley of Sennett; special cousins, Pete and Paula Hoadley of Owasco; several nieces and nephews; and many dear friends.
In addition to his parents, Tom was predeceased by his twin brother, Timothy J. Hoadley Sr.
Visitation will be from 2 to 4 and 7 to 9 p.m. today, Dec. 18, at Langham Funeral Home, 75 E. Genesee St. Family prayers will be at 9:15 a.m. Friday at the funeral home, followed by a Mass of Christian burial at 10 a.m. in Sacred Heart Church, with the Rev. Jim Enright officiating. Burial will be in St. Joseph's Cemetery, Fleming.
In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Seneca Cayuga ARC Center, 188 North St., Auburn; or to Hospice of the Finger Lakes, 1130 Corporate Drive, Auburn, NY 13021, in memory of Tom.

Nadine Annabelle HOBDY

[I073679]
EAST PEORIA - Nadine Ann Stewart, 80, of East Peoria passed away Friday morning, July 8, 2011, in the emergency room at OSF Saint Francis Medical Center in Peoria.
Ann was born on Sept. 14, 1930, in Mooresville, Ind., to Thomas and Pauline Eaden Hobdy. She married Otho Stewart Jr. in Terre Haute, Ind., on April 10, 1948. Otho died on Jan. 21, 1994, in Peoria.
Among those surviving are one son, Pastor Ronald (Cindy) Stewart of East Peoria; two daughters, Tracey (Stephen) Darche of Eureka and Lisa (Mark) Yemm of Bartonville; two sisters, Beatrice Knight and Lois Moore, both of Peoria; one brother, the Reverend Robert (Dorothy) Hobdy of Creve Coeur; 12 grandchildren; and 16 great-grandchildren.
She also was preceded in death by one sister and three grandchildren.
Ann was a homemaker and assisted in the family business, Stewart's Upholstery, for more than 40 years prior to her husband's death.
She was an active and faithful member of The Journey Church in East Peoria.
Ann was a loving wife, mother, grandmother and sister who was dedicated to her family and church. She loved the simple things in life; family, friends, flowers, pets, her faith in God and people. She was a cornerstone for her family, praying daily for everyone and a prayer warrior when needed by those who were in pain, lost or in need. Her gentle demeanor was loved by everyone. She was a peacemaker who saw conflicts in people's life and reached out in the same gentle manner to see them through it. She saw everything and everyone through rose-colored glasses.
Funeral services will be at The Journey Church in East Peoria on Tuesday at 11 a.m. with Pastor Brock Winkler officiating. Burial will follow in Lutheran Cemetery in Peoria. Visitation will be on Monday from 5 to 8 at Mason Funeral Home in Germantown Hills Chapel and one hour prior to services at the church.
Memorials for Ann may be directed to her church.
Online condolences to her family may be made at www.masonfuneralhomes.com.
Peoria Journal Star, 10 July 2011
