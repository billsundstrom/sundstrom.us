Roy Frederick LEDREW

[I045741]
On Saturday, April 5, 2014, Roy Frederick LeDrew passed away peacefully with grace and dignity holding the hands of his sweetheart Madeline, his beloved wife of 73 years. Roy was born in Grand Falls, N.L. on June 12, 1920. Son of the late John "Jack" LeDrew and his wife, the late Mary Jane Dawe LeDrew, Upper Gullies, N.L. A student of Grand Falls High School, Roy was active in track and field sports, winning a silver medal and a bronze medal in the Labour Day Sports Games July 29, 1935. He was a member of the Guards Baseball Team when they won the Tie Cup Championship in 1936. Roy was the last surviving member of that team. Roy was a past employee of Goodyear and Sons, The Grand Falls Coop Store, The A.N.D. Company Paper Mill, The Canadian Johns Manville Asbestos Mining Company in Asbestos, Que., from where he retired after 36 years. The family lived in Danville, Que. Roy was active in: Trinity United Church, The Boy Scouts Association where he served 15 years on the Group Committee, Cub Master, Gilwel Scout, Assistant District Commissioner. He was a past member of the Grand Falls I.O.O.F. Lodge. Roy retired to Brighton. He was a member of Digby Branch 20 Royal Canadian Legion. He served twice as President and on several committees. He was a dedicated Poppy Campaign worker missing only one year in 22 years. He was awarded a Life Member and the Legion Meritorious Service Medal. He was a Past Commander Zone 1, a Life Member of Cornwallis Heritage Museum Committee, The Navy League of Canada, The Newfoundland Naval Association, appointed a Commissioner of the Supreme Court of Nova Scotia and The Malta George Cross Island Association. When Roy moved to Dartmouth he became an active member of Centennial Branch 160 serving on committees and the Annual Poppy Campaign. He was awarded Veteran of the Month for February 2011. When he moved to Ontario he transferred to the local Legion. Roy was a Royal Navy Veteran of the Second World War (1940-1946). He was an Anti Air Craft Gunner on H M S Ships: Penelope, Montclare, LST427 and a fleet minesweeper, Lioness. He served in the Defense of England, The Battle of The Atlantic, The Battle of Italy, Africa and Malta, and minesweeping the shipping lanes to the United Kingdom. Roy was awarded the 1939-45 Star, Battle of The Atlantic Star, The Italy Star, The Newfoundland Volunteer Service Medal, The Defense Medal, The War Medal, The Queen Golden Jubilee Medal, The 50th Malta George Cross Island Medal, presented by the Maltese Consul General at a ceremony in Toronto, Ont. Roy is survived by his wife, Madeline (Graves); their children and spouses, Rodger (Patricia), Dartmouth; Angela (Mark), Washington; Charlene (Dennis), Washington; five grandchildren; three great-grandchildren; two sisters-in-law and several nieces and nephews, cousins and many friends near and far away. In lieu of flowers, anyone wishing to may make a donation to Chester United Baptist Church or Digby Branch 20 Royal Canadian Legion. Funeral entrusted to Davis Funeral Home, Chester. Visitation in Davis Funeral Home on Sunday, April 13, from 2-4 p.m. Funeral Service on Monday April 14, at 11 a.m. in Chester United Baptist Church. Interment immediately following in Pine Hill Cemetery, Rev. Dan Green officiating. Reception to follow in the church hall.

Dorothy Louise Cooper, 82, passed away peacefully on Monday, February 14th at Sanforf Medical Center.
Dorothy Louise Larson, daughter of Reverend J. Gottfrid and Ardis (Pearson) Larson, was born November 25, 1928 in Litchfield, Minnesota. She graduated in 1945 from Litchfield High School and attended Gustavus Adolphus College in St. Peter, Minnesota.
Following college, she moved to Fort Dodge, Iowa where she met and married her husband Joe Cooper. Dorothy was a stay-at-home mom while her daughters were young, attending their needs and activities.
A strong supporter of the Sioux Falls Community Playhouse, she volunteered countless hours managing "behind the scene" reservations, memberships, box office, scheduling, and ushers. She also was named recipient of the Herb Bechtold Award.
In 1976, Dorothy was hired at Crippled Childrenʼs Hospital (now Childrenʼs Care) as Administrative Assistant in the Business and Finance Office where she served for 13 years until retirement.
Dorothy loved the outdoors especially the summers spending time with her children, grandchildren and the great grandchildren. Her greatest joy was her love of family. That love was returned when her grandchildren often called her by name "Grandma Darling."
Blessed for having shared her life are her husband of 59 years, Joe, and four daughters, Connie (Don) Altman Sr., Sioux Falls, Debra (Curt) Davey, Renner, SD, Jill (Dave) Bockorny, Sioux Falls, Melissa (Brian) Connot, Sioux Falls, 21 grandchildren, 24 great grandchildren, 3 great-great grandchildren, and a very special friend, Bonnie Johnson, Blaine, MN.

[I090013]
Ella St. Claire Lee Mays , 89, resident of Chinquapin, widow of Calhoun Allen Mays , Jr., died Sunday, March 5, 2017, at Abbeville Nursing Home.
Born in Greenwood, July 28, 1927, she was a daughter of the late Fitzhugh Lee and Anne Greene Lee. Raised in what would later be considered the historic district of Greenwood on Cambridge Street, she considered that to be "her most favorite block in the entire universe." She attended Leslie Elementary, and graduated both Greenwood High School and Lander College (University).
Her autobiography includes this description of her life: "After being married for over 63 years and having two children and one daughter-in-law, whom I consider to be my second daughter that I always wanted, 5 charming granddaughters and 4 great-grandchildren -- I would consider these facts as proof that Calhoun's and my union was a happy one."
A member of First Presbyterian Church of Greenwood, she was also an active person for most of her life, enjoying horseback riding, tennis, golf, bridge and other sports and teaching them to others.
She is survived by her son, Calhoun Allen Mays , III and his wife, Cathy Faulkner Mays ; her daughter, Anne Mays Clinkscales; five granddaughters, Hunter Mays Ward and husband Jason Ward, Lee Mays Varner and husband Daniel Varner, Alison Ruth Mays , St. Claire Lee Clinkscales and Sarah Catherine Clinkscales; and four great-grandchildren, Harrison Ward, Kate Ward, Daniel Varner, Jr. and Charlie Varner.
Memorial services will be conducted at 3 p.m. Sunday, March 12, at First Presbyterian Church of Greenwood, with Rev. Dr. Kyle Hite officiating.
Family members will be at their respective homes and will receive friends in Alexander Hall immediately following the service.
In lieu of flowers, memorials may be made to the Greenwood Soup Kitchen, 929 Phoenix Street, Greenwood, SC 29646; or the Humane Society of Greenwood, PO Box 49776, Greenwood, SC 29646.
The family would like to extend a heartfelt thank you to the staff and nurses of the Abbeville Nursing Home for their personal care and kindnesses conveyed to Mr. and Mrs. Mays during their stay.

Leona M. LEE

[I092789]
Leona M. "Lee" Mehls , 98, formerly of 44 E. Elm St., Chippewa Falls, died Sunday, Oct. 6, 2002, at the Rutledge Home in Chippewa Falls.
She was born July 21, 1904, in Onalaska to Laura (Peterson) and Peter Lee. She grew up in Onalaska, where she was baptized and confirmed at the Evangelical Lutheran Church.
Lee graduated from Onalaska High School in 1921 and graduated from La Crosse Normal School in 1924. She continued her education, attending Berkeley University in California, Columbia University in New York, and the University of Wisconsin in Eau Claire.
Leona married Carl F. "Hoot" Mehls on May 31, 1935, by Pastor C. E. Skoien at Central Lutheran Church in Chippewa Falls.
Lee taught physical education and dance for many years in Chippewa Falls. She then taught physical education at the University of Wisconsin-Eau Claire in 1955; in the Thorp Public Schools from 1955 to 1962; at McDonell High School in 1963; and at Chippewa Falls Junior High School from 1964 to 1970, when she retired. She was inducted into the Cardinal Athletic Hall of Fame at Chippewa Falls Senior High School in May 1984.
She was a member of Central Lutheran Church in Chippewa Falls; a 50-year member of Chippewa Falls Chapter #112 Order of the Eastern Star; a charter member of the Chippewa Shrine Auxilitary; a member of Amaranth; Chippewa Valley Retired Teachers; and A. A. Retired Persons.
Survivors include: her two sons, Charles (Anne) Mehls of New Richmond, and William (Kathy) Mehls of Chippewa Falls; six grandchildren, Mary Beth Raddatz, Peter Mehls , and David Mehls all of New Richmond, Daniel Mehls of Stillwater, Minn., and Christopher and Cassandra Mehls of Chippewa Falls; three great-grandchildren, Scott, Randy and Gretchen Raddatz, all of New Richmond.
She was preceded in death by: her husband on April 13, 1990; her parents; and one brother, Alton Lee. Funeral services will be held at 1 p.m., Thursday, Oct. 10, at Central Lutheran Church in Chippewa Falls, with the Rev. Rick Hoyme officiating. Burial will follow at Forest Hill Cemetery, Chippewa Falls.
Friends may call one hour prior to the service Thursday at the church.
Memorials in Leona 's memory may be directed to the Chippewa Valley Family Y.M.C.A., the Chippewa Falls Education Foundation, or Central Lutheran Church.
The Chippewa Herald, 7 October 2002

FRANK LEEPER, 90, DIES AT DEXTER
Funeral services for Frank Leeper were held last Thursday, Aug. 16 at the Penn Center Methodist Church, south of Dexter.
Mr. Leeper died Aug. 14th at the Dexter hospital. He was 90 years of age.
Frank Allen Leeper, eldest son of George and Elizabeth Lenocker Leeper, was born May 16, 1872 in Penn township. He was raised on the farm and spent his life in farming, living in the area where he was born. He helped to build the present church at Penn Center, where his membership has been ever since his youth. For many years he served on the official board of the church and was active in its organization.
He was united in marriage Oct. 18, 1899 with Lillian E. Morford. Mr. and Mrs. Leeper observed their 60th wedding anniversary in 1959.
He was preceded in death by his parents, three sisters, an infant son, and three brothers. He is survived by his wife, Lillian; two sons: Merle J. Leeper of Earlham and Claire F. Leeper of Winterset; one daughter, Mrs. Edmond Clarke of Stuart; seven grandchildren; ten great-grandchildren; two sisters: Mrs. Essie Moore of Dexter, and Mrs. Orville Maulsby of Chicago, Ill.
