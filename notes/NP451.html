Anna K. RICE

PENDLETON - The family and friends of Anna K. Selover may call at Bishop Funeral Home between 9 a.m. and 5 p.m. Tuesday, Sept. 20, for viewing and visitation. A celebration of life graveside service for Mrs. Selover will be held at 11 a.m. Wednesday, Sept. 21, at Olney Cemetery with vault interment.
Mrs. Selover, 92, of Pendleton, died Friday, Sept. 16, 2005, at Cant Adult Foster Care Home in Pendleton.
She and her twin brother, Arthur, were born July 29, 1913, on a homestead near Fort Rock, to Charles and Elizabeth Rice. She attended school at Lebanon, and graduated from high school in 1931. She graduated from business school at Portland then worked at Portland and Eugene.
On Dec. 7, 1947, she married Robert Selover at Eugene. They moved to Portland where Mr. Selover was employed at the Weather Bureau. In 1954, they made a permanent move to Pendleton to raise their family. Mr. Selover was employed by the U.S. Forest Service as a radio technician and Mrs. Selover was a homemaker.
The couple were active in the Rocket Squares dance club for many years and were members of the Presbyterian Church.
Mrs. Selover enjoyed making latch hook rugs, sewing, reading, camping, and pinochle.
Survivors include her daughters, Margie Gaboury and Jeanne King and her husband, Mike, all of Pendleton; grandchildren, Amanda Gaboury, Kevin Gaboury, Wendy King, and Cody King; several nieces and nephews.
She was preceded in death by her parents, by her husband, Robert, and by five brothers and a sister.
Memorial contributions may be made to Hospice, or a charity of choice.
Bishop Funeral Chapel of Pendleton is in charge of arrangements.
East Oregonian, 19 September 2005

Mrs. Sorter died Wednesday, Sept. 5, 2012, at the Grace Living Center in Clinton, after a long battle with breast cancer. She was born April 1, 1939, in Severance, Kan., the daughter of Howard and Ruth (Soden) Rice.
She was the second of five children and was reared in Robinson, Kan., before moving to Wichita, Kan., as a teenager where she graduated from Sacred Heart Academy (a Catholic all-girl high school) in 1957.
Mrs. Sorter moved for a brief time to Pasadena, Calif., but returned to Wichita, Kan., to work in a variety of clothing retail stores.
She relocated to Oklahoma in the mid-1960s and settled in Clinton where she was employed at both at the Best Buy Turkey Plant and the Kellwood Factory.
In her later years she worked in a local pet store and owned and operated her own dog grooming service. She was also the owner and operator of a local clothing store for women as well as a lounge on Frisco Avenue.
Mrs. Sorter was a life-time member of the VFW Ladies Auxiliary and proud member of the Republican Party.
Preceding her in death were her parents; a sister, Sharon Rice; and a nephew, Terry Rice Sr.
Survivors include a son, Randy Sorter, Denver, Colo.; two brothers, Jerome Rice, Golf, Kan., and Roger Rice, Clinton; a sister, Phyllis Branch, Rayville, La,; as well as several nieces and nephews, great nieces and nephews and great-great nieces and nephews. Frederick RICHARDS

[I088701]
CHELSEA -- Frederick "Fred" Richards, 70, of Hallowell Road died Thursday, Jan. 10, 2008, at MaineGeneral Medical Center in Augusta.
He was born in Newton, Mass., on Aug. 14, 1937, the son of Langdon and Beatrice (O'Leary) Richards.
Fred attended Cony High School. He later joined the Navy and retired after 20 years of service. He served on the USS Perry, USS Hornet and the USS Puget Sound. After his retirement from the Navy, Fred worked in the kitchen at the Veterans Hospital at Togus and also at Bernards Foodland.
Fred had a great love for the art of photography and he wanted to share it with as many people as he could. In order to do this, Fred established the Capital Area Camera Club in 1990 as a forum where local photographers, whether amateurs or professionals, could gather and learn about photography by sharing photographic techniques, having discussions and viewing each others' work. Fred served as the club's president for several years and continued to be a member of the club until his passing. During those many years, he freely shared both his knowledge and his love of photography with the members, and his encouragement and enthusiasm were greatly appreciated.
Fred was a great photographer who was on a continual quest for knowledge. He loved trying new approaches in his work and was never one to be bound by rules when it came to his photography. He studied with a number of internationally acclaimed photographers, most notably Freeman Patterson and Andre Gallant, whom he greatly admired. Freeman once said, "The camera sees both ways." This is very evident when you look at Fred's photographs, as each image shows you the world through Fred's eyes.
Fred left a large and wonderful body of work for us to enjoy, and through that work, Fred will always be with us.
He is survived by his wife, Diana Richards, of Chelsea; a son, Kurt Richards, of Augusta; three daughters, Kandi Waits, of Augusta, Kelly Richards, of Gardiner and Kim Church and her husband, Jeff, of Farmingdale; two brothers, Langdon Richards Jr., of China Village, and John Richards, of North Whitefield; a sister, Judith Blethen and her husband, Harold, of East Dennis, Mass.; and nine grandchildren, Haven, Kove, Liam, Clay, Matthew, Benjamin, Emily, Laura and special granddaughter Jessica. He also is survived by a brother-in-law, Charles Poulin and his wife, Mary Jane, of Waterville; his father-in-law, Alfred Poulin, of Winslow; and several nieces, nephews and cousins.
Fred also will be sadly missed by his beloved dog, Tara, and all his friends at Dunkin' Donuts.
There will be no visiting hours. A memorial celebration of life will be held at 11 a.m. on Wednesday, Jan. 16, at Knowlton and Hewins Funeral Home and Cremation Service, 1 Church St., Augusta. Burial will follow in the Maine Veterans' Memorial Cemetery, Mount Vernon Road, Augusta.
In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the Kennebec Valley Humane Society, 10 Pet Haven Lane, Augusta, ME 04330.
Published in Central Maine, 14 to January 2008
