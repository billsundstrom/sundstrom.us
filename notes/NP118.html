Gerald Robert DAVIS

Gerald "Bob" Davis , 76 of Nevada, Iowa passed away on Wednesday, October 9, 2013 at the Israel Family Hospice House in Ames.
Funeral services were held on Saturday October 12, 2013, at Mitchell Family Funeral Home in Marshalltown. Memorials may be directed towards the Israel Family Hospice House, Nevada FFA or to Quakerdale. Mitchell Family Funeral Home cared for Bob and his family.
Bob Davis was born on December 15, 1936, in Marshalltown, Iowa, to Ralph and Ruth Ware Davis . On July 21, 1955; Bob was united in marriage to Vieve Galloway and they later divorced in 1987. Bob was enlisted in the United States Army from 1955 until 1957 where he was stationed at Fort Campbell Kentucky. After the Army; Bob attended Iowa State University for one year for Agriculture and then went on to farm. On December 27, 1988; Bob married his second wife Janice Heiberger; they were only married six short months before she would pass away. Bob's interests and activities were many. He loved riding his bike and attending "Bike Night". He loved to travel and went on seven trips to Germany and Amsterdam; he would go to Mexico for two weeks out of every year; he enjoyed his annual fishing trip to Michigan and he took his whole family on a cruise. Bob even loved his many trips to Maid Rite, Applebee's and his daily trips to Dairy Queen. Even after retirement Bob continued to farm.
Left to cherish his memory are his children: Kindra (Barry) Jones of Nevada, Kraig Davis of Phoenix, Arizona, Kyla Davis of Des Moines and Keith (Denise) Davis of Nevada. Grandchildren: Amber and Ashley Jones, Lauren and Austin Davis , Zach and Kolin Davis and Brad Flanagan. Great grandchild: Kaine Jones. Siblings: James Davis (Francine) of Scottsdale, Arizona, Beverly (Allan) Everist of Scottsdale, Arizona, Nancy (Neal) McLemore of Kent, Washington, Ralph (Judy) Davis of Glenwood, Iowa and Jaynane Aiken of Mount Vernon, WA. And a very special companion Merna Appelgate of Minneapolis, Minnesota.
Bob is preceded in death by his parents, his second wife Janice and a niece Cindie Davis Nevada Journal, 16 October 2013

TIMBERLAKE, Lillie SHEPARD MUNFORD TIMBERLAKE Lillie Shepherd Davis Timberlake was born in Decatur, Georgia August 25, 1918, the daughter of Robert Franklin Davis of Stevens Pottery, Georgia and Lillie Mae Shepherd of Madison, Georgia. Shep was raised in Decatur, Georgia, attended Decatur Girls School and the University of Georgia. Noted lifelong for her grace and beauty, Shep married in Atlanta in 1941 to Dillard Munford. She led a busy social and civic life in Buckhead while raising five children. She was a member of Peachtree Road United Methodist Church where she enjoyed Bible study and taught Sunday school for many years. Shep is remembered as a loving mother and beloved grandmother and as a caring and dear lady by all who knew her. Additionally, friends and family remember her for her organizational abilities as well as her wit and compassion. Shep had a long time appreciation and knowledge of antique ceramics. Later in her life she developed and pursued artistic endeavors for which she was appreciated and will be fondly remembered. Shep is predeceased by her parents, brothers Robert Davis and Wilson Davis and son, Dillard Munford, Jr. She is survived by her loving husband of 21 years, Dr. Lloyd Timberlake of Atlanta; daughter Page Munford Mallory of Cebu, Philippines and Augusta, Georgia; daughter Aubrey Munford and son's Robert "Bob" Munford and Henry Munford, all of Atlanta; grandchildren Lara Munford of Lookout Mountain, Tennessee; John "Will" Mallory IV of Martinez, Georgia; Shep Mallory Gallagher of Evans, Georgia; Clayton Joiner of Atlanta; Alexa Munford of Jacksonville, Florida; Robert "Grey" Munford and Dillard Ingram Munford of Los Angeles, California, and Henry "Mac" Munford of Atlanta, niece Luisa Davis Peacock of Atlanta; as well as ten great grandchildren. Shep loved and enjoyed her extended Timberlake family including Lloyd "Tim" Timberlake of Washington D.C.; Anne Timberlake Wright and Dr. Mark Hurt Timberlake of Atlanta; and Susan Timberlake Walzer of Geneva, Switzerland; as well as the Timberlake grandchildren and other loving family members. Shep Timberlake died peacefully on January 19, 2012 at age 93 in her residence at Lenbrook. Her loving husband and children were by her side. A visitation with family will be held at 10:00 a.m. January 28, 2012 at Peachtree Road United Methodist Church. At 11:00 a.m. Shep's ashes will be interred in the churches Memorial Gardens and will be followed by a funeral service held in the Moore Chapel of the church. Long-time family friend, the Reverend Don Harp, will conduct services. Following the service a luncheon will be held for family members at Lenbrook. In lieu of flowers, gifts honoring Shep's life may be made to Peachtree Road United Methodist Church, 3180 Peachtree Road NW, Atlanta, Georgia 30305. Funeral arrangements by H. Patterson & Son's, Spring Hill, Atlanta, Georgia.
