William Clifford ROHRBACHER

[I045809]
Dallas- William Clifford Rohrbacher, passed away on the family farm, his favorite place to be on Friday, May 4, 2007 at the age of 73. He was born in Seattle, Washington on April 2, 1934 to Clifford and Gertrude (Eaton) Rohrbacher. Bill grew up in the Bay Area of California and joined the United States Marine Corps in 1953. He served for three years, honorably discharged in 1956 as a sergeant. Bill enjoyed being with his kids, grandkids, and his great-grandkids, all animals (especially his pet crow, chickens and geese), photography, hunting, and fishing.
Bill is survived and will be greatly missed by his children; Joni Claveria of Aloha, Tim Mason of Newberg, Rob Rohrbacher of Hillsboro, Clifford Rohrbacher of Newberg, Steve Rohrbacher of Independence, Jeff Rohrbacher of Klamath Falls, and Kimberly Zeeb of Salem; fifteen grandkids, and nine great-grandkids. He was preceded in death by his son William C. Rorhbacher, Jr. in 1980.
Family and friends will hold a BBQ in Bills memory on the family farm. For more information please contact Dallas Mortuary Tribute Center at (503) 623-2325. On-line condolences may be sent to www.dallastribute.com.

Ross Duane ROLAND

[I062160]
Ross Duane "Like a Rock" Roland , 71, of Sycamore, Ill., died peacefully Thursday, Jan. 26, 2006, at his son's home.
Born June 22, 1934, in DeKalb, he was the son of Bill and Blanche (Plapp) Roland . He married Ruth Hove on Sept. 26, 1953.
Ross was a plumber for 45 years, retiring in 1995. He was a member of the Plumbers Local 612 in DeKalb for more than 30 years and a lifetime member of the Sycamore Moose Club. His motto was "I've lived a good life." He taught all his sons a good trade, and they are now following in his footsteps and teaching their sons and daughter the same trade - which makes four generations of plumbers.
Ross is survived by three sons; a daughter; 11 grandchildren; and six great-grandchildren.
He was preceded in death by his parents; his wife; a daughter, Tara; an infant granddaughter, Shannon; and a sister, Punky.
Cremation has taken place at Butala Funeral Home and Crematory in Sycamore.
A Celebration of Life will be held at noon Saturday, Feb. 4, at the home of his son, Terry Roland , in Sycamore.
The Daily Chronicle, DeKalb, IL, 5 February 2006

Betty Ann ROLLER

Betty Ann Davies July 29, 1922 - September 4, 2015 Resident of Saratoga, California Betty Ann Davies a long-time resident of Saratoga passed away peacefully in her sleep on Friday, the 4th of September at Vasona Creek Healthcare Center in Los Gatos. Betty had been recovering from a recent stroke and heart attack. Born on July 29th, 1922, Betty was raised in Los Angeles, California. She graduated from Immaculate Heart High School and attended University of Southern California (USC). During her youth, she was a member of the Los Angeles Beach Club and had a great fondness for the ocean. Throughout her life going to the beach was her favorite pastime. Her other interests were working in her rose garden and she used to say, "plants are like children, the older they get, the easier they are to take care of." In her 40's, she loved playing golf with her late husband Edwin L. Davies and continued to watch and follow TV Golf games every weekend. Betty loved being social and hosting parties for friends and neighbors at her home, as well as, having all of her children and grandchildren surround her during holidays and special occasions. Betty will always be loved, always be missed, and never forgotten! Betty is survived by her (6) Children: Thomas, Christine, Cathy, Susan, Pamela and Patricia, (17) Grandchildren and (13) Great Grandchildren. A funeral Mass was held in memory of Betty Ann at Sacred Heart Catholic Church on Friday, September 18th at 10:00 am.

Allan and Eileen retired in 2000. In their later years they enjoyed spending their winters in Mexico and Arizona surrounded by her many friends. She enjoyed socializing with her friends and large extended family.

John J. ROONEY

[I071265]
John J. "Jack" Rooney of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania died Thursday, May 8, 2014 at Bayonne Medical Center. He was 83 years old. Jack was born April 19, 1931, son of the late John A. Rooney and the late Alta Rooney (nee - VanOsten). He is the beloved husband of the late Diane R. Rooney (nee Krier); father of Michael Rooney and his wife Susan and Scott Rooney and his wife Alyce; grandfather of Scott, Jr., Christopher, Stephen, John, and Jordan Rooney; great grandfather of Angela; brother of Edward, Rita, Dottie, and Patsy.
Jack was born, raised, and educated in Philadelphia. He grew up in the neighborhood of 21st and Brown and graduated from Murrell Dobbins Vocational School in January of 1948. Jack entered the United States Air Force in March of 1951. He was honorably discharged in March of 1955 at the rank of Airman First Class. During his service he received Army Occupation Medal (Germany), Good Conduct Medal, and National Defense Service Medal. During high school Jackʼs sister introduced him to her friend Diane Krier. Jack and Diane were married at the end of his military service in 1955 and moved to 1131 Alcott Street and started a family. They moved to Benton Street in the early 2000s. Jack was employed by AMF during his working career where he was a mechanic for the various types of bowling equipment AMF manufactured. Jack remained with AMF for 28 years and retired in the mid 1980s. Jack enjoyed bowling, golf, and baseball. He was the head bowling coach at Little Flower Catholic High School for Girls and the Summerdale Boys Club for many years and loved working with the children. Diane and Jack enjoyed taking cruises, traveling to destinations as diverse as Las Vegas, Hawaii, and Germany, and also enjoyed gaming at the casinos. Most of all, Jack loved and was dedicated to his family and friends.
Relatives and friends were invited to his viewings, Thursday, May 15, 2014 from 5:00 to 9:00 P.M. and Friday, May 16, 2014 from 12:00 P.M. and funeral service at 1:00 P.M. all taking place at Wetzel and Son Funeral Home, 419 Huntingdon Pike, Rockledge, PA 19046. His graveside service and military honors rendered by the United States Air Force followed at Forest Hills Cemetery, 101 Byberry Road, Huntingdon Valley, PA 19006.
Religious services were conducted by Rev. Sandra Brown, pastor of the former Holy Spirit Evangelical Lutheran Church (Closed 2013), 1268 Robbins Avenue, Philadelphia, PA 19111 and current visitation pastor of St. Johnʼs Lutheran Church, 3101 Tyson Avenue, Philadelphia, PA 19149.
