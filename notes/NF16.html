In a floral setting at St. John's United Church of Christ, Eden, on Sept. 5, Miss Irene White became the bride of H. Eugene Sheffield. The Rev. Harry N. Goodlin performed the ceremony for the daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Norman White and the son of Mr. and Mrs. Harold A. Sheffield, all of Eden. A reception followed at the Eden Trap and Rifle Club.
The bride's gown of bouquet taffeta and chantilly lace was styled with a sabrina neckline and a fitted bodice encrusted with seed pearls. A panel of chantilly lace enhanced the full skirt and edged the chapel-length train. Her princess crown of Aurora Borealis and seed pearls held a tiered veil of illusion and she carried a bouquet of white and pink roses.
Miss Joan White was made of honor for her sister. She wore a gown of pink nylon and taffeta, with fitted bodice and a bell-shaped skirt, and she carried a tailored bouquet of glamelias. In similarly styled gowns of blue nylon and taffeta were the bridesmaids, Mrs. Richard Sheffield and Miss Elizabeth Bishop, a cousin of the bride. Their bouquets were of shrimp glamelias.
Burdette Sheffield was his brother's best man. Ushers were Richard Sheffield, another brother, and Harry Freer, Jr. Ronald White, brother of the bride, and Marshall Freer served as junior ushers.
Mrs. White was attired in a royal blue knitted dress. A royal blue pillbox hat and white accessories completed her ensemble. The groom's mother chose a dacron print dress with white accessories. Both mothers had corsages of white glamelias.
After a trip through the middle States, Mr. and Mrs. Sheffield will be at home at 2345 Eden Evans Center Rd., Eden.
The Hamburg Sun, 17 September 1964

St. John's United Church of Christ, Eden, was the setting the morning of July 11, for the marriage of Miss Patricia Carole Christian, sister of Mrs. Raymond A. Sheffield, to Burdette Norwood Sheffield, son of Mr. and Mrs. Harold Sheffield. The Rev. P. Walter Meisenheimer performed the double-ring ceremony.
The bride, escorted by her brother-in-law, wore a gown of lace organza and taffeta, made with a tiered train. Her shoulder-length veil was attached to a tiara of seed pearls and she carried a bouquet of butterfly roses and seed pearl sprays.
Matron of honor was Mrs. Santo Borzelleri, of Angola, sister of the groom. She wore a turquoise gown of nylon over taffeta, with matching petal headpiece. Miss Phyllis J. Cole of Eden, bridesmaid, wore a yellow nylon gown. Both attendants carried tailored bouquets of roses.
James H. Clees of Eden was best man for his brother-in-law. Ushers were H. Eugene Sheffield, brother of the groom, Richard C. Sickau and Charles J. Coughlin, all of Eden.
Mrs. Raymond A. Sheffield, the brideʼs sister, was attired in a gown of blue lace over taffeta. The groom's mother wore a light blue printed dacron dress. Both wore corsages of cymbidium orchids.
A reception was held at the Eden Trap and Rifle Club. Following a honeymoon in Pennsylvania, Mr. and Mrs. Sheffield will reside on First Street, Eden.
The Hamburg Sun, 23 July 1964

RICHBURG - George and Irene Fisk of 11 E. Water St., Friendship, celebrated their 60th wedding anniversary with a celebration from 1-5 p.m. Aug. 23, 1997 at the home of Steve and Debora Cook of Richburg.
The event was hosted by George and Lois Case, niece of the couple,
of Olean.
The former Irene Wells and George Fisk were united in marriage Sept. 4, 1937, at the Friendship Catholic Church Rectory. Glenn and Evangeline Williams, cousin of the bride, attended.
The couple has two nephews, Charles Hawkes of Little Genesee and Gordon Hawkes of Obi; and three nieces, Mrs. Case of Olean, Marion Buchanan of Shinglehouse, PA, and Caroline Mitchell of Smethport, PA.
Mr. Fisk has been retired from Acme Electric in Cuba since 1979.
Previously, he worked at Daystrom in Friendship. Mrs. Fisk taught school and retired from Allegany County Social Services in 1972.
The couple are members of their local Catholic church.
