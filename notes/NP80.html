Mary Helen CHAMBERLIN

[I024853]
AUBURN - Mary H. O'Hora , 83, of 95 Lansing St., Auburn, passed away peacefully surrounded by her family Sunday, April 9, 2017 at University Hospital Community Campus, Syracuse.
The daughter of the late Harold and Helen Miller Chamberlin, Mary was born on Sept. 21, 1933 in Auburn. She was a graduate of Weedsport High School and Auburn Business School. Prior to her retirement, she had been employed with Red Star, Mustad, and Welch Allyn of Skaneateles. She was an avid fan of SU basketball, watching them many times over the years. Her greatest love was her children, grandchildren, and great-grandchildren. She enjoyed attending family gatherings and seeing her family grow over the years.
Mary is survived by her two daughters, Deborah (Thomas) Killmore, of Auburn, and Helen (Howard) Evener, of Scipio; four sons, Michael O'Hora , of Belton, Mo., William (Siobhan) O'Hora , of Weedsport, Edward (Karen) O'Hora , and Thomas (Erin) O'Hora , all of Auburn; many grandchildren, and several great-grandchildren; a sister, Cora Phillips, of Pattersonville, N.Y.; a brother, Paul (Debra) Chamberlin, of Jordan; a daughter-in-law, Shelley O'Hora , and several nieces and nephews. She is also survived by two close friends, Leo Shaw and Pat Moore, with whom she spent many evenings watching "Wheel of Fortune."
Mary was predeceased by her husband, Edward J. O'Hora in 1990; a son, James H. O'Hora in 2003, and a sister, Betty Nichols.
Funeral services will be held at 10 a.m. Thursday at White Chapel Funeral Home, 2719 Erie Drive, Weedsport. Burial will be in St. Joseph's Cemetery, Fleming. Friends may call on the family from 2 to 4 and 6 to 8 p.m. Wednesday at White Chapel Funeral Home.
The Citizen, Auburn, 11 April 2017

Beverly Jo CHAMPION

[I081913]
HELENA - Beverly (Champion) Cunningham, 74, died of complications from pulmonary disease Tuesday in Helena.
A celebration of her life is 1 p.m. Nov. 17 at the Eagles Lodge in Great Falls. Hagler-Anderson Mortuary is in charge of arrangements.
Bev was born in Great Falls in 1934 to Edgar E. and Isabelle Champion of Fort Shaw. She attended Fort Shaw and Simms schools, and Montana State at Bozeman. In 1951, she won a state-wide bookkeeping competition.
Bev worked as an accountant, legal secretary, and later, as a licensed real-estate broker. Her true calling was clothing design. In the 1980s, she designed silk and cashmere knitwear for Silk & Suede.
Bev was a versatile and quick-minded individual. She loved to play duplicate bridge, and in 1975 ranked as a Life Master. She was a talented pianist who loved music and dancing. Throughout her life she could sit down and play any song she heard.
Beverly was preceded in death by her parents and a brother, Donald, of Great Falls.
She is survived by two daughters, Debra Blossom and Lisa Cunningham-Trigg; four grandchildren, Shane Cunningham, Seth, Peyton, and Rikki Trigg; a brother, Robert and family of Helena; and a sister-in-law, Carole Champion of Fort Shaw.
Great Falls Tribune, 8 November 2009

Helen William CHAPIN

[I030196]
Stonington, CT - Helen Chapin "Hennie" Conger, 97, passed away Monday at Fairview Odd Fellows Home in Groton.
She was born in Hartford on Nov. 16, 1911, to Sarah Newton Chapin and Robert Dudley Chapin.
She graduated from the Hartford Public Schools.
She married David Link Conger of Newtown, Conn. on May 25, 1935. He predeceased her in 1980.
During her life she was a Welcome Wagon Supervisor for New London County, and later was in real estate in the Mystic-Stonington area. She and her husband moved to Stonington in 1968, where she designed the Memorial Garden for Calvary Episcopal Church, where she was also a member of the Vestry, the Building and Grounds Committee and acted as Flower Chairman for the church. She was a member of the Wadawanuck Club, a member of the Junior League of Hartford and the Cotillian Club. She was a member of the Herb Society of America and served as chairman of the Connecticut Unit and also served on the National Board. She was also a member of the Stonington Garden Club.
She is survived by three children, Cicely Penny Shoemaker, Helen Chapin DeCiantis, and Alice Conger; nine grandchildren and twelve great-grandchildren.
She was predeceased by four sisters and a brother, who was killed in World War II; as well as her son, David Christian Link Conger.
A Memorial Service will be conducted at 10 a.m. on Saturday at Calvary Episcopal Church.
In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to Calvary Church Memorial Garden or Fairview Odd Fellows Nursing Dept.
Published in The Day on 9/9/2009 www.theday.com
