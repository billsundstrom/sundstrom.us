Homer Carroll SUMMERS

[I078827]
Homer Carroll Summers Sr., age 83, passed away Friday morning Aug. 29, 2008, surrounded by his family and close friends.
Homer was born July 17, 1925, in McAllen, Texas. He was a World War II Army veteran and received a Bronze Star. He worked all over the world for DynCorp as a field representative, retiring after 42 years. Homer was a Mason and lifetime member of the Cavite Lodge No. 2 in the Philippines. He was also a Shriner and member of Asiya Shriners, San Mateo, Calif. and Parker County Shrine Club. Homer was past president of Saigon Shrine Oasis, Saigon, Vietnam. He was an Eastern Star member and Past Patron of Heidelberg Chapter No. 3, Heidelberg, Germany and Weatherford Chapter No. 21.
Homer was a resident of Hudson Oaks for the last 14 years and an active and beloved member of Lakeshore Drive Baptist Church.
He was preceded in death by all of his immediate family, which included his five siblings.
Survivors include his wife of 46 years, Luz; daughters, Carola Summers and husband, Julian Harry, Janet Summers; son, Homer C. Summers Jr.; grandchild, Preston Grace Summers; and lots of nieces, nephews and cousins.
Memorial service is 10:30 a.m. Friday, Sept. 5, at Lakeshore Drive Baptist Church.
In lieu of flowers, please make a donation to Lakeshore Drive Baptist Church, 200 S. Lakeshore Drive, Hudson Oaks, Texas 76087.
Weatherford Democrat, 3 September 2008

Sarah SUMNER

[I019445]
Sally (Sarah) Sumner -Rowden, wife of Bill Rowden, joined her Lord and Savior on 24 October 2011. She passed quietly without evidence of pain or other discomfiture. Her son, John, daughter, Sarah Jane, and Bill were with her to the end. Her son, Tom, was engaged with Carrier Strike Group 11 at sea at that time.
She was born in Rockford, IL on 31 January 1932. She grew up in Rockford and Dixon, IL, and graduated from Cornell College (the -"little" Cornell) in Mount Vernon, Iowa in 1953. She taught school in Davenport, Iowa for two years, but she could not escape the powerful lure of California living and summered there for a couple of years between school sessions. They met in Long Beach, CA the summer of 1955 and became engaged in the Fall. Bill departed for a home port ship change to Sasebo, Japan in January, 1956. She followed a little later and they were married on 14 April in Sasebo. Thus began a wonderful career, serving their country, that took them all over the Western Pacific, Mediterranean, Europe and the US for the next 55 years. At retirement, they moved to the Northern Neck and lived there for 12 years before moving to a retirement complex near Lancaster, Pennsylvania.
They reared three children: Sarah Jane, a full-time Mom in Portland, Oregon; Rear Admiral Thomas, USN; and Dr. John, the family expert on all feathered creatures in the world. They have five grandchildren, three boys and two girls.
Her life will be honored at Arlington National Cemetery on Friday, 30 March 2012, commencing at 9 AM.
Lancaster Intelligencer Journal/New Era, 28 November 2011

Betty Jeanne SUNDLING

[I117373]
Betty Jeanne Root (nee Sundling ) passed away on Friday, March 19, 2010 in Rocklin, California at the age of 83 from complications related to Parkinson's and Alzheimer's.
Betty Jeanne was born in Raymond, Washington on September 30, 1926. Her father, Ernest Sundling Sr., came from Sweden, and her mother, Olga Kanerva Sundling , came from Finland. Her older brother was Ernest Sundling Jr. They all predeceased her. She is survived by her husband, Robert S. Root, and his family. She is also survived by her brother's family which includes two nieces, Emelia Harper of Rocklin, Calif., and Ellyn Newman of Seabrook, Texas, along with their children and grandchildren.
Betty Jeanne requested no services. She will be inurned at the Fern Hill Cemetery in Menlo, Washington next to her parents and brother.
Betty Jeanne graduated from Raymond High School in 1944. She continued her education at State College of Washington for a B.A. in Home Economics, Iowa State University for an M.S., and Michigan State University for a Ph.D. in Family Ecology. Betty Jeanne held teaching positions in California and Washington. She was also State Extension Specialist for the State of Arkansas.
After retirement, she cared lovingly for her mother until her death in 1990. She married Robert Root in 1993 and was able to enjoy the companionship of a husband with whom she traveled extensively. She lived in the Olympia area before moving to California for medical reasons in 2008.
The Daily World, Aberdeen, 28 March 2010

Despite the information about Isaac being Lovisa's brother, it is my opinion that he was her son. The following is from the 'History of the Swedes of Clay County, South Dakota:
Isace Sundling is believed to have been a brother of Mrs. John Eric Sundstrom, and the father of Charles Sundling of Vermillion who was a resident of Beresford for many years. His birth date is unknown, but he came to the United States for Nobottens Län, Sweden, in 1870, and to Clay County in 1872, at the time Sundstrom came. He filed on the southeast quarter, Sec. 2, of Glenwood Township, and obtained patent after final proof, May 24, 1879. Isaac Sundling returned to Sweden after selling his land and there are no further records of him here. He was single while here, but may have married after returning to Sweden.
