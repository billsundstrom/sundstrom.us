David NILES

[I091741]
David W. Niles, age 76 years 2 months 5 days, passed away on February 3, 2014, at the Golden Living Center in Wisconsin Dells. David, son of John and Helen (Neuzum) Niles was born on November 29, 1937 in Baraboo.
David worked for Badger Ordnance for over 20 years as a pipe fitter. He also served in the U.S. Air Force from 1956 until he was honorably discharged in 1960.
He enjoyed fishing, hunting, and bird watching. David loved the outdoors, even just walking through the woods. He loved to read, especially Louis L'amour, and was a fan of John Wayne movies.

Philip NILES

[I091754]
Philip "Phil" Franklin Niles of Baraboo, at the age of 72 years 3 months 27 days passed away from cancer at the St. Clare Hospice House on Friday, April 25, 2014. Philip, the son of John and Helen (Nuzum) Niles, was born on Dec. 29, 1941 in Baraboo.
He was a 1960 graduate of Baraboo High School and on April 22, 1995 he married Susan Hiller in Baraboo.
Phil worked for the Sauk County Sheriff's Department for over 21 years and retired as a detective. He had also been employed at St. Clare Center, managed the school bus company, and spent his last 13 years working in security at Ho-Chunk Casino. Phil always had a ready smile and enjoyed greeting and visiting with the people at the casino. He retired from the casino in 2007. In addition, Phil worked part-time as a driver for Classic Limousine, did lawn maintenance in the neighborhood, and numerous odd jobs throughout the summer season.
Phil so loved and enjoyed his little grandsons, Drake age 6, and Garrett age 3, as they loved riding in the cart behind his lawn tractor. They looked forward to green grass and those rides around the neighborhood with grandpa.
Phil also enjoyed time spent at the shooting range with his older grandson, Matthew, when visiting from Georgia.
