Perley Morse GRIFFIN

[I098794]
Perley Morse Griffin.
Son of Miles Flint Griffin and Cynthia Louisa Alden, a descendant of John Alden, born at Methuen, Mass., May 14, 1841; united with the First Congregational Church, Woburn, Mass., July 5, 1857; was graduated from the Woburn high school, 1860; entered Harvard College in the autumn of 1860, but left in the following spring to join the army, where he served as an artilleryman, June, 1861, to June, 1864, and as clerk in the quartermaster's department, 1864-65. After the war he went to Kansas, and engaged in business for a time. Later he entered Washburn College, and was graduated, 1869, and was graduated from this Seminary in 1872 after taking the full course. He was licensed to preach, June 6, 1871, by the Andover Association, at Andover; was ordained, July 13, 1873, as pastor at Parsons, Kan.; dismissed, August 19, 1879; supplied at Fredonia several months; leaving for the seacoast on account of ill health, he was missionary at Woods Hole, Mass., August to October, 1880; was acting pastor at East Bridgewater, Mass., January, 1881, to August, 1890; acting pastor at Parsons, Kan., 1890-91; agent for Washburn College, with residence at Topeka, Kan., 1892-93; acting pastor of the Olivet Memorial Church (independent), Brockton, Mass., July, 1893, to July, 1899, having had a part in the organization of the church. He worked as a missionary of the American Sunday-School Union in Vermont, August and September, 1903, and in Aroostook County, Me., during the following winter; was chaplain of the Home for Intemperate Women, Brookline, Mass., July, 1904, to June, 1905, when he was appointed as missionary of the American Sunday-School Union. He served on the school committee at Parsons, Kan., and at East Bridgewater, Mass., He married, July 20, 1869, at Topeka, Kan., Eva Jane Huntoon, daughter of Joel Huntoon and Ellen Sanger Richardson, of Topeka. She survived him with two sons and two daughters, one son and three daughters having died. Mr. Griffin died at his home at Neponset (Boston), Mass., of heart-failure, July 5, 1905, aged sixty-four years, one month, and twenty-one days.

Peter Alden GRIFFIN

[I098830]
Peter Griffin considered himself a professor first. He was a dedicated mathematics professor at California State University, Sacramento, but it was gambling - blackjack in particular - that kept his mind active and made him well-known.
Mr. Griffin wrote two books on blackjack, "The Theory of Blackjack" and "Extra Stuff, Gambling Ramblings."
Mr. Griffin, 61, died last Sunday of prostate cancer, which was diagnosed four weeks ago.
A native of New Jersey, Mr. Griffin grew up in Chicago and Portland, Ore. After earning his bachelor's degree from Portland State University, he came to California to attend graduate school. Mr. Griffin received his master's in mathematics from the University of California, Davis, and began teaching at CSUS in 1965.
"He was renowned in the gambling world. We traveled the world - Europe, Australia, across the U.S. - for gambling," said Lydia Griffin , his wife. "People would ask him to analyze gambling problems.
"Teaching was his main love, but yet he loved gambling, and the spark of his life became the math in gambling," Lydia Griffin said. "He was not a gambler at all. He really didn't like gambling. But he enjoyed the mathematics of it and the people he met through it."
Mr. Griffin worked for Las Vegas and Reno casinos as a consultant, his wife said. A witty man, Mr. Griffin was contacted often by newspapers, radio and television for his insights on statistics and probability in casino gambling and the lottery.
Anthony Curtis, a longtime friend and publisher, said of Mr. Griffin : "There's a case to be made that there's no bigger name in the world of blackjack. Peter was considered the dean of the science of blackjack. His "Theory of Blackjack' was literally the bible for all serious blackjack players."
Curtis said that although many books were written about how to play blackjack, Mr. Griffin 's book explained the theory and the math behind it.
"For all of his mathematical firepower, this guy was equally verbally powerful," Curtis said. "He was a tremendous speaker."
In addition to his wife, Mr. Griffin is survived by a sister, Barbara Dann, and a brother, Alan Griffin , both of Salem, Ore.
At his request, there will be no service. A celebration of his life will be held Nov. 1 from 2-5 p.m. at the Old Spaghetti Factory, 1910 J St. The family requests that any remembrances be made to the SPCA or the Sacramento Zoo.

The Sacramento Bee, 25 October 1998

Alice Elizabeth GRIFFITH

[I074528]
Alice Elizabeth Kliber, age 100, beloved wife of the late Ralph James Kliber, of Grosse Pointe Woods, passed away on July 4, 2013, after a brief illness. She was the dearest mother of Thomas, Elizabeth, and the late James. She was a loving step-grandmother of 5 and mother-in-law of Laura.

She was raised in Toledo, Ohio, the youngest of 5 children. Her father was a railroad engineer and her mother a former typesetter at the Owosso Press. She graduated from Central Catholic High School in 1931 and later entered the Mercy School of Nursing in Toledo. She became a registered nurse in 1937. The flu epidemic brought her to Detroit and she began working in 1939 at Mount Carmel Hospital (now Sinai Grace Hospital).
She met her future husband on a blind date and they were happily married for 65 years. Ralph proposed to Alice on the day Pearl Harbor was bombed. They moved to Grosse Pointe Woods in 1956 and raised three children there.
Alice was active in the community. She was a parish member at Our Lady Star of the Sea for over 50 years, a member of the Rosary Altar Society, Studied painting under the late Dorthy Hurley, a girl scout leader, member of various bridge clubs, member of the Nomads travel club, longtime member of the Grosse Pointe Artists Association, and 53 year member of the Grosse Pointe Yacht Club and recipient of the 2012 Member of the Year award.
Alice was an avid reader and finished two books every week. She traveled the world with her husband and family. She loved to play cards. Alice was a formidable opponent in a daily Gin Rummy game with her children, winning at cards within days of her passing. Alice was a talented painter, and sold many paintings. She loved being a part of the Grosse Pointe Yacht Club community and treasured her friends there. She was deeply committed to her family and faith. Alice weathered the challenges in life with grace and a level head, and demonstrated great resilience and courage on many occasions, especially during significant health challenges that she faced. To her family, she was their role model and hero. Alice passed on July 4th, Independence Day, and now for her family, a day of celebration of a life long and well lived.

Ellis Romaine GRIGGS

[I011847]
KILLED AS SCAFFOLD COLLAPSES ELLIS GRIGGS FATALLY INJURED AND TWO OTHERS HURT
Ellis R. Griggs, 63, prominent resident of southwest Auburn, died at the City Hospital yesterdad afternoon from injuries received in a fall from a scaffold at the home of his son, Charles Griggs, 46 Aspen Street. Mr. Griggs sustained fatal internal injuries.
Mr. Griggs, and his son with Alex Follan had been engaged in shingling the house and were preparing to come down from the roof. The scaffold gave way, letting all three to the ground.
They were taken to the City Hospital where Ellis Griggs was found to be internally injured and died in less than one hour. Mr. Follan was also thought to be fatally injured but he revived and this morning was reported ont of danger. No bones were broken. Charles Griggs escaped with several bruises, none serious.
Ellis R. Griggs was born in North Victory but had resided in Auburn over 30 years. He was a machinist by trade and had worked in the Mclntosh-Seymour shops, the International Harvester Company plant and at the time of his death was employed at the Columbian Rope Company. His home is at 22 Warren Avenue.
He was a Republican in politics and for many years was an active party leaders in the Ninth Ward. He was. a member of Hardenburgh Lodge of Odd Fellows, a genial gentleman and good citizen.
Surviving are his wife: five daughters. Alice Connors, Elsie Ward, Ethel Jackson, Elraa S. Simmons, Hattie Jakway; one son, Charles Griggs.
The funeral will be from the late home, 22 Warren Avenue, at 2:30 o'clock Wednesday, with burial in Mt. Pleasant-Cemetery, Port Byron.
The Auburn Citizen, 8 September 1925

GRIMALDUS

[I015361]
Grimaldus m. Unknown. According to the genealogy of the Suffolk family of Bacon, one Grimald, or Grimaldus, a relative of the Norman chieftain, William de Warrenne, came to England in 1066 at the time of the Conquest and settled near Holt, in County Suffolk. His son, or Great Grandson Robert, took the surname Bacon, or rather assumed the use of the place-name as a surname. In the north of France the surname Bacon is still in use. William Bacon in 1082 endowed the Abbey of Holy Trinity at Caen. The surname Bacon is found in the Battle Rolls in England in the eleventh century and in the Hundred Rolls in the thirteenth. There are occasional variations in spelling, such as Bacun and Bachun, and in some instances the surname Bacon may have been corrupted from Beacon. Some derive the surname from the Saxon baccen or baccen, a beech tree.
