Harold William KOCH

[I105217]
Harold William Koch, age 82 of Kimberly, Idaho, passed away Tuesday, Jan. 24, 2012, in Kimberly.
Harold was born May 29, 1929, in Lamar, Mo., the son of Ray and Pearl Peterson Koch. He later moved to Twin Falls. He married Evelyn Tramel and had six children: Steven, Margaret, Jim, Robert, Charlette and Melvin. After his divorce from Evelyn he married Geraldine "Jerry" Baggett, who preceded him in death in 2002.
Harold started farming at a young age and farmed until he retired. In addition to farming, he was employed at Idaho Frozen Foods for several years and retired from Southside Bean House in Twin Falls.
In addition to being a farmer, he was a wonderful gardener. He loved kids and was a very patient man. He loved to build things in his shop. He was always thoughtful of others and helped many. You could never leave his house hungry.
Harold was preceded in death by his parents, Ray and Pearl; sisters, Opal Spencer of Salem, Ore., and Fern Harris of Joplin, Mo.; brothers, Ray and Wayne Koch, both of California.
Survivors include his children, Steve (Eva) Koch, Margaret (Mike) Mort, Jim (Janice) Koch, Robert (Tina) Koch, Charlette (Steve) Reynolds, Melvin (Mary) Koch and 13 grandchildren.
A memorial service will be conducted at 1 p.m. Saturday, Feb. 4, 2012, at Farmer Funeral Chapel in Buhl. Inurnment will be in West End Cemetery.
Posted in Obituaries, 30 January 2012

Jack Damone KOCH

[I105904]
LACON - Jack D. Koch , "Cookie," 83, of Lacon died at 4:20 p.m. Wednesday, April 12, 2006, at his residence.
He was born on March 30, 1923, in Lacon, the son of the late Jesse C. and Francis M. Koch . He graduated from Lacon High School and attended Bradley University.
He married Mildred "Mid" Buechele on Aug. 8, 1948, in Winslow, Ind.
During their 57-year marriage, they were the parents of three children, Shelly (Gary) Griswold of Lake Wildwood, Varna, Mitch (Marty) Koch of Roanoke and Susan (Kenneth, deceased) Harms of Varna. He was the loving grandfather to David Harms of Varna, Amy (Matt) Schwanke of Varna, Steffin (Kathleen) Griswold of Minneapolis, Cory (Sheryl) of Roanoke, Kristi (Darren) Chioni of Roanoke, and Julie and Kelli Harms of Varna. As well as being a grandfather, he was the proud great-grandfather of Peyton Koch of Eureka, Emily and Kayla Schwanke of Varna, Loghan and Mason Chioni of Roanoke and Kaylin Chioni of Germantown Hills.
During World War II, Jack was a radio operator, serving in the Army in the Pacific Theater. He was a 50-plus member of Lacon American Legion Post 593 and Veterans of Foreign Wars.
He served the public in many ways. He was a former member of the Marshall County Board, the Lacon City Council, St. John Lutheran Church Council, was a former magistrate of Marshall County, a special deputy for Marshall County, the former director of the Marshall County Civil Defense, a former member of the Lacon-Sparland Fire Department and a Boy Scout leader.
He, too, was involved in the Masons and Scottish Rite. As a 32nd degree Mason, he was a former member of the Lacon and Peoria Masonic Lodge as well as a 50-plus member of the Mohammed Temple, Shrine.
As a previous avid golfer, he was a former member and bondholder of the Lacon Country Club. He also enjoyed hunting, fishing and RC airplanes.
For 21 years, he worked at Hiram Walker & Sons in Peoria, where he retired in 1981. He was a former boilermaker, working out of Local 60.
Jack was a member of St. John Lutheran Church in Lacon, where a memorial service will be at 11 a.m. Monday, April 17, 2006, with visitation one hour before. The Rev. Richard Hanson will officiate. Military rites will be accorded after the service. The burial of ashes will be in a private ceremony at a later date. Lenz Memorial Home is in charge of the arrangements.
He was a loving husband, father, grandfather, great-grandfather and a loyal friend. He will be sorely missed by all who knew him.
The Peoria Journal Star, 14 April 2006
