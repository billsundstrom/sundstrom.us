Dorvin Chancey EMERY

[I122714]
Dorvin Chancey Emery passed away peacefully on Friday, July 10, 2009. Dorvin was born June 7, 1939 to Eugene and Mamie (Potter) Emery in Jay County, Indiana. He grew up working on the family farm and saw mill. On March 31, 1961 he married Barbara Jean (Bobbi) Davidson and together they raised four children.
Dorvin spent his working years managing cabinet factories in Indiana, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Texas and Minnesota. He retired in 1993. Dorvin and Bobbi moved to Indiana in 1994 and in 1996 he opened Indian Trails Sporting Range at Modoc, IN. While in Indiana Dorvin also trained and broke horses. Dorvin loved to train and work with horses and shoot his guns. He was a cowboy in the wrong century. Dorvin was a Certified Private Pilot and owned a couple of his own planes. Dorvin would have rather been out flying than doing anything else.
He was involved with the National Guard for 13 years. Ottertail County Sheriff's Posse, McLeod County Sheriff's Posse, Lion's Club, NRA and he was a Safety Gun Instructor. He also volunteered at giving riding lessons to handicapped children.
Dorvin is survived by his wife of 48 years, Bobbi of Askov, MN; one son Carl (Leslie) of Arlington, MN; three daughters Teresa (John) Towle of Mora, MN, Marie (Kevin) Lemke of Green Isle, MN and Vicki (Mike) Lahlum of Detroit Lakes, MN; eight grandchildren; sister Beatrice (Dale) Briggs of Goshen, IN; brother Weldon (Janet) Emery of Florida; special brother-in-law Glen Royer of Ohio and numerous nieces and nephews.
He was preceded in death by his parents Eugene and Mamie Emery; brother Albert; two sisters Gaye and Mary; and one grandson Clayton.

Lois Romaine EMERY

[I062951]
Lois Emery Nielson, 91, of Dallas, Texas, went to be with her Lord and Savior on Sunday, July 13, 2014 - 12 years to the day following the death of her beloved husband, Dr. Robert G. Nielson. She was born on September 6, 1922, the second of four sisters, to Elizabeth and Maurice Emery in Warren, Pennsylvania. After meeting her husband, Bob, in college, they were married on August 22, 1944, having dated only each other. She went on to earn her master's degree in English literature from Boston University. Following short stints in Missouri and Kansas, the couple came to live in Dallas in 1953. It was there that she and Bob raised their two sons and served in long and fruitful church ministry. Lois' calling was as a piano teacher, and she taught for 71 of her 91 years. Dozens of her students went on to become teachers and performers themselves - her impact on her students was truly inspirational. She was fond of quoting Henry Adams, who said, "A teacher affects eternity; [she] can never tell where [her] influence stops." She is survived by her three sisters; two sons, Stephen (Carolyne) Nielson and Niel (Kathleen) Nielson; five grandchildren (Jon, Dan, David, Christiana and Caroline); and three great-grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at 2 p.m. on Thursday, July 17, at Park Cities Presbyterian Church in Dallas, with a reception to follow.
Dallas Morning News, 16 July 2014

Maurice R. EMERY

[I062949]
WARREN - Maurice R. Emery, of 38043 Center Ridge Road, Ridgeville, O., a former member of the Warren Borough Council and Warren Selective Service Board was dead on arrival at 4:30 P.M., Saturday (Aug. 5, 1961) in the Memorial Hospital, Elyria, O. Death was attributed to a heart attack.
Mr. Emery, who was born in Warren, served on the Warren Borough Council for 12 years.
He was a member of the Advisory Board and Treasurer of the Pittsburgh District of the Church of the Nazarene. Mr. Emery was also a trustee of the Eastern Nazarene College at Walliston, Mass., a post he had held for 25 years.
He moved to Ridgeville two years ago and was proprietor of the Ridge Motel at North Ridgeville. Mr. Emery was a member of the Avon Lake, Ohio, Church of the Nazarene.
Survivors include his wife, Elizabeth; four daughters, Mrs. Rachel Fisk, Avon Lake, Mrs. Lois Neilson, Dallas, Tex., Mrs. Eleanor Akers, Beaver Falls, and Mrs. Marilyn Oddo, Kansas City, Kan.; also 10 grandchildren.
Funeral services will be held at 2 P.M., Wednesday in the Peterson Funeral Home with Rev. Gordon Graves of Bradford, officiating. Interment will be in Oakland Cemetery.

Donald Richard ENDER

[I115046]
ONALASKA - Donald Richard Ender of Onalaska passed peacefully Jan. 29, 2012, at the age of 83.
He was born on Dec. 22, 1928, in West Salem to Martin and Helen Ender . He married Elizabeth (Betty) Hayes in March of 1957 and lived an extraordinary life with a devoted family of five children, six grandchildren and a growing number of great-grandchildren. Donald lived under the credo "God first, family second and all else third."
Service was at the heart of his life. A 20-plus year veteran of the United States Air Force, he traveled the world, from Newfoundland to England to Italy to Vietnam, often with his family by his side, before retiring from the military to work at Gundersen Lutheran Medical Center. Donald was passionate about music and athletics. He played clarinet in high school and went on to win several singing competitions, sang in the choir at St. Patrick's parish, coached little league, volunteered for softball leagues, and was an avid supporter of his favorite teams.
His greatest service was to his wife Betty, children Beverly, Donald , Gloria, Denise and John, along with grandchildren Chris, David, Michael, Jennah, Claire and Ava, as well as his great grandchild Ethan. He was a dedicated family man who made each of us feel treasured and loved. He was preceded in death by his sister, Betty Thurston.
Donald talked daily with a vast network of family and friends, who supported him throughout his life. His family would like to thank Kent Davis; La Crosse Home Health Care, especially Pollie Banashak, Julie Dietz, Brenda Elliott, Karen Havey, and Dan Kaminski; Gundersen Lutheran Hospital Palliative Care, especially Gerilynn Brasic and Marsha Kendall; and the Zablocki VA Hospital Spinal Cord Unit for all the great care they gave him.
Donald 's last wish was to live out his final days at home with his wife Betty, who selflessly provided compassion and care for him, granted his final wish and shared with him one of the greatest love stories ever witnessed. It was a privilege and blessing the world was graced with the life of Donald Ender .
A Mass of Christian burial will be held at 10:30 a.m. on Friday at St. Patrick's Catholic Church, Onalaska. The Rev. Steven Kachel will officiate. Burial with military honors will be in Onalaska City Cemetery. Friends may call from 5 to 7 p.m. Thursday, with a 5 p.m. rosary, at Schumacher-Kish Funeral Home of Onalaska, and from 10 a.m. to services Friday at the church.
Onalaska Holmen Courier-Life, 1 February 2012
