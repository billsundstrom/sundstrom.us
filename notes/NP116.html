William M. DARLING

[I119376]
William Darling, Jr. was born in Washington, DC to Louise Anderson Darling and William M. Darling, a DC printer. A telling, early fascination with airplanes and flight lead a grade school age Bill to sneak off to pay five dollars to the pilot of a 2-seater plane for his first airplane flight from the fields around DC.
He graduated from Central High School, then from the University of Maryland in 1941 with a BS in Mechanical Engineering, focus on aeronautical engineering. He immediately found work as a civilian at the Special Weapons Branch of the Army Air Corps at Wright Field in Dayton, Ohio. His work was centered on designing and testing the aerodynamics of the glide bomb prototypes in a race to catch up with German weaponry.
After the start of the war, Bill joined the Army Air Corps (now the Air Force.) After training, he was sent to an airbase in La Junta, Colorado, and assigned to a B-25 bomber squad. During this time, he met, fell in love and married the love of his life, Patricia Stedman in September of 1943.
Instead of being deployed to the Pacific, Bill's old bosses at the Special Weapons Branch had him ordered back to Wright Field because they could not replace him on the glide bomb project. He and Patti moved back to Dayton, where their son William III was born in August of 1944. His work there included a crash program to replicate an unexploded German Buzz Bomb recovered in London, which they were able to manufacture by the war's end.
In 1946, with the birth of their son, John, and Bill's discharge from the Air Force, the family moved to Los Angeles and Bill began his career with North American Aviation. His first work was on a missile known as the Navaho, whose rocket engines, guidance system and vehicle design laid the groundwork for rocket development for the next several years. Bill and Patti's third son, Douglass, was born during this time, in 1949.
By the 1960's, Bill was Vice President of contracts and pricing at North American during the space exploration years. He was rightfully proud to have been a part of the success achieved in the Mercury Space Capsule, Apollo Command Module and the Space Shuttle Orbiter programs.
It was during these heady years that Bill and Patti developed a life long love affair with Hawaii, especially a patch of Waikiki, where every October they would gather under a tree with a diverse group of friends to enjoy the sun and warm water. When they discovered the joy and peace they felt on the ocean, they learned to sail. Their first boat was a Lido 14, and later they had a beautiful wooden Herreshoff 28 built for them. Some of the family's fondest memories are of sailing the South Bay and trips to Catalina's Avalon and the Isthmus. Bill and Patti maintained strong friendships with the boating community in King Harbor, friendships that continued long after they gave up their boat.
After Bill retired, it was not long before the joys of grandchildren began to arrive in numbers. First son Bill and his then wife Charlene's boy, John, then son John and wife Karen's boys Eric and Kyle, followed by son Doug and wife Ann's daughter, Kate, and son, Peter. The babysitting, beach days, sleepovers, soccer games and sailing trips filled Bill and Patti's days and helped to ease the pain they felt when their son John passed away in 1984. In the past few years, the arrival of great granddaughters Whitney (John and Angela) and Elle and Alice, (Kyle and Sara) brought special delight to "Great Grandpa Bill."
On May 5, 2018, William passed away peacefully with family at his side. No services are being planned at this time. Please sign the guest book at www.dailybreeze.com/obits
Palos Verdes Peninsula News, 30 May 2018

Vera Maxine DARLINGTON

[I079371]
Vera M. Walters, 90, passed away in Pendleton, Oregon, on April 26, 2019. She was born October 19, 1928, to Ethel and Raymond Darlington in Baker City, Oregon.
Vera grew up in Baker and graduated from Baker High School in 1947 where she enjoyed participating in many theater productions. Just days after graduation, she married Gurney Walters and they settled and started a family in Pendleton. They were married over 62 years until his death in 2009.
Vera was a dedicated homemaker, raising three sons, David, Richard and Stephen, and helping Gurney with the family business, Walters Chevron, by faithfully banking the business deposits every day. They retired after 40 years and spent their winters in Yuma, Arizona, along with dozens and dozens of friends from around the country. Together, their greatest joy was hosting the Yuma/Pendleton party many times over the years and the family has boxes of photographs that document this fact.
In addition to being an active volunteer in her childrenʼs sports, Vera also enjoyed years of working her weekly shift at the Pendleton Bargain Counter, especially in her later years with her good friend, Gloria Moxley. Walters Chevron was also a sponsor to so many softball and baseball teams over the years and she was known to attend many of those games. Vera played league bowling for decades and made lifelong friendships at the bowling alley.
Vera was preceded in death by her parents, husband, son David, daughter-in-law Toni, grandson Christopher, brother Bill Darlington and sister-in-law Rosie. She is survived by Stephen and his wife, Michele, and their daughter Aimee; Ric, his wife Pat and their children Valerie and Peter; grandchildren Clint, Brian and Sarah; and brother Ernie and his wife Roberta of Baker City. Her family also included many great-grandchildren, nieces and nephews.
Vera will be interned at the Olney Memorial Garden with family and a memorial celebration with friends and family will be held in July.

GEORGE DARNELL ELECTROCUTED
FRUITLAND MAN KILLED WHILE TRYING TO PUT OUT FIRE ON POWER LINE POLE
George Darnell, ditch rider for the Farmers' ditch company, was instantly killed Monday morning by contacting with the current of a power line. He was burning trash along the ditch and when an electric power line pole caught fire he went to the ditch and brought a bucket of water with which to extinguish the flames. In throwing the water against the pole contact was formed, as water is a conductor, and the current passed through his body killing him instantly. The hand which held the water bucket and one of his heels, in which there were heavy nails, were burned, showing that the current passed through the body from the hand to the foot. His helper was near by and saw the accident and his body fall, but when he reached the unfortunate man life was extinct.
Mr. Darnell had been a ditch rider on this irrigation system which carries water past New Plymouth to Fruitland and was a faithful worker and a good, clean citizen. The suddenness of his death was a great shock to everybody as he was well known and highly respected.
The Payette Independent, 28 November 1929

Memorial services will be held at 2 p.m., Wednesday, Sept. 26, 2007 at Cloverdale Funeral Home, 1200 N. Cloverdale Rd., Boise. Services are under the direction of Cloverdale Funeral Home. Lee was the daughter of Glenn and Bertha Darnall , early residents of the Payette Valley. Born in Ontario, Ore., she was reared in New Plymouth, Idaho. She was a graduate of Fruitland High School. After graduation she attended Link's Business College in Boise, and then worked as a secretary in Boise until 1941 when she married Henry Allen Embree of Ontario. When the war broke out in 1941 Allen enlisted as an Air Force Cadet, graduating from pilot training in Stockton, Calif. For many years Leona and Allen were an Air Force couple living at many Air Force bases throughout the United States. They were the parents of four adopted children, one of whom died of an accidental fall in 1958. Allen retired from the Air Force in 1965, and they returned to Idaho. They were divorced in 1968. Leona, then known by her nickname of "Lee", married Malcolm Spence, from whom she was divorced in 1971. She then went to work for First Security Bank in Boise, where she worked as a Marketing Officer until her retirement in 1982. While working for First Security she also attended Boise State University, graduating with the 50th anniversary class in 1982 with a degree in communication. After her retirement from First Security Lee traveled to Seattle to attend Ernest Holmes College, graduating with a Religious Science Ministerial Degree in 1984. After this she returned to the South where she had lived for many years while married to Allen Embree. She was a minister at Churches of Religious Science in Charleston, South Carolina and Atlanta, Ga. She later transferred her work with churches to Unity where she became a Licensed Unity Teacher. She returned to Boise in 1995. In 1997 she reestablished herself with her first spiritual love, The Church of Religious Science, where she remained an active member until her transition. She is survived by her brother, Glenn Darnall and his wife, Dorothy, of Boise, a son, Larry Embree of Spokane, two daughters; Barbara Werner and Susan O'Hara of Tacoma, Wash., three grandchildren; Timothy and Tyler O'Hara and Amanda Robinette; a niece, Rebecca Parry (Michael) of Boise, and a nephew, David Daman (Barbara), of Germany. The family expresses grateful appreciation to the staff at Marquis Care for their attentive care and concern for Lee during her stay at that facility. If you so desire, memorials in Lee's name may be sent to: The Center for Spiritual Living, 600 N. Curtis Road, Boise, Idaho 83706.

Skip was born to Benjamin and Alice Mary (Sexton) Darnell and raised in Lincoln, Nebraska with his two brothers Dale and Don Darnell and his sister Mary Alice Burr.
He served in the navy during WWII as a Seaman First Class; returning to Lincoln to marry, start his career with State Farm Insurance Company and raise a family. He married Ardeen "Dee Dee" Miller in 1948 and their union lasted nearly 60 years until her death in April of 2008. In 1970, the family moved to Greeley, as he continued his career with State Farm, retiring in 1989 after forty-two years with the company. His family was the cornerstone of his life; he often commented that raising and spending time with his children, then his grandchildren and great-grandchildren, provided him his greatest joys. Skip enjoyed working with people and could always be counted on to provide sage advice with his trademark wit and wicked sense of humor.
He will be missed by his three children, Katherine Darnell, Peggy Darnell Stone and John (Michelle) Darnell; eight grandchildren, Amanda (Mauricio) Orihuela, Brandon Darnell, Brett (Sarah) Stone, Mandy (Ted) Harris, Sheldon Darnell, Cody Darnell, Angie (Jake) Huff and Nicole (David Velarde) Pickett; and seven great grandchildren.
