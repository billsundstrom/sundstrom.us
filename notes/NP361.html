Janet Anne MCMAHON

[I099614]
Born in Coblenz (Koblenz), Germany, the third largest city in Rhineland-Palatinate, and lived in Bavaria until she was 3 1/2 years old, with German as her primary language. Her father was stationed in Germany, serving with the U.S. Army. Both parents were American-born.
Attended Depauw Univ for 1st 2 yrs; transferred to and graduated from University of Alabama w/ B.A. in Drama, magna cum laude; a Kappa Kappa Gamma; served with Red Cross during WWII in Germany; manic-depressive depressive; committed suicide by hanging.
Fluent in German; could "get by" in French and Spanish; read Latin; educated to read Greek classics, but didn't care for it.
Loved to play bridge, penuncle, cribbage, gin rummy, canasta, Scrabble, Monopoly, word games, and Carom.
Voracious reader; mysteries, historical novels, gardening and landscaping books, English history, American and English plays, political non-fiction, and ancient history.
Favorite artist: Mary Cassatt
Loved impressionist art; despised modern and abstract art. Shared a love for musicals with her husband. Performed in locally-produced musicals with husband for several years in Maryland. Favorite musicals: Man of La Mancha and The Tenderloin.
Her first-born granddaughter called her Mammy.

FRANCIS MCMENAMY, 84 EASTON RESTAURANT PROPRIETOR
A funeral Mass was said yesterday in Immaculate Conception Church in Easton for Francis J. McMenamy, proprietor of the McMenamy House of Hamburger in Easton since 1954.
Mr. McMenamy died Friday at Goddard Memorial Hospital, Stoughton. He was 84 and a lifelong resident of Easton.
He graduated from Bentley College in 1923 and was a member of the Brockton Chamber of Commerce, the Century Club at Stonehill College, the Bentley Alumni Association and the Easton Lions Club.
Mr. McMenamy leaves his wife, Edith (Rowland); two daughters, Helen Collins of Braintree and Nancy Carter of North Easton; a stepson, Richard W. Reynolds of Needham; four brothers, Lewis of Brockton, Frederick of West Yarmouth, Edward of New Jersey and James of North Easton; two sisters, Alice Schrafft of Whitman and Mary Louise Newman of North Easton; 12 grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

Surviving with his mother are his wife, Shirley Wood McNally; three sons, Michael, James and Thomas, all at home; a daughter, Miss Maureen McNally of Youngstown, Ohio; two sisters, Mrs. Helen Kennedy of Warsaw and Mrs. Katherine Coughlin of Elba; aunts, nieces and nephews.

Joyce Moody, 76, was welcomed into heaven by her loving husband's arms on December 30, 2014. Joyce was born in Caryville, Florida, on January 10, 1938, to Bernard and Maud'S McWilliams. She attended Jones College in Jacksonville, Florida. On a blind date on Valentine's Day, 1955, she met Robert Moody, the love of her life. They were married the following June and began the living example of a loving, faithful and successful marriage that lasted 59 years.
Joyce, our family's wolf pack leader, was a devoted Navy wife, mother, grandmother and great-grandmother, who had a never-ending supply of love and support to her family and friends. She was preceded in death by her husband, Bob Moody; son, Robbie Moody; parents, Bernard and Maud'S McWilliams; and sister, Tommy Lynn Murphy. She is survived by daughters Lisa (Jeff) Neff and Karen Buller; daughter-in-law, Nancy Moody; sisters, Judy (Henry) Russ, Joanne (John) Player and Linda (Hertis) Davis; brothers Walton and Steve McWilliams; 9 grandchildren, 10 grandchildren and a host of other family and friends.
Our hearts are broken but we know hers is mended now that she is reunited in heaven with her loving husband and son.
A memorial service will be held in the Broadus-Raines Chapel on Saturday, January 3, 2015, at 6:00 pm with visitation beginning at 5:00 pm. A private burial will be held at a later date at Jacksonville National Cemetery. Those who wish may make memorials to Shriner's Orthopedic Hospital in Tampa, Florida.
