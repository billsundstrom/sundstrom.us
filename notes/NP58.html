Pauline BROWN

[I097307]
HIGH POINT - Mrs. Mattie Pauline "Polly" Brown Cameron passed away on February 3, 2012 at Piedmont Crossing Retirement Center in Thomasville, after 103 glorious years.
A long time resident of High Point and faithful member of its First Presbyterian Church, she was born to Dr. James Steven Brown and wife Mattie Phlegar Brown on March 24, 1908, in Flat Rock, NC.
After a childhood in Hendersonville, NC she pursued her formal education at Agnes Scott College and the Cincinnati Conservatory of Music. She lived in Kingsport TN and Miami FL, before moving to High Point in 1941 shortly after her marriage to Mr. Robert Cameron .
In addition to her parents and her husband, Bob Cameron , she was predeceased by her three brothers and one sister. She is survived by her loving children: Judy Sherman of Greensboro, Bob Cameron of Davidson, NC, and Ben Cameron of Maplewood, NJ; three grandchildren: Brian Teer of Virginia Beach, VA, Susan Cameron of Memphis, TN, and Rob Cameron of Charleston, SC; and numerous nieces and nephews. A memorial service will be held in Hendersonville, at a later date.
In lieu of flowers, the family requests that donations be made to Hospice of Davidson County, Piedmont Crossing of Thomasville, or First Presbyterian Church of High Point, NC.
Services are being arranged by Davis Funerals & Cremations of High Point.
News & Record, Greensboro, 6 February 2012

Roger Hope BROWN

[I071894]
Dr. Roger H. Brown, 95, died on Monday, November 24, 2014 at Havenwood Heritage Heights, in Concord, NH.
Dr. Brown was born on June 19, 1919, in Providence, RI, the son of the late Wendell S. and Elizabeth (Daboll) Brown.
He grew up in Rhode Island and graduated from Cranston High School, with the class of 1937. He later attended Brown University. In 1943 he graduated from Harvard Dental School, DMD.
Dr. Brown was a veteran and served as captain, in the US Army Dental Corps from 1943 - 1946. In April 1945 he participated in the invasion of Okinawa.
From 1946 - 1987, he operated a dental practice with Dr. Frank F. Bliss, in Cranston, RI.
He was a member of Trinity Baptist Church, in Concord, NH.
Dr. Brown was a life member of Roger Williams Family Association, American Dental Association, Rhode Island Dental Association, and Sons of The American Revolution.
He was a former member of New England Dental Society, Academy of General Dentistry, Academy of Operative Dentistry, and the Academy of Gold Foil Operators. Dr. Brown was past president of Cranston District Dental Society and Rhode Island Society of Dentistry for Children.
His hobbies and interests include; sailing, mountain climbing, traveling and keeping in touch with his family.
On May 28th 1944, he married his beloved wife of 70 years, Janet Ray of Brookline, MA. and they had 4 children; three boys, and a daughter, David, Gordon, Beverly, Stephen; seven grandchildren and 4 great grandchildren.
A memorial service will be held at Havenwood Heritage Heights, 33 Christian Avenue, in Concord, NH on Saturday November 29th. Interment will take place at a later date in North Kingstown, RI.
To view an online obituary and memorial please go to www.bennettfuneral.com
It has been suggested that those who wish may make memorial gifts to HHH Resident Support Fund, in memory of Dr. Brown.
Arrangements under the direction of Bennett Funeral Home, Concord, NH.
Partially published in the Providence Journal, 21 December 21 2014
