Dillard MUNFORD

[I072369]
Dillard Munford was former chairman of Munford Inc., a convenience store chain that operated 600 Majik Market stores at the time it was sold in 1988.
In 1964, Mr. Munford was the first in Atlanta to sell food and gasoline at roadside markets, according to an article in The Atlanta Journal. He started building a vast retail business in 1946 when he formed Confederate Rock Wool Co. to sell insulation material. The company added overstock items to its inventory, and expanded as a chain of Munford Do-It-Yourself stores.
In 1962, the company merged with Atlantic Ice and Coal and 127 E-Z Market convenience stores. Munford served as president and CEO of the combined company. He acquired World Bazaar import stores, LeeWard Creative Crafts stores, Majik Markets and Minit Markets, and changed the name of the company to Munford Inc.

Dillard Munford, 75; Founded a Chain of Stores
Dillard Munford, the wealthy founder of a chain of convenience stores, died on Wednesday at Emory University Hospital in Atlanta. He was 75.
A family spokesman said that the cause was heart failure, and that until his death Mr. Munford continued to write editorial columns that appeared in 30 weekly newspapers in Georgia.
Mr. Munford, who had homes in Atlanta, Highlands, N.C., and Delray Beach, Fla., was active in civic affairs in Atlanta. He was director of the Atlanta Chamber of Commerce and a trustee for more than 20 years at Morris Brown College in Atlanta, which awarded him an honorary degree in 1976.
His interests were wide-ranging. Until his term ended in April, he was a trustee of the Freedom Forum and its predecessor organization, the Gannett Foundation. The forum is a nonprofit organization that promotes journalism education, assists minority journalism students and conducts international internship programs.
Mr. Munford was born in Cartersville, Ga. He graduated from the Georgia Institute of Technology with a degree in mechanical engineering.
He founded the Munford Company to manufacture rock wool insulation, primarily for Sears, Roebuck & Company, and then ventured into retailing. The first Munford Do-It-Yourself store opened in 1952; 10 years later the chain merged with Atlantic Company. Acquisitions and mergers led to further expansion into a chain of 46 stores.
He is survived by his wife, the former Danne Brokaw Shaw; four children, Page Mallory of Atlanta, Aubrey Morris of Big Canoe, Ga., Robert, of Cranbury, N.J., and Henry, of Atlanta; three stepchildren, Melissa Fleming of New York City and Deirdre Shaw and Crawford Shaw Jr., both of Atlanta; a brother, R. Sims, of Jackson, Miss., and a sister, Dr. Faith Price of Houston.
By KATHLEEN TELTSCH, The New York Times, 18 September 1993

Hugh Benjamin MUNSON

[I122621]
Dr Hugh Benjamin "Ben" Munson was a physician who performed abortions in Rapid City, South Dakota. His practice was illegal prior to the Roe vs. Wade U.S. Supreme Court decision in 1973. Until his retirement in 1986, he was the only physician in South Dakota to perform abortions.
Munson opened his practice in 1967. He was arrested in 1969 for performing an abortion on a 19-year-old patient. Munson challenged his conviction, winning in circuit court. The state appealed, and the South Dakota Supreme Court ruled against him. He appealed this decision, which was rendered moot by Roe.
Munson was tried for manslaughter in the deaths of abortion patients in 1973 and 1985, but was acquitted.
Munson was the son of James and Rena Gilligan Munson, and the father of one-time Rapid City Mayor Jerry Munson.
He died at age 87 in a nursing home in Burlington, Vermont, of complications of Alzheimer's disease.
