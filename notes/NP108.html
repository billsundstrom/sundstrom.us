Mrs. A.D. Morse, R.N. 1962
The communities of Berwick and the surrounding countryside were saddened by the death of Mirtie E. Morse, wife of Aubrey D. Morse, of Berwick, which occurred at an early hour Monday morning, September 10, at Blanchard Fraser Memorial Hospital, after three months of illness following surgery.
She was born in Morristown in 1887, the daughter of the late Willoughby and Eura Hutchinson Barteaux. She spent her early life in Morristown, where she received her early education, afterward attending Acadia University, where she studied two years and then entered training in a Westboro, Mass., hospital, from where she was graduated in 1911. She had previously taught school three years in public schools in Nova Scotia.
In 1912 she married Aubrey Morse of Morristown where they resided until 1925, when they moved to Berwick.
Mrs. Morse was active in the social, church, and community life of Berwick as long as her health permitted. She was a member of Morristown Baptist Church; she and Mr. Morse transferring their membership to the Berwick Baptist Church when they came here to live. She was a member of the WMS and the Ladies Aid and had been active in Red Cross and hospital work.
She had become much interested in the raising and culture of African violets, which, starting as a hobby, became a lucrative business at one time having over 1000 plants, of more than three hundred varieties; many of them rare and beautiful. She was a member of the African Violet Society of America.
Beside her husband, she is survived by three sons, Harold, Donald, and Richard and two grandsons, all of Berwick, and by two sisters, Mrs. Lou Barteaux of Brookline, Mass.; and Miss Erma Barteaux of East Braintree, Mass; the latter who flew down for the funeral.
Funeral services were held from her late home, Maple Avenue, Berwick, yesterday (Wednesday) afternoon, at three o'clock, with her pastor, Rev. J.D. Davison officiaiting. Mrs. F.J. Baltzer was soloist with Mrs. Fred Simpson accompanying. Interment was in the family plot in Berwick Cemetery. The pallbearers were Arthur Lyons, Joe Lonergan, Hiram Thomas, and Howard Margeson.

She worked at the Christian Education Centre Day Care for several years and will be remembered by many who she served in her capacity as Avon representative for 11 years in the 1960s. Hubert Garland CROOK

[I022592]
Hubert Garland Crook traveled to his eternal rest on Monday, May 25, 2020, while living at a long-term care facility in Mandan, ND.
Garland was born at home in the Friendship neighborhood of Attalla County, Mississippi on August 12, 1924 to Jasper Earl and Lena Belle (Ellis) Crook, the first of nine children. He attended primary school at Friendship School, learned shape-note singing at Friendship Church, and completed high school at nearby French Camp Academy. At eighteen, he was called to serve in World War II in the US Army and was on the Normandy beaches on D-Day. Later he served as the personal driver to General Lee, and during this time in France and Germany he met many dignitaries including General Patton and the Duke of Windsor and the Dukeʼs wife. On VE Day, the general asked him to stay on in Europe, but he said, "Iʼm going home!" After military service which included the Korean and Vietnam wars, he retired from the Army as Sergeant First-Class in June 1970.
He married Marian Silbernagel, of Rhame, ND, in 1951 and they traveled the world with their children in the US Army life, including a few years they all lived in Okinawa. After his military retirement, they farmed and ranched in the Deep Creek township of Slope County, ND. He loved bluegrass music, football, and the outdoors, mostly gardening, camping, and his lifelong passion: fishing. He was very proud of his membership in the ND Walleye Whopper Club. He was a true handyman and held a wide variety of jobs in his long lifetime.
In 1986, he married Cheryl Hilden and they made their home in Mississippi, Dickinson, and more recently Bismarck, ND. He was very committed to and had Life memberships in The American Legion, Veterans of Foreign Wars, Disabled American Veterans and Forty et Eight. Through the years for these organizations he served on numerous committees, and held offices on the local, state, and national levels. He and Cheryl were members of Calvary Free Lutheran Church in Bismarck.
He is survived by his wife, Cheryl Crook, his children, Sarah (Craig) McLaughlin, Andy Crook, Lillian Crook (Jim Fuglie), Thomas (Betty) Crook, and Beckie (Jason) Walby, his step-son Brian (Marijo) Swenson, grandchildren and their spouses, Eric (Carrie) Crook, Christina (Chris) Lowe, Kathleen McLaughlin (Jesse) Richardson, Tommy (Whitney) Crookson, Lisa Casserly, Matthew (Jaimee) McLaughlin, Michael McLaughlin, Chelsea and Rachel Sorenson, Duncan and Loren Crook, Jacob and Ryan Walby, Chance and Brody Porsborg, many great-grandchildren, one great-great-grandson, and his sisters Frances (Marshall) May, Ruth Puddephatt, Patsy Craft, and Judy Majovski, his brother, Ronnie Crook, and his sister-in-law Sybil Crook, as well as many nieces and nephews. He was proceeded in death by his parents Earl and Lena Belle, his grandson, Craig Charles McLaughlin II, and his sisters and their husbands, Opal and Paul Pender and Edna Earl and Ruel Nix, his brother, James Crook, his sister-in-law, Margaret Crook, and his brothers-in-law, Mark Majovski and Dewitt Craft.

WEST SWANZEY, N.H. -- Isabel E. (Crouch) Flagg , 83, formerly of 38 Forest Ave., died March 4, 2002, at Maplewood Nursing Home in Westmoreland.
She was born in West Brattleboro, Vt., July 29, 1918, the daughter of Arthur E. and Gertrude (Packard) Crouch.
She was born in the same room as her father, in what is currently the oldest existing house in Brattleboro. She attended schools in Brattleboro and Northampton, Mass. She had lived in Colrain, Mass., then West Leyden, Mass., from 1948 to 1973, before moving to West Swanzey.
Flagg was a member of the Community Church of West Swanzey and had spent many years volunteering at church dinners and fund-raisers.
An avid gardener, she raised and preserved her own food. She enjoyed cooking and baking, music and animals.
Her husband, Arthur R. Flagg , died in 1972.
Survivors include two sons, Raymond N. of Bremerton, Wash., and Edwin E. of Erving, Mass.; a daughter, Janice A. Sevene of West Swanzey; a sister, Dorothy Barre of Northampton; eight grandchildren; nine great-grandchildren and three nieces.
Services were Thursday at the Community Church of West Swanzey.
Spring burial will be in West Brattleboro Cemetery.
