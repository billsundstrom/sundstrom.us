Axel Henning LUNDBERG

[I054366]
Axel H.Lundberg, 67, Dies in Hospital
Axel H. Lundberg, 67, 507 Eighth St., prominent Regan grocer, died Monday at 6:20 p. m. at a local hospital. He had been a patient at the hospital for 4 1/2 months prior to his death.
Mr. Lundberg was born May 23, 1880, at Hastings Minn., the son of Mr. and Mrs. John Lundberg. He attended Hastings schools and at the age of 12 came to Bismarck with his parents and worked in grocery stores here. He moved to Canfield Lake near Regan in 1905 where he started a grocery store. His marriage to Martha Schilling took place in Bismarck Sept. 23, 1908. Some years later he and his wife went into the grocery business in Regan at the time of the founding of the town. He had resided at Regan until six months ago when he and his wife moved to Bismarck. He also was engaged in farming in the Regan area.
Mr. Lundberg was active in civic affairs. He was a member of the Lutheran church, Bismarck Elks lodge and the Bismarck Blue lodge of the Masons.
In addition to his widow he leaves a daughter, Mrs. Thomas Scholl; and a son Robert Lundberg, both of Regan; two grandchildren; and two sisters, Miss Nellie Lundberg, 507 Eighth St., and Mrs. Esther Rhodes, Seattle. Wash. His parents, two brothers and two sisters preceded
him in death.
The body is at the Convert-Boelter funeral home. Funeral services will be held Thursday at 2 p. m. at the Regan church with Rev. Opie S. Rindahl officiating. Burial in the Regan cemetery.
Pallbearers will be August Westerman, Benjamin Lein, Andrew Fischer. F. C. Uhde, William Uhde, George Schilling, Cecil Elkins and Frank Neustal.
- Bismarck Tribune, 23 March 1948
