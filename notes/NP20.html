Sarah Elizabeth BAIRD

[I069771]
Sarah Elizabeth 'Betty' Herring , 95, died of natural causes on November 24, 2014, at home in Lakeland. She is survived by her daughter, Mary Frances 'Francie' Fuhrel (Bob); her son, Robert Jay 'Bob' Herring (Gaye), all of Lakeland, and 7 grandchildren, 12 great grandchildren and 5 great great grandchildren. She was preceded in death by her husband, Leslie Merrill Herring , born in Dexter, Iowa; her brother, Frederick Pelton Baird of Mt. Dora, Florida; her daughter, Jeanne Leslie Gonzales Ober of Miami; and one granddaughter, Jeannine Nicole Davidson of Hawaii. Betty was born on July 5, 1919, in Jacksonville, Florida, to parents Mary Pearl Pelton Baird of Somerset, Kentucky, and Fred John Baird of Warwick, New York, who instilled a lifelong love of traveling in Betty. The family traveled extensively throughout North America. Betty graduated from Georgia State College in Douglas, where she received a degree in Home Economics. Near the end of World War II, Betty married the love of her life, Les Herring , a lieutenant commander in the Navy at Guantanamo, Cuba, stationed out of Jacksonville, who was her brother's best friend. They married on one of his leaves. After his service, they made their home in Jacksonville briefly, and then Miami, Florida. These were very happy, through sometimes lean years, raising a family and enjoying beautiful Miami. Betty sold Avon products for 25 years, using her earnings to pay for the family's many wonderful vacations. Les and Betty eventually retired and moved to Port St. Lucie and then to Ft. Pierce. She and Les started the Orchid Society and the Bottle Club in Port St. Lucie. Les's keen interest in geology and ancient ruins took them to Mexico, Peru, Guatemala, and to many locations in the American Southwest. They also traveled to Cuba, Jamaica, Costa Rica, Panama, and Europe and to Betty's favorite place of all, Hawaii. Betty had many artistic talents. She painted, made pottery, and shell art which she enjoyed selling at craft shows. Betty was a great wife and mother, very social, talkative, independent, and spunky all her life. She will be remembered by all as a genuine Character with a capital C. She will be missed by all who knew her. A celebration of her life will take place in Lakeland at a yet to be determined time. Her final resting place will be at Florida National Cemetery, in Bushnell, Florida, next to her husband. In lieu of flowers, please consider supporting your local public television station, which she loved.
The Ledger, Lakeland, 27 November 2014

Samuel A. BAKER

[I075941]
Samuel A. Baker, who lived for a number of years south of Creighton, died at the home of his daughter, Mrs. Mullen, near Lynch, Nebraska last Sunday, March 28, 1897 of heart failure.
Samuel A. Baker was born January 9, 1828 in New York state. He moved to California in 1849 and removed to Iowas in 1854.
He was married to Miss Cordelia M. Warigh. From this union seven children were born, of whom all survive.
In 1879, he with his family located in Antelope county, four miles south of Creighton, where he has lived up to about two weeks ago, when he moved to Lynch to live with his daughter.
Mr. Baker was looked upon as a good moral citizen and had many admiring friends.
He raised a family of intelligent boys and girls, whom with their mother survive to mourn.
The funeral took place at Lynch, Wednesday, March 31. The remains were laid to rest at that place.
The Courier joins their friends in extending a word of sympathy to the bereaved family.
The Creighton Courier 1 April 1897
