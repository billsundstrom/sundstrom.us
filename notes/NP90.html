Audrey Clem Bauman , 72, passed away peacefully on April 7, 2010, at Duke Hospice Meadowlands, in Hillsborough, surrounded by her loving family. Audrey , known to her family as "Scottie," was born on July 20, 1937, to the late William A. and R. Evelyn Clem in Worcester, Massachusetts. The family moved to Alexandria, Virginia, where Audrey grew up with her sister, Joyce. She was a graduate of George Washington High School and the Washington School for Secretaries and worked as secretary and office manager for firms in the Washington, DC, and the northern Virginia areas. Employed as a secretary for a private research firm in Falls Church, Virginia, in 1959, she met and fell in love with Robert D. Bauman , one of the organizers of the company. They were married in 1964. Moving to Chapel Hill in 1973 with Bob and their two daughters, Audrey was the staff administrator of Volunteers for Youth for several years. Before moving to North Carolina, Audrey was a gifted ice skater, and she was thrilled to find an ice rink in the Orange County area. She treasured Chapel Hill and became an avid UNC Tarheel Basketball fan. When their daughters were growing up, Audrey volunteered as co-leader of the local Girl Scout troop with two friends. Here, she delighted in scouting and camping experiences with girls in the community and joyfully shared cooking and craft skills with them. Another of Audrey 's passions was helping youth to learn and to love the game of tennis. As a volunteer with the Carrboro branch of the Arthur Ashe National Junior Tennis League, she was a resource for kids to develop skills for leadership and academic excellence through tennis. Audrey loved designing and decorating the home that she and Bob built in Chapel Hill and was accomplished in sewing, art, crafts, photography, painting, and those other unique talents that transform a house into a home, warmed by her love and personality. She is survived by her devoted husband, Bob; son Tom Allen (Trisha) of Raleigh; daughters Stacey Bauman and Susan Bauman Smith (Josh) of Chapel Hill; sister, Joyce Hickey of Fearrington; sister-in-law, Bette Bauman Rittinger of Nederland, CO; three grandsons, Thomas Allen, Jackson Allen, and Jordan Allen, one granddaughter, Sophia Smith, and several nieces, nephews and other relatives. Carl Ross CLEMANS

[I059526]
Carl Clemans, 63, Dies in Fall from Scaffold
Carl Clemans died Thursday afternoon of last week following a fall from a scaffold while helping reshingle a house on the Joseph Wagner farm five miles south of Creighton. He was working from a scaffold along with Mr. Wagner and his son, Matt, who occupies the farm, when the scaffold gave way.
Mr. Clemans fell backward to the ground. He died 15 or 20 minutes later.
Matt Wagner jumped clear of the scaffold and was unhurt. Joseph Wagner was stunned but was not seriously hurt.
Funeral services for Mr. Clemans were conducted Sunday, Oct. 7, at the Creighton Methodist Church with the Rev. H.F. Beebe officiating. Interment was in Greenwood Cemetery. Pallbearers were Otto Balleweg, Fred Wulff, Ed Wulff, Anton Konopasek, Ivan Copeland and Harry Tyler.
Carl Ross Clemans, son of Ozzie and Lillian Clemans, was born in Marion, Ia. Feb. 7, 1888. He lived at Marion until he was 20 years old, when he accompanied his parents to Bayard.
On Sept. 25, 1910 he was united in marriage to Miss Grace Pullen at Harrison. In 1912 they moved to Knox county. To this union three sons and one daughter was born.
On Dec. 11, 1941 he was united in marriage to Miss Helen Marie Steinberg at Pierce. They have made their home in Creighton ever since. He worked at the carpenter trade until his death.
Surviving are his wife, Mrs. Helen Marie Clemans of Creighton; his mother, Mrs. Lillian Clemans of Cedar Rapids, Ia.; four children, Marvin Clemans of North Platte, Willard and Alvin Clemans of Lexington and Mrs. Vera Reutlinger of San Carlos, Calif.; two brothers, Vern of Marion, Ia. and William of Plainview.; two sisters, Mrs. Bertha Boyer of Marion, Ia and Mrs. Gretta Richardson of Cedar Rapids, Ia and six grandchildren.
The Creighton News, 11 Oct 1951

Clifford Alvin CLEMENS

[I037583]
Clifford "Cliff" Alvin Clemens transitioned peacefully at age 89 to eternal life with God on Friday, February 11, 2011, in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, surrounded by deeply loving family and in the care of the thoughtful and highly professional staff of Luther Manor. Cliff was born in Milwaukee on February 22, 1921, to George B. and Mary G. (Gregor) Clemens, the seventh of eight siblings. He served his country as a member of the US Army's 105th Cavalry, being honorably discharged as a corporal in 1940. With an exemplary work ethic and as one who truly loved his job, he retired as Interstate Forging's certified purchasing manager after 43 years of employment. He had a lifelong love of horses and dogs, and was an avid golfer. He epitomized the kind and considerate gentleman, and had an endearing smile and a wonderful sense of humor.
Cliff was preceded in death by his dearest wife Judith Yvonne (Lunde) after more than 50 years of marriage, and his brothers and sisters, Lillian, Viola, Howard, George, Harvey, and Norma. He is survived by his beloved daughter, Susan C. (Roger L.) Schneider, grandson, Jacob J. Schneider, and close sister, Muriel C. Patzer, and loving nieces, nephews, cousins and friends.
Cliff enjoyed humming tunes while he went about his daily activities.
Just eleven days short of his 90th birthday, he lost his hum. Donations in Clifford's name to charities, especially those helping our military servicemen, or Luther Manor will be appreciated by the family. Private services will be held at Krause Funeral Home in the spring.

Mary Olive CLEVELAND

[I038492]
Mary Olive Viand, October 3, 1921 - October 3, 2006.
Passed peacefully in her bed on the morning of her 85th birthday. Born in Franklin, Massachusetts, she was a 1941 graduate of Emmanuel College, Boston, MA. Mary spoke fluent Latin and French and was proficient in other romance languages. She was an accomplished pianist and played harp.
Mary worked for the War Department during WW II helping to break Japanese code. While there she met and married Maurice Viand, a young Marine lieutenant. They moved to California and raised their 3 children on the San Francisco peninsula. They divorced after eighteen of marriage and Mary went back to work outside the home. She was an office manager, seamstress, clothing designer, salesperson & eventually fulfilled a lifelong dream of owning and operating a nursery in Half Moon Bay. She is remembered fondly for her ability to figure anything out using a book. "Look it up" was one of her favorite expressions. In her prime she could wire, plumb, frame, design, landscape, etc. and she loved learning new lessons and sharing them with her children.
Living the last two decades in Humboldt County she shared the home of her son in Fields Landing for the last 4 1/2 years. Maryʼs family would like to thank the women who cared for her, which enabled her to remain at home with her family during her final years: Irene Petropulos, Joylin Carrigan, Angel Putney, Mary Genoar, Jennifer "Kat" Gabriel, Jana Hoffman, Allison Tresslor, and numerous others.
Mary is survived by her son, Maurice Viand and his partner Kathy Moley of Fields Landing, CA; her daughter and son-in-law, Patricia and David Salvione of Milwaukie, OR; her son, Kenneth Viand of Felton, CA; her four grandchildren, Zoe Booth of Milwaukie, OR, Nat Salvione of Seattle, WA, Noah Salvione of Milwaukie, OR, Camille Stoaks-Viand of Fields Landing, CA; and her six great grandchildren, Nathan & Anthony Salvione of Seattle, WA, Samuel, Isabelle & Amelia Booth of Milwaukie, OR, and Vincent Salvione of Milwaukie, OR.
