Kerry Alan STRAYER

[I077996]
Kerry Alan Strayer, 56, of Kansas City, MO, passed away August 1, 2013. A memorial service will be held 1 p.m. Tuesday, August 6 at John Knox Presbyterian Kirk, 11430 Wornall Rd, KCMO followed by a reception in the atrium of the church. In lieu of flowers, the family asks that memorials in honor of Kerry be donated to the Kerry Strayer Scholarship Fund at the UMKC Conservatory of Music and Dance, 5100 Rockhill Road, Suite 229, Kansas City, MO 64110.
Kerry was born December 22, 1956, the son of Ernest and Marjorie (Spradling) Strayer, in Fairbury, NE. He graduated from Fairbury High School-Class of 1975. He married his high school sweet heart, Gailyn Kenning on August 10, 1979. He received his Bachelor of Arts Degree from Doane College, Crete, NE in 1981; and his Master of Music in Saxophone Performance from the UMKC Conservatory of Music and Dance in 1985. He was a member of John Knox Presbyterian Kirk, where he served as an elder, sang in the choir and coordinated the acoustics and sound system projects.
As a talented musician, Kerry was a saxophonist, recording artist, bandleader, composer, arranger, adjudicator and private instructor. He led several performing groups and taught private lessons. Kerry released seven recordings, including two critically acclaimed recordings with his septet, Jeru Blue featuring Randy Brecker and Mentor with Gary Foster. He released a recording with his 17-piece big band in 2006 of music he wrote for the Plaza Lighting Ceremony, Christmas in Kansas City: Music from the Plaza Lighting Ceremony. Kerry was the Music Director for the Plaza Lighting Ceremony from 1996 to 2009. Kerry?s groups have performed at several local and regional concert series including Jazz in June in Lincoln, NE and Jazz on the Green in Omaha, NE. As a member of the Tommy Dorsey Orchestra, Kerry had the honor of performing for the inauguration of President George Herbert Walker Bush in 1989. In 1999, Kerry performed a Tribute to Gerry Mulligan concert at the Mt. Hood Jazz Festival in Portland, OR. The Kerry Strayer Orchestra performed for the inauguration of Missouri Governor Bob Holden in 2001. In 2010, Kerry was appointed Artistic Director/Conductor of the Kansas City Jazz Orchestra, after serving as the group?s baritone saxophonist since its inception in 2003. He had also performed numerous times with the Kansas City Symphony's Pops Series.
Aside from his musical talents and accolades, he enjoyed baseball and cheering on the Kansas City Royals. He had visited multiple stadiums across the country and stated, "if I can't be on the bandstand, then there is no place I'd rather be than at the ballpark."
Kerry was preceded in death by his father, Ernest; and sister, Linda Sand. He is survived by his wife of 34 years, Gailyn; mother, Marjorie; brothers, James Strayer and wife Kay of Aurora, NE and Tom Strayer and wife Marion of Augusta, GA; nieces, Staci Lunders of Lincoln, NE; Rene Sand and fiancé Jordan Brown of Arvada, CO; Darcy Strayer of Denver, CO; Jennifer Strayer and husband Lane Sorensen of Bloomington, IN; nephew, Matthew Strayer and wife Manon of Papillion, NE; great-nieces, Morgan and Anna Lunders of Lincoln, NE; brothers in-law, Don Sand of Lincoln, NE and Kenneth Kenning of Endicott, NE; mother in-law, Janis Kenning of Fairbury, NE; and aunts, uncles, cousins and a host of friends from church and the music community locally and around the country.

EXETER - Margaret Catherine Schulz , 103, of Exeter, formerly of Abington, Pa., died peacefully Friday, June 6, 2008, at St. Josephs Hospital in Nashua.
She was born in Philadelphia on Sept. 8, 1904, the daughter of Charles and Charlotte Stricker. As a young woman, she worked at the Stetson Hat Company and later as a telephone operator until she married Henry Irwin Schulz in 1928. Their son, James, was born in 1936.
In 1991, she moved to Dover in order to be near her son and family. Gifted with a "green thumb," she created beautiful gardens wherever she lived, continuing her gardening until the last two months of her life. She also enjoyed music and dancing throughout her life.
She later moved to Exeter, and early this year was presented with the town's "Boston Post Cane", an honor bestowed upon the town's oldest living resident. Known for her humor, friendliness, and "housecleaning," the residents and staff of Exeter Langdon Place and Sunbridge/Exeter, where she spent her last years, enjoyed her presence.
She was predeceased by her husband, Henry Irwin Schulz , in 1963.
She is survived and loved by James Henry Schulz , her son; Ann Tibbitts Schulz, wife; her three grandchildren, Stephen Clark Schulz, Jean Chambers Schulz, and Linda Ann Schulz; and four great-grandchildren.

Eugene "Gene" Stricklan
August 27, 1936 - October 15, 2018
Gene left this earth to join his Lord on October 15, at HopeWest Hospice Care Center.
He was born in Salmon, Idaho to Frank Stricklan and Marie Chaffin Stricklan. The family moved to Grand Junction in 1945 and to Collbran in 1950. Gene graduated from Collbran High School in 1954, and joined the Navy, where he served in San Diego and Pearl Harbor on a sub tender. After his service, he returned to Grand Junction and began his career as a heavy equipment mechanic. He first worked for Grand Junction Steel where he fabricated steel used in the Plateau Valley School. Next he helped build Vega Reservoir and the South Side Canal. He worked construction in many different states, including the Aleutian Islands and Prudhoe Bay, AK where he helped build the Alaskan Pipeline.
He "retired" in 1999 and moved back to Collbran, where he spent the next 18 years as busy as ever, repairing ATVs, snowmobiles and anything else that needed it, gathering firewood for church members, and guiding at Wallace's hunting camp. He was always willing to help a neighbor in need. He also stayed busy for several years restoring a 1971 Ford Bronco.
Gene loved camping with his family, hunting, fishing, snowmobiling, traveling, riding ATVs, and riding horses and mules. He was an active member of the Grace Bible Church in Collbran. He was a loving husband and father, and was very proud of his kids and grandkids.
He is survived by his wife of 59 years, Helen; daughter, Lori (Bret) Guillory; son, Kent (Lori) Stricklan; daughter, Kay (Jim) Malloy; sister, Frances (LeRoy) Bauman; grandsons, Todd (Kortnie) Stricklan, Scott (Ariel) Stricklan, Peter (Christina) Malloy, Brian Malloy, and Jared Guillory, and three great-granddaughters.
Services will be held on Saturday, October 20, at 11:00 a.m. in the Collbran Auditorium.
The Daily Sentinel, 18 October 2018

Roy Thomas STRICKLAN

HAMILTON - Roy T. Stricklan, Jr., 69, of Hamilton passed away Tuesday, Jan. 29, 2013 at the Marcus Daly Hospice Center as the result of cancer due to Agent Orange while serving in Vietnam.
He was born Oct. 10, 1943 in Salmon, Idaho to the late Roy T. and Margaret Stricklan Sr. Following his completion of high school he enlisted in the U.S. Navy where he served aboard the USS Seminole.
He married Chris Stricklan. Roy worked for the Boeing Company for 32 years. He always felt so fortunate to come back to this area where the best fishing in the world was. Roy also loved elk and bird hunting.
At last a grown man, Roy purchased an Arabian colt. He called him "Bucky." All the cowboys knew for sure he would end up being Roy's worst enemy. But, Roy offered up his friendship and they were best buddies for years.
He was preceded in death by his parents.
Surviving include his wife Chris; brothers, John Stricklan of Salt Lake City, Utah, William Stricklan of Salt Lake City, Utah, and Robert Stricklan of Rose Lake, Idaho.
A memorial service will be held on Saturday, March 16, 2013 at 2:30 p.m. at the Daly-Leach Chapel in Hamilton with military honors presented by the U.S. Navy, Hamilton American Legion and the Marine Corps League - Bitterroot Detachment. There will be a reception following in the funeral home's community room. The family suggests memorials to St. Jude's Children's Hospital.
Ravalli Republic | 13 March 2013.
