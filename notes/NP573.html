Ernst John WATTS

[I072394]
Judge Ernst John Watts, 73, died February 2 of pancreatic cancer.
He had recently returned from a trip to Moscow, where he served on the faculty of a training program for Russian judges.
Watts was elected Walworth County judge in 1960 and became the county ºs first circuit judge when he outpolled fellow jurist Erwin Zastrow in a special election in 1964. He was re-elected to a second six-year term in April 1970.
In December 1973 he was appointed dean of the National Judicial College in Reno, NV. He resigned that post in 1985 and spent the next 12 years traveling the world to help train judges. In recent years, he made nine trips to Russia for this purpose.
Watts was a lifelong bachelor and had resided in Reno for the past 22 years.
The Third Branch, spring 1997

Doris WAY

[I085762]
Doris "Dolly" (Way) Rudder, of Canton, passed into transition with dignity and grace on March 26, 2014, at home surrounded by her family.
Born in East Haddam, November 28, 1926, daughter of the late Oscar and Madeline (Gleason) Way, she lived in Hartford until 1948.
She modeled for the former G.Fox & Company and in ads for SNETCO where she was also a switchboard/telephone operator. Moving to Farmington and then Canton, where she has lived for 66 years, she raised her four daughters, Donna Lyn, Darcy Joy, Dale Robin, and Dawn Ivy, here.
An independent, alluring, and outgoing woman, Dolly and Donald built the Cob-Web Shop in 1964; her Flea Market in Canton was her passion. She also organized the first two Brownie troops in the Canton area and was active in the Girl Scouts for many years. She attended the First Congregational Church of Canton Center then became a dedicated member of the Rosicrucian Order. AMORC, she believed was that life was about learning lessons and that love could conquer all.
She was a proud member of the Red Hat Society, Phoebe Humphrey Chapter DAR, and the Order of Eastern Star. She purchased a camper she called "Dolly's Folly" and traveled all over the US. A past member of the Ladies Aid Group at First Congregational Church of Canton Center, Canton Volunteer Fire Department Ladies Auxiliary, Life member of Canton Historical Museum, NCHA, and Yankee and Mid Florida L.O.W's. (Loner's on Wheels)
Besides her parents she was predeceased by her former husband Donald Rudder and leaves her four daughters Donna L. Rudder of New Britain, Dale R. Hadeka and her husband William of Castleton, VT, Darcy R. Griswold of Raleigh, NC, and Dawn Rudder Zeffiro with whom she made her home in Canton; eight grandchildren Andrew, Kristin, Kate and Will Hadeka; Emily and Harris Griswold; and Donnie and Renee Zeffiro; and two great grandchildren Andie and Annabell Hadeka; and two nephews and two nieces Thomas and Robert Rudder and Penny Woodford and Sharon Edgerly.
She was also predeceased by her sister Mildred Leyden.
Her family will receive friends Wednesday, April 2, 2014 from 5-7 PM at the Carmon Funeral Home & Family Center, 301 Country Club Road, Avon. Fred W. WAY

[I067133]
Frederick W. Way, 60, of Briar Lane, Corry, Pa., died Wednesday, May 23, 2012, at UPMC Hamot from injuries sustained in a motorcycle accident.
Fred was born in Corry, Pa., on August 5, 1951, a son of the late Charles A. and Dorothy L. Sherman Way.
He was raised and educated in Corry and graduated from Corry High School in 1969. He then enlisted in the U.S. Army and served during the Vietnam War with D Company, 11th Calvary, nicknamed "The Blackhorse." When he returned to the United States, he moved to Idaho and volunteered to fight forest fires. Fred then moved to Eden, N.Y. and drove trucks and later moved to Corry where he was a truck driver for Campbell Trucking.
Fred attended Spring Creek Community Church. He was a member of the American Legion Elmer C. Carrier Post 365, Corry, and coordinator of the Spring Creek Fiddler's Convention, as well as a certified EMT. He enjoyed riding and restoring motorcycles, and collecting guns. He was an avid history and Civil War enthusiast.
He is survived by a son, Brandon Way, of Corry, Pa.; three stepdaughters, Connie Bockus and her husband, David, of Buffalo, NY., Amy McCallum, of Buffalo, NY., and Cheryl McCallum, of San Diego, Calif.; two sisters, Ann Payne and her husband, John, of Spring Creek, Pa., and Mildred Pedercine and her husband, Ronald, of Corry, Pa.; and a brother, Charles Way and his wife, Mary, of Corry, Pa. Also surviving are two grandsons, Nick and Riley McCallum; and several nieces and nephews.

Earl WEATHERFORD

[I102143]
Earl Weatherford, 89; Orange Park, Fl passed away peacefully under the gentle care of Community Hospice at the Hadlow Center June 4th, 2008. In Earl's final days, he was surrounded by the loving and caring members of his family and remained a fighter to the end. Earl was born, August 8th, 1918 to Nathan and Mamie in Columbia, Mississippi and spent his formative years living in both Mississippi and San Angelo, Texas. As part of the "Greatest Generation", he served in both the Civilian Conservation Corps and "The Fighting Seabees" in New Caledonia in the South Pacific. He returned from the war to "his bride", Christine Rowe, who remained "his bride" for over 66 years. Together, they raised 5 children; Judy Johnson (David), Dianne Soulby (Bob), Jill Stump (Jon), Robert (Joann) and Jim (Lynn). As the family continued to grow, there were 16 grandchildren and 12 great grandchildren. After the war, he returned back to Florida where he worked Civil Service until his retirement in 1973. He operated his own plumbing business and when he retired, he enjoyed gardening and fishing. Earl was dedicated to community service. He served as a Lieutenant in the Orange Park Volunteer Fire Department and was a member of the Orange Park United Methodist Church. Visitation will be 5:00 to 7:00 pm, June 5th, 2008 at Jacksonville Memory Gardens Funeral Home and graveside services will be at same location at 11:00 AM, June 6th, 2008. In lieu of flowers, please make donations to the "Meals on Wheels" program in Clay County or Community Hospice on Sunbeam Road.

Burr and Gladys had a daughter Nancy Burr Weaver born in Schenectady, N.Y. Feb. 22, 1930 who married Dr. John Mackay on May 16, 1953 and moved to British Columbia.

Burr and Gladys are both buried in the Fruitvale Cemetery.

Carla Lee WEAVER

[I061542]
Carla Lee (Weaver) Brown, 75 years young, born August 14, 1936, in Grand Coulee, passed away Friday, Jan. 20, 2012, at Sacred Heart Medical Center in Spokane.
Carla attended High School in Grand Coulee. She received her A.A. Degree from Big Bend College.
Carla was an independent business owner with her love for sewing that started in her younger years. She designed and hand-created most all of her own clothes since college. Carla's creative designs were a passion that continued to please and comfort her in raising her family. She worked many jobs to sustain herself and successfully raise a wonderful, loving family. She was a seamstress and had her own company called, The Back Room for quite some time. One could say that, "The One who dies with the most fabric-Wins!" Well-Carla WON! She mended and repaired whatever landed on her door step, in her own due time.
Her other hobbies consisted of beautifully designed and hand stitched cross stitching, that took hours of her time and made with unconditional love. Many of her beautiful creations can be found on the walls of her home and many in her family.
Carla was also active in Rebecca Lodge in Grand Coulee, she was activities director at the Grand Coulee Hospital and the Nespelem Nursing Home. Carla also provided an in home care service for some of the locals.
Carla's favorite hobby was mostly her cooking. She enjoyed having special meal nights such as taco night, steak night or fried chicken night. Carla could be found many times shopping at the local stores usually offering kind words to all that she encountered during her shopping extravaganzas. All appreciated her wonderful creative cooking and she always seemed to have enough for whoever showed up for supper. Carla loved to have all three healthy meals prepared for her family. Everyone, one who knew Carla, knew that her cooking was her comfort.
Carla also loved her cats, there was many times she would be snuggled up in her chair with her cats curled up in her lap reading a book. The cats and her enjoying the love and attention they all shared between them. There was not a day that did not go by that the cats were not surrounding her returning the love she provided for them.
Carla was very active in her family. She enjoyed keeping the family up to date and stayed connected. She always made time to talk to anyone that had a problem or just new news to share. One could say she loved to help others and never complained. Carla was happy with a very simple life, she was a warm, loving, humble woman to share your most intimate thoughts. In return she would offer her loving ear and soothing words of advice only if asked.
Carla was loved unconditionally by all who knew her. Her words of wisdom, advice, support and loving touch will be deeply missed by all that she touched in this world. May she rest peacefully knowing she will live forever in our thoughts, prayers and most of all, our hearts.
Survived by her loving husband of 14 years, Clarence S. (Charlie) Brown, Coulee Dam; children: Michael J. White, Coulee Dam, Jessica J. Jardine, Electric City; Rick L. Seibold, Valerie R. Lehmer, Deann K. Brewwer, Ohio; Joyce L. Johnson, Louisville, Texas; siblings, Leta Johnson, Vancouver, Wash., and Kaye Hathaway.
She was preceded in death by her parents Helen and Ernie Weaver, siblings: Arlene Kelly, Collier (Bud) Weaver, Jema Jean McDonald, William Kirchner; and children, Christine L. Seibold.
Burial Services will be held at Spring Canyon Cemetery on Friday 1:00 p.m., January 27, 2012.
Memorial Services will be Saturday 11:00 a.m., January 28 at Faith Community Church 16 Grand Ave, Electric City, with a luncheon to follow. Both services are open for anyone wishing to attend and offer support to the family.

A memorial service will be held at the Shreveport Elks Lodge #122, 310 East Preston Ave., Monday, July 22, 2013, at 2 pm. The service will be conducted by Rev. He worked his entire professional career in the insurance industry and attained his CPCU and ARM designations. He served as president of the Bellevue Rotary Club and the South Omaha Social Settlement Association. He was secretary and treasurer of various Shrine, Masonic and Scottish Rite groups. FUNERAL SERVICES HELD FOR WILLIAM WEAVER
Funeral services were held last Saturday from the Linus Wurtz Chapel in Boston for William Alonzo Weaver formerly of Hamburg and the father of the late Willis E. Weaver and Charles F. Weaver, the last owners of the Erie County Independent. Mr. Weaver died of pneumonia on Feb. 24 within three weeks of what would have been his 78th birthday anniversary. He had long been associated with the Independent.
The Rev. William F. Melberg, pastor of the Boston Community Church conducted the services at the Chapel and later in Forest Lawn Cemetery, Buffalo.
Mr. Weaver came to Hamburg when his sons purchased the Independent in 1929. In his younger days he was one of the foremost railroad signal engineers and today many of his basic patents are still in use in various safety and control signal devices. He was consultant for foreign railroads and for many years held responsible positions with the Santa Fe.
He is survived by two brothers, Sheldon E. Weaver, 80 years of age now retired and living in Fredonia, a younger brother, Edson P. Weaver, 75, of Rockville, Md., three nieces, Mrs. George Hack and Mrs. Leslie Bull, both of Fredonia and Mrs. Newton Weller of Dunkirk, also Blanche Merchant of Schenectady and Edson Merchant of Fredonia.
Hamburg Sun and Erie County Independent, 4 March 1948

Minnie Mabel WEBB

[I034137]
Minnie Mabel Webb was the 5th child of Charles Douglass and Laura Deborah Webb. She was born in Lowell, Wisconsin and grew up on her father's farm in North Lowell.
In her early 20s she became active in the Free Methodist Church.
For many years she worked as an evangelist spreading the good news of salvation through Christ in small communities of Wisconsin.
On July 19, 1893 she was united in marriage with fellow Free Methodist preacher, Clarence Victor Hughes. To this union one child was born, Clarence Victor Hughes Jr., on April 19, 1894.
On June 6, 1894 her husband died. Thus, in the space of one year, Minnie became a wife, mother and widow.
On March 11, 1903 she married Frank Eugene Doubleday in Beaver Dam, Wisconsin. To this union one child was born, Neal Frank Doubleday.
On March 24, 1924 her second husband died in Lake Mills, Wisconsin.
Minnie outlived all her siblings. In the last years of her life she lived with her son, Neal Doubleday, in Decatur, Illinois where Neal was a professor at Millikin University.
Minnie was a committed follower of Jesus her whole life.
Her family members knew her as "Aunt Minnie."
