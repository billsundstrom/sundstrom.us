Richard Allen DIX

[I062453]
Richard Allen Dix, age 80, of Janesville, died Sunday, June 23, 2013, at the Waverly Health Center in Waverly, Bremer County, Iowa, from complications of renal failure.
Richard was born on July, 15, 1932, in Cedar Falls, Blackhawk County, Iowa, the son of Harvey and Mabel (Rogers) Dix.
He was raised in rural Janesville and graduated from Janesville High School in 1950.
In 1953, Richard entered the United Sates Army where he served in Japan during the Korean War. He was honorably discharged in 1955.
Richard was united in marriage on June 25, 1961, to Sharon Clark at Trinity United Methodist Church in Hazelton, Iowa.
He spent his entire life as a self-employed farmer, running his truck garden and general farming. Richard was a member of Trinity United Methodist Church in Waverly for over 50 years where he enjoyed singing in the choir. He also belonged to Farm Bureau, Cattleman's Association, and was active in the Bremer County Republican Party. Richard also served on Bremer County Zoning Board, Bremer County Board of Adjustment and as a Jackson Township Trustee. Richard enjoyed cracking walnuts, traveling, and singing, however, what he enjoyed most was working and spending time with his family.
Richard's memory is honored by: his wife of 52 years, Sharon of Janesville; a son, William (Gerri) Dix of Shell Rock; two daughters, Melinda (Ed) Szynalski of Rochester, Minnesota and Michelle (Tony) Smith of Lake Mary, Florida; eight grandchildren, Michael, William (Will), and Elizabeth (Libby) Dix, Mary and Katherine Szynalski, and Eric, Noah, and Bennett Smith; three brothers, Deforest (Carol) Dix of Janesville, Larry Dix of Janesville, and David Dix of Mount Vernon; and a sister, Patricia (William) Bowen of Reinbeck.
He was preceded in death by his parents and a sister-in-law, Karen Dix.
Funeral services will be held at 1:00 p.m. on Wednesday, June 26, at Trinity United Methodist Church in Waverly. Burial will follow at West Point Cemetery in rural Janesville. Visitation will be held on Tuesday, June 25, at the Kaiser-Corson Funeral Home in Waverly where the family will greet friends from 5 to 7 p.m. Memorials may be directed to Richard's family and online condolences may be left at www.kaisercorson.com.

Duane Harrison DOCKINS

[I106023]
Duane Harrison Dockins of Newport, OR passed surrounded by family on March 17, 2019 to be with his Lord, our Father. He was born to Joseph & Ida (Moffatt) Dockins and raised on wheat and cattle farm in Clyde, Kansas. He developed a strong sense of determination and family while enduring hard times and experiencing the Great Depression as a young man. He attended a small local one room schoolhouse and graduated from Clyde High School in 1942.
With the outbreak of WWII, Duane joined the United States Merchant Marines and trained at the U.S. Maritime Service Base at Catalina Island. He served his country as a Ship Helmsman sailing throughout the world providing resources and much needed supplies to our fighting forces.
During one of his stops in Portland, OR. Duane met the love of his life, Bernice Duncan. They later married in June of 1947. They went on to complete their family with 2 daughters and 2 sons, remaining a dedicated twosome for the rest of their time together celebrating their 71st Wedding Anniversary on June 27, 2018. Bernice was one of sixteen children and he lovingly teased her that he had gotten "the pick of the litter."
Duane worked selflessly throughout the years, providing a home and security to his family. Ever the humble and grateful father, he never sought more than he needed and taught his children the importance of an honest dayʼs pay for an honest dayʼs work. Duane spent his free time as a family man dedicating himself to the raising of his children, involving himself in family activities, joyful automobile trips and of course teaching his sons to pitch and coaching little league baseball, the game he loved.
Duane held many jobs during his life. He contracted to the US Postal Dept. as a mail transfer between mail trains and the Clyde Post Office until moving to Portland, OR in 1952. He worked firing stationary steam boilers for Malarkey Inc., a door manufacturing plant. He was also employed by Simpson Timber. In 1960, the move was made to Lincoln County where the family spent the majority of their years in Waldport. Once there, he worked as a truck mechanic for Lundy Bros. and, in 1968 until his retirement, he worked at Georgia Pacific Toledo Division first in the yard crew, the switch crew, and finally as a warehouse scale man.
In his retirement, Duane spend his days with walks on the beach collecting agates and polishing them, attending family get togethersʼ, listening to country music, and always looking forward to the Little League World Series. He was articulate and well read. He loved to discuss politics, cars, baseball and of course he loved to regale any and all with the stories of his adventures on the high seas. For awhile he served on the board of directors of a local chapter of retired Merchant Marines helping to get benefits for fellow Marines.
He will be greatly missed by all who loved him. He is survived by his wife of 71 years, Bernice. His younger sister Julia of Beaver City, NE and his children Darla Bauer of Newport, Sharon Dockins of Waldport, Stephen Dockins of Philomath and Joe (Shelly) Dockins of Eddyville. Grandchildren Michael, Melissa, Jace, Derik, Bryce, Taylor, Matt, Tristan & Kelly. Great Grandchildren Logan, Myrah & Kharter and numerous nieces and nephews. He was preceded in death by his parents Joseph & Ida, and his brothers Edward, George and Frank Dockins.
Newport News Times, 17 March 2019
