Edwin Doss BARLOW

[I092366]
Edwin Doss "Tar" Barlow died April 16, 2014, in Greenville.
"Tar" was born July 19, 1924, to Edwin and Ima Bickham Barlow in Greenville. He graduated from Greenville High School in 1941 and New Mexico Military Academy in 1943. He entered the Army and mustered out in 1946. He was last stationed in Panama.
"Tar" graduated with a business degree from Southern Methodist University, then entered the family florist/nursery business from which he retired after 46 years.
"Tar" served as chairman of the Greenville Hospital Authority, the entity which planned, financed, and built Hunt County Hospital, now known as Hunt County Regional Medical Center of Greenville.
"Tar" was for many years active in various civic groups and organizations, including Rotary, Masonic Order (32nd degree Mason), YMCA, Little League, MOAA, and the American Wine Society.
He was a member of United Presbyterian Church in Greenville where he for many years designed, planned, and planted the flower gardens.
Surviving are a daughter, Kathleen Hodges of Dallas; daughter and son-in-law Beckie and Donald Dreibelbis of Lebanon, Pa.; grandchildren Hayden Hodges and wife Erin of Dallas, Sally Fienup and husband Chris of Memphis, Tenn., Kara Julius and husband Robert of Towson, Md., Capt. Sheldon Hodges of Fort Stewart, Ga., LTJC Ellen Dreibelbis of Bethesda, Md., and Samuel Dreibelbis of State College, Pa.; and great-grandchildren Jo Fienup, Clark Julius, Henley Grace Hodges and Huck Julius.
"Tar" was preceded in death by his parents and his wife of 60 years, Sally Edson Barlow.
A memorial service will be Saturday at 3 p.m. at the United Presbyterian Church in Greenville. Chris Campbell will officiate.
Memorial donations may be made to the United Presbyterian Church, Landscape Designated Fund, 5905 Stonewall Street, Greenville, 75402.
16 April 2014

Levi A. BARNARD

[I068716]
Levi A. Barnard Dies at Hospital
Veteran Merchant and Prominent in Fraternities Here - Was 67 Years Old
Levi A. Barnard, aged 67 years, died at midnight, last, at the City Hospital, following an operation yesterday afternoon. Mrs. Barnard is the only near relative surviving.
Mr. Barnard became ill less than two weeks ago. His condition continued to grow worse and an operation was determined upon. The operation was successful, but heart failure developed and death came shortly after midnight.
Mr. Barnard was born in December, 1843, in Massachusetts. He came to Ithaca when a young man and engaged in the grocery business. He was employed for a number of years by the Quigg Company, and later he conducted a grocery with Charles A. Brown, at the corner of State and Aurora Streets, under the firm name of Brown & Barnard.
Mr. Barnard was prominent fraternally. He was one of the oldest members of Ithaca Lodge 71, I.O.O.F., and was a charter member of Hawthorn Rebekah Lodge 163, and of Cascadilla Lodge 89, Knights of Pythias. Both lodges will attend the funeral in a body.
The funeral will be held at 3 o'clock Tuesday afternoon, from the home. The Rev. Wallace E. Brown will officiate. Interment will be in the City Cemetery.
Ithaca Daily Newsm 27 May 1911. page 5.

Bernard Barnes, 77, Dies; Retired Time Inc. Executive
Bernard Barnes, a retired vice president and secretary of Time Inc., died Sunday of a stroke while vacationing in Isla Verde in Puerto Rico. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan and Bethlehem, Conn.
Mr. Barnes joined Time Inc. in 1931, after graduating from Harvard University, and became promotion manager of Fortune and then of Life. During World War II, he served in the London Bureau of the Office of War Information.
After he returned to Time Inc. in 1945, Mr. Barnes became director of special projects, and was elected vice president in 1953 and named secretary in 1960. He retired in 1968.
Mr. Barnes, an avid gardener, organized and served on the board of the Bethlehem Land Trust and served as a director of the Friends of the Litchfield Nature Center and Museum in Connecticut.
He is survived by his wife of 50 years, Carolyn Payne; three sons, Timothy, of Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y., Peter, of Great Falls, Va., Fred, of Cobbleskill, N.Y.; a daughter, Molly, of Manhattan, and 11 grandchildren.
A memorial service will be held at 4 P.M. next Thursday at the Harvard Club.
The New York Times, 5 March 1987

Bertha BARNES

[I064425]
Bertha Jane Barnes, the daughter of Ira Barnes and Eliza Carnegie, was born at Alexandria, N.Y., 4 Oct. 1827, and brought to Sugar Grove, Ill., as a child. She married first in 1848 Erastus Terry, who died in 1850. She married secondly in 1852 Benjamin Westover. Bertha and Benjamin Westover kept a farm, first in Kane County, and later at Genoa in DeKalb County. They were the parents of five children. In 1871 when a great fire broke out in Chicago, Mrs. Westover looked outside and commented that there must be a big fire somewhere because of all the smoke. When a man in a huge wagon came by the farm to collect food and clothing to take to the people of Chicago, Mrs. Westover baked and made food to be sent to the people. Bertha Westover died of congestion of the liver, complicated by rheumatism. She was buried three days later on Dec. 18, 1894.

Clifford Henry BARNES

[I064920]
Clifford Henry Barnes, the son of Clarence Barnes and Mabel Banker, was a member of Wesley United Methodist Church, the Kane County Farm Bureau, Aurora Historical Society, Sugar Grove Historical Society, and the Big Rock Plowing Match Association. Big Rock's annual Plowing Match is a small country fair in the rural community of Big Rock, Illinois, located just west of Sugar Grove. The first Big Rock Plowing Match was started in 1895 to provide a competition for farmers to plow against one another. It is one of the last plowing match competitions still in existence in the U.S. Clifford Barnes passed away when he was 83 at Mercy Center. Funeral services were held on July 5th from the Healy Chapel, with burial in Sugar Grove Cemetery. The family suggested that, in lieu of flowers, memorial donations be made to the Big Rock Plowing Match Association. He was preceded in death by his wife, Frieda (Runge) Barnes and one grandchild.
