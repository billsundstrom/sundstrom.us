Henry TEMPLEMAN

[I057109]
Henry Templeman passed away at home in Scottsdale, Arizona, on July 25, 2014, at age 75, after a long and courageous battle with cancer and heart disease. Henry was born in Vilnius, Poland on January 4, 1939, to David and Reeva Colish. After crossing the ocean by ship alone as a young boy after the war he lived with relatives in Winnipeg and was then adopted by Wolf and Sophie Templeman from Portland, Oregon in 1951. Henry graduated from Grant High School in Portland in 1957 and obtained his law degree from the University of Washington. After working as a Snohomish County Deputy Prosecuting Attorney for a year under Lloyd Meeds he joined the firm of Jordan & Britt in 1965 and continued working as a well-respected trial attorney in Snohomish County until he and Darlene retired in the fall of 2006 and moved to their home in Scottsdale. Henry is survived by his best friend and domestic partner of 28 years, Darlene (Hayes) Kuehn; his sons, Steven (Joanne) and their boys, Drew and Bryce, of Buckley, Wash.; Dan (Kristle) and their boys, Brady and Colby, of Lake Stevens, Wash.; and Jason (Mindy) and son, Hayden of Sammamish, Wash. He was preceded in death by his son, David, (Danna and two of their children. Steven and Jordan, reside in Arizona, and Richard is currently in Louisiana). He is also survived by a granddaughter, Ashley, and great-granddaughter, Brooklynn. Henry 's love, sense of humor and mischievous smile will be greatly missed by all who loved him, and he will never be forgotten. He was so grateful to be able to spend his retirement years in the Valley of the Sun. It was a rare day that he didn't say, "I love it here." At his request, no services will be held. Please make donations in Henry 's memory to the American Cancer Society or a charity of your choice.
The Daily Herald, Everett, WA, 27 July 2014

Owen Rockefeller TERRY

[I117803]
Owen Rockefeller Terry Jr., 72, former deputy director of the American Association of Dental Schools, died of prostate cancer May 29 at Seasons Hospice in Baltimore. He was a resident of Columbia.
Mr. Terry worked for the association from 1968 through his retirement in 1996. He was twice acting executive director and interim director. Upon his retirement, he was named an honorary life member.
He was born in Detroit and moved with his family to Evanston, Ill., when he was a child. He graduated from Princeton University in 1958 and spent several years as a buyer for Montgomery Ward stores in Chicago.
After he joined the association as an assistant secretary, the organization relocated to Washington, and Mr. Terry followed as well. Mr. Terry, a gifted writer, was the unofficial historian of the organization and served as director of the office of communications and membership, as well as special secretary to the executive director.
He was interested in architecture and was an ardent reader. He also was a fan of classical and jazz music.
A son, Michael Owen Terry, died in 2005.
Survivors include his wife of 47 years, Constance Ann Terry of Columbia; and a son, Mark Rockefeller Terry of Sykesville.

Ruth was an active member of the Troy Presbyterian Church for many years. She was a member and past president of the Presbyterian Women's Association and the Thursday Study Club. She served as both Brownie and Girl Scout Leaders. Ruth enjoyed working with young children and always had stories and poems for every occasion. She and Jim traveled extensively in the United States and in their retirement they also went to Mexico, Canada, England, Austria, Switzerland and the Caribbean. But she always the happiest at her home on Redington Ave. She enjoyed cooking and baking, her flowers and garden, classical music and time spent with her grandchildren.
