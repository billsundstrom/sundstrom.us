Phillip Dean NELSON

[I116924]
Phillip Dean Nelson was born on April 4, 1942 to Wendell and Charlotte Nelson in Rose Park, Utah, where he spent his childhood. Phil graduated from West High School in 1960 and went on to serve a mission in Bavaria, Germany for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. After his mission, Phil attended barber school and the University of Utah. He was also in the Air National Guard reserves. He married Inge Boeckmann in July of 1966 and they had three children, Dean, Deanna, and Jennifer. Phil and Inge were later divorced.
During his career years, he worked as a barber to put himself through college, then for LSI Testing, Walter Kidde & Company, and finally for Hewlett Packard in 1977. He traveled the world doing computer systems installation and repair. He retired from HP in 2000, after working there 27 years.
In November, 1992, he married his wonderful wife, Janet Barker, in Boise, Idaho. During their 27-year marriage, they lived in Boise, Marsing, and finally Nampa, Idaho. They loved to travel in their RV, and spent many winters in Mesa, Arizona until they settled year-round in Nampa.
Phil enjoyed many hobbies and interests. He loved to fish and hunt with his Dad. He and his good friend, Ron Bullock, loved to fish at Strawberry Reservoir in Central Utah. He liked camping and river rafting. He was a member of the Experimental Aircraft Association and flew his own plane for many years. He loved woodworking, experi-menting with his 3D printer and building CNC machines. He was, at one time, president of his local Airstream club. When he passed, Phil was president and one of the founders and instructors of the Boise CNC club that met at Woodcraft of Boise.
Phil was preceded in death by his brother, Val, mother, Charlotte, father, Wendell, sister, Peggy Turner, and stepmother, Pearl Nelson.
He is survived by his wife Janet Nelson, his children, Dean (Maryann) Nelson, Deanna (Kelly) Bassett, Jennifer (William) Morey, his brother, Earl Nelson, and nine grandchildren, Michelle, Curtis, Danielle, Kendall, Sarah, Aidan, Rayne, Elyse, and Ashlyn.

D. Marie NEUROTH

[I003763]
Paper: Argus Leader (Sioux Falls, SD)
Deceased: Marie Heglin, big-hearted
Date: May 26, 1999
BERESFORD - Standing only 4 feet 10 inches tall, Marie Heglin was small in stature, but had a big heart.
"If everyone was as kind as her, the world would be a better place," said her daughter, Ann Hoffman of Beresford. "She always saw the bright side of any situation."
D. Marie Heglin, 73, died Monday, May 24, 1999, at Avera McKennan Hospital of an apparent heart attack.
Heglin worked as a clerk at Casey's General Store in Beresford for the past 18 years, not quitting until she entered the hospital a week ago.
Whenever the store needed extra help, she always was willing to work on her day off, her daughter said.
"She was always there when we needed her," said her former manager and friend, Pat Bickett.
Having never owned a car, Heglin walked to work everyday in all types of weather.
Heglin and Bickett worked together for 12 years and got to know each other very well.
"I loved her like my own," Bickett said. "Customers loved her."
If someone couldn't afford an item, Heglin was happy to give them money without expecting anything in return.
"Many people have said it (the store) will never be the same without her friendliness," Hoffman added.
Heglin was a member of Beresford Senior Citizens, where she enjoyed spending time with her friends.
D. Marie Neuroth was born Dec. 3, 1925, in rural Union County. She attended rural school in Emmett and then Beresford High School.
She married Arnold Klopstad, and he later passed away.
She then married Ralph Heglin. They lived in the Beresford area. Her husband died in 1996.
In addition to her daughter, survivors include four sons: Michael Neuroth of National City, Calif., Arnold Klopstad Jr. of Beresford, Art Klopstad of Sioux Falls and Greg Klopstad of Yankton one more daughter, Roseann Boldridge of Overland Park, Kan. five grandchildren and four brothers: Robert of Centerville, Leo of Beresford, Tony of Reed Springs, Mo., and William of Beresford.
Visitation will be from 2 to 8 p.m. today at Wass Funeral Home in Beresford, with a prayer service at 7 p.m.
A private burial service will be held on Thursday.

Anne Agnes NEVILLE

[I095025]
Anne Agnes Bullock married 2nd Gilbert Bullock, 3rd John Maltravers. Will written Feb 1375. (Fourteenth Century England. Volume 4. By J. S. Hamilton. p88/9) --- The Bull Inn at Barkham is named after the Bullock family of Barkham and Arborfield who were Lords of both Manors for several centuries. When in Barkham, they lived in the manor house, Barkham Court, that was once surrounded by the old moat next to the church. Here can be seen the late 13th century wooden effigy of Agnes Bullock, daughter of Sir Thomas Neville, from whom the family inherited this estate. --- The wooden effigy in the church porch of a recumbent lady in a long, loose dress and linen head-dress is reputed to be an effigy of Anne Nevill or Agnes de Nevill (b 1316 Arborfield, Berkshire, d after 1343) daughter of Thomas Nevill, married (1330 or 1342) Gilbert Bullock (b 1312) second son of Robert Bullock. In 1331 Gilbert Bullock was lord of the manor of Arborfield. In 1327 Agnes de Nevill brought an action for waste was brought against John Botiller who claimed that John held the manor of Barkham for life of her inheritance. In 1334 Agnes gained possession of the manor of Barkham from John Maltravers who had been conveyed the manor in 1330 by Philip and John Botiller the sons of John Botiller. In a later settlement the manor was passed to John Bullock with a life interest reserved to an Agnes Ganefield against whom a suit for dower was brought by Agnes the widow of John Maltravers in 1368.
