Walter Wilhelm KLOCKO

[I091668]
PORTAGE - Walter Wilhelm Klocko, 85, of Portage, passed away Saturday, Oct. 27, 2007, at Divine Savior Healthcare in Portage.
Walter was born in Pomeroy, Iowa, on Dec. 29, 1921, the son of Fred and Etta Duis Klocko. On May 30, 1939, he was united in marriage with Arlene Madole in Olewine, Iowa. He served his country in the Navy during World War II. He worked at International Harvester Implement in Waterloo, Iowa, until he moved his family to Portage in 1950. Walt worked at Stutzer Granite and spent many years at Martin Marietta Aggregate, where he became the first certified blaster in Wisconsin. He enjoyed fishing, woodworking, cribbage and working on the race car of his son-in-law, Cary.
Survivors include daughters, Barb (Cary) Lalor and Jane (Gordy) Kabele; grandchildren, Steve (Stephanie) Genrich, Sandy (Kevin) Solterman, Greg Hibner, Angie (Ron) Tetzlaff, and Konnie (Chuck) Corso; great-grandchildren, Ashley Genrich, Kristen Genrich, Steve LaPointe, Nathan Hibner, Collin Waech, Tim Tetzlaff, Tom Tetzlaff, Brittany Klocko and Samantha Corso; numerous nieces and nephews; and his very special friend, Lucy Davidson.
Walt was preceded in death by his beloved wife of 59 years; his son, Tom; his parents; brothers, Jack, Cass, Pit, Hank, Fred and Ben; and his sisters, Lena, Tena, Anna, Ida, Frieda and Martha.
Funeral services will be held at 11:30 a.m. Tuesday from St. John's Lutheran Church in Portage. Pastor Alan Boeck will officiate with burial to follow at Silver Lake Cemetery in Portage. Friends may call at the church Tuesday from 10:30 a.m. until the time of services. Portage Community Funeral Home is assisting the family.

Richard D. KNADLE

[I087062]
HILLSBORO, Wis. - Richard "Dick" Knadle, 69, of Hillsboro passed away Thursday, Jan. 27, 2005, at St. Joseph's in Hillsboro.
Dick was born Oct. 26, 1936, in Hillsboro to Ernest and Beatrice (Walker) Knadle, who preceded him in death. He was also preceded in death by his brother, Rodney Knadle.
Dick grew to adulthood in Hillsboro, graduating from Hillsboro High School in 1954. He was united in marriage to Mary Cook on Sept. 7, 1957, in Hillsboro. He graduated from the University of Wisconsin in 1959.
Dick and Mary raised their children in the Kansas City area where Dick was the senior vice president of Bartlett and Grain Co. Dick and Mary returned to Hillsboro in 1994, after retirement. Since that time, one of Dick's finest accomplishments was his dedication to the Good Samaritan, an organization in Hillsboro that provides food and clothing for the less fortunate. He was also a member of the Hillsboro Lions Club, a former member of the Hospital Foundation Board, and an avid volunteer for any worthy cause. Dick will be forever remembered in Hillsboro for his passionate support and love for his community.
Survivors include his wife, Mary of Hillsboro; daughter, Kathleen (Norm) Brasher of Atlanta; daughter, Kelly (Paul) McKenzie of Overland Park, Kan.; son, Kyle (Lori) Knadle of Olathe, Kan.; siblings, Randy (Kathy) Knadle of La Crosse and Bob (Barb) Knadle of Chilton, Wis.; grandchildren, Amanda Brasher, Emily Brasher, Kristen McKenzie, Jack McKenzie, Curtis Knadle and Collin Knadle; Uncle Emil (Wilhemina) Knadle of Hillsboro; and Aunt Agnes Stowell of Richland Center, Wis.
Funeral services will be held at 11 a.m. Monday, Jan. 31, at First Congregational Church in Hillsboro, with the Rev. Donald Ehler officiating. Burial will be in Mount Vernon Cemetery in Hillsboro. Friends may call at Picha Funeral Home in Hills-boro from 3 to 6 p.m. Sunday. The family requests that memorials may be given for the St. Joseph's Hospital Foun-dation or the Good Samaritan.

Mrs. Quinlan also had earned a paralegal certificate and worked as a volunteer court observer in Buffalo courts.

An art lover, she was a member of the Albright-Knox Art Gallery. She also actively supported the Buffalo Philharmonic Orchestra, the Irish Classical Theatre, other area theater companies, local chamber music performances and ballet.
Her husband, C. Paul, died in 1991.

Janet loved the Adirondacks and enjoyed her camp in Bald Mountain Colony on Third Lake in Old Forge, NY for over 50 years.
She spent her winters in North Fort Myers, FL for the last 18 years.
