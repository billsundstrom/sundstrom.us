Eileene Louise DAEHLER

[I090765]
Eileene L. Butler of Homewood at Crumland Farms died on August 27th. She had been diagnosed as having progressive supranuclear palsy (PSP), a degenerative neuromuscular disease.
Eileene Louise Daehler was born on April 4, 1925 on the family farm in northern Illinois. After graduating from Chadwick High School she attended and graduated from the University of Illinois (BA, MA), where she was inducted into Phi Beta Kappa. She taught school in Gibson City and Dixon, Illinois until she married William N. Butler in 1951.
The Butlers lived in various Washington D.C. suburbs as well as in Hawaii and Germany. While living in Potomac MD Eileene worked at Mark Twain School and, after retirement, was a 1000-hour volunteer at the Regional institute for Children and Adolescents (RICA).
After moving to Frederick in 1995 she became a regular winner of awards at the Frederick County Fair for her fine crocheting work. Her other interests included linguistics, literature, and travel. Until recently she and Mr. Butler spent their winters in Naples FL.
In addition to her husband, Mrs. Butler is survived by her children, Jocelyn Gail Butler (Feryl Masters) of Chocowinity, N.C.; Shauna Lani Saunders (Bruce) of Durham, N.C.; and Mark Jeremy Butler (Karen) of Frederick; and by grandchildren Duncan, Luca, Miciah, and Lilani Masters; Diane Saunders; and Allison Butler.
A memorial service will be held at the Unitarian Universalist Congregation of Frederick at a later date. Memorial contributions in Eileene's name may be sent to the congregation at 4880 Elmer Derr Road, Frederick MD 21703.
The Frederick News-Post, 29 August 2014

Anna DALASSENA

[I015537]
Anna Dalassena was an important Byzantine noblewoman who rose to position of sort of Empress-Mother during the reign of her son Emperor Alexius I.

She married Johannes Komnenos, a nephew of Emperor Isaac I Comnenus (emperor 1057-1059).

Her younger son Alexius rose to the throne after vicissitudes of politics. Alexius was for many years under the strong influence of her eminence grise. She is described as a wise and immensely able politician.

She was in a uniquely irregular fashion, crowned as Empress Augusta by her son the emperor, instead of the rightful claimant to the title, Alexius' wife Irene.

Anna Dalassena was the effective administrator of the Empire during Alexius' long absences in war campaigns: she was constantly at odds with her daughter-in-law and had assumed total responsibility for the upbringing and education of her granddaughter Anna Comnena.

David Martin DALE

Pastor David Martin Dale was born July 23, 1900, in Dale, Norway. He and Hildur Erickson of Duluth, Minnesota, the daughter of George and Wilhelmina Erickson were united in marriage in 1926. They were the parents of five children: Phebe, David Jr., twins John and Jo Ann and Milton.
Pastor Dale served five congregations in the Bagley, Minnesota area before coming to Sacred Heart in 1929. He served Trinity Lutheran Church as their pastor until 1942. While serving Trinity, his wife, Hildur died in 1936 and his son, John died in 1933.

Pastor Dale and his family moved to St. Paul in the spring of 1942, to organize Rosetown Lutheran Church, now Roseville Lutheran Church.

Richard Lemont DALES

SOUTHERN PINES, N.C. - Richard Lemont Dales , MD, 67, of Southern Pines, NC, passed away October 6, 2013, in Fox Hollow Senior Living Community from complications of Parkinson's Disease.
Richard was born July 16, 1947, in Columbia, SC, to Lt. Col. Robert E. Dales (Ret.) and Edna Stork Dales .
Richard had many accomplishments throughout his life. He graduated from Summerville High School in South Carolina in After graduation he attended Clemson University where he obtained a Bachelor of Science degree in 1968 and a master's degree in zoology in 1972. From Clemson he went on to the Medical University of South Carolina in Charleston, SC, and earned his Medical Degree in 1976. Continuing on with his education, he served a one year internship and one-year residency in OB/GYN in Greenville, SC. He went on to complete a one-year externship in Orthopedic Surgery at Fort Knox Army Post in Kentucky. In 1980 he began his General Surgery residency in Eisenhower Army Medical Center at Fort Gordon in Augusta, GA. After completing his residency he joined the General Surgery staff at Womack Army Medical Center at Fort Bragg, NC, in 1986. His career continued at Pinehurst Surgical Clinic in Pinehurst, NC, from 1986-2006. He also retired from service in the U.S. Army Reserve as a colonel that same year. He was very proud of his accomplishments in the medical field and also in the U.S. Army, serving his country in places such as Grenada and was recalled to active duty during Desert Storm. During his military service, he received the Army Service Ribbon, Army Commendation Medal, Armed Forces Reserve Medal/Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal, Meritorious Service Medal and National Defense Medal.
Richard was predeceased by his father, Robert E. Dales , and younger brother, James M. Dales .
He is survived by his wife of 43 years, Sheryle "Sherry" S. Dales of Southern Pines, NC; one son, Richard "Rick" L. Dales , Jr.; daughter-in-law, Racheal C. He is also survived by his mother, Edna Stork Dales , brother, Fred Dales , and sister, Debra Riddle, all of Columbia, SC. Also surviving is his older brother, Robert "Bob" E. Dales , Jr. of Summerville, SC.
A private service will be held at the family cemetery in Columbia, SC. A memorial service in Pinehurst, NC, will be held at a date to be announced.
In lieu of flowers, donations to the Clemson University Alumni Association would be appreciated. Everyone who knew Richard knew that his "blood ran Clemson orange." His orange scrub suits and orange running shoes will be missed by all at the Surgical Clinic and Moore Regional Hospital.
