ANNA ENQUIST
It is with great sadness, we announce the passing of Anna Enquist (nee Brunel), on June 5, 2016, at the Saul & Claribel Simkin Centre. Anna is lovingly remembered by her children, Irene (Robert Memka), Marcel (Doris) Monney, Denise (Randy Waylett) and Carol (Mark Thiessen), 14 grandchildren and 19 great-grandchildren; her sister Colombe Chabbert; sisters-in-law, Mary Enquist and Evelyn Enquist, and numerous nephews and nieces. She was predeceased by her loving husband Ernest (2010); her son Edouard Monney (2013); her parents, Edouard and Alphonsine (Bourrier) Brunel; her brothers, Alphonse (Marthe), Leon (Marie), Roger (Flore), Gilbert (Germaine); and by her twin sister Melina (Julien) Thorimbert; and brother-in-law Louis Chabbert. Anna was born on December 31, 1922 and raised on the farm at St. Lupicin. In 1941, she married Camille Monney in St. Lupicin and moved to Winnipeg, where she worked at Pelland's Catering and the Marion Hotel in the 1950s. She married Ernie Enquist in 1965 and together they operated the Ile des Chenes Hotel in the late 1960s, where 14 hour work days were common. In 1980, they built their cottage at Bird River, where many years of enjoyment were had with relatives and friends playing cards, boat rides, water skiing and fishing. They enjoyed many trips to the U.S. with their 23 foot trailer and parking in a shopping centre was a common occurrence. The whole family has great memories of gatherings at Anna and Ernie's for what was a Christmas Eve tradition, plenty of food and gift exchanges with all the grandkids, ending way into the night. Anna was skilled at many hobbies including gardening, sewing, crocheting and knitting. She made beautiful afghans for each of her children and grandchildren to treasure. We would like to thank the Saul & Claribel Simkin Centre and prior to that, the Golden Links Lodge for the wonderful love and care the staff showed to Anna. She was surrounded by her children in her final hours sharing fond memories that will not be forgotten. Granny, as she is known to her grandchildren and great-grandchildren, has left an indelible stamp on each of their lives. In lieu of flowers, memorial donations may be made to the Canadian Cancer Society or the Alzheimer Society. Viewing will be held at 10:30 a.m. with Mass in celebration of Anna's life at 11:00 a.m. on Thursday, June 9, 2016 at St. Timothy Parish, 135 John Forsyth Road, St. Vital. Interment will follow at the St. Boniface Cemetery.
Winnipeg Free Press, 7 June 2016

William B. BRUNER

[I061847]
William could not read or write, so no written record is available. The information recorded for the Bruner's was set down by Fred Leroy Bruner from information from Martha Bruner and Hiram Parkman.
The Bruner stock came originally from Germany and settled in Virginia sometime before 1850. Great Grandpa Bruner's family spoke only German. The next generation moved West into Kentucky. They spoke both English and German in the home.
William and family homesteaded on 160 acres at Palo Verde, Arizona. Those were rugged times. Most of the country was brush infested with rattlesnakes, coyotes and other forms of wild life. The Buckeye canal was a young project and had to be patched up after every rain. The brush dam gave a lot of trouble. Water ran the year round in the Gila and floods made the dam expensive to maintain. It was up to the farmers to do much of the repair work. So the women spent much of their time holding down the home and keeping things going. Mother planted an orchard and vineyard, and practically all the fruit we ate came from it. There were apricots, pears, peached, figs, almonds, plumbs, quinces and crabapples, and of course mulberries. There were not as many insect pests to contend with, and I never knew of a poison spray of any kind being used. In the vineyard were muskets, tokays, mission, thompson seedless, and a wine grape. From the orchard, and the vineyard and a garden came the major portion of the living.

Darwin Vernon BRUNS

[I024035]
Spencer - Longtime Spencer resident and businessman Darwin Vernon "Bud" Bruns passed away Sunday, Sept. 30th, at the Veteran's Hospital in Sioux Falls.
Bud was born on March 31, 1925, near Spencer, where he resided his entire life until losing his home in the Spencer tornado in May of 1998. he and wife Eileen then moved to Salem, where he resided until his death.
He graduated from Spencer High School before serving 4 years in the U.S. Navy as a ship-to-shore radioman on the U.S.S. Missoula, a highly decorated battleship. Upon returning to Spencer, he married Eileen May Jenneman on May 21, 1949. A short time later he took over his father's egg and creamery business, Bruns Produce. Failing health forced him to retire in 1995 after nearly 40 years of business. He was a member of the VFW, serving as Quartermaster for several years. Bud was an avid golfer.
Those grateful for having shared his life include wife, Eileen, daughter, Susan and her husband Pastor Jon Lindekugel of Hutchinson, Minn., sons, Barry and wife Mary of Spencer, Mike and wife Sheri of Chanhassen, Minn., and Steve of Sioux Falls, SD, Steve's special friend, Deb Kinney, 8 grandchildren, as well as many family and friends. He was preceded in death by a daughter, Mrs. Roger (Kim) Richter and his sister, Helen Zulk. Surviving siblings include Marge Voigt of Sioux Falls, Don Bruns of Spencer, and Ona Mae Jewell of Georgetown, Texas.
A memorial services will be held Wednesday evening at 7:30 p.m. in the Kinzley Funeral Home in Salem. Funeral services will be held at the Trinity Lutheran Church in Spencer Thursday at 2 p.m.

Robert Joseph BRUNSTAD

[I092800]
Robert J. "Bob" Brunstad, 86, of Chippewa Falls, passed away peacefully, Wednesday, October 10, 2012 surrounded by his family at Colfax Health & Rehabilitation Center while under the care of St. Joseph's Hospice.
He was born April 1, 1926, in Chippewa Falls, to Joyce (Kelly) and G. Robert Brunstad. He attended Bartlett Grade School in the town of Eagle Point. He graduated from Chippewa Falls Senior High School in 1944. Bob farmed in the town of Eagle Point and helped operate Brunstad's Dairy.
Bob married Yvonne Vincent on May 8, 1948, at Trinity United Methodist Church in Chippewa Falls where he was a life-long member. They moved to Bemidji, Minn. where he was employed at J.C. Penney and later went to Portage where he worked at Portage Hardware Store before moving home to Chippewa Falls in 1955. He was employed by Chippewa Plastics and later Applied Research, which became Amoco Chemical. Bob was transferred to Fullerton, Calif., where he was co-manager at the Amoco facility. He and his family returned to Chippewa Falls in 1967, where he continued with Amoco, retiring in 1988. During retirement, Bob enjoyed driving the CESA 10 van.
Quiet times in his woods behind the family farm, his little cabin, chopping wood and watching wildlife were Bob's favorite activities. He played tennis in the Over 33 Men's League and enjoyed canoeing with the Flambeau Flockers, sailing, and Sunday drives. Bob was a caring family man and good neighbor. If snow needed to be shoveled, a windshield scraped, or a load of wood delivered, Bob was there lending a hand.
Survivors include his wife of 64 years, Yvonne; a daughter, Ellen (Dale) Yindra of Chippewa Falls; two sons, William (Mary) Brunstad of Chippewa Falls; Thomas (Gina Zappa) Brunstad of Mahtomedi, Minn.; six grandchildren, Heather (Adam) Rogge, Zachary Brunstad, Lauren (Kevin) Brunstad Monks, Leslie (Nick) Bauer, Anthony (Peyton) Brunstad, Vincent Brunstad; five great-grandchildren, Evan, Seth and Ashton Rogge, Fiona Monks and Beau Bauer; and two brothers, Dean (Betty) Brunstad of Merlin, Oregon and Loyd (Fern) Brunstad of Chippewa Falls.
He was preceded in death by his parents and one brother, Glen Brunstad.
A memorial service will be held at 11 a.m., Saturday, October 13 at Trinity United Methodist Church, 201 W. Central St., Chippewa Falls with the Rev. Terri Koca officiating.
Visitation will be from 4-7 p.m., at Trinity United Methodist Church, Chippewa Falls and one hour prior to the service Saturday, also at the church.
Memorials in Bob's honor may be designated to St. Joseph's Hospice, the Alzheimer's Association, or to Trinity United Methodist Church.

Henry Joseph BRUZDA

[I094914]
Henry Joseph Bruzda died Jan. 20. He was 77.
Known as "Hank" to his friends and "Joe" to his family, Henry was a beloved husband, father, grandfather and great-grandfather, as well as a retired materials engineer, a journeyman tool-and-die maker, an avid golfer, an accomplished ballroom dancer, a lover of theater - especially the American musical - and an RV enthusiast. He will be remembered for his integrity, patience, curiosity, persistence, humor, energy, problem-solving, innovations, and courage.
Born in Tiltonsville/Yorkville, Ohio, Henry served two tours in the Navy. He received honorable discharges in 1944 and 1946. He was called up again for Korea in 1950, but was excused early under an honorable hardship discharge.
Henry was present at the commissioning of the aircraft carrier, U.S.S. Rendova, in Portland on Oct. 20, 1945.
After his Navy service, he completed his machinist and tool-and-die making apprenticeship at Wilbur Wright Technical Institute and the Cass Apprentice School in Detroit, Mich. His high school equivalent diploma was granted by the State of West Virginia, Department of Education.
Henry was a journeyman tool-and-die maker when he moved into engineering through on-the-job training and examinations at Ford Motor Company in Livonia, Mich. He worked in the automotive, space and materials engineering fields in Michigan and California until he retired in 1989.
Henry first settled in Detroit, Mich., where he met Loretta Dorothy Arie. They were married on April 24, 1948.
He moved from there to Westlake Village, Calif., in 1968, and to Corvallis in 1975. He moved to Florence in 1992 while maintaining his property in Corvallis.
Survivors include his wife; son, Joseph Randall Bruzda ; daughters, Cynthia Bruzda Chapman, Deborah Denise Frassetto and Carolyn Bruzda Rose; grandsons, Andrew Joseph Chapman, Anthony Frassetto and Alexander Bruzda ; granddaughters, Gina Marie Frassetto-Tallerino, Marina Chapman-Groenewald, Angela Frassetto-Shamoon, Kelsey Bruzda , Emily Ferries, Lauren Ferries and Jennifer Bruzda ; great-granddaughter, Marissa Arianna Tallerino; and sister, Bernice Hebert.
Henry was preceded in death by his parents, Rose and John; brothers, John and Adam; sisters, Helen Fry, Annette Jarrett, and Rose Walewski, who passed this past March.
A memorial mass will be at 1 p.m. Saturday, April 23, at St. Mary, Our Lady of the Dunes Catholic Church in Florence. Following the service, a reception for family and friends will be at 3 p.m. in the club house at Florentine Estates.
A bench overlooking Heceta Churn, just below the Heceta Head Lighthouse, will be installed to memorialize Henry through the Oregon State Parks Trust. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions can be made to the trust, marked for the " Henry Bruzda Memorial Bench," in care of Cynthia Chapman, 329 N.W. 23rd St., Corvallis, OR 97330.
Corvallis Gazette-Times, 20 April 2005
