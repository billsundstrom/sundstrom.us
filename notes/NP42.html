Kendrick William BLODGETT

[I089832]
Kendrick W. Blodgett, Jr. passed away peacefully at his home on June 9, 2007, at the age of 81.
Born July 7, 1925, in Ottawa, IL, to the late Kendrick W. Blodgett, Sr. and Kathryn Boyd Blodgett, he attended Purdue University and was an airman in the United States Air Force during WWII. He worked with his father who developed hydroponics and carried the process overseas during the war. Mr. Blodgett worked for the DuPont Company for more than 30 years including 12 years in Montague, MI, before relocating to Wilmington, DE. He was the "Captain" of the musical production of Showboat for annual Montague Band Boosters fund raisers. Ken loved to sing. He was an avid golfer and seasoned fisherman. He was a member of the DuPont Country Club and was a Marshall during McDonalds LPGA Championship tournaments.
He is survived by his wife Patsy Loyd Blodgett, his brother Herbert B. "Boyd" Blodgett, 5 daughters, Kaye Weaver, Cindy Blodgett, Gini Gladstone, Patsy Von Stetten and Betsy LeRoy; a son Rick Blodgett; 4 granddaughters, Brenda Krasuski, Karrin Krasuski Burns. Kendre Von Stetten and Lauren Gladstone; and 5 grandsons, Max Krasuski, Kurt Von Stetten, Jason Stoltz and Kyle Stoltz.
A Celebration of the Life of Mr. Blodgett was private.
Memorial donations may be made to "A Gift of Life," c/o Maggie Palminteri, 1180 6th Avenue, Suite 1400, New York, NY 10036.
The News Journal, 19 June 2007

Ralph Brian BLOOD

[I085167]
PEPPERELL/GROTON, MA -- Ralph Brian Blood, 58, of Wimauma, FL, formerly of Pepperell and Groton, MA, passed away, his hand in his wife's hand, on May 28, 2015, after a short, courageous battle with esophageal cancer.
He was born Nov. 8, 1956 in Nashua, NH, and lived his entire life in Pepperell, MA and Groton, MA prior to retiring to Florida last year. He retired after a 31 year career with NSTAR Electric and Gas, and also operated his own commercial cleaning company for over 25 years.
Brian loved spending time at his family camps on Moosehead Lake, Maine; and considered that to be the most peaceful place on Earth. He was an avid hockey player, and continued to play up until the time of his diagnosis in November 2014.
Brian was predeceased by his mother, Mabel Jean Blood and his brother, Hosmer Blood.
He leaves his loving wife, Sharon Blood of Wimauma, FL; his father, Ralph Blood of Pepperell; and his sister, Marcia (Lenny) Scofield of Pepperell.
Brian leaves a daughter, Ashley Blood (Greg Creighton) of Pepperell; a son, Travis Blood (Megan) of Providence, RI; three step-sons, Brett Berberian (Jennifer Ty) of Grafton, Nathan Berberian (Jaclyn Goldsworth) of Upton, Adam Berberian of Natick; and two grandchildren, Riley and Mia Berberian. He also leaves four children that he helped raise and loved as his own, Ben Thibault of Lincoln, NH, Greg Thibault of Brookline, NH, Andrew Thibault of Salisbury, and Julia Metcalf of Santa Monica, CA. He leaves many nieces and nephews, in-laws, extended family and more friends than could ever be counted.
Brian was loved by all who met him and he touched so many lives. He will be greatly missed. He lived life to the fullest and loved the expression "we are not here for a long time, we are here for a good time."
Lowell Sun, 7 June 2015

Mrs. E.S. Sundstrom Taken by Death
Mrs. Emily S. Sundstrom, of 310 Linden avenue, died yesterday afternoon at her home at the age of 85 years. She had been ill for several weeks.
Born in Sweden Jan. 23, 1865, she came to the United States with her parents when she was less than a year old. The family settled in Andover where she lived for more than 30 years, coming to Joliet in 1898.
Mrs. Sundstrom was a charter member of the Svea Lodge, Vasa; and was active in the Bethlehem Lutheran Church; the Sunshine Circle of the King's Daughters; and the Ridgewood Ladies' Aid.
Surviving are two sons, Frank Olson of Cortez, Colo; Gilbert Olson of Loraine, O.; four daughters, Mrs. Irwin Schieber, Miss Bernice Sundstrom and Mrs. Arthur N. Thunberg all of Joliet; and Mrs. Paul R. Pearson of Springfield; a step-daughter, Mrs. I.S. Olson of Los Angeles, Calif.; 12 grandchildren and 18 great-grandchildren.
Funeral services will be held at the Carlson Funeral home here tomorrow evening at 8 p. m. with the Rev. G. F. Andree officiating. The body will be taken to the Lutheran church at Andover Thursday morning where last rites will be Thursday afternoon at 1:30 p. m. Burial will be in the Lutheran cemetery at Andover.

COUDERSPORT, PA - William R. (Billy) Bobinko, 59, of 6 Locust St. died Monday (July 2, 1990) in Port Allegany after an apparent heart attack.
Born June 17, 1931, in Coudersport, he was a son of John and Mary Cirnak Bobinko. On Oct. 29, 1988, in Coudersport, he married the former Clara Skibinski, who survives.
A resident of the Indiana, PA for many years, Mr. Bobinko had owned and operated the Betty Miller Hotel in Indiana before returning to Coudersport 10 years ago.
He was a member of the St. Eulalia Catholic Church of Coudersport and the Antique Auto Club of America.
Surviving besides his wife are two sisters, Mary Delimen of Sheffield and Mrs. Joseph (Peg) Pesock of Coudersport; and many nieces and nephews.
He was predeceased by three sisters, Elizabeth Bobinko, Helen Pesock, and Anna Dugan; and two brothers, George Bobinko and John Bobinko.
