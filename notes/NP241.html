John H. HENN

[I013490]
OBITUARY:
John H. Henn was born Jan. 28, 1862, in Campbell township, Warrick county, on the farm where he resided until his death. He was married to Anna Engel Dec. 2, 1883, who departed this life Jan. 5, 1913. To this union seven children were born. Edward, the youngest, died in childhood, and the oldest, Mrs. Joe Young, preceded him to the grave seven years ago. Surviving are two daughers, Mrs. Louis Michel of Chandler and Mrs. Frank Basler of Mt. Vernon, and three sons, August Henn of Evansville and Albert and George Henn of this county, also eight grandchildren and four sisters: Mrs. Ben Engel, Mrs. Joe Zapp and Mrs. Philip Klippel all of Evansville and Mrs. John Bawell of Buckskin. Mr. Henn was one of Warrick county's most prominent citizens and was county commissioner at the time of the building of the new courthouse. Mr. Henn was a kind and conscientious father and neighbor and will be missed by all who knew him. Funeral services will be conducted by Rev. Bretz Friday morning after which burial will take place at the Asbury cemetery.
Boonville, Indiana Standard, 26 March 1926

Patty Lou HENN

[I031227]
NEW HARMONY -- Pattie L. Owen, 73, of New Harmony, passed away on August 28, 2005, at the home of Carol and Rodney Logan, who cared for her during her short illness.
Pattie was born on February 13, 1932, in Millersburg, Ind., to George and Helen (Heim) Henn. Besides her parents, she was preceded in death by her husband, Harold Owen, in 1991, a son, Timothy Hoehn, in 2004, a sister, Hazel, and brothers, Gilbert and Clarence.
Pattie enjoyed baking and gardening and sharing the fruits of her labor with friends. Many will remember the "goody bags" at Christmas. Pattie loved animals. In her youth she enjoyed horseback riding and trick riding. She enjoyed the outdoors and watching the many species of birds that came to her feeders. She volunteered twice a week with bingo at New Harmonie Healthcare.
She is survived by a daughter, Toni Lyke; a son, Tom Hoehn; six grandchildren, Jason, Josh and Jenny Lyke, Susan, Simon and Samantha Hoehn, all of Mount Vernon; a daughter-in-law, Carolyn Darr; close friend, Rodney Logan, whom she loved as a son; and sisters, Margaret Jones of Corydon, Ind., and Lucille McFadden of Mount Vernon.
Friends may call today, August 31, from 4 to 8 p.m. at Werry Funeral Home in New Harmony.
Services will be at 10 a.m. Thursday, September 1 at Holy Angels Catholic Church. Burial will follow at Maple Hill Cemetery in New Harmony. The family and friends would like to thank the VNA Hospice Nurses. Memorial contributions can be made to the Visiting Nurse Association Hospice.
Evansville Courier & Press, 31 August 2005

Funeral services will be Aug. 19, 1994 at 11 a.m. at the First United Presbyterian Church in Winterset. The Rev. David Bauer will officiate with the body to be cremated. Cremains will be buried at a private graveside service for the immediate family at the Chase-McDonald Cemetery.
Visitation will be Thurs. Aug. 18 from noon to 9 p.m. with family visitation from 6:30 - 8 p.m.
She was born April 30, 1964 in Dexter to Donald F. and Donna Marie Henry. She married Scott Rustan on Feb. 22, 1986. Prior to her death, she was engaged to Kevin L. Baudler. She has lived in Madison County for 25 years and was a member of the First United Presbyterian Church. She was a 1982 graduate of Winterset High School and attended Northwest Missouri State University, and Simpson College and was a 1993 LPN graduate of Southwest Community College. She was employed by the Madison County Memorial Hospital, working in the offices of the Health Trust Physicians Clinic.
Surviving relatives include her father and step-mother Donald F. Henry and Nancy Clark of Earlham; mother and step-father, Donna M. Vaughan and Milton Vaughan of Winterset; fiancé, Kevin L. Baudler of Winterset; two sons, Donald Scott Rustan and Kevin Michael Baudler, both of Winterset; one future step-son, Dustin Baudler of Winterset; three future step-daughters, Shannon, Melissa and Kelli Baudler, all of Winterset; one sister, Christine L. Thornburg of Winterset; and grandmother, Ethel Henry of Earlham.
