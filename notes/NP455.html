Ernest RITTER

[I068850]
Ernest Ritter , 96, of Patril Hollow Road, the retired manager of the Hardwick Farmers Cooperative Exchange, died on Sunday in Mary Lane Hospital.
In 1920, he was named superintendent of the Calvin Paige Agricultural Fund. He retired from the positions in 1959.
He was also a former cemetery commissioner for the town, and a past chairman of the Hardwick Community Fair. He was also known for planting trees at the library, the cemetery and on the town common.
Born in Chicopee, he lived in Hardwick most of his life, and was graduated from the University of Massachusetts.
He was a veteran of World War I.
He was a 25-year curator and past treasurer of the Hardwick Historical Society, and a member, deacon and a past treasurer of the Unitarian-Universalist Church. He was also a past trustee of the Lucius Library.
He belonged to the Grange, the Eden Masonic Lodge, the John Weir American Legion Post, and the Hardwick Rod and Gun Club.
He had also been a 4-H leader, a scoutmaster, and a Boy Scout committeeman.
He leaves his wife of 69 years, the former Sibyl G. Fiske; two sons, Henry F. of Venice, Fla., and John H. of Hardwick; a daughter, Audrey Ritter Madden of Brooklyn, N.Y.; 10 grandchildren, and 17 great-grandchildren.
A private graveside service will be conducted later. Charbonneau Funeral Home of Ware is in charge. There are no calling hours.
Memorial contributions may be made to the Historical Society, P.O. Box 114, Hardwick, 01037.
The Republican, 6 November 1990

John Hawkes RITTER

[I068855]
NORTH PORT, FL John Hawkes Ritter of North Port, FL, 81 died peacefully Monday, May 14, 2012, at the Complex Care Hospital, Sarasota, FL, surrounded by family.
A native of Hardwick, MA, he was the youngest child of the late Sibyl (Fiske) and Ernest Ritter and is predeceased by his brother, Henry F. Ritter and his sister, Audrey ( Ritter ) Madden and his son, Ernest Ritter II.
John is survived by his wife of 58 years, Olive (Taylor); two children, Jane Comerford, and husband David of Millis MA, Garrett Ritter and wife Julie of San Jose, CA, and daughter-in law, Diane Ritter -Gardner and husband Dennis of St. Augustine, FL. He will be dearly missed by his six grandchildren, Colleen Ann Comerford of Millis, MA, Heidi Ritter of Richmond, VA, April Ritter , currently serving as an Army nurse in Afghanistan, Elaine Ritter of Amelia Island, FL, Megan Ritter of Whittier, CA, and John Ritter of Tempe, AZ.
John graduated from Northfield Mount Hermon in 1949 and from the University of Massachusetts in 1953. He was an active member of fraternal order of Theta Chi. He was the proprietor of Hardwick's Goodfield Dairy from 1955 to 1979, and then owned and operated a franchise of Cornwell Tools from 1979 to 1995. Through the years, he served various terms on the Hardwick Board of Selectman and School Committee.
Upon retirement, John and Olive headed south and became residents of La Casa retirement community in North Port, FL where he served on numerous committees and boards.
John set an example for others, with his dignity and compassion, loyalty, diligence, humility and fair judgment. He will be greatly missed by his family and the many friends accumulated over an extraordinary lifetime. He will be long remembered by those who loved him.
There are no calling hours. A graveside service and burial will be conducted at the Hardwick Cemetery at a later date. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Hardwick Historical Society to the John Hawkes Ritter Memorial, P.O. Box 492, Hardwick, MA 01037

The Larimer County coroner's office has identified a man who drowned near Wellington as 43-year-old Bruce Ritts.
Deputy Coroner Greg Fairman said that Ritts died Friday when his canoe capsized as he was trying to install a water gauge in Mattingly Reservoir. He was not wearing a life jacket.
Ritts' wife went to the reservoir to search for him after he failed to return home Friday night. Authorities said she called 911 after finding a capsized canoe in the water.
Rescue divers found him about 30 feet from the shore in 6 to 8 feet of water.
The Fort Collins Coloradoan reported that he did not know how to swim and said he was a local ranch hand.

LAST RITES FOR RALPH C. ROBBINS
Overlooking the city in which most of the 59 years of his life were spent, Ralph C. Robbins was buried Monday in Highland cemetery with a simple, impressive ceremony. Scores gathered at the services to pay their last respects to a man they had known as a cheerful, kind citizen of Gladwin. The Reverend Lerouy Whitney officiated.
Robbins' sudden death Friday afternoon following a heart attack while he was sleeping at his home was a shock to the community. Apparently in the best of health until that afternoon, he had gone home from his grocery store a little earlier than usual, complaining of a slight pain around his heart.
He died a few minutes after he had gone to sleep.
CAME HER IN '81
Born November 4, 1874 near Mt. Pleasant, he came to Gladwin in 1881 with his parents, Mr. and Mrs. Enoch Robbins, who opened a grocery store near the present site of the Henderson grocery.
After graduating from Gladwin high school Robbins married Maude Black in 1892. After teaching district school for a few years, he went to Idaho, where he was engaged in farming until 1903, when he returned to Gladwin and bought his father's grocery business.
From that date until his death Robbins scarcely missed a day at his business. Among the merchants along the street it was often said that "you can set your watch when Ralph Robbins goes past to work."
LOVER OF SPORTS
A great lover of sports, there was hardly a contest that went on that he missed. He was a great fisherman.
He was a member of the I.O.O.F. For many years he was chairman of the Board of Public Works. Last winter he was superintendent of the CWA sewer project here in Gladwin.
Surviving are his wife; two sons, Kenneth of Pinconning; and Eldon of Detroit; a sister, Mrs. Nettie Deacon; two brothers, Dr. Bernard Robbins of Milwaukee, and Gerald Robbins of Lansing; a hald-sister, Mrs. I. E. Foutch; a half-brother, Enoch Robbins, of Sherman township.
Those from out of town who came for the last rites were: Mr. and Mrs. Kenneth Robbins of Pinconning, Mr.and Mrs. Eldon Robbins of Detroit, Gerald Robbins, Lansing, Dr. Bernard Robbins and daughter, Gwendolyn, of Milwaukee, Wis., Mr. and Mrs. E. L. Good of Pinconning, Dr. and Mrs. Hayden Neely of Detroit, Mrs. Lee Crispen of Lansing, Mr. and Mrs. T. E. Smith and daughter, Barbara, of Manistee, Mr. and Mrs. Freeman Herbison of Jackson, Mr. and Mrs. Frank Ripley of Mt. Pleasant, Mr. and Mrs. A. H. Bradley of Midland.
Gladwin County Record, 19 April 1934
