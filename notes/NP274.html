Lawrence Jaros was a farmer, engineer, attorney and an Army officer.
Mr. Jaros , a lifelong resident of Bainbridge Township, owned farms in Russell and Bainbridge townships, where he grew corn, wheat and soybeans.
He was founder of Tetrad Co. in Cleveland, a valve manufacturer, and had a law practice while running the company.
Mr. Jaros , 82, of Bainbridge Township, died of complications of cancer Wednesday at Anna Maria Nursing Home in Aurora.
He was born in Orange Township and graduated from the former Bainbridge High School in 1929. He earned a degree in civil engineering from the Ohio State University in 1933.
He served in the Army during World War II and was reactivated as a reservist during the Korean War. He retired from the reserves in 1973 as a lieutenant colonel.
He earned his law degree from the former Cleveland Law School in 1939 and earned his doctorate in law from Cleveland-Marshall Law School in 1968.
He maintained a law practice in his home until his recent illness.
Mr. Jaros was also an independent surveyor.
He was an avid golfer.
Services will be private. Arrangements are being handled by Stroud- Lawrence Funeral Home in Chagrin Falls.

Louis Amedee JEAN

DRACUT, MA -- Louis Amedee Jean, 88, passed away peacefully at Lowell General Hospital Saints Campus on March 30, surrounded by a loving family that he dearly loved. He was the beloved husband of the late Helen Rita (Durand) Jean to whom he was married to for 52 years.
Louis was born in Lowell on January 20th 1925, son of the late Hector and the late Cecile (Trudel) Jean. His early life was spent in Lowell where he attended St. Joseph Grammar and St. Joseph High School. He later attended Bradshaw Business School, Lowell, MA. - And having completed the Accounting Course received his Diploma on March 17, 1955.
He was a very pious Catholic man and attended daily Mass for many years until illness prevented it. He served as a Eucharistic Minister, Altar Server and Usher at Saint Therese Church in Dracut now known as Saint Marguerite D'Youville. He was elected as President of Saint Therese Men's club for two terms.
He served in the Navy during World War II and the Korean War from July 1943 to 1946 as a radioman 3rd class and then from Feb. 1948 to Feb. of 1953. He was involved in the D-Day Invasion at Normandy, France and helped established radio communications in Cherbourg, France. Some ships he served on were the USS Bordelon DD881, USS Boston CA69, USS Canisteo A099 and the USS CHIPOLA A063. He received the following medals: World War II Victory Medal American Area, European- African Area 1 Star Asiatic Pacific Area.
In addition to his late wife he was predeceased by his son Louis Jean and their five infants, his brothers Earnest Jean and Marcel Jean, his sisters Doris Durand and Therese Lambert, his sister-in-laws Patrice Jean, Shirley Jean, his brother-in-laws Andrew Marion, David Finney and David Lambert.
