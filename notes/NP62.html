Brynhild BUDLASDATTER

[I008192]
n Norse mythology, Brynhildr (German: Brünnehilde) was a shieldmaiden and a valkyrie. According to the Volsungasaga, she is the daughter of Budli and brother of Atli (Attila the Hun). She was ordered to decide a fight in between two kings, Hjalmgunnar and Agnar. The valkyrie knew that Odin himself preferred the older king, Hjalmgunnar, yet Brynhild decided the battle for Agnar. For this Odin condemned the valkyrie to live the life of a mortal woman and cursed her to sleep until any man would rescue and marry her. Brynhild was imprisoned in a ring of fire that only the greatest hero could enter. Sigurd (Siegfried), the son of Sigmund, entered that ring after killing the dragon Fafnir and awoke Brynhild. Later Sigurd gave her his ring, Andvarinaut.

Sigurd duly betrayed her and married Gudrun when bewitched by the sorceress Grimhild to forget Brynhild. Gudrun's brother, Gunnar, then sought to court Brynhild but was stopped by the ring of fire that still surrounded her. Sigurd exchanged shapes with him and entered the ring of fire a second time. He then took the ring Andvarinaut from her finger and gave it to Gudrun. Gunnar and Brynhild married, but she realised Sigurd's betrayal when she saw Gudrun wearing the Andvarinaut and then plotted to have him killed. Her brother-in-law Guttorm killed Sigurd, Brynhild herself killed Sigurd's three-year-old son, and then she willed herself to die.

Brynhild bore Sigurd a daughter, Aslaug, who later married Ragnar Lodbrok.

Mildred D. BUECHELE

[I105906]
LACON - Mildred B. "Mid" Koch , 84, previously of 909 Harmony Lane, Lacon, and currently residing at 947 Brookside Drive, Varna, Ill., passed away at 3:10 a.m. Wednesday, April 23, 2008, after a six-month battle with cancer.
She was born in Winslow, Ind., on July 25, 1923, the daughter of the late Fredrick G. and Martha Russ Buechele.
She attended Winslow Grade and High School, Hanover College and graduated from Indiana University.
She married Jack D. Koch of Lacon in Winslow, Ind., on Aug. 8, 1948. He preceded her in death on April 12, 2006.
They had three children, Mitchell (Marty) Koch of Roanoke, Ill., Susan (Kenny deceased) Harms of Varna and Shelly (Gary) Griswold of Lake Wildwood. She was a loving grandmother to David (Rebecca) Harms of Washington, Ill., Steffin (Kathleen) Griswold of Minneapolis, Minn., Amy (Matt) Schwanke of Varna, Cory (Sheryl) Koch of Roanoke, Kristi (Darrin) Chioni of Roanoke, Julie Harms (fiance Lucas Speakman) of Jacksonville, Ill., and Kelli Harms of Varna. She was also the proud great-grandmother to Peyton, Emily, Maddie, Lohgan, Kayla, Leta, Mason, Zach and Kaylin. She hoped to live to see her first grandchild David have his first child, due in June.
She also leaves behind her brother, Victor (Joy) Buechele of Mountain Home, Ark.; and her sister, Roberta (retired Brig. General Daniel) Daley of Colorado Springs, Colo.; along with numerous nieces and nephews.
She was preceded in death by her sister, Caroline Buechele.
Her greatest accomplishment, besides her family, was her 36 years of teaching. She taught Spanish in Venice, Fla., Sparland High School and Mid-County Junior-Senior High. She also taught English, science, and math at various times in her teaching career. She was most proud of her ability to have a number of her students go on trips with her to Spain and Mexico.
Although a full-time teacher and mother, she made time to participate in various social organizations. She was a member of Phi Mu Sorority, past matron of Order of the Eastern Star, a charter member of St. John Lutheran Church, a past bond holder of the Lacon Country Club, a 12-year survivor of breast cancer, a past member of the Lacon Public Library Board, a past member of the Lacon Junior Women's Club and an avid bridge and canasta player.
She and Jack loved to travel. The two enjoyed trips to Hawaii, England, Mexico and Spain. If their health hadn't declined, they would have continued to travel.
In lieu of funeral services, she wished to be cremated and have a memorial service, which will be at 11 a.m. Saturday, April 26, 2008, at St. John Lutheran Church in Lacon. Pastor Wendy Popp will officiate at the memorial service. Burial of ashes will be private at a later date.
The Peoria Journal Star, 24 April 2008

Marjorie Maxine BUELL

[I078250]
Margie Knauff, long time Paradise resident, passed away Friday, October 26, 2007 after a lengthy illness.
Along with her late husband, Lynn, Margie moved to Paradise just after World War Two. Having worked at Marin Naval Shipyard during the war, the apple orchards and pine forests of Paradise looked like Heaven on earth and she vowed never to move again.
Margie lived for her family and friends, but after raising two sons, Ron and Don, who survive her, Margie decided to open a small family cafe, where she served great home-cooked meals which are still remembered today.
Retiring in 1974 she spent her last years surrounded by children, grandchildren, her friends and gardens. In spite of her vow, Margie did leave her earthy Paradise on October 26, 2007.
In addition to her sons, she is survived by grandchildren Sunny, Ryan and Kristin Knauff and great grandsons Daniel and Cameron. She will be missed by all who knew her. Private family services are planned at a later date.
Paradise Post, 29 Octobert 2007

Mass of Christian Burial will be 10:30 a.m., Saturday, July 1, 2017 at St. Agnes Catholic Church in Roscoe, MN for John N. Buermann, age 86, who died Wednesday surrounded by his loving family at Koronis Manor in Paynesville. Burial will be in the parish cemetery.
John was born in Richmond, MN to John and Elizabeth (Blonigen). He grew up on Big Lake near Richmond and attended school at the local country schoolhouse. His first jobs were as a mechanic and an electrician. He enjoyed tinkering and fixing and was known for being charitable and helpful with his skills. John started his own road maintenance company and serviced many townships until his retirement. He married Lorraine Catherine Hageman of Roscoe in 1958 and they were blessed with 6 children. John loved music, especially old-time music, playing concertina, and leading his "Buermann Family Band". Music was his passion, and he dedicated his life to it since first starting to play at the age of 16. After 16 years of marriage to Lorraine he became widowed and was fortunate to connect with and marry his lifelong friend Elizabeth "Betty" Wolf of St. Joseph. They played music together and traveled frequently until Bettyʼs passing. John could fix anything, loved to socialize, was very spiritual, and will be remembered for his gift of music which he shared with the world. He was a member of Knights of Columbus, St. Agnes Church Choir, and Parish Board.
Survivors include his children, LeAnn (Brad) Beck, John Jr. (Edith), William (Dorine), Alan (Lynn), Janelle (Landi) Bregu, Charlene (Jim) Provost; siblings, Roman, Herbert, Joe, Irma, Martha; 22 grandchildren and 10 great-grandchildren.
He was preceded in death by his wife, Lorraine (Hageman); wife, Elizabeth "Betty" (Wolf); infant daughter, Mary; siblings, Martin, Edmond, Norbert, Robert, Alma, Eleanor and Rosalia.
