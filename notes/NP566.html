Elmer Lee WALDEN

[I086727]
COUDERSPORT, Pa. - Elmer L. Walden, 81, of Roulette, died Sunday (Nov. 5, 1972) in Charles Cole Memorial Hospital after a long illness.
Born in Raymond, Potter County, Feb. 24,1891, he was a son of Gaylord and Amelia Byuam Walden. He married the former Leah Bump. On June 13, 1941, he married the former Bernice Haskins, who died Sept. 29,1971. Mr. Walden had been a resident of Coudersport since 1936, moving to Roulette a few months ago.
He was a retired employee of the Potter County Rest Home and attended the Coudersport Free Methodist Church.
Surviving are a son, Arnold Walden of Troy; two daughters, Mrs. Walter (Edna) Coy of Wellsville, N.Y., and Miss Reva Walden of Corning, N.Y.; 10 grandchildren and 29 great-grandchildren; three step-children, Duane Haskins of Niagara Falls, N.Y., Mrs. Jesse Gould of Williamsport and Mrs. Samuel (Orrel) Wheeler of Aspin Wall; seven step-grand-children and 10 step-great-grandchildren; and a brother, Joseph W. Walden of Wellsville.
Friends may call at the Grabe-Fickinger Funeral Home, 210 N. East St., Coudersport, after 7 p.m. today and after 2 p.m. Wednesday. Funeral services will be held there Thursday (Nov. 9,1972) at 2 p.m. with the Rev. H.D. Youngblood, minister of the Roulette Baptist Church, officiating. Burial will be in Homer Cemetery, Inez.
Orlean Times Herald, 7 November 1972

Richard Leo WALDRON

[I080422]
PORT HENRY -- Richard "Pickles" Waldron, Sr., age 67, died at his home on Oct. 13, 2007.
He was born on October 8, 1940, in Port Henry to the late Milford and Marion (Grant) Waldron.
He graduated from Port Henry High School where he was known throughout the North Country as an outstanding athlete.
He married Esther Fish on January 7, 1967, and resided in the town of Moriah until his death.
Dick was an avid hunter and fisherman who shared his love for the outdoors with his family.
In his youth he had a love for fast cars and racing.
He served for many years as a Town of Moriah ambulance squad member, Town of Moriah police officer and chief of the Port Henry Fire Department.
He retired from International Paper Co. and spent his retirement years hunting in the Adirondacks, deep sea fishing in Maine.
He was preceded in death by his parents.
Funeral services will be held at 11 a.m. on Wednesday, Oct. 17, 2007, at St. Patrick's Church, Port Henry.

My husband Daryl Walker was born and raised in New Sharon, Iowa. Several of his relatives are buried in cemeteries nearby. Three of his grandparents, one great and two great-greats, were buried without markers in the Stuart Cemetery. All the records were destroyed in a fire many years ago. My daughter Diana Mandity and son Mike Scholtus also tried their hand at finding graves. Such a good day for Daryl! Thanks Phyllis for the help.

Doralene Olson SIGOURNEY, age 85, of Sigourney, died Tuesday, January 17, 2017, at the Keokuk County Health Center in Sigourney.
Doralene was born January 2, 1932, in Grinnell, the daughter of Howard and Harriett (Mintle) Walker and received her education in What Cheer and Barnes City schools. On December 12, 1959, she married Darrell Olson in Omaha, Nebraska. The couple lived in Des Moines, where Doralene was a manager at Elko Photo Products for 15 years. They then moved to the Sigourney area in 1970, where Doralene worked at Kingsbury Homes until it closed. In 1979, they returned to Ankeny, then back to Sigourney in 1989, where she was a day care provider for several years. Doralene attended the What Cheer Christian Church and enjoyed ceramics, attending craft shows, leisurely time at the casinos, traveling, spending time with her family and her dog, Cocoa.
Survivors include her husband of 57 years, Darrell; three children: Deborah (Ronnie) Repp of Sigourney, Thomas (Kala) King of Des Moines and Brad (Vicki) Olson of What Cheer; two step-sons: James (Tracie) Olson and Jack (Patricia) Olson, both of Des Moines; 20 grandchildren; 24 great grandchildren; three sisters: Jackie Culp of What Cheer, June Leydens of Des Moines and Dorothea Walker of Delta and one brother: Daryl Walker of San Antonio, Texas. She was preceded in death by her parents; three brothers: Calvin Walker, Arlan Walker and Gary Walker and two sisters: Roberta Hartwig and Jeannine Hartwig.
Memorial visitation will be held from 12:00 p.m. until 2:00 p.m. on Saturday, January 21, 2017, at Powell Funeral Home in Sigourney. Burial will be at Garrett Cemetery near Delta at a later date.

He lived by the scripture found in Acts 20:35: "There is more happiness in giving than there is in receiving." Gary was known to all as a generous person. He would go out of his way to show hospitality to everyone. No one was a stranger to him. He also looked forward to the promise found in Revelation 21:4: "He will wipe out every tear from their eyes, and death will be no more, neither will mourning nor outcry nor pain be anymore. The former things have passed away." This promise was very real to him.
Gary's hobbies included fishing, reading Louis L'Amour and Erie Stanley Gardner novels and watching sports of all kinds [especially the Hawkeyes]. Isabelle P. Ross of Horseheads, NY passed away at the Arnot Ogden Medical Center, July 15, 2013.
Isabelle was called to Heaven by her Lord and Savior. Isabelle resided with her daughter at 305 Watkins Road, Horseheads, NY.
She was then a resident at Appleridge Assisted Living, also in Horseheads. Isabelle was born July 1, 1914 in Lindley, NY to Charles and Nettie (Pangborn) Walker and grew up in Corning, NY.
She was the youngest and only daughter of six children. She married John W. Ross December 24, 1971. He predeceased her on August 6, 2007.
Isabelle is survived by her son Merland "Chub" (Denise) Kilburn of Big Flats; her daughter, Creata Martin of Horseheads; two step-sons: James (Mary) Ross of Henrietta, NY, Thomas Ross of Florida; six grandchildren: Kelly Cardi, Elmira, Kammy (Michael) Gervais, Albany, Richard Martin, Corning, Joanne (Christopher) Giammichele, Horseheads, Lynnette (Brian) Schlicher, Lindley and Suzanne (Robert) Halm of Painted Post.
Isabelle was known as Gee-Gee to her 10 great grandchildren: Jessica and Jamie, Jacob, Caleb and Olivia, Brandon, Nicholas and Jason and Ashley and Trevor.
Isabelle was a homemaker most of her life.
She had also been employed and retired from the Imperial Club in Painted Post. She was a volunteer for Meals on Wheels for many years.
She was a great cook and served many holiday dinners to her family in the early years.
Her homemade fudge and pies were the best. Isabelle was involved with cub scouts, baseball and football with her son.
She was also a Girl Scout leader and traveled with the Postettes and Baronettes drill teams with her daughter. A traveling MOM for sure.
Isabelle and John, aka "Izzy and Jack", spent winters in Florida and summers at Keuka Lake. They had thirty five years of fun in the sun. They were best friends.
Izzy enjoyed all the family gatherings through her many years; birthday's, wedding's, holiday's or just because.
Her "Keuka Lake Crew" were very important to her and she loved sharing fish fries with them. Family, friends and laughter were her life.
She had wonderful times with special friends and family; Joe and Carolyn Nares, Pat Pierson, the Ervay family, her nephew Stan (Pat) Walker and special sister-in-laws, Beaty Burnap, Mil Schneckenburger and Julia Moore.
Isabelle liked watching golf as long as Tiger Woods was playing.
She played cards, put puzzles together and of course, loved chocolate candy.
Izzy and her great grandson, Jacob, always picked on each other about who would get the last piece of chocolate candy and Gee-Gee always won.
She enjoyed watching Jeopardy, Wheel of Fortune, Law and Order, Ben Matlock and her favorite movie was Pretty Woman.
Isabelle enjoyed Gospel music by the Jacob Brothers and the Gaither Brothers. One of her favorite songs was, "On the Wings of a
Snow White Dove."
Friends and family are invited to call at Phillips Funeral Home and Cremation Service, 17 W. Pulteney St., Corning, NY on Monday, July 22, 2013 from 3:00 to 4:00pm. A service will immediately follow at 4:00pm, Rev.
Larry Taylor officiating as Isabelle requested. He is married to Isabelle's niece, Gail. Burial will take place in the family plot at Hope Cemetery, Corning, NY.
Donations in Isabelle's name can be made to Kings Kid Camp, P.O. Box 68, Dillsburg, PA 17019 or to the North Syracuse Baptist Church, 420 South Main Street, North Syracuse, NY 13212, c/o Rev. Larry Taylor.
