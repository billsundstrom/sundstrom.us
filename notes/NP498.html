Beverly Clarice SIMCOX

[I074507]
LAURIUM - Rev. Beverly S. Eaton, 84, a resident of Laurium, passed away at her home with her family by her side on Tuesday night, March 27, 2007.
She was born to Ward and Hilm Simcox in Marinette, Wis. on May 2, 1922. The family moved to Neenah in 1930. She attended the Neenah Schools where she became the head majorette for the well-known Neenah High School Marching Band. She led them to three gold awards in three years before graduating in 1940. She then led the Kimberly Clark Band for two years.
She worked as an assistant to the oral surgeon, Dr. R.P. Jorgenson for three years in Neenah. She moved to the Copper Country in 1947 when she married Lorimer D. Eaton of Laurium and resided here since that time.
As a National Baton Association instructor, she trained majorettes for the Calumet High School for 30 years. From 1965 to 1976, she produced annual variety shows with the young twirling groups at the Calumet Theater for the benefit of local fundraisers.
She had served on the boards of the Easter Seal Society, Bay Cliff Camp and the American Cancer Society. She organized the Reach to Recovery Program for breast cancer patients in the Houghton/Keweenaw area in 1975 and was the coordinator for the program for 25 years. In 1976, she became the trainer for the Reach to Recovery volunteers for the Upper Peninsula. In 1987, she was the recipient of the Michigan Distinguished Volunteer Award.
Beverly worked for the Calumet Hospital as a Physical Therapist and as Patient Educator for breast cancer for six years.
She was a member of the CLK Council of Churches, the American Legion Auxiliary and the Skeaters Cross Country Ski Group. Beverly was a member of the Christ Episcopal Church in Calumet, a licensed Lay Reader, Chalice Bearer and Eucharistic Minister since 1990. On February 26, 2006, she was ordained to the Priesthood by Bishop James Kelsey of the Episcopal Diocese of Northern Michigan.
She spent four to five months of the year at her "favorite place on earth", Bete Gris in the Keweenaw.
She was preceded in death by her parents; her husband, Lorimer "Larry" on August 5, 2001; and two sisters.
Surviving are three children, Laura Mary Eaton of Laurium, Lorimer David (Linda) Eaton of Port Ewen, New York and Elizabeth Ann (Chris) Berg of Waterford, Mich.; nine grandchildren, Dawn (Craig) Stelzer, Lance (Tsaytah) Peterlin, Heather (James) Rilei, Jess Peterlin, Sarah Manning, Emily (Bobby) Rech, Lorimer Eaton III, Grace Eaton and Katie Berg; eight great-grandchildren, Seth Stelzer, Madison Peterlin, John Peterlin, Austin Rilei, Jaden Rilei, Jackson Rilei, Carson Rech and Alex Rech.
Funeral services will be held on Monday, April 2, 2007 at 11:00 a.m. in the Christ Episcopal Church in Calumet with Bishop James Kelsey to officiate.
Friends may call at the Erickson Crowley Funeral Home in Calumet on Sunday from 4 to 8 p.m. or leave condolences for the family at EricksonCrowley.com.
Burial will be in the Lake View Cemetery in Calumet.
In lieu of other expressions of sympathy, the family suggests donations to one of the following:
Christ Episcopal Church, 56771 Rockland St., Calumet, MI 49913; St. Peter by the Sea Church, c/o Sue King, M-26, Eagle Harbor, MI 49950; Bay Cliff Health Camp, P.O. Box 310, Big Bay, MI 49808.

Dottie married William F. Stees on June 24, 1956 at St. John's Lutheran Church in Cornell. She was employed by the Soo Line Railroad in Eau Claire, and the Santa Fe Railroad in Albuquerque, N.M. She relocated to Wisconsin where she was employed at the Northern Wisconsin Center for the Developmentally Disabled. Dottie worked as a benefit specialist until her retirement in 1998. During this time, she received the Social Security Performance Award, which was the first to be awarded in Wisconsin in 10 years.
Dottie loved spelling, crossword puzzles, and Scrabble to "keep her mind sharp." Also, she found pleasure in the meaning of words, song birds, and singing ("Blue Eyes Crying in the Rain"). Let's not forget her "zest in life" for flannel shirts, cherubs, the color red, and "bling". Dottie's greatest pleasure was found in time spent with children/grandchildren, siblings, and friends.

Ronald Vernon SIMMONS

[I019668]
Funeral Services for Ronald V. Simmons will be held on Wednesday, January 26th, at the Roseau Free Lutheran Church at 11:00 a.m. Visitation will be one hour prior to the service at church.
Ronald Simmons was born on August 18, 1926 in Grafton, ND to Bessie (Monson) and Ray Simmons. He lived in Grafton for a short time until moving to the Randeen Ridge near Gatzke. It was here that Ron attended school.
Ron married Viola Knutson on November 8, 1947. They had eight children, Wayne, Karen, Karyl, Kay, Wesley, Wally, Warren, and Willard.
Ron liked growing up in the country and loved to hunt deer. He hunted right up until the end of his life. He was a practical jokester and enjoyed playing tricks and games with his grandchildren. He even played tricks on the nurses at the hospital and care center. He tinkered a lot, made crafts, toy boxes for the kids, and wishing wells. He was also a good welder. He used his welding skills on the farm to make hay stackers, trailers and wagons.
Ron farmed for many years, drove school bus for 25 years, and worked at Marvinʼs for a while. He was treasurer of the United Lutheran Church in Gatzke, and served on the Moose River Township Board. He was also the President of the eastern Marshall Sportsman's Club. When he was a child his family attended the church on the Randeen Ridge. Ron was the first to be baptized there. Ironically, his youngest child, Willard, was the last baby to be baptized there before its closing.
Ron Simmons passed away on Saturday, January 22, 2011 at the Thief River Care Center. He was 84 years old.
He is survived by seven children, Karen (Merle) Lorenson of Strathcona, Karyl (Ardell) Nelson of Wannaska, Kay (Dwight) Lane of Barnesville, MN, Wesley (Colleen) of Noble, OK, Wally of Warroad, Warren (friend Michelle) of Buffalo, MN, and Willard (Mary) of Zimmerman, MN; 23 grandchildren; 43 great-grandchildren; sisters Gloria Swanson of Warroad, and Betty Larson of Stillwater; brother, Roger Simmons of Bloomington, MN; sister-in-law, Joan Simmons of Roseau, MN; daughter-in-law, Linda Simmons of Middle River, MN, and several nieces, nephews, and friends.
He is preceded in death by his wife, Viola in 2008, son, Wayne, his parents, grandson, Paul Lorenson, siblings: Bob (Doris), Russell (Mildred), and Richard; sister-in-law, Edla; brothers-in-law, Bob Larson and Leroy "Bud" Swanson.

Jehiel H. SIMONDS

Captain J. H. Simonds died in Greenfield, Mass., last Friday, 18th, and his remains were brought to Windsor on Tuesday for interment in the new cemetery. He was the well known proprietor of the Windsor House in this place, of which he had the management for nearly forty years, previous to 1882, when Captain R. J. Coffey became landlord. Before engaging in the hotel business he was one of the most famous stage drivers in this section of the Connecticut valley. For the past few years he had been in feeble health, and his death must be attributed to the general breaking down of the system attendant upon old age.
