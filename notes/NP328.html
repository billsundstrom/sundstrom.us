James Lewis LIPSETT

[I089788]
J.L. LIPSETT PASSES AWAY AT HOME HERE
Pioneer Business Man Succumbs to Illness of Long Standing
WAS RESIDENT 63 YEARS
An illness with infirmilities of old age from which he had suffered for several months proved fatal this afternoon, July 11, 1938, at 12:40 o'clock, to J.L. Lipsett, pioneer resident and business man of this city. Mr. Lipsett was 84 years of age. Death came to him at the Lipsett residence, 315 East Spruce street.
James L. Lipsett, son of Francis and Sarah Lipsett, was born August 25, 1854, in Gray county Ontario. He lived on the homestead farm of his parents until he was 18 years of age when he moved to Annon, Ontario, where he served three years apprenticeship to become a blacksmith. He came to Sault Ste. Marie in September, 1875, and started a smithy; a trade which he followed for ten years. His business expanded with the town and from 1880 until 1887, Mr. Lipsett operated a livery and stage business. For two years his stage carried the mail from St. Ignace to the Sault. During this time he held a contract to fill the grade of twelve miles of the Duluth/South Shore and Atlantic railway.
Mr. Lipsett stared one of the first farm machinery and implement businesses in the Sault which was the start of the present Lipsett companies in Chippewa county. For a time he was in the construction business as a member of the firm of Lipsett and Gregg, and at that time (the late 1880's) built the Ferguson block and five brick buildings at Fort Brady.
Mr. Lipsett, former mayor of Sault Ste. Marie and thirty-three years president of the Central Savings Bank, had been confined to his bed since May 25. His illness had been years long, however, following a paralytic stroke some eight years ago.
He married twice and is survived by his wife, Mrs. Minnie Lipsett to whom he had been married almost 55 years. Funeral arrangments this afternoon still were indefinite, but the funeral will be held at the home at 315 E. Spruce Street, probably Thursday afternoon.
He is survived by: His wife, Mrs. J.L. Lipsett. Four children, Verne Lipsett; Mrs. Clarence Alphord of Flint; Mrs. Miriam L. Irvin of the Sault; and James L. Lipsett, Jr. of the Sault. Three sisters--Mrs. Rebecca Watson, Portland, Oregon; Mrs. J.A. Douglas, Miami, and Mrs. Sara Agnew, Detroit. Eleven grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren. Due to illness, he was an inactive member of the Knights and Pythias, Masonic Lodge and Rotarians.
The Evening News, 11 July 11, 1938

Alverda Eileen LITTLE

[I099768]
ALVERDA EILEEN REYNOLDS, age 94, entered into the presence of her Lord and Savior on Tuesday, March 17, 2015. The eldest of four children, she was born October 26, 1920, to Charles Edgar and Evarena Little in Dowell, Illinois. Growing up on her parents farm, she attended Dowell Grade School, graduated from Elkville High School, and studied for two years at Southern Illinois University before earning a teaching certificate during World War II.
In 1941 Eileen met her future husband Joe Reynolds, and they courted briefly before he was stationed in Guam with the U.S. Army. Immediately upon his return from the war, they were married on April 19, 1945. Eileen and Joe welcomed daughter Lara in 1946 and daughter Jo Ann (Joni) in 1949, and eventually settled in Oglesby, Illinois to raise their family. After staying home briefly to raise her daughters, Eileen returned to teaching at Washington and Lincoln Elementary schools in Oglesby, and in 1966 earned her Bachelor's Degree from Illinois State University. She later served as Head Principal at Lincoln Elementary School.
A lifelong follower of Christ, Eileen had a strong passion for studying and teaching the Bible and encouraging others in their faith. As a teenager, she regularly attended Sunday morning, Sunday evening and Wednesday evening church services, leading a Women's Bible study and Bible schools during the summers.
Eileen is survived by her daughter, Joni (Ron) Karlich; granddaughters, Rachel Fruin, Nickie Fruin, and Cat Sorrels; grandson, Erik Fruin; great-granddaughter, Nadia Fruin; siblings, Carroll (Doris) Little and Dee (Don) Burroughs; and many nieces, grandnieces, and grandnephews. Eileen was preceded in death by her husband of 42 years, Joe Reynolds; daughter, Lara Akey; parents, Charles and Evarena Little; and brother, Larry Little.

Raymond "Ray" Little, 93, passed away on Sunday, December 17, 2017 at Park Place Nursing Home.
Ray was born January 22, 1924 in Northfield, MN to Clarence and Ethel (Hatfield) Little. He attended Little Prairie a one room school in Dundas, MN for his elementary years. In 1942 he graduated from Fairbault High School, Fairbault MN.
Ray joined the army after graduation and served during WWII in Japan and Korea. He helped maintain the power generators of the Press Train in the DMZ.
He attended Dunwoody Trade School Minneapolis, MN before joining a fledgling agricultural manufacturing company known as New Holland in 1948. His first territory was traveling North and South Dakota calling on dealers until he moved in 1953 to the Branch Office in Minneapolis, MN. He worked his way up to Branch Service Manager when in 1969 he moved his family to Great Falls MT to become the Western Montana Service Representative for New Holland.
After 33 year of service with New Holland he retired in 1981. Following retirement he was Mr. "Fix It" at the Grandview Plaza Apartments for six years. In his spare time he liked to putts in his garden, work on family genealogies, photography and snow carvings.
On December 12, 1951 he married Victoria Bateman in Long Prairie, MN and they shared the ups and downs of life for the next 54 years until she passed away in May of 2005. They had five children .
Ray leaves behind three sons; two daughters; 8 grandchildren; and 12 great-grandchildren.
He was preceded in death by his dad, Clarence R. Little, his mother Ethel Hatfield Little Fjeld, step-father Sverre (Steve) Fjeld, and brother Robert Fjeld.
