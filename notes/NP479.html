Wallace Amos SCHRONTZ

[I101047]
PINEHURST - Lt. Col. Wallace A. Schrontz , U.S. Air Force retired, 90, of Pinehurst, died Sunday, July 27, 2014.
A memorial service will be held Sunday, Aug. 10, 2014, at 2 p.m. in Boles Funeral Home Chapel in Southern Pines with the Rev. Archie Stevens officiating.
Mr. Schrontz was born in Bangor, Mich., on Nov. 15, 1923, and attended high school in South Haven, Mich. graduating in 1941. He was employed at Ford Motor Company prior to enlisting in the U.S. Army Air Force as an aviation cadet in in 1943. After serving his country as a bomber pilot during World War II, "Wally" was honorably discharged in December 1945 and worked at a tool and die company where he lost a thumb and finger in a die press accident. Mr. Schrontz rejoined the U.S. Army Air Force in 1947 and served for a total of 28 years before retiring as a lieutenant colonel in 1971. His duties included contracting officer for the 1500 unit base housing project at Seymour Johnson Air Force base in North Carolina and administrator for the World Wide Civil Air Carrier program.
Lt. Col. Schrontz began his second career at Manatee Junior College in Bradenton, Fla. where he retired again in 1989 as assistant to the vice president having received a master's degree in education in 1975 from the University of Southern California.
Lt. Col. Schrontz relocated to Pinehurst, in 1989, with his wife and created Schrontz Development Corporation and along with his partners built 25 residential homes in the area.
Lt. Col. Schrontz was a member of the Masons, American Legion, Kiwanis, Daedalians and served on the board at Pinehurst Trace.
Lt. Col. Schrontz is survived by his wife of 70 years, Patricia Leslie Schrontz ; a son, William and fiancee Linda of Pinehurst; a daughter, Sheryle Dales of Southern Pines; a grandson, Richard Dales Jr. and wife Racheal of Holly Springs; a great-granddaughter, Harper Dales; and a brother, Lester Schrontz and wife Mary of Pinehurst.
In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Wounded Warrior Project, PO Box 758517, Topeka, KS 66675.
Boles Funeral Home of Southern Pines is assisting the family.

Born in Allentown, she was the daughter of the late Mahlon P. and Cecilia A. (Deysher) Schuler. She was a 1954 graduate of Emmaus High School and served on the reunion committee. She was employed by Day Timers for 25 years, retiring in 1996. She was a member of Jerusalem Western Salisbury Church and was a member of the Sears Retiree Organization.
Survivors: Husband, Donald Bitting ; son, Dwight D. Bitting and wife Cheryl of Coplay; grandchildren, Blake, Justin, and Kaela; and great-granddaughter, Jocelyn Gehris. She was predeceased by a daughter, Carol L. Bitting ; and sisters, Eleanor Bortz, Mabel Krause, Margaret Stimmel, Florence Kiess, and Lorraine Fegely.
Services: Funeral Service Monday, December 23, 2013 at 2 p.m. in Jerusalem Western Salisbury Church, 3441 Devonshire Road, Allentown, PA 18103. Viewing 1 to 2 p.m. in the church.
Interment will follow in Western Salisbury Cemetery. Schantz Funeral Home, P.C., Emmaus is handling arrangements.
Contributions: In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to Jerusalem Western Salisbury Church Memorial Fund.
The Morning Call, Allentown, 20 December 2013
