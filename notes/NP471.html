George Elon SANDERSON

[I014718]
George M. Sanderson, 75, who has resided at the home of W. L. Dickinson since retirement from active work a year ago, died yesterday at 4 p.m. there.
Mr. Sanderson, a native of this town, was born on the Sanderson place, Indian Hill, Sept 27, 1864, the son of Elon and Abbie (Rice) Sanderson. He had always lived here. For many, years he conducted a dairy and tobacco farm, and Whatley Glen, which was well patronized by residents of the valley until his retirement on leasing his farm.
Aside from deafness Mr. Sanderson had been in good health until last fail. In March he underwent an operation for amputation of the left leg in the Franklin county hospital. Since his return here he had been confined to the bed and had failed steadily.
Surviving are a daughter, Mrs. Phillip Kierstead of Hulls Cove, ME; a son. Nelson H, Sanderson of Greenfield; a sister, Miss Laura Sanderson of Indianapolis, Ind.; a nephew, William E. Ward of South Deerfield; and a granddaughter, Elizabeth Sanderson of Sunderland.
Mrs. Sanderson, the former Clara Dickinson, whom he married in April 1896, died suddenly six years ago.
Funeral services will be in the local Congregational church, Wednesday at 2 p. m., with Rev. Byron F. Gustin officiating. Burial will be in Center cemetery. Mt. Sugar loaf Masons of South Deerfield. of which he was a member, will conduct the committal service.
Greenfield Recorder-Gazette, 13 May 1940

Howard R. SANDERSON

[I070632]
WILLIAMSBURG - Howard R. Sanderson, 91, a lifelong resident of Williamsburg, passed away Aug. 20, 2012, at home after a short illness.
Born March 20, 1921, in Williamsburg, he was the son of the late Frederick and Hazel (Warner) Sanderson.
Howard enlisted in the U.S. Army in 1942 and served at the German prisoner of war camp at Fort Smith's Camp Chaffee in Arkansas. After receiving an honorable discharge in 1946, he returned home, married Rhea M. Bridgman of Florence and resumed his job at the Hampshire County Sanitorium in Leeds (The Overlook at Northampton), where he worked until he retired in 1981.
After his Army service he attained a steam fireman's license from Peterson's School of Steam Engineering in Boston.
He served the town of Williamsburg in many positions. He joined the Fire Department in 1940 and served as chief from 1954 until his retirement in 1978. He was a member of the Western Massachusetts Fire Chiefs, Massachusetts State Fire Chiefs and New England Fire Chiefs associations. Twice he was appointed the Water Department, serving from 1948 to 1955 and again from 1988 until his death. He also served as a special police officer in both Williamsburg and Northampton. President Nixon sought out his advice regarding National Fire Training. Howard served Williamsburg in several other capacities as well.
Howard is survived by Rhea, his spouse of 65 years; two children, Paul Sanderson and his wife Carol and Mary Bisbee and her husband Roger, all of Williamsburg. He is also survived by four grandchildren, Sean Sanderson, Amy Bisbee, Angelia Bisbee and Lauren LeBlanc.
Due to allergies, donations in lieu of flowers may be made to Highland Ambulance, P.O. Box 842, 56 Main St., Goshen, or to VNA & Hospice of Cooley Dickinson, P.O. Box 329, Northampton.
Burial will be private, with a memorial service at the Williamsburg Congregational Church on Thursday, Aug. 30, at 11 a.m.
Howard will be remembered not only for love of his family and dedication to the community, but also for his colorful high-top sneakers.
Daily Hampshire Gazette, 25 August 2012

Marjorie Mae SANDERSON

[I070580]
NORTHAMPTON - Former longtime Amherst resident Marjorie Sanderson Babb, 86, mother, grandmother, business woman, friend, and community volunteer, passed away Wednesday, August 23, 2017, at the Rockridge Retirement Community.
Marj was born February 23, 1931 at the family farm house in Whately, the daughter of the late William E. and Madeline (Gifford) Sanderson. She attended local schools in Whately, and was a graduate of Deerfield High School, Class of 1948. She later graduated from Northampton Commercial College.
Marj began her business career as the bookkeeper at Frampton Buick in Amherst. She owned and operated Pray Street Laundry, Amherst Sunshine Ice, Amherst Sunshine Center Laundromat, and Amherst Sunshine Car Wash for over 40 years.
She was a member of the Whately Grange for 72 years, the National Grange, and the Whately Historical Society. Marj was an active member of the Amherst Senior Center, a member of Amherst Police SALT, a precinct warden for Amherst elections, and a generous supporter of every charitable request she received.
Marj always had a smile for everyone and will be dearly missed by those who love her. She will be remembered for the countless acts of kindness she gave to those most in need. She was adored by her family and is survived by her two sons Raymond, his wife Melanie and their daughters Marisa and Mackenzie and Richard, his wife Connie with their children Richard and his wife Kristin, Andrew, Ashley, and James. The family is very grateful for the loving care given to Marj by Dr. Andrew Hall, Rockridge staff, Comprehensive Home Care, and Cooley Dickenson VNA Care.
A Memorial Service will be held at the Whately Congregational Church at 10:30 a.m., on Wednesday, August 30 followed by a committal service in Whately Center Cemetery. Calling hours at the Douglass Funeral Service in Amherst will be Tuesday, Aug. 29, from 4 to 7 PM.
The Recorder, 28 August 2017

MYRA E. KIERSTEAD , 86, of South Atlantic Avenue died yesterday at St. Elizabeth Home, Providence. She was the wife of the late Rev. Philip P. Kierstead , founding rector of the Church of the Resurrection.
Born in Springfield, Mass., a daughter of the late George and Clara (Dickenson) Sanderson, she moved to Warwick in 1965.
Mrs. Kierstead was a 1931 graduate of Gordon College. She was an alumna of the Boston Conservatory of Music. Before her marriage, she was an elementary school teacher in western Massachusetts, and she taught English in the Chinatown section of Boston. She was a communicant of the Church of the Resurrection. She was a member of Episcopal Church Women, Hamilton House, and the Cranston Leisure Learning Center.
She leaves a daughter, Anneke M. Kierstead of Somerville, Mass. She was sister of the late Nelson Sanderson and Edith Beers-Crumley.
A memorial Requiem Eucharist will be concelebrated Sunday at 3 p.m. at the Church of the Resurrection, 336 Norwood Ave. Burial will be in the Evergreen Cemetery, Caribou, Maine.

Neal Bruce SANDERSON

[I070579]
WHATELY - Neal B. Sanderson, 88, a lifelong Whately resident, passed away on Thursday, February 11, 2016 at Highview of Northampton Nursing Home.
He was born in Whately, MA on April 10, 1927, son of the late William and Madeline (Gifford) Sanderson.
Neal attended Whately Elementary School and graduated from Deerfield High School in 1944. He was a dairy farmer for many years on the family farm known as "Fairview Dairy" and was known as a milk man with a personal touch. After the dairy farm was discontinued, Neal worked at Consolidated Cigar Corporation as a foreman for many years. Neal left the tobacco business for a career in banking as a teller at Northampton Institute for Savings, and worked his way up to Branch Manager. He was a member of the First Congregational Church of Whately, Mt. Sugarloaf Masons, Whately Lions Club, the Whately Grange, an Elector under the Will of Oliver Smith, and served on the town's Financial Committee. Neal served as Board of Selectman for almost a decade where he is best remembered for his tenacity and diligence in seeing that Whately secured an additional ramp off of Interstate 91 at the Deerfield-Whately line. He was also Registrar of Voters for the town of Whately for 31 years. Neal was an avid bowler, had a love for horse racing, and was a legendary card player.
Neal was predeceased by his first wife, Rosemarie (Duseau) Sanderson; his second wife, Julie A. (Nehring) Sanderson; and his brother, Alan Sanderson. He is survived by six children, David Sanderson and his wife Fran of Naples, FL, Keith Sanderson and his wife Mary of Hinsdale, NH, Sydney MacKay of Northampton, Robert Nehring and his wife Susan of Hopedale, MA, Daniel Nehring and his wife Ginny of Jacksonville, FL, and David Nehring and his wife Sue Fortgang of Northampton; a sister, Marjorie Babb of Amherst; 16 grandchildren; 3 great-grandchildren; and several nephews.
Neal's family would like to extend a heartfelt thank you to the nurses and CNA's at Highview of Northampton for the love and compassionate care given to Neal during his stay.
A funeral service will be held on Wednesday, February 17th at 11 a.m. at the First Congregational Church of Whately, 177 Chestnut Plain Rd. Whately.
Burial will be private in the spring. Neal's family will receive relatives and friends at the visiting hours on Tuesday, February 16th from 4-7 p.m. at the Ahearn Funeral Home, 783 Bridge Rd. Northampton.
In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made in Neal's memory to the Whately Historical Society, P.O. Box 7, Whately, MA 01093 or the First Congregational Church of Whately, P.O. Box 247, Whately, MA 01093-0247 or Holy Family Church, 29 Sugarloaf St. South Deerfield, MA 01373.
The Recorder, 15 February 2016

Kenneth Alex SANDSTROM

[I112861]
Kenneth Alex Sandstrom of Beaver Dam passed away on June 2, 2011, at UW Hospitals and Clinics surrounded by his loving family.
Born in Chicago in April 1933 to Axel and Hannah Sandstrom, Ken was an avid experimenter in various industrial arts from an early age. He excelled in blacksmithing, foundry work, machining and technical illustration. He spent time in the late 1950s in Redstone Arsenal and White Sands Proving Grounds working on guided missile technology for the U.S. Army.
He was a founder of Funk Sandstrom Incorporated of Illinois where he applied his talents as an experimental machinist and practical designer in the successful fabrication of a series of sophisticated model petroleum plants. While living in Garden Grove, Calif., and Minocqua, Wis., he developed and innovative and widely used design for driveshaft balancing machines.
He loved restoring antique crs and building wooden boats. Ken left behind a legacy of finely crafted homes and woodwork. He also was a talented clarinet players.
Ken is survived by his loving wife Cynthia, his sister Mary, his children Ann and Perry and their mother Maureen. He will be fondly remembered by a number of lifelong friends.
