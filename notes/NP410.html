NORTHAMPTON - Edith Brooks Warner , 84, of North Farms Road, Florence, died March 29 at her home.
Born June 21, 1921, she was the daughter of the late Paul W. and Ethel F. (Brooks) Packard.
She grew up in Goshen and graduated from the former Williamsburg High School, where she was class president. She attended the former Northampton Commercial College and worked briefly at Massachusetts Mutual Life Insurance Company in Springfield, and was a secretary at Look Park, before working for many years as a receptionist for Dr. Falk and Dr. Lane in Northampton.
She had been a member of the Florence Congregational Church for over 50 years and had been in the church's Mother's Club and the Friendship Circle and served on many boards.
She also was a member of the Over 55 Club at church and had been a Cub Scout den mother for many years. She enjoyed gardening, skiing, bowling, line dancing and golfing. She also liked to travel.
She leaves four sons, Richard Stowe, of Easthampton, Brian Stowe, of Colombia, South America, Gary Warner , of Florence, and David Warner , of California; a daughter, Virginia Osley, of Hatfield; 10 grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.
She was predeceased by two sisters, Pauline Atherton and Florence Eldred.
The funeral will be Monday at 10 a.m. at the Florence Congregational Church, with burial in Center Cemetery, Goshen.
Calling hours are Sunday from 3 to 7 p.m. at the Pease and Gay Funeral Home, 425 Prospect St., Northampton.
In lieu of flowers memorial contributions may be made to the Florence Congregational Church, 130 Pine St., Florence, 01062 or to the VNA and Hospice of Cooley Dickinson Hospital, P.O. Box 329, Northampton, 01061-0329.
Daily Hampshire Gazette, 31 March 2006

Alfred V. PADILLA

[I054352]
MAY 1935 to AUG 2010
Al Padilla , 75, of Turlock, passed away at Alexander Cohen Hospice House on Friday after a short battle with cancer. He was born in Hanford to Nazario and Soledad Padilla . He spent most of his growing up years in Mexico City before moving to Chicago. He served in the US Navy from 1955 through 1963. After his discharge from the Navy he started working with the Owens Corning Fiberglass Company where he worked as a Quality Control Supervisor. He was married to Carol (Anderson) Padilla . They lived in San Jose and moved to Tumwater in Washington when he retired in 1994 after 35 years with Owens Corning Fiberglass. His wife Carol preceded him in death in June of 2000. The next year he moved to Turlock.
Al was a fun-loving guy who loved camping, fishing, and dancing. He especially loved sun-bathing. When the family got together they enjoyed playing poker and he was known to enjoy playing practical jokes on people.
Alfred is survived by four daughters: Jorja Smith of San Jose, CA, Donna (Tracy) Ariza of Hilmar, Cheri Polugar of Turlock, and Maria (James) Weaver of Turlock; a number of brothers and sisters; and 12 grandchildren, and siblings: Eva Ibarraran, Rosemary Reed, and Roberto Padilla . He was preceded in death by his parents and siblings: Martha Padilla , Hector Padilla , and Maria Esperanza Padilla .
Visitation for Alfred will be at Allen Mortuary on Thursday, August 12th from 10 to 12am. A Rosary and Mass of Christian Burial are scheduled for Thursday starting at 2pm at All Saints Catholic Church, 4040 McKenna Drive, Turlock, CA 95382. Memorial contributions may be considered for Alexander Cohen Hospice House c/o Community Hospice, 4368 Spyres Way, Modesto, CA 95356.
