Joseph F. Gummel, 80, passed away June 13, 2010 at Taylor Hospice, Ridley Park, Pa. Born in Mt. Carmel, Pa, he has been a long time resident of Wallingford, Pa. Robert Edward GURITZ

[I018145]
Robert Guritz, 91, a loving husband, father, grandfather and great-grandfather, passed away peacefully at home April 1, 2017. Bob will be greatly missed. Bob is survived by his wife of 53 years, Dorothy Guritz; his sister, Rita Majer (Ken), of Cosby, Tennessee; daughter, Nancy Villella, of Phoenix, Arizona; son, Robert Guritz, of Midlothian, Illinois; and son, Richard Guritz (Susan), of Fairbanks. He also is survived by four grandchildren in Fairbanks: Rodney Guritz (Sky), Jessica Guritz, Brian Guritz and Tyler South; as well as three great-granddaughters: Ayla Ongtooguk Guritz, Yana Ongtooguk Guritz and Sienna Fagan.
Bob was born Jan. 7, 1926, in Bellwood, Illinois, to Edward Louis Guritz and Angela Ann Orth. He joined the Navy in 1944, helped commission the AKA67 USS STARR which operated in the Pacific during World War II. After the war, he served in the Navy Reserve for eight years. He worked in Glenora Farms Dairy for 10 years and owned the Little Esquire Ice Cream Parlor in Evanston, Illinois. He married Dorothy Setterberg in 1964, and adopted her three children, Nancy, Robert and Richard.
Bob enjoyed scuba diving, photography, skydiving and many other outdoor activities.The family moved to Las Vegas in 1968 and built a home in the desert. Bob and Dot became ham radio operators, WA7OWW/KL7IAA and NL7KM. They enjoyed running phone patches for researchers at McMurdo Station, Antarctica. Bob loved to travel and make radio contacts with county hunters.
Bob's interest in goldmining brought the family to Alaska in 1965, 1968 and 1970. During these trips, Bob enjoyed photography, taking many pictures of active goldmining operations, including hard rock, placer, and floatation dredges in operation. They continued to enjoy traveling to Alaska for the summers, and they spent their first full winter at Cleary Hill Mines in a log cabin with no water or electricity during the winter of 1977.
Soon after their son Rick married in 1981, they decided to return to Fairbanks to stay. They built a small frame home in the Rosie Creek area, in close proximity to family. About three years ago, they moved in with Rick and Sue because of declining health. It has been a blessing for those of us who got the chance to spend more time with him near the end of his life.
Daily News-Miner, 9 April 2017

John Reginald GURTON

[I075647]
John "Jack" Gurton was born June 18, 1916 in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. The youngest of three children, Jack and his family moved to the United States when he was 12 years old where they resided in St. Paul, Minnesota. They later moved back to Canada and he graduated from High School in Canada. After High School, Jack again returned to St. Paul to attend college and he graduated from the University of Minnesota with a degree in Agriculture. Jack served in the United States Navy in World War II as a Corpsman. Jack married Janet Wells, from St. Paul, Minnesota in November, 1939 and they moved to Eugene, OR in 1949 after residing in several different states. Jack was the Four-H Extension Agent in Eugene from 1949-1968. He also served the post of Extension Agent in Newport and Redmond, OR before retiring. Jack passed away peacefully on December 29, 2012 at the age of 96 in Hemet, CA. Jack is survived by his wife of 73 years, Janet Gurton of Eugene, OR; and he is survived by their five children: John E. Gurton of Reno, NV; Tim Gurton of Eugene, OR; Marjory Streeter of Hemet, CA; Lisa Abernathy of Central Point, OR; and MaryAnn Gardener of Idaho. Jack had 13 grandchildren, 17 great-grandchildren and 2 great-great-grandchildren. The family may plan a memorial at a later date.
The Register-Guard, Eugene, OR, 4 January 2013

John Alfred GUSTAFSON

[I053750]
John A. Gustafson was born on September 25, 1926 in Gillette, Wyoming, the son of Alfred and Rose (Williams) Gustafson. He attended country school west of Gillette and Campbell County High School. After his schooling he worked for a seismograph crew locally and in Oklahoma. In 1950 he was drafted into the Army, and honorably discharged on September 19, 1952, and on September 20, 1952 married Ruth Ingle in Indianapolis, Indiana. After the Army he went to work for Western Electric in Indianapolis, Indiana to be a tool and die maker. In 1959 he went to work for IBM in New York and New Jersey. After retiring in 1989 from IBM John and Ruth moved to Minnesota and later returning to Gillette, WY where he worked for Wackenhut and Pinkerton Security providing security for local mines.
John loved being outside hunting, fishing, and taking drives in the country with Ruth. He also loved to spend time with family, for whom will miss him dearly.
He is survived by his wife of fifty-one years, Ruth; one son, Drew and his wife, Mardy Jo of Burlington, CO; one sister, Joan Shannon of Upton, WY; two grandsons: Brandon of Burlington, CO, and John Austin of Ft. Collins, CO; one granddaughter, Morgan Robinson of Ft. Collins, CO; one great-granddaughter, Annie Robinson of Ft. Collins, CO, and many nieces and nephews.
John was preceded in death by his parents; two brothers: Herb and Hank; one sister, Carol Dickinson; one grandson, Jason Gustafson.

Drew pursued his dream of being a disc jockey and hosted several morning radio shows over the years.

He was also very actively involved in the Burlington and Cheyenne Wells Lodge of Masons #77.
He had a love of motorcycle riding. In the past he was a member of Kiwana's.
He loved spending time with friends, family and especially his grandchildren. In the past he worked in the computer industry and loved working with new technology.
Drew spent his last days in the community of Burlington. He said it was his only real home, and he always appreciated the love of friends and neighbors that flowed so freely.
Drew is survived by his loving wife and best friend Mardy Jo; his brothers Neil Thomas (Joy), Victor Jr. (Brenda), and Joel (Michelle).
