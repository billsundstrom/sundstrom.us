Actor and ice skater Bill Turner died in Downey, Calif. on Jan. 20 after a prolonged battle with emphysema and congestive heart failure. He was 71.
Born in Los Angeles, he was credited as both Bill Turner and Bill Ward. Turner started his ice skating career with the Los Angeles Figure Club and then skated with the Ice Capades, Holiday on Ice and the Hollywood Ice Revue.
In the 1970s he created and staged the Canadian TV show "Stars on Ice" hosted by Alex Trebek. With producing partner Larry Dusich, he produced and performed in the Hawaiian premiere of "The Rocky Horror Show" in Honolulu in the 1980s. PALMER - Douglas E. Warner, Jr., 84, of the Three Rivers section of Palmer, died Tuesday, October 30, 2012 following a brief illness, with his family by his side. Born in Springfield, August 18, 1928, he was the son of Douglas and Mary Madeline (Cahill) Warner. He was a graduate of Springfield Technical High School, later received a Bachelor's degree in Business Administration from AIC, and served in the Massachusetts National Guard. He was employed as planning and distribution manager at Monsanto Chemical Company for more than 35 years. He will be deeply missed by his wife of 59 years, Marlene (Kenyon) Warner, his 2 daughters, Susan Prosperi and her husband Larry, of Three Rivers, and Ann Before and her husband Keith of Belchertown. RICHBURG - Gary Dean Warner, 52, of 191 Broad St., died Wednesday (Sept. 20, 2000) at Strong Memorial Hospital in Rochester after a brief illness.
Born Nov. 23, 1947, in Wellsville, he was a son of Jesse and Josephine Baxter Warner. On Nov. 6, 1965, in Obi, he married the former Donna Buchanan, who survives.
Mr. Warner had lived most of his life in the Friendship and Richburg areas. He attended schools in both townships.
He served with the United States Army in 1967, then for a short time was employed at Friendship Dairies. For several years he drove truck for Arborio Construction Co. Later he worked at Macler Industries in Friendship and then Heritage Cutlery in Bolivar. He also was employed at Fibercel Co. in Portville.
Mr. Warner had been a drummer with family and friends in a band called The Country Raiders.
He was a past member of the Friendship American Legion Post 1168. In addition to his wife, Mr. Warner is survived by his mother of Friendship; four daughters, Beverly A.
