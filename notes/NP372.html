Lois Arlene MILLER

[I065068]
Funeral services for Lois Anderson of Cyrus, will be held Monday, October 3, 2011 at 10:30 am at Immanuel Lutheran Church of rural Starbuck. Reverend Paul Johnson will be officiating. Visitation will be held Sunday from 4 to 7 pm at Pedersen Funeral Home in Morris, as well as one hour prior to services at the church on Monday. Burial will be in the church cemetery.
Lois Arlene Anderson, daughter of Leo and Pearl (Hesse) Miller, was born January 14, 1941 in Sauk Centre, Minnesota. Lois was baptized and confirmed at the First Lutheran Church in Sauk Centre. She grew up and received her education in the Sauk Centre area until moving with her family to West Port, Minnesota in 1959. Shortly thereafter, she moved to the Twin Cities where she worked as a nanny and for Kapplan Box Company.
On May 18, 1963, Lois was united in marriage to Lyle Jerome Anderson of Starbuck at Immanuel Lutheran Church. Following their marriage, Lois and Lyle lived on a farm north of Cyrus, farmed with Lyle's parents, and raised their daughter Nancy. During their marriage, Lois worked outside of the home for Fingerhut Corporation in Benson, Roart in Morris, and the Villa of St. Francis in Morris. On February 2, 2006 Lois retired after 28 years of service at the, now named, West Wind Village where she had worked as a housekeeper and laundry aide.
Lois enjoyed many hobbies including cooking; baking; fishing; playing bingo; watching her favorite soap opera, Days of Our Lives; completing word puzzles; socializing and visiting with neighbors, friends, and those confined due to health reasons; but her favorite hobby was travelling. Lois, who was a long-time member of the Lois Club, went on her first Lois Convention in January 1995 to Las Vegas, NV and over the years had traveled to many states including visits to Little Rock, AR; San Francisco, CA; Denver, CO; Orlando, FL; Boise, ID; Des Moines, IA; Branson, MO; New York, NY; Fargo, ND; Seattle, WA; Green Bay, WI; and San Juan in the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico.
Lois died on Friday, September 30, 2011 at the Stevens Community Medical Center in Morris, having lived to reach the age of 70 years.
She is survived by her husband: Lyle Anderson of Cyrus; daughter: Nancy (Jeff) Miller of Morris; sisters: LaVonne ("Bonnie") (Jim) Dickinson of Starbuck; Muriel Karrow of Alexandria; brother: Ken Miller of Alexandria; brother-in-law Howard Anderson; many nieces and nephews; and special friends Wayne and Brenda Skare and Roger and Eileen Sachs and families. She was preceded in death by her parents; sister: Darlene Upton; and brothers: David Miller and Glenn Miller.
Pallbearers for the service are: Wayne Skare, Donny Eblen, Dean Eblen, Joe Hesse, Skip Hesse and Doug Dickinson. All those who called Lois "Friend" will serve as honorary pallbearers. Dee Syverson will serve as organist for the service, with Sheryle Dahl serving as soloist.

Lora Jean MILLER

[I082128]
Jean Smith , born Lora Jean Miller on June 21, 1923, passed away in Richland, WA, on December 11, 2006. She was born in Thedford, NE, to Logan and Della Miller. Jean grew up and graduated from high school in Lincoln, NE. She was working for the phone company, in Lincoln, when she and Mary Kay Clark, nee Baker, heard of higher paying jobs at the Hanford Engineer Works in Richland.
In June of 1944, the two arrived at Hanford and Jean went to work in the bank, living in the women's barracks, then the women's dorm and then a prefab with friends. During that time she met a tall, handsome engineer and on August 12, 1950, she became Mrs. Howard C. Smith . Two years later, she became the mother of a daughter, moved into an "F" house and set up a household. A son was born 18 months later and the Smith family was complete. Jean spent the next 15 years raising children, keeping house and playing bridge. She was also very creative and developed expertise in knitting, sewing, cross-stitch and other crafts. And then there were all of her roses!
As the children grew older, she decided to return to work. She started out part-time in an ophthalmologist's and then went to work for the Department of Energy in the Federal Building. There she spent the next 10 years as a secretary for members of upper management, completing her career as secretary to the manager of the DOE, Richland Operations. She was loved and respected for her professionalism, knowledge and care.
Jean and Howard enjoyed their retirement supporting local sports teams and visiting their children as they moved about the country. They both were active members of All Saint's Episcopal Church in Richland. Jean sang in the choir and was church secretary. When the Columbia Basin Racquet Club opened, both Jean and Howard became avid members. Jean was their first "Aerobicizer of the Year" when she was in her early 60's and continued working out on weights and machines with personal trainers until she was almost 80.
Jean became a caregiver to her husband as his health faltered, so it wasn't until 1998 that she was diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease. She had been showing symptoms for three of four years before but wouldn't slow down long enough to find out about it. After Howard died in 1998, Jean continued to enjoy her love of music and travel, attending many local cultural events and traveling to Las Vegas, LA, New York City and Europe. When her Alzheimer's got to be too much for her, she moved into Guardian Angel Homes, in Richland. She spent the last 2 1/2 years of her life there, enjoying good times, good friends and good care.
Jean Smith will be remembered as a "class act." She made her own happiness, no matter the situation, and was always there to help others. She is survived by her son, Chad (Seattle); daughter, Kendal (Richland); and sister Ardeen Darnell and family (Greeley, CO.)
Tri-City Herald, Kennewick, 15 December 2006

Muriel Fay MILLER

[I065060]
Muriel Fay Karrow, the daughter of Leo and Pearl (Hesse) Miller, was born on November 20, 1936 in Sauk Centre, MN. Muriel lived in Sauk Centre with her parents, two brothers and three sisters. While attending high school she met Earl Eblen. They were united in marriage on July 17, 1954. Eight children were blessed to this union Jean, Betty, Karen, Robert, Donald, Katherine, Dean and Fay. Muriel raised her family in Forada before moving to Westport. She cooked, cleaned, baked and helped Earl on the farm with milking chores and keeping the books for Eblen Construction. She tended a large garden of many different vegetables and enjoyed canning.
Murielʼs children fondly remember her baked goods and large home cooked meals. Muriel enjoyed cooking for large groups of people. Every holiday her entire family gathered around the table for a meal and good conversation. As her children were growing up, Muriel worked at numerous restaurants in the surrounding towns. She also worked for a short time at the bakery in Glenwood, the Douglas County Hospital Gift Shop and at WASP.
Earl died in 1981 and several years later Muriel met Harvey Karrow. They were united in marriage on June 11, 1987. Together they spent time dancing and visiting with friends and family. Murielʼs grandchildren have fond memories of learning how to polka and waltz with their grandparents. Murielʼs joy in her life was her family. She loved making everyone she met feel comfortable, loved and always fed. Her welcoming spirit with a pot of coffee, ready to be poured, will forever be in the memories of the family and friends she loved.
Muriel F. Karrow died on Wednesday, March 11, 2015 at the Douglas County Hospital at the age of 78. She is survived by her daughters Jean (Scott) Kaylor-Jacobs of Nampa, ID, Betty Eblen of Little Falls, Karen (Casey) Wildman of Villard, Katherine (David) Tauber of Villard and Fay (Jeffrey) Koep of Circle Pines; sons Robert Eblen of Atlanta, GA, Donald (Sue) Eblen of Forada and Dean Eblen of Villard; 18 grandchildren; 22 great grandchildren; step children Rodney (Carol) Karrow and Vicky (Jerry) Roquette and their families; brother Kenneth Miller of Alexandria; sister LaVonne (Jim) Dickinson of Starbuck; and numerous nieces, nephews and cousins. She was preceded in death by her parents, 1st husband Earl Eblen, 2nd husband Harvey Karrow, sisters Lois Anderson and Darlene Upton, brothers David Miller and Glen Miller; grandson Eric Bartz and great grandson Bailey Kovarik.
A funeral service was held on Monday, March 16, 2015 at Zion Lutheran Church in Alexandria. Interment was in St. Johnʼs Lutheran Cemetery in Garfield. Casket Bearers were Casey Wildman, David Tauber Sr., David Tauber Jr., Jeff Koep, Conner Koep, Greg Wildman and Elmo Eblen. Honorary Casket Bearers were all of Murielʼs grandchildren and great grandchildren.

Mrs. Thelma White and Mrs. Leland Patten sang two selections at the service. "In the Sweet By and By" and "Rock of Ages." Orville S. Miller was born July 21, 1868 in Forest township, the son of Charles F. and Louisa Miller. During his boyhood years he lived on the family farm and attended the Forest township schools. He farmed with his father during his younger years and later was associated with him in the manufacture of sorgum syrup, a product for which the MIllers were well known throughout this part of the country. MARRIED AT LITTLE PRAIRIE On November 26, 1896, the late Mr. Miller and Mary Blanche Tupper were united in marriage at Little Prairie. Following their marriage they lived in California for a short time before returning to Faribault where Mr. Miller purchased a farm north of Faribault. Until 1939 Mr. MIller operated this farm. He was a member of the Church of Christ for many years and was an ordained elder of the church. Since the sale of his farm Mr. MIller has been making his home in Rose Creek with his sons Vernon and Clarence. More recently he has been living with Clarence since Vernon moved to Oregon.
