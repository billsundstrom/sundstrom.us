On July 1, at the St. Stanislaus Church, Amsterdam, the Msgr. Stanislaus Gospodarek officiated at the wedding ceremony of Miss Regina G. Marie Rzeznik, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Joseph J. Rzeznik of Amsterdam, and Craig Richard Chesbro, son of Mr. and Mrs. Gilbert Chesbro of Buffalo.
Given in marriage by her father, the bride was attended by Miss Ann Pastorini, of Long Island, maid of honor; Mrs. Shirley Rzeznik, Amsterdam, Miss Betty Jo Bartman, Scotia, bridesmaids; Miss Linda Felczak, junior bridesmaid; Miss Dana Prahl, flowergirl.
Jamie Zaleski of Buffalo served as best man. David Oldman, Buffalo, Keith Smith, Kevin Traskos, and Michael Opalka, all of Amsterdam, ushered. David Seege, Cheektowaga, was the ringbearer. Following the ceremony, a reception was held at St. John's Hall.
A graduate of Wilbur Lynch High School and the State University College at Geneseo, the bride was an elementary librarian at the Galway Central School. She has accepted a librarian's position in Lancaster.
Mrs. Chesbro, a graduate of the University of Buffalo, is a mathematics teacher in the Buffalo School System. He is attending Canisius College preparing for his M.A. in Education.
Following a wedding trip to the Bahama Islands, the couple will reside at Lisbon Avenue, Buffalo.
Schenectady Gazette, 17 July 1967

At the home of the bride's parents, last Thursday evening, occurred the wedding of Miss Helma Larsen, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Nelson Larsen, to Albert Wickstrom, Rev. Mr. Greenwood, pastor of the M.E. church, officiating. Miss Jennie Larsen, a sister of the bride, came home from Ypsilanti to act as bridesmaid, and Emil Johnson was best man. Mrs. Earl Koenig played the wedding march. The groom is a diamond drill setter for the St. Clair Exploration company stationed at Loretto. It was while he was doing similar work at Quinnesec that he met and won Miss Larsen. The bride is one of Quinnesec's fairest daughters, who has many friends on the range. After the ceremony a wedding dinner was enjoyed, the happy couple departing on the evening train for a honeymoon trip to Chicago, Bay City and Detroit. They will reside in Loretto.
Iron Mountain Press, 4 April 1918

The nuptials of Mr. Morton L, Parker and Miss Alice B. Hawkes were solemnized last evening at 8 o'clock at the home of the bride's parents, Dr. and Mrs. D. B. Hawkes, at No. 310 North Water street. The officiating clergyman was Bev. W. H, Prestley, pastor of the First Presbyterian Church, and the attendants were Miss Sophie and Mr. H. W. Elwood. The ceremony was witnessed by a company of forty lends of the happy pair, who received earnest congratulations. The bride, who has long been a social favorite in this community, was very tastefully attired in becoming costume of India mull and lace, the ornaments being natural flowers. The brides- maid was similarly attired. The couple received a large collection of beautiful gifts. A choice collation was served party fashion, and between 10 and 11 o'clock Mr. Parker and bride departed for the depot, and left for the east on a six weeks' bridal tour. They will visit New York City and points in Ohio and Connecticut. They take with them the best wishes of a large circle of friends. Mr. Parker is the cutter at J. B. Race and has formed many acquaintances in the com- munity during his residence of two or three years in our midst.
Decatur Weekly Republican, 7 August 1884
