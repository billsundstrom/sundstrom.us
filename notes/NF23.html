Rev. Francis J. Toolan, pastor of the Church of the Epiphany in Sayre, heard the exchange of vows, performed the double ring ceremony and celebrated the nuptial mass this morning at 11 o'clock when Miss Janice Wolcott of Route 4 Penn Yan became the bride of Edward Saxe of Sayre. The bride is the daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Cecil Wolcott of Route 4, Penn Yan. Mr. and Mrs. Donald Saxe of 116 Center St., Sayre, are the bridegroom's parents. Pink gladioli and pink carnations decorated the church for the occasion. Mrs. John Sudnick, organist, accompanied Mrs. John Olisky, soloist. Mrs. Richard Benson, South Waverly, sister of the bridegroom, was matron of honor. Bridesmaids were Miss Linda Young of Lawrenceville, Miss Mary Eaton of Sayre, cousin of the bride, and Mrs. Jerome Cahill, of South Waverly, sister of the bridegroom. Serving as best man was John K???sh of Sayre. Guests were ushered by Richard Benson of South Waverly. Jerome Cahill, Smith Waverly and Bruce Wolcott of West Bloom-field, N.Y., brother of the bride. Kimberley Wolcott, niece of t h e bride, was flower girl and Eugene Cahill, nephew of the bridegroom, was ring bearer. Escorted to the altar by her father, the bride was attired in a floor-length gown fashioned of Korate linen with a modified scoop neckline and ???? length sleeves outlined in veniee lace. The A-line skirt featured an empire waistline which was also trimmed with Venice lace. A detachable watteau, attached at the waistline, flowed into a chapel train. Her triple tiered illusion veil fell from a daisy cap with profile streamers of pearls. She carried a cascade of while roses. The matron of honor was attired in a turquoise colored floor length sheath of Korate styled with a scoop neckline and short sleeves. A detachable watteau adored the back. She carried a colonial bouquet of pink carnations. The bridesmaids wore identically styled gowns in peach. Each carried a colonial bouquet of yellow and orange carnations. Mrs. Wolcott, mother of the bride, chose a powder blue bonded knit suit, which she accented with while accessories. Mrs. Saxe, mother of the bridegroom, selected a lavender and white sheath jacket dress, which she complemented with white accessories. Both mothers completed their ensembles with a corsage of white sweetheart roses. A wedding breakfast was served to the wedding party and the immediate families at O'Brien's Inn immediately following the ceremony. The couple greeted their guests at a 2 p.m. reception at the Moose Home in Waverly after which they left for a week's trip to Bermuda. For traveling, Mrs. Saxe selected a blue ami white knit tunic suit. The former Miss Wolcott is a graduate of Penn Yan Academy and of the Robert Packer Hospital School of Nursing. She is employed at the school of nursing. Mr. Saxe is a graduate of Sayre High School and has served four years with the U.S. Navy. He is employed by I.B.M. in Endicott. Following their wedding trip, the couple will reside at 400 N. Main St., Athens. The bride was entertained prenuptially at a shower given by her attendants at the Moose Home in Waverly, at a shower given by the personnel of the first floor at the Robert Packer Hospital.
The Evening Times, Sayre, 10 June 1967
