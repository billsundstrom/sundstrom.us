Mary Maxyne Heath
Feb 6, 1922 - March 21, 2012
Mary Maxyne Heath was born to Mike and Clara Hayes on February 6, 1922, in Chickasha, Oklahoma. She attended Pocasset Public Schools and graduated in 1941. After graduation, she attended Draughn's School of Business. Upon graduation, she was employed by Bell Telephone Co. In November of 1943, she married John Dale Homan at Randolph Field in Wichita Falls, Texas, where he was a pilot in training. On February 7, 1945, she gave birth to twin boys, Gerry and Terry Homan, and in November of that same year, Lt. John Dale Homan died in a plane crash. In November of 1949, Maxyne married JC Heath in Hot Springs, Arkansas, and this began a wonderful marriage that lasted until her death on March 21, 2012. James Michael Heath (Mickey) was born to this marriage on May 31, 1951. Maxyne leaves behind a loving husband, JC Heath , of the home; Mickey Heath and wife Lorna of Blanchard; Terry Homan and wife Dixie of South Fork, CO; and Gerry Homan and wife Mary of Norman. Maxyne was preceded in death by her parents and all her brothers and one sister. She leaves behind seven grandchildren and 14 great-grandchildren and one niece and nephew. Maxyne was a wonderful wife, mother, grandmother, daughter and aunt. She also leaves behind a great legacy.

ATWATER, Calif. - Celia Ann Hays, 76, passed away Monday, Dec. 12, 2011 in Atwater, Calif.
She was born May 28, 1935 in Lewistown, Mont. Her early childhood was spent in Scottsbluff. Celia taught a few years in country schools in Nebraska before moving to the Atwater area in 1964. She taught in the Atwater School District until she retired.
Celia loved to travel and went overseas several times. She loved music and played the piano and organ. Celia had a big heart and was always helping other people. She especially loved her family, including her many nephews and nieces. Celia was a member of the Church of Christ in Atwater.

Albion man dies in Lake Ontario SHERIFF:
Rough waters left two exposed to elements nine miles north of Point Breeze
A man died Saturday after the boat he and another man were using sank in rough waters in Lake Ontario, Orleans County sheriff's deputies said.
The deceased was identified Sunday as Carl F. Hazel , 66, of Albion.
Details were provided by the survivor and responders were transported to where the men had been lost in the water for at least two hours before being recovered.
The men were fishing in 500-foot deep waters about nine miles off the shoreline when waves reaching five to seven feet crashed over the boat, which took on water and sank, Sheriff Randall Bower said in a statement Saturday. That would place them about as far north from Point Breeze as the lakeshore hamlet is from the village of Albion.
An investigation into the sinking is underway, with both the Orleans and Niagara County Sheriff's Offices taking part.
According to initial reports, both men were wearing life jackets at the time, and were able to communicate with one another for a short period of time. They were unable to stay together in the rough waters.
Exposed to the elements, they floated apart until being spotted by a Canadian freighter. The U.S. Coast Guard and its Canadian counterpart were notified, with the first call to the Orleans County 911 Center arriving shortly after 11:30 a.m. Both men had been recovered by then, with Hazel unresponsive and his companion able to talk despite being in shock from the trauma.
Officers from the Orleans County Sheriff's marine enforcement unit and two COVA medics responded, taking the Sheriff's Marine One boat to meet a responding USCG cutter approximately five miles north of Point Breeze. Attempts to save Hazel 's life were unsuccessful, and the Coast Guard transported his body to the marina.
The surviving boater was rescued by a private fishing vessel from Ohio, and was also transported to Lake Breeze marina. He was treated for exposure to the cold water and released.
Bower thanked the assistance of the U.S. and Canadian Coast Guards, the Olcott Fire Rescue Boat, COVA Ambulance, Carlton Fire Department and others who assisted in the response.
The Daily News, Batavia, 27 August 2018

Harold Matthew HAZEL

[I106313]
On December 10, 2006, Harold Mathew Hazel passed away after a 10 year struggle with Alzheimer's disease. He was known to his family and friends as Hal. Born in Lakewood, Ohio, he grew up on a farm during the Great Depression. As a boy he loved to swim in the summer and skate and sled in the winter. When WWII broke out he joined the Army. He trained countless recruits how to swim at the pool at Schofield barracks. After the war he met and married Betty Reese Coffey. Their love lasted 49 years. Under the G.I. bill, he earned a degree in Mechanical engineering from Case Institute in Cleveland. Following school, the couple moved to Arizona to get away from the snow where he and Betty Reese raised their two boys, Bruce and Scott. Hal worked at Garrett Air Research as an Aerospace Engineer for the next 32 years. In retirement, he started the Phoenix chapter of the American Aviation Historical Society as well and the Garrett Air Research Retirees Club. He also enjoyed spending time with his two grandsons, Chris and Gary. He lost the love of his life in 1995 and his oldest son in 1998. These losses took a toll on his body, mind and mostly his spirit. In recent years, Hal moved to San Diego to be closer to family. He had a life-long love of airplanes and enjoyed attending air shows with his family and friends. With regards to politics, he always considered himself a Barry Goldwater Republican. Hal was involved in all of the best things in life. He was a good son, husband, father, and grandfather. He was a good provider for his family. His values anchored the lives of his family and friends. He was a good example to his sons and grandsons. We will miss his physical presence in our lives but his spirit will live on with us. Good bye old fellow. A celebration of his life is planned for this spring in Arizona. Hal is survived by his son Scott, daughter-in-law Karen and grandsons Chris and Gary and his many friends.
The San Diego Union-Tribune, 14 December 2006

Maria E. HAZLETT

[I065524]
MRS. MARIA RICHARDSON
Died After Long Illness on Monday of This Week
Was Seventy-Five Years Old
Funeral Services Held Wednesday at 1:30 from the Home and 2 O'clock from the Methodist Church.
On Monday the community was shocked upon learning of the death of Mrs. Maria Richardson, who passed away at 9:30 a. m., after a long illness, aged seventy-five years, six months and seventeen days.
The deceased was an early settler and came to Genoa from Nelson, Tioga County, Pennsylvania in the year of 1848, and was born at the same place June 4, 1828, being the youngest daughter of Samuel and Mary Hazlett.
Mrs. Richardson had been ailing for some time and about two months ago was taken seriously ill. Her death came a surprise to the immediate family as well as her many friends, owing to her improving condition just before her death.
After coming to Illinois in the year of 1848 she taught school in the old Genoa school which then stood on the corner of Main and Sycamore streets. She followed these duties until the year of 1850 when she was united in marriage to George Washington Richardson, who, before his death was better known as Washington Richardson. She lived on a farm south of town with her husband until his death which occurred June 29, 1887. Two years later she purchased the little home on Emmett street where she passed a peaceful life until the time of her death.
In the year of 1885, she joined the Methodist Episcopal church of Genoa, and since that time has been one who has devoted much time to the work of Christ. She was a lady well thought of in all communities and lived a peaceful life among her many friends. She was the youngest of a family of nine children, and was the last to depart.
She was the mother of five children of whom four are living. They are: Mrs. Lida Anderson, Mrs. C. A. Snow and Edgar H. of Genoa and Geo. W. of dane, Wis.
The funeral service took place Wednesday at 1:30 from the home and at 2 o'clock at the M. E. church, Rev. T. E. Ream officiating. The remains were interred at the Genoa cemetery.
Genoa Republican, 25 December 1903
