Ira Clapp MOODY

[I033599]
News of the sudden death at her home in Forest Hill of Mrs. Iva Upton Moody, came an a shock to family and friends in this section of the state. Mrs. Moody, a life-long resident of this community, was stricken with apoplexy while at the telephone Thurs. morning. She had called the Wm. E. Moody home in Alma to discuss plans for conveyance of her husband, Ira C. Moody, who had been ill in bed, to an Alma hospital and had been sticken with apoplexy before the conversation was ended. She was found lying on the floor when Wm. E. Moody, hurrying to the home, arrived about 7:30, a short time later, death apparently had been almost instantaneous.
Iva Upton, the daughter of Mrs and Mrs. Joshua Upton, pioneer residents here, was born in Shepherd April 19, 1874 and always made her home in this community. She was married March 30, 1921, after having taught school in Shepherd for some years, to Ira C. Moody of Forest Hill.
Mrs. Moody had been ill for about 3 years,
Surviving with the bereaved husband and step-son, Wm. Moody, are 1 sister Mrs. Myrtle Upton, and a brother J.W. Upton, Shepherd Hardware merchant.
Interment was made in Salt River cemetery.

James Melvin MOODY

[I062613]
Feb. 21- The funeral of James Melvin Moody was held at the Baptist church last Thursday at 1 p. in., the Odd Fellows having charge and assisting in the ceremonies. Rev. Winchester of the First Baptist church of Fredonia gave the scriptural reading and delivered the discourse. The interment was at the Christian Cemetery. Mr. Moody had been a life long resident of this place until about four years ago; he moved his family to Arcade, where his death occurred after one illness of several months. Last Tuesday he passed from this life and the remains were brought to the home of E. L. Griswold, the evening before the funeral. Mr. Moody was a man of excellent character and highly respected by all. He leaves a wife and daughter who have the sympathy of their many friends. He also leaves two sisters: Mrs. Wheeler and Mrs., Sears. Beautiful floral tributes were given by the Odd Fellows, W. C. T. U. and relatives.
The Fredonia Censor, 21 Februay 1910

Donald John MOORE

Donald John Moore, Sr., 77 of Albany, passed away Tuesday at his home surrounded by his family. He will be remembered for his enjoyable sense of humor, intellectual curiosity and woodworking prowess.
Donald was born in Chariton, Iowa to D.P. and Pauline Moore. He attended Des Moines Technical High School in Des Moines, Iowa and then enlisted in the U.S. Army. Upon his honorable discharge, Don received his Bachelor's Degree in 1965 from Monmouth College in Illinois, and his Master's Degree from Northern Illinois University in 1971. He taught middle school in Crystal Lake, Illinois for 27 years.
Don married Joan Doughton in Lebanon, OR on June 27, 1981. They lived in Salem for 19 years before moving to Albany. He was a member of the McHenry Co. Jaycees, National Education Association, and the Woodstock Opera House.
Donald is survived by his wife, Joan; children, Laura P. Moore of Alexandria, VA, Donald J. Moore of Brookfield, WI, Sherrie Shank of Albany, and Lisa Beiles of Chicago, IL; brother, David Moore of Lockhart, TX; sister, Susan Byrnes of Plymouth, MN; 11 grandchildren and 2 great grandchildren.
