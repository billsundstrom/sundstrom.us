Johan WIJKSTRÖM

[I002956]
Johan Wijkström 1761 - 1842
Johan was born since April 17, 1761 in Rutvik, Nederluleå. His parents were former corporal, District Judge Per Persson Dyrg and Mary Pehrsdotter from Bensbyn.
He enrolled at the Härnösands gynasium in the fall of 1778 and in Uppsala in 1782. On June 16, 1787 he was ordained. He called for a curate in Överkalix by Olof Hamrén and married to tradition wedding to his daughter, Brita (born June 24, 1772). He was the grace pastor in Nedertorneå in 1791 and curate there from the 1792.
On January 4, 1812 he was appointed rector of the Swedish share of Övertorneå pastorate, and on 27 January 1813 to be the head of Västerbotten fourth district.

John married twice. First with the above Brita Catharina with whom he had five children:
Brita Catharina, May 14, 1790 - August 13, 1790
Per Olof, September 15, 1793 - June 29, 1851. MA 1818, Bachelor of Laws eq, auditor at the Västerbotten Regiment, ordained 1836, vicar of the Finnish church in Stockholm.
Johan Erik, July 26, 1796. Student in Uppsala 1820.
Christina Catharina, March 22, 1797 - Apr 1 1862. Married August 1, 1820 to the lecturer, later pastor of Jukkasjärvi, Ingemar Lindstedt.
Carl Gustaf February 20, 1800. Curate in Hietaniemi.

After Brita Catharina's death married, Johan married Agatha Montin (1771-1860), a vicar's daughter from Kemi.

Mary Elizabeth WILCOX

[I046979]
Mary Elizabeth Wilcox Nov. 18, 1954 ~ Jan.20, 2009 Resident of Walnut Creek Mary died unexpectedly at home in Rossmoor. Born in Richmond to Jeanne Wilcox and Max Wilcox, Jr., her earliest years were spent in El Cerrito and later in Orinda. She attended Miramonte High School, spent a semester in Stockbridge, Mass., and was a graduate of The Athenian School in Danville. With her insatiable quest for knowledge, she spent her four college years at U.C. Santa Cruz, The Monterey Institute of International Studies, The Columbia University program at University of Madrid, Spain, and earned her B.A. at U.C. Berkeley, graduating with the Class of 1976 as a Spanish Major. She went on to Hastings College of the Law where she was honored scholastically as a member of the Thurston Society from 1977 through 1978. She graduated with the Class of 1980 as a distinguished member of the 'Order of the Coif'. Mary started her practice with the firm of Miller, Starr, and Regalia where Edmund Regalia became her special friend and mentor. Eventually, Mary was appointed to the 1st District Court of Appeals in San Francisco as a research attorney working for Justice James Scott and later, Justice Gary Strankman. Probably most fulfilling was her selection by Chief Justice of the California Supreme Court, Ronald George, to become a member of his staff as research attorney. She left the Supreme Court in 1996. Mary was a member of the State Bar of California, the Rossmoor Tennis Club, and a Rossmoor library volunteer. She was also a member of the Diablo Valley Literacy Council, the Democrats of Rossmoor, and a well-loved member of a Rossmoor Wisdom Circle. Mary was mostly quiet and unassuming with an engaging smile that belied her inner struggles. It is remarkable that she was able to achieve so much in her lifetime, given the degree of anxiety and depression which began in early childhood and became increasingly overwhelming over the years. In 2005, she retired and moved to Rossmoor to be closer to her parents, providing invaluable help and support during the final months of her father's illness. She made many loyal friends in Rossmoor and enjoyed countless happy hours with her close-knit tennis group on the Rossmoor Buckeye courts. Mary was predeceased by her father, retired Superior Court Judge Max Wilcox Jr. and two sisters, Cindy McDowell and Denise Rae. She is survived by her mother, Jeanne Watson Wilcox of Rossmoor; brother, Dr. Gregory Wilcox of Davis; sister and brother-in-law, Terri and Randy Giovanetti of Danville, as well as numerous nieces and nephews. A Celebration of her Life will take place this spring, the date to be announced later. In lieu of flowers, a contribution in memory of Mary may be made to one's favorite charity. Max WILCOX

Last Rites for Max Wilcox, California Newspaperman, Held--
Private funeral services for Max Wilcox, retired advertising manager of the News Call Bulletin, San Francisco, Calif, and a leading figure in the city's advertising community over a span of 4 decades, were held Wednesday, Aug 24, in San Francisco.
According to an article in the San Francisco Examiner, "Mr. Wilcox, always an impressive attired man and always with a warm smile for his numerous friends and clients, died at Mt. Zion Hospital Sunday, Aug 21. He would have observed his 71st birthday on Aug 30."
Mr. Wilcox, the eldest son of Mrs. Elsie Wilcox of Bridgeport, was stricken with a heart attack some time ago, and entered the hospital for treatment of emphysema 2 weeks before his death.
He retired in September of ??,(probably 1965) closing a career of more than 40 years in newspaper advertising. He joined the Call's Advertising staff on March 23, 1925 and was named head of the unit staffs of the Call and The Bulletin when the 2 afternoon dailies were merged in 1929.
After 1959, he became advertising manager for the Call-Bulletin and The News following their merger into the News Call Bulletin.
Born in Redington, he attended the University of Nebraska, served in WW I and after brief post-war service with the Omaha Bee, published a weekly newspaper in BAyard from 1919 to 1923.
Then he turned to the West, migrating to Los Angeles where he joined the advertising sales staff of the Daily News in 1924. The next year he went to San Francisco to join The Call's staff. He was a member of the San Francisco Advertising Club.
Besides his mother, he is survived by his widow, Mrs. Mae Wilcox; one son, Max Jr. of Orlinda, Calif; and 5 grandchildren.
