John J. Devich, 100, of Rogers, AR passed away Wednesday, February 26, 2014 at Innisfree Retirement Home. He was born November 10, 1913 in Virginia, MN to Anthony and Josephine Kolbuphar Devich.
He had a love for his family, sailing, boating, skiing (summer and winter), windsurfing, building, tinkering, and for life itself. He built sailboats, furniture, two homes, and could fix anything from an injured finger to an electrical blackout.
John earned a football scholarship to North Dakota State and after graduation, proudly served in the United States Navy before spending a career at 3M. He never missed a day of work. That statement is indicative of who he was: someone who gave his all and was loyal to a fault.
He is preceded in death by his wife, Leone (Green) Devich; his parents; one daughter, Joan Rossbach; and 10 siblings.

Josiah DEWEY

[I011887]
The son of Thomas "The Settler" Dewey & his wife Frances, later the wife of George Phelps. Dewey married on 6 Nov 1662 at Northampton, MA to Hepzibah Lyman the daughter of Richard & Hepzibah Ford Lyman. The Deweys located at Northampton, MA about 1663 where Josiah learned the carpenter's trade and became a freeman in 1666. During King Philip's War he was a Sargent of the Guard at Westfield, MA.
He was a Quaker and in his religious discovery he says; "When I was entred into a married state I saw myself now under former ingagemnts of attending heart-searching & hearing Mr. Mather on the hearts hardness assert that there was no plague like unto that, I was affrighted there at & soon after hearing Mr. Eliot (now of Gilford) on a lecture sermon was so awakened as to resolve no longer to delay but to fall to search my own heart. But I found it hard & difficult work to keep my mind to it & sometimes I found that my heart would slip from ye work almost as soon as I was at it. So that I could find little rest."
He seems to have found that rest as he became a Deacon in the church at Westfield. In 1696 he moved to Lebanon, CT where he helped found the town, and where his name appears often in the early records.

Headstone inscription:
Here lies the body of Dn Josiah Dewey He was born in Windsor 1640 Long Servd The Church of Christ in Westfield and Ordained Deacon Removed to Lebanon In the Begining where he done much Sarvis for God & Man he lived a Holy & a Religious life & lived to a great Age & he slept in Jesus Septemr 7, 1732 In the 92 year of his age.

Douglas E. DEWITT

[I113184]
STOCKTON, Iowa - Doug DeWitt, 78, of Stockton, passed away May 7, 2016 at ManorCare, Davenport.
He was born Feb. 9, 1938, in Davenport to William and Magdalene DeWitt. In 1966, he married Doris Harding and they had 50 wonderful years together.
In 1955, he joined the Navy, and went through Great Lakes Naval Training after graduation. He went to Portsmith Naval Hospital Corps School. He served two years at Jacksonville Naval Hospital, then served two years on the USS Jupiter, a supply ship; then on to the USS Norton Sound, a guided missile ship. He also went to several countries, including China, Philippines, South America and Japan.
Upon his discharge is 1961 he returned to Davenport. He was employed at Evan Ambulance for about a year. For the next six years he worked at various other jobs. He was employed as a roving inspector for 32 years at French & Hecht (later Titan Wheel), from where he retired. He was the mayor of Stockton for 14 years.
He is survived by his wife, and brother, Marvin Petznick, and sister, Jan Short, both of Tempe, Ariz.
QUAD CITY TIMES, Davenport, 10 May 2016

Virginia Mae DEXTER

[I064239]
Virginia M. Murphy passed away peacefully at Champlain Valley Physicians Hospital after a sudden, brief illness on Sept. 25, 2016. Virginia was born in Troy, N.Y., on Aug. 15, 1927, the daughter of Oliver Dexter and Virginia Carpenter Reid.
Virginia resided most of her life in Watervliet, N.Y., briefly in Hamburg, N.Y., and later enjoyed some of her retirement years living in Glass Lake, N.Y. Over 20 years ago, she and her late husband, J. Joseph Murphy, moved to Plattsburgh and resided on Point au Roche to be closer to family.
During her younger years, Virginia was employed by the New York Telephone Company but for most of her life, she was a devoted and loving homemaker and caregiver to her family. While raising her family, Virginia enjoyed family vacations to the mid-Atlantic states and later in life looked forward to her trips to Maine and Florida. Virginia enjoyed fishing, gardening, knitting and reading, but her most beloved past time was following baseball, particularly the New York Yankees. Virginia will always be admired and loved for the strength, courage, and independence she showed throughout her lifetime, especially during times of adversity.
Survivors include her daughter, Linda Leidner and her husband Scott, one grandson, Brett Leidner, all of Plattsburgh; her sister, Susan Dexter Arroyave and her husband, Roberto of Houston, Texas; as well as several nieces and nephews. In addition to her parents, Virginia was predeceased by her other late husbands, William Scales(1953), James Hutchinson(1970), son, Mark Scales Hutchinson(1971), her brother, Donald Dexter, as well as several aunts, uncles, sisters-in-law and brothers-in-law.
Calling hours will be held at 10 a.m. on Friday, with a funeral service to follow at 11 a.m. at the Bowen & Parker Bros. Funeral Home, 97 Old Loudon Road, Latham, N.Y. Burial will immediately follow at Albany Rural Cemetery, Menands, N.Y.
In lieu of flowers, donations in Virginia's memory may be made to the FitzPatrick Cancer Center at UVM Champlain Valley Physicians Hospital, Plattsburgh, or to the charity of one's choice.
