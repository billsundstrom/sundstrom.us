Marguerite EDSON

[I061355]
Marguerite Ninneman passed away peacefully, Sunday, June 16, 2002 at Belmont Terrace Nursing Home. She was born to Otto and Eva (Pitts) Edson in Bremerton on January 16, 1913.
Marguerite lived most of her 89 years in the Bremerton area. She, her sister Dorothy and their brother Don attended local schools and graduated from Bremerton High School.
After her parents divorced, she set aside her dream of becoming a teacher to answer a newspaper advertisement for ''petite blond ladies'' and became one of the original usherettes at the brand new Roxy Theater to provide much needed depression era income to her family.
Marguerite and the dashingly handsome Ken Ninneman were married in 1935 and later moved to East Bremerton to raise their three children. After years of summer rentals, Marguerite's passion for Hood Canal prompted the family's move to the South Shore where she became an active participant in PTA, a member and President of the School Board, and a prime mover behind the effort to create a high school for the ''Belfair kids''.
Widowed after 58 years of marriage, she enjoyed family outings, duplicate bridge games, and lunch at the Boat Shed until declining health limited her mobility.
She is survived and will be lovingly missed by her children: Jeanne (Ninneman) and her husband Dick Burger, Nancy Mosely and Don Ninneman and his wife Bonnie; her five granchildren and eight great-grandchildren.
For those desiring to remember Marguerite through gifts, the family suggests Unity Church of Bremerton, 1712 Trenton, Bremerton, WA. Private family services are pending.

Marguerite Ninneman, 89, of Bremerton died June 16, 2002, at Belmont Terrace Convalescent Center in Bremerton.
She was born Jan. 16, 1913, in Bremerton to Otto M. and Eva (Pitts) Edson. She graduated from Bremerton High School in 1931.
She married Kenneth E. Ninneman on Jan. 10, 1935, in Bremerton. He preceded her in death in 1993.
Mrs. Ninneman was one of the original usherettes at the Roxy in downtown Bremerton. She also had worked at Bremer's Department Store for many years.
She had served as president of the North Mason School Board and volunteered with the Election Board. She also was a member of the Orthopedic Guild and Unity Church of Bremerton.
She enjoyed reading and playing golf.
Survivors include a son, Don P. Ninneman and his wife, Bonnie, of University Place; two daughters, Nancy Mosely of Marysville and Jeanne Burger and her husband, Dick, of Seattle; five grandchildren; and eight great-grandchildren. She also was preceded in death by her brother, Donald Edson, and her sister, Dorothy Roberts.
No services will be held. Arrangements are under the direction of Lewis Funeral Chapel with inurnment at Forest Lawn Cemetery. An online memorial can be seen at www.lewischapel.com.
Memorial donations may be made to Unity Church of Bremerton, 1712 Trenton Ave., Bremerton, WA 98310.
Kitsap Sun, 19 June 2002

Sarah Martha EDSON

[I092364]
Sally Edson Barlow died at home November 3, 2009, in Greenville.
Sally was born August 23, 1927, in Dallas to Walter S. and Norma Edson. She graduated from Woodrow Wilson High School where she was valedictorian in 1944. Sally attended the University of Texas, majoring in languages. She was elected to Phi Beta Kappa and graduated in 1948.
On January 21, 1950, she married Edwin D. Barlow. She was active in school and civic activities including the YMCA, United Way, Community Concert Association, and library board. She was a Girl Scout leader for many years and was on the Tejas Girl Scout Council.
Sally had been an active member of First Presbyterian Church in Greenville since 1952 where she was a Sunday school teacher, Presbyterian youth sponsor, and Circle Bible leader.
Sally earned her master's degree in education from East Texas State University and enjoyed a long teaching career. She taught Spanish at Greenville High School and was a sponsor of the Spanish Club and Foreign Language Association. Following formal retirement she became trained to teach in the MTA program for dyslexia and taught at First Presbyterian MLC program.
Sally was a member and loved clubs, including City Federation, Greenville Garden Club, Junior Pallas, Zodiac Club and Garden Study Club. She loved to travel and enjoyed trips with her husband, family and friends.
Surviving are her husband of Greenville; a daughter, Kathleen Hodges of Dallas; daughter and son-in-law Beckie and Donald Dreibelbis of Lebanon, PA; grandchildren Hayden Hodges of Dallas, Sally Fienup and her husband Chris of Memphis, Tenn., Kara Julius and her husband Robert of Baltimore, MD, 2nd Lt. Sheldon Hodges serving in Camp Tajii, Iraq, Ellen Dreibelbis of Philadelphia, PA, and Samuel Dreibelbis of State College, PA.
She was preceded in death by her parents and two sisters, Jo Moffet and Patricia Posey.
Memorial services will be Friday, November 6, 2009, at 1 p.m. at the First Presbyterian Church under the direction of Coker-Mathews Funeral Home. Rev. Chris Campbell will officiate. Burial Will be in Memoryland Memorial Park.
Visitation is from 5 to 7 p.m., Thursday, November 5, 2009 at the funeral home.
Memorial donations may be made to the Jewel Rose Scholarship Fund or the MLC Scholarship Fund at First Presbyterian Church, 5905 Stonewall St. Greenville, TX 75402.
