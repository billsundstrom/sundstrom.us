Ruth Janet HATFIELD

[I022624]
Ruth Janet ("Jann") Peterson passed away on November 29, 2017. Jann was born on May 27, 1934 in Deer River, Minnesota. She was the youngest child of Wilbert Marion Hatfield and Ethel Marion Atkinson Hatfield.
Jann and her family moved to Florida when she was a child. She had wonderful memories of living in Florida near her great uncle Oliver Graham, who owned citrus orchards and had a pet alligator named Cleo.
During World War II, Jann's father worked as a master carpenter at the naval shipyard in Jacksonville, Florida. As soon as the war ended, Jann and her parents moved to Washington. The family eventually settled in Summit Valley, near Addy. Jann enjoyed attending Jenkins High School in Chewelah and made many lifetime friends. She graduated from high school in 1952.
Jann married Ernest ("Ernie") Peterson on August 27, 1960 and moved to the Kelly Hill area, where she lived for the rest of her life. She was involved in many community activities, including serving as PTA president and co-founding the swimming lesson program at Pierre Lake. She was a secretary for many years in the Kettle Falls School District.
Jann was the most wonderful mother. She was supportive and encouraging, and shared her love of books, nature, history, art and cooking with her children. Jann valued education and recognized the importance of creativity, curiosity and an inquiring mind. She also had a fabulous sense of humor and loved to laugh.
She enjoyed traveling, especially with her children, and took trips with them to England, Scotland, Ireland, Canada and all over the United States, including Arizona, California, Florida, Minnesota, Montana and South Dakota.
Jann is survived by children Vance, Betsy, Deb and Gary Peterson; grandchildren Renee (Sean) Peete, Mike Peterson and Sarah (Kurt) Ennis; six great-grandchildren; numerous nieces, nephews and cousins; and special friends that she considered family, such as Arlyne Dehning and Jo Nullet. She was preceded in death by husband Ernie Peterson, daughter Leslie Gaye Peterson, sister Alice Hatfield Davis and brother Wilbert ("Bill") Hatfield.
A celebration of Jann's life is planned for spring 2018 at the Kettle River Grange Hall.
The Statesman Examiner, 20 December 2017

Ruth Janet "Jann" Peterson, who was found passed away near her car in a field by Addy in late November, originally graduated from Jenkins High School in Chewelah and had many connections to the small community.
While listed as an 82-year old Northport resident in an article about her passing away, information taken from official police sources, Peterson was actually a resident of Orient.
Peterson was reported missing on Nov. 28 after she left her home near Pierre Lake to go to a dentist appointment in Colville. The report came in at 5:30 p.m., and near 10:30 p.m. a Washington State Patrol Officer located her car in a field near Zimmer Road in the Addy area. The car had become stuck in a drainage ditch and Petersonʼs body was found in the Colville River.
Peterson was born in Deer River Minnesota and her family moved to Washington shortly after World War 2. The family settled in Summit Valley near Addy and according to an obituary submitted to the Independent by the family, she made many lifelong friends at JHS.
She married Ernest Peterson in 1960 and moved to the Kelly Hill area where she lived for the rest of her life. Peterson was involved in the community up north as a PTA president and co-founded the swimming lesson program at Pierre Lake. She was also a secretary for many years in the Kettle Falls School District.
According to her obituary, she enjoyed traveling, especially with her children, and took trips with them to England, Scotland, Ireland, Canada and all over the United States, including Arizona, California, Florida, Minnesota, Montana and South Dakota.
A celebration of Jannʼs life is planned for spring 2018 at the Kettle River Grange Hall.
The Independent, 3 January 2018

Wilbert Marion HATFIELD

[I019018]
Wilbert Marion Hatfield age 90 of Republic died on January 4, 2016 at home in Republic. He was born December 15, 1925 in Grand Rapids, MN to parents Wilbert M. Hatfield Sr. and Ethel M. Atkinson. Wilbert (Bill) grew up in Spring Lake, where he had his first job as a trapper for the state of Minnesota, trapping ground hogs at 8 years old. When he was 9, his family moved to Florida where he worked on his fatherʼs chicken farm. Later he became a cowboy breaking horses and worked on a dairy. At 16, he started work in the shipyard working as a high scaler. There he married his welder, Imogene Hughes, in 1944. Bill then joined the Army and served both in Europe and the Pacific. After the war he moved to Addy, Washington where he raised cattle. He later moved to Grand Coulee where he worked on Grand Coulee Dam. He also worked on the Sherman Pass Highway. Bill also worked in two gold mines and two sawmills. He retired from the Knob Hill Mine, and then went to work as a carpenter. Bill retired again and started doodling cartoons for the Republic Newsminer.
Bill is survived by his children, Billie M. VanSlyke and James Boyd Hatfield; his sister, Ruth Janet Peterson, eight grandchildren and nine great grandchildren.
He was preceded in death by his parents, wife Cora Imogene Hughes, and sister Alice May Davis
A Military Funeral Service will be held on Saturday, January 23, 2016 11:00 AM at the Bergh Chapel in Republic with interment at the Republic Cemetery.

Thomas John HAVLICEK

[I110067]
Thomas John Havlicek, 73, died Thursday, January 10 at his home in Minong, WI. He was born March 17, 1945 to George and Mary (Hady) Havlicek in Watertown, WI, attended St. Henryʼs School and graduated from Watertown High School in 1963. He attended Waukesha Technical College and graduated in 1971 as a journeyman electrician.
He married Linda Reimer on January 7, 1967 in Watertown, WI. In 1975, they moved to Rice Lake, WI where they spent the next 30 years raising their children. Tom operated the Bungalow Bar and over the years promoted many activities on the lake including snowmobile racing, a softball tournament and stock car ice racing.
In 2004, they sold the bar and moved to Minong, WI, where Tom went to work for a wood stove company and later owned Wood Stoves & More. Tom loved the outdoors, fishing, hunting and watching birds. He prided himself on his ability to identify many bird species just by hearing their song. He was an avid Packer, Badger and Brewer fan.
Over the years, Tom served as President of the Watertown Conservation Club, President of the Barron County Tavern League, Trustee at Our Lady of Lourdes Catholic Church, and Supervisor on the Minong Town Board.
Playing music was his soul. He played in many bands over the years from The Ramchargers and The Wildwoods in Watertown, to The Bungalow Express, Revision, and Golden Rocker in Rice Lake and finally Second Time Around Band in Minong.
He is survived by his wife, Linda, of 52 years, 2 sons Michael (Patti) of Sun Prairie, WI and James (Lisa) of Green Bay, WI and a daughter Karen (Mike) Helgeson of Green Bay, WI; 4 grandsons: Ben, Alex, Jack and Ryan; 4 sisters: Helen (Bob) Smith of Altoona, WI, Jeanne (David) Auerbach of Napa, CA, Geralyn Havlicek of Richmond, CA, Barb Brueggen (Bob Fanning) of Cameron, WI and many nieces and nephews. He was preceded in death by his parents, a sister and brother-in-law, Mary & Walt Miner.
