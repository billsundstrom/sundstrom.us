Vivian Lee Speegle Box, 58 died Thursday, April 14, 2016, at her home in South Bexar County where she and her husband Robert raised three children and have enjoyed five grandchildren among the pine trees and wildflowers that she had tended for over 37 years. Vivian received a paralegal degree from San Antonio College and worked for several years in downtown law firms. She homeschooled her three children while also taking care of her mother. While blessed with a tender heart, Vivian was no-nonsense when it came to behavior in public and the occasional copperhead that met up with her garden hoe. She enjoyed unearthing vintage treasures from thrift shops, pickup softball games in the backyard, the music of Michael Buble, and baking. We will miss her biscuits, breads, and pies.
Vivian and Robert Box are longtime members of Community Baptist Church, Elmendorf, TX. Clayton Blanchard SPENCER

[I090848]
CLAYTON BLANCHARD SPENCER 1937-2016 A Taft Legacy Clayton Blanchard Spencer, 79, died at midnight December 9th, 2016 at his home with family beside him following a long battle with cancer. He leaves his wife of 36 years, Susan (Fischer) Spencer; his daughter Jane; his son, Jonathan; his son Oliver; his daughter Jennie; his sisters, Carolyn Means, Ginny Giddens, Franny Means; five grandchildren, and many close friends. Born in Hartford and raised in West Hartford, the son of Clayton Burr Spencer and Francis Means, he was a lifelong resident of Litchfield as well as Watertown, and the Fenwick community in Old Saybrook. He attended the Kingswood-Oxford school and graduated from the Taft School. He attended Yale University, graduating in 1960 with a Bachelors of Arts in History. Years later he completed his Masters in History from Trinity College. Following Yale, he was commissioned an Ensign in the US Navy where he served as a surface warfare officer in the Pacific. Following his Naval service, he briefly worked at the Hartford Bank & Trust until he went to work at the Taft School teaching History and serving in the Admissions Office. Known by all as Chip, he was selected at a very young age to be the headmaster of the all boys school, McTernans in Waterbury, where he oversaw the merger with the all girls school, St Margaretʼs, now known as the Chase Collegiate School. After 7 years as headmaster, he left to work for Billings & Co, a subsidiary of Advest for many years. In 1994, he returned back to the Taft School as the Director of Planned Giving where he remained for over 10 years leaving a lasting legacy to the school. He loved and cherished his work at Taft having developed long and lasting friendships with the faculty, staff as well as countless numbers of donors and contributors. He never considered fundraising to be a job as much as a call to service for the school he loved, attended, and his children attended. Over the years, Chipʼs fundraising expertise and reputation led him to be called upon as an advisor and consultant by many organizations. Clayton met his first wife, Letitia Butler, while at Yale and was married for 14 years until her early death. He married Susan Fischer of Litchfield and enjoyed a long life at their home in Litchfield and summer residence in Fenwick. They spent many hours passionately toiling in the gardens and working the land he loved. He served on the boards of St Michaelʼs parish, the Litchfield Historical Society, the Litchfield Montessori School, Litchfield Preservation Trust, and the Lynde Point Land Trust. An avid sailor, skier, golfer, tennis & paddle tennis player, he was a longtime member of the Litchfield Country Club, the Dauntless Club, of Essex, and the Sanctum Club of Litchfield. A memorial service will be held on December 17th at 11am at St Michaelʼs Parish in Litchfield. A separate gravesite service will be held in the spring in Old Saybrook. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to St Michaelʼs parish, the Litchfield Historical Society, or the Taft School.

John Edward Spiess John Edward Spiess, 90, a longtime resident of Naples, Fla., died May 8, 2011, in Bonita Springs, Fla. He was born May 10, 1920, in Elgin, Ill., the son of Joseph and Edna Spiess. John grew up in Elgin, graduating from the Elgin Academy, where he was on the track and field and football teams. He received a bachelor of science degree in commerce from Northwestern University. After graduation, John worked at Joseph C. Spiess Company in Elgin as a buyer and merchandise manager. He rose to the position of president and CEO of the Joseph C. Spiess Company. John was active in the community, serving as president of the Illinois Retail Association and Elgin Chamber of Commerce, and as a board member of the First National Bank of Elgin and Elgin Community Chest of United Way. In 1949, he married his wife, Priscilla, and together they raised four children. John and Priscilla were longtime members of the Church of the Redeemer and Elgin Country Club. John along with his wife, were summer residents of Minocqua, Wis., and belonged to the Minocqua Country Club. John and Priscilla became residents of Naples, Fla., and were members of the Moorings Country Club in 1978. Survivors include three daughters, Anne (Neil) Levy of Los Angles, Calif., Mary (Douglas) Smith of Zionsville, Ind. and Joan (James) Julow of Bonita Springs, Fla.; as well as eight grandchildren, Sarah and Hannah Levy, Bradley (Jacque), Geoffrey (Meghan) and Emily Smith, and John (Jenny), Joshua and Jaclyn Julow. He was preceded in death by his parents; his wife, Priscilla, with whom he shared 49 years of happy marriage; and their daughter, Sally West. A private burial service will be held at Bluff City Cemetery in Elgin with the Rev. Mary Harriss, Deacon Emeritus officiating. Laird Funeral Home is handling the burial arrangements. Memorials may be sent to the Church of the Redeemer, 40 Center St., Elgin, Ill.
