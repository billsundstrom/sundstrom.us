Lee Everet SHORT

[I089365]
Lee Everet Short 1982 - 2008 Lee Everet Short, 26, beloved brother, son, fiancé and friend to all who knew him, passed away on Oct. 12th, 2008 in a tragic car accident in Salt Lake City.Lee was born on April 4th, 1982 in Port Angeles, Washington to Karen Rose Kremkau and George Lawerence Short. Karen and George were married in 1975 at the Westvale Presbyterian Church in West Valley City, Utah. As a young boy Lee was involved in athletics and enjoyed the outdoors with his dad Larry and spending time with his mom Karen going to the zoo, the park and just being an adventurous kid. At the tender age of 16 Lee was incarcerated for nine years in Washington State. Wrong place, wrong time, wrong group of friends. While locked up, Lee developed his God-given talent as an artist, sketching stunning works from nothing more than a golf pencil. Most young boys hitting hard time at that age would relinquish themselves to a life tainted with criminal actions and a hardened psyche. Lee, however, did not. He re-entered society in 2007 with his "head held high and his dignity and integrity in tact" Lee defied the odds stacked against him and successfully put his life back together, working several jobs as a video production assistant, carpenter and tattoo artist. He developed friendships quickly and with his shining personality, became a favorite with everyone to light up a room. Lee's spirit was a magnet for children as he always made time to put smiles on their faces. Lee started a family in 2007, engaged to marry his high school sweetheart Michelle Pippin and become a father to her two sweet daughters. Lee, your unwavering spirit, love of family and ability to always find happiness in the simplest things will never be forgotten. We all love you, and will miss your huge smile and enormous laugh forever. Lee is survived by mother Karen Rose Kremkau, brother Thomas Calvin Zdunich, grandmother Alyce Mae Kremkau, aunt Kristine Mae Kremkau, great aunt Francis Rubliotta, cousins Brittany Lee Hanson and Brooke Lindsay Rogers, fiancé Michelle Lynn Pippin and her daughters Namoi and Makenzie.
Salt Lake Tribune, 19 October 2008

Ralph Edward SHORT

[I083594]
Ralph Edward Short, 77, of Merrifield Drive, died peacefully, Tuesday, Oct. 6, 2009 at the Southern Maine Medical Center, following a courageous battle with cancer. Ralph was born May 21, 1932 in Cambridge, a son of Dr. Ralph W. and E. Roselle (Fuller) Short. He graduated from Wellesley High School and Wesleyan University in Middletown, Conn. Ralph went on to earn his Masters degrees from Andover Newton Theological School in Newton Center, and from James Madison University in Harrisonburg, Va. Ralph served his country in the U.S. Army first for three years in Germany, and then for four years, starting in 1966 as a military chaplain including a tour in Vietnam, where he was awarded the Bronze Star Medal. On June 12, 1965 he married June Carolyn Fagg. As an educator, Ralph taught for 25 years at schools in Virginia and Missouri. He was headmaster and teacher of English at Staunton Military Academy. He was headmaster of the junior division and instructor of the high school and junior college as well as Academy Chaplain at Wentworth Military Academy. His teaching included English, Ethics, and Religion. Ralph was a former member of the Belmont Lions Club, belonged to the Sons of the American Revolution, and was a Mason. He was a member of the South Congregational Church in Kennebunkport, where he sang in the choir for many years. He also shared his musical talents performing with the Seaglass Choral, and the "Senior Moments" singers. During his retirement years, he volunteered as a chaplain at Maine Medical Center, Portland. Ralph is survived by his loving wife of 44 years, June; his son, Major David S. Short and his wife Lisa; his brother, William Short and his wife Janet of Concord, N.H.; his sister-in-law, Priscilla Caswell of West Paris, Maine; and four nephews and one niece. ARRANGEMENTS: Calling hours will be held on Friday, Oct. 16 from 5 to 7 p.m. at Bibber Memorial Chapel, 67 Summer St., Kennebunk, Maine. A memorial service will be held at 11 a.m. on Saturday, Oct. 17 at South Congregational Church on Temple Street in Kennebunkport. Reception to follow. A graveside service, for family members, conducted by Rev. Karen Fritz, will be held at Mt. Auburn Cemetery in Cambridge. Family flowers only. Memorial donations may be made to. Protestant Chaplains, Pastoral Services Department Maine Medical Center, 22 Bramhall St., Portland, ME 04102-3175; or to the charity of one's choice.
