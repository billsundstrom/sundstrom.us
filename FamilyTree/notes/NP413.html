Joseph Benjamin PARGIN

[I094269]
Joseph Benjamin Pargin, Jr. "Joey" Joseph "Joey" Pargin, 62 of Austin, passed away on July 6, 2007, after a long battle with cancer. Joey was born in Temple, Texas, on December 7, 1944, to Sibyl McGlothing Pargin and Joseph Benjamin Pargin. He is preceded in death by his father, Ben and his son, Preston. Joey grew up in Temple, Texas, and graduated from Temple High School in 1963 where he co-captained the Temple Wildcat basketball team. He was "Mr. Temple High" and a member of the National Honor Society. Joey was baptized and attended Temple Methodist Church. He attended Temple Jr. College and the University of Texas, where he earned a degree in Business Administration in 1970. He and wife Emma "Dee Dee" moved to Austin in 1998. Joey served in the Texas Air National Guard and earned his jump wings during his service as well as becoming an expert marksman. He was a self-employed, successful business man, selling office furniture throughout Texas with partner Joe Murphy of Dallas. Joey is survived by his wife, Dee Dee Pargin; son, Joey Pargin and wife, Kimberly of Austin; son, Russell Pargin and wife, Diane of Austin; mother, Sibyl Pargin, of Temple; brother, Scott Pargin and wife Margaret of Austin; grandchildren, Kaleigh, Presley and Nicholas; niece, Emily Pargin; nephew, Benjamin Pargin; and step-sons, Steven and Michael Ortiz. Everyone whose life was touched by Joey never forgot his loving manner and depth of character. He loved and excelled in sports all of his life as witnessed by his many close golfing partners who appreciated Joey's sharp wit and talent. Along with golfing, he was an avid fisherman and this love brought friends and family together on many occasions. He particularly enjoyed fishing on the Texas coast with his son, Russell and on Lakes Belton and Travis, where he earned the nickname "Crappie Joe." The family would like to thank the doctors and staff of Seton Medical Center in ICU and 7th floor for his care and support to family members
Austin American-Statesman, 8 July 2007

Daniel Clay PARKER

[I039294]
Daniel Clay Parker, former East County Resident, died on February 8, 2013 in Vancouver. He was 68 years of age.
Daniel was born on April 28, 1944 in Portland, Oregon and was the son of Chester and Edith (Kindred) Parker. He graduated from Gresham High School in 1962 and later attended college. In 1965, he enlisted in the United States Navy and served during the Vietnam War. He received an honorable discharge in 1970 and was awarded the National Defense Service Medal and the Vietnam Service Medal.
On May 15, 1966, Daniel married Kathleen Linton at the United Methodist Church in Gresham. He started working for GTE (Verizon) in the 1970's as a Lineman in Gresham and in the 1980's he accepted a Supervisor position in Aloha. He retired from Verizon in 2002 and moved to LaPine.
He was a member of the American Legion and enjoyed hunting, fishing and playing cards with friends. He also enjoyed telling stories and jokes, playing golf and playing ball with his dog. His greatest love was spending time with his children and grandchildren.
He is survived by his wife, Kathy of LaPine, children, Tony Parker of Gresham, Michelle (husband, Ken) King of Gresham, Chet (wife, Heather) Parker of Otis and Danelle (husband, Jay) Spencer of Battleground, brother, Bob (wife, Dottie) Parke of Gresham and sisters, Bev Shaffer of St. Helens and Linda Huffer of Estacada along with 9 grandchildren, Megan Parker, John King, Austin Parker, William Parker, Katie King, Michael Parker, Lillian Parker, Kameron Spencer and Daniel Spencer.
A Celebration of Daniel's life will be on Sunday, February 17, 2013 at 1:00 pm at the Moose Lodge, 16411 NE Halsey Portland, OR 97230 . Visitation will be on Thursday, February 14, 2013 from 10:00 am - 5:00 pm at Bateman Carroll. Contributions may be made in memory of Daniel to the Peace Health Southwest Medical Center Foundation, PO Box 1600, Vancouver, WA 98668.

Julius Joel PARKER

[I061080]
Julius J. Parke r died Thursday evening, Jan. S, 1914, at a few minutes after six o'clock at his home, 377 East Main street, Fredonia.
In the death of Mr. Parker the village loses one of its most prominent and sterling citizens. Born in Fredonia February 9, 1S3S, Mr. Parker was a son of Joel R. and Levina Scott Parker, his father being a pioneer in the seed business.
Julius J. Parke r was twice married, his first wife being Miss Mary Wheelock, whom he married in 1860. She was a daughter of Rev. Alonzo Wheelock, for many years pastor of the Fredonia Baptist church. Four children blessed this union, three daughters and a son, the latter, Joel Wheelock Parker, dying 15 years ago. The surviving daughters are Mrs. Helen Calkins of Seattle; Mrs. Donald Miles of Pioneer, Iowa; and Mrs. Edward Carpenter of Chittenango, N.Y.
Mr. Parker's second wife was Miss Lucy H. Tremaine, who survives him, whom he married in 1880. She is a sister of the late G. M. Tremaine of Fredonia.
Mr. Parker was a charter member of Fredonia Grange and his services in organizing and work in behalf of the organization were so highly appreciated that a few years ago he was elected an honorary member. He was a member of Fredonia Lodge of Masons and has always been active in temperance work.
In the death of Mr. Parker Chautauqua County loses a good citizen an able market gardener and horticulturist, and the family has the sympathy of all who know them. The funeral was held Monday afternoon at. 2 o'clock at his late residence. Burial in Forest Hill.
The funeral service was conducted by Rev. M. J. Winchester, pastor of the Baptist church, and Rev. B. R. Larrabee, pastor, of the Presbyterian church, in connection with the Masonic service of Fredonia Lodge, F. & A. M. Many attended, although the day was inclement with a severe blizzard. Mr. Parker is survived by his twin sister, Mrs. Julia Clark of Dunkirk. His daughter, Mrs. Calkins, arrived from, Seattle two weeks ago and was with her father in his last hours. He had been gaining, but had a relapse the night before he died. Mr. Parker was an old citizen who will be generally missed.
The Fredonia Censor, 14 January 1914
