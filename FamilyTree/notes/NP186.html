John V. FOSTER

[I044968]
John V. Foster, 76, of Salem, husband of Alice (Ford) Foster, died October 24, 2007, following an illness of several weeks. He and his wife had celebrated 57 years of marriage on October 15, 2007.
Born in Stoughton, the son of the late John and Rose (Iervilino)
Foster, he was raised in South Boston.
After completing his education at South Boston High School, he served in the Army.
Shortly after his discharge from the Army, Mr. Foster married his wife, and they began a family. A mechanical engineer and sheet metal inspector, Mr. Foster was employed in the space industry for NASA, manufacturing components for rockets. He was later employed by Fabro Manufacturing and retired from D. J. Fabricators.
Mr. Foster enjoyed building and flying model airplanes and helicopters, a passion he shared with his family on weekends at the Sylvania parking lot in Danvers for many years. He had also enjoyed hunting and bass fishing and was very happy puttering around his home and being with his family, grandchildren and great-grandchildren who affectionately call him "Da."
In addition to his wife, Mr. Foster is survived by two daughters, Kathleen and her husband, Thomas Camire and Cherylann Foster, all of Salem; seven grandchildren, Tracey Goode, John Camire, twins Shan and Sean Foster, Joshua, Joanell and Andrew McCarthy; two great-grandchildren, Jeremy and Alyssa Goode; a brother, Francis and his wife, Barbara Foster, of Maine; and many nieces and nephews. He was also the father of the late Alice McCarthy.
His funeral was held October 29 from the Murphy Funeral Home of Salem, followed by a funeral Mass celebrated in St. Anne Church, Salem. Interment was in Greenlawn Cemetery, Salem.
To sign the online guest book, visit www.MurphyFuneral-Home.com.
Salem Gazette, 2 November 2007

Julia FOSTER

[I085656]
Born in Greenville, SC,to Abiel A.Foster and Julia Worthington Foster, a twin to Lewis Worthington Foster...was a teacher, until marriage to John A.Leathers. Died at age 71.
Per obit in The Spartanburg Herald, Spartanburg,SC, Mrs. Julia Foster Leathers, 71, of 176 Avant St. died at 6:00 yesterday morning at Spartanburg General Hospital. Mrs.Leathers was born in Greenville,a daughter of Abiel A. and Julia Worthington Foster.
She moved to Spartanburg in early childhood, where she went to Miss Gamewell's School, later attending Converse College. She was a member of the class of 1893, the first class graduating from Converse. Mrs. Leathers taught school in Abbeville and Greenwood, later returning to Spartanburg where she was secretary to Dr. Robert P. Pell, President of Converse College, for a number of years. In 1922 she was married to John A.Leathers, Spartanburg Cotton Broker, who died in 1941. She was a life-long member of the First Presbyterian Church of Spartanburg.
Mrs. Leathers had been in declining health for several years and suffered a stroke the latter part of last week.
Surviving are two step-sons, L. Hudson Leathers of Orange, N.J. and John A. Leathers,Jr. of Spartanburg,SC; two sisters, Miss Edith Foster, who is doing missonary work in Brazil, and Mrs. Albert Fant of Belton; one brother, Alfred Foster of Belton, and several nieces and nephews.
Funeral services will be held at J.F.Floyd mortuary this morning At 11:00 with Dr. A.W.Dick, pastor of the First Presbyterian Church officiating, followed by burial in Oakwood Cemetery.
Active pallbearers will be John Bell, Dr.L.M.Kennedy, Dr. E.B.Hammond, James McCutcheon, John Cantrell and Howard Carlisle, Jr.

PEORIA - Mary Irma "Aunt Mary" Foster Hiner, formerly of Peoria, passed away surrounded by family in Greenville, Texas, on Saturday, Sept. 5, 2015, at the age of 100.
She was only 23 days from her 101st birthday. The official cause of death is end stage Alzheimer's. She passed away eight days after breaking her hip in a fall.
Aunt Mary is survived by her niece, Joyce Scott of Greenville, Texas, with whom she resided the last few years; nephew, Tom Foster of Marietta, Ga.; niece, Dianna Ogburn of Grafton, Wis.; and several great- and great-great- nieces and nephews.
She was preceded in death by her mother and father, Mary Charlotte "Lottie" Kapp Foster and James Wesley "Wes" Foster of Kingston Mines, Ill.; brother, Lloyd Foster of Chillicothe, Ill.; and husband, Jacob "Jake" Hiner of Pekin, Ill.
Mary was born on Sept. 28, 1914, in Kingston Mines, Ill., to James Wesley and Mary Charlotte "Lottie" Foster.
She graduated from Manual High School in Peoria, Ill., in 1933, and married Jake Hiner on March 8, 1941.
After moving to Peoria, Ill., she began working for Bemis Brothers Bag Company for 12 years and was the original cover girl for the company publication featuring her work driving a fork lift in the Shipping Department (1946), where she transferred from Inspections.
