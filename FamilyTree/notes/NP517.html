Linda STEELE

[I038288]
LINDA CAROLE STEELE Linda Carole Steele, 61, of Hypoluxo, wife of Michael Hanrahan, died at her home on Friday morning April 7, after a long illness. Her husband and sons were at her side. Linda was born in Lexington, Ma., Sept. 22, 1944, the daughter of Gordon and Blanche Steele. She graduated Ithaca College (N.Y.) as a music major. She earned her Masters in Liberal Arts at SUNY Stony Brook, NY. Her 29 years as a music teacher led her through Massachusetts, Florida, Georgia and finally to Bay Shore, Long Island, N.Y. where she taught at Gardiner Manor School for 24 years. She was a member of the Suffolk County Music Educators Assn. (SCMEA) and the Music Educators National Conference (MENC). Linda taught continuing education piano courses, gave private music lessons and performed professionally in various L.I. restaurants including the Garden City Hotel. She retired from teaching in 1999 and moved from Brightwaters, L.I. to Palm Beach County where she married her joy, her confidant and loving husband Michael, formerly of Kings Park, L.I. She is survived by her husband; a daughter, Leah Basciano of Lake Worth, and her sons Vincent and Dean Basciano of Long Island. She is also survived by four sisters; Bonnie Clark of Plano, TX; Susan Weems of Brunswick, ME; Jane Milchen of Nashua, NH. and Virginia O'Conner of Anaheim, CA. She leaves many close teaching friends on Long Island and a special group of friends she gathered in South Florida. Linda's family and friends will dearly miss her thoughtful, compassionate and loving spirit, exhibited by a smile that shone light and radiance through her presence. All her knew her, believed her to be a special woman. A memorial service will be held at the Boynton Memorial Chapel, 800 West Boynton Beach Blvd. At 2 p.m., Tuesday, April 11. Following the service, a reception will be held at the Hanrahan home in Hypoluxo. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made in Linda's name to the Palm Beach Habilitation Center in Lake Worth. Lorene Pearl STEESE

[I105045]
Long time Polson resident Lorene P. Farrell passed away on May 28, 2009 at her home in Polson, while surrounded by members of her loving family. Lorene was born February 9, 1915 in Minneapolis, Minnesota to Fred William Steese Jr. and Ethel (Miller) Steese. When Lorene was 2 years old, her mother relocated the family to Polson following the accidental death of Lorene's father. Lorene attended grade and high school in Polson, graduating in 1933. Following high school she worked for Doctor P.C. Noble and during this period sang in many local choir groups. On August 8, 1940, she married Thomas A. Farrell, a high school classmate, in Polson. The couple lived at Kerr Dam, where Tom was employed, until 1942 when Tom enlisted in the U.S. Army. During the time her husband was overseas in the Philippines, she lived with her uncle, Claude Miller in Los Angeles, CA where her first son was born. At the conclusion of WW II, Lorene and Tom returned to Kerr Dam where Tom was once again employed. Lorene went to work for Dr. Tanglen in Polson. She continued singing in Polson city choirs and sang in the summer Show Boat productions. In 1964, Lorene went to work for Sheneman's Clothing Store (First Resort) on Main Street in Polson. She continued working there until the early 1980's when she retired. Lorene has been an active member of the Polson Christian Church since the 1920's sang in the church choir and participated in numerous church activities. She also did volunteer work at the Polson Country Store. She loved working in her garden, cultivating beautiful roses and caring for all of her cats. Husband Tom preceded Lorene in death in 1985. She is survived by brother Harold Steese of San Diego, CA: sister Winifred Scott of San Lorenzo, CA: sons Thomas J. Farrell (Dianne) of Polson: Richard L. Farrell (Sharon) of Boise, and daughter Carol Jo Farrell of Polson. Four grandsons and four great grand children also survive her. She was a dedicated and nurturing mother, who was very proud of her family and received an abundance of blessings in return. In lieu of flowers, memorials on behalf of Lorene may be made to the Heritage of Faith Christian Church (John Payne, pastor), the Polson Humane Society or the Polson Senior Citizens Center. A memorial service for Lorene will be held at the Heritage of Faith Christian Church on June 13, 2009

Wynafred Scott of San Lorenzo Jan. 23, 1917 - Oct. 13 2010
Wynafred Scott, known to her family as "Dede", at age 93, passed away peacefully Wednesday morning of natural causes. She was a long time resident of San Lorenzo. Wynafred was preceded in death by her loving husband Anthony James Scott Jr. She is survived by her children Marilyn Camp, Dennis Scott, Peggy Oxenford, Sue Olson and Anne Woosley. She left behind 10 grandchildren, 5 great grandchildren and her brother Harold Steese who is 97 years old. Wynafred was born in Chippewa Falls, Wisconsin and as a small child moved to Polson, Montana. As a young woman she attended Dillon Normal School where she received her teaching credential. She taught third grade in Polson, Montana during World War II. In 1941 Wynafred moved to Los Angeles where she met Anthony. Anthony and Wynafred were married in a little church in Montabello on December 5, 1943. When the war ended Wynafred and her husband moved to Oakland, California and then settled in San Lorenzo in 1955 where she remained until she moved to Rosegate Assisted Living to spend her final years. Wynafred was a Girl Scout and Campfire leader and active in the PTA while her children were in school. She was a member of the "Mother Singers of San Lorenzo" for several years. She took care of neighborhood children and taught in a community nursery school for a short time. She had a life long love of music and children. She enjoyed singing and playing the piano for her children and grandchildren. Wynafred had a personal relationship with Jesus Christ and read the Bible daily. There will be a private family memorial on Saturday at Santos and Robinson Mortuary in San Leandro. Interment will be at Holy Sepulcher in Hayward directly after.
East Bay Times, 15 October 2010

Robert Frederick Steffenhagen, 88, of Red Wing, died Sunday, July 15, 2012 at the Seminary Home.
He was born May 26, 1924 in Red Wing, to Charles and Lillian (Bremer) Steffenhagen. He grew up in Frontenac on the Hill Avenue family farm and attended country school. On December 22, 1943, he married Dorothy DeRoy at St. John's Lutheran Church, Frontenac and they farmed on Hill Avenue. During the 1950's he helped out at the turkey farm in Frontenac and he also worked at Durkee Atwood for 30 years, retiring in 1989. In 1986, he and Dorothy moved to Red Wing. In their younger years, they enjoyed dancing, playing cards and listening to old time music. Dorothy died in 1990. Growing up on a farm, Robert grew to love horses and tractors, something that he enjoyed his entire life. As he grew older, he could often be found with the remote in his hand, enjoying a television show--especially if it was a western.
He is survived by his children, Darrell (Carole) Steffenhagen of Red Wing, Barbara (Myron) Hanson of Eagan, Norma (Michael) Nemeth of Red Wing, Linda (Jerry) McNamara of Pine Island and Debra Steffenhagen of Windsor, Calif.; 13 grandchildren and 6 great-grandchildren.
