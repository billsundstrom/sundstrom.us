T. Dean STUCKEY

[I107667]
T. Dean Stuckey, 84, longtime Lexington resident, died Wednesday, Feb. 28, 2007, at Bryan LGH East Hospital in Lincoln. Visitation was Friday, March 2, 2007, at the Reynolds-Love Chapel in Lexington. Funeral services will be Saturday, March 3, 2007, at 10:30 a.m., at First Presbyterian Church in Lexington, with the Rev. Caroline Vickery officiating. Burial with military honors will be in the Greenwood Cemetery at Lexington. Reynolds-Love Funeral Home in Lexington is in charge of arrangements.
Survivors include his wife of 63 years, Betty Stuckey of Lexington; two sons, Tom Stuckey Jr. of Omaha, and Ed (Sally) Stuckey of Appleton, Wis.; two daughters, Diana (Richard) Pitcher of Grand Island, and Dixie (John) Bednar of Omaha; one brother, David Stuckey of St. Petersburg Beach, Fla.; one sister, Helen Barrett of Lexington; nine grandchildren, Corrie (Don) Wesley, Melinda Westerlin, Joe Westerlin, Andy Pitcher, Ryan (Kelley) Pitcher, Katie (Chris) Lyon, Rachel Stuckey, Ben Stuckey and Allyson Stuckey; and five great-grandchildren, Alli, Dillon, Casey, Nic and CJ.
He was preceded in death by his parents; three brothers, Jack, Jim, and Bob; and one sister, Ruth.
Dean was born Dec. 9, 1922, to Ed and Gertrude (Memmott) Stuckey at Lexington, and lived there until his death with the exception of the time he spent in the United States Navy. He graduated from Lexington High School in 1941. He was inducted into the United States Navy in May of 1944, and served on a PC-603 Patrol Craft as a Gunner's Mate 3rd Class.
Dean was united in marriage to Betty Fellers on Feb. 4, 1944, at Lexington. The couple recently celebrated their 63rd wedding anniversary. To this union four children were born, Diana, Tom, Dixie and Ed.
Dean was involved in farming and ranching all of his life. He was the head cattle buyer for 30 years for Cornland Dressed Beef in Lexington. Throughout his lifetime he was awarded several honors. He was chosen Young Farmer of the year in 1958. In 1963, he was awarded the Omaha World Herald's Sportsman of the year. He and his wife Betty were elected commander and president, respectively of the Veterans of Foreign Wars and its auxiliary April 4, 1950, the year the Lexington Veterans Club was built. That same year he coached a group of young boys who won a marble tournament. He was involved as a coach with American Legion Baseball and Basketball for many years.

STURK, Edmund Belford - 86, Morristown, Kings Co., passed away on Saturday, March 31, 2012, in the Valley Regional Hospital, Kentville, surrounded by family. Born on July 25, 1925, in Morristown, he was the elder son of the late Alton R. and Charlotte E. (Saunders) Sturk.
Edmund was a veteran of the Second World War, serving in the RCAF as a flight engineer. He was a member of the Royal Canadian Legion, Ortona Branch No. 69, Berwick. Edmund had worked several years at Formex in Kentville, where he became a member of the Turtle Club after a hard hat saved his life. Edmund owned and operated Mapleglade Farm in Morristown, a fourth generation mixed family farm. He was a member of the Nova Scotia Fruit Growers Association and the Apple Hall of Fame. Edmund took pleasure in tapping trees to make maple syrup and he worked in the woods into his early 80s. He loved Sunday drives, looking at machinery and farm equipment, reading, and meeting people. The family hosted hundreds of guests through the Nova Scotia Farm & Country Vacations program and their B&B. Edmund enjoyed introducing city kids and their parents to milking cows and tractor rides.

James Luther SUDDARTH

[I097666]
James Luther Suddarth went to be with his Savior, Jesus on November 25, 2012 at the age of 80. Jim was born on February 6, 1932 in Chickasha, Oklahoma to Luther James "Bill" Suddarth and Beulous "Penny" Suddarth. While attending Oklahoma A & M (now Oklahoma State University) in Stillwater, Jim served as an intern at Boeing in Seattle for two summers. Upon graduation, he accepted an offer to work for them as an Aeronautical Engineer where he stayed until his retirement in 1992, 37 years later.
Jim loved music, photography, airplanes and camping. He played both the accordion and the organ and many Sunday mornings, the sound of classical hymns from the organ would be heard prior to the family going to church. He was known by all for his photography-both beautiful scenic shots and pictures and films of friends and family. No one was safe from his camera-especially when eating! And then there were the planes . . . A childhood passion, he managed to turn it into a career that he loved-staying at the same company from right out of college until his retirement. Air shows were a common family outing, along with feeding the ducks at Lake Washington while watching the planes take off and land. It probably wasnʼt an accident that he lived five minutes from a major airport. What some considered noise, he considered "music to the ears."
Jim loved the beauty of the outdoors and was determined to expose his family to it as much as possible. Hiking in the Pacific Northwest, visiting Yellowstone and the Grand Canyon; looking for agates along the Pacific Coast, hunting for arrowheads in the New Mexico dessert, laying on top of the dunes at White Sands star gazing-all memories that he gave to his family. Many summers were spent camping at Banks Lake, waterskiing and enjoying family and friends. Jim had a ʻwicked" sense of humor which his children didnʼt necessarily appreciate, but his grandchildren certainly did and apparently inherited the trait.
The most important things in Jimʼs life, though, were his family and friends and his faith. They were intricately intertwined with each other. Jim lived his faith and his knowledge of Godʼs Word was the foundation of everything he did and said. He was a man of deep commitment, integrity and honor.
Jim is survived by his loving wife of 56 years, Carol; his children, Kathy Klemz (Craig), Judy Goodanetz, Sharon Carter (David); his ten grandchildren and numerous nieces and nephews. He will be deeply missed, but we know that this separation is only temporary. He is at home with Jesus, completely whole and at peace.
A Memorial Service will be held on Sunday, December 30, 2012 at 2:00 pm at Our Saviorʼs Baptist Church in Federal Way, Washington.

Robert George SUGGETT

[I094926]
LAKELAND - Robert "Junior" G. Suggett died Sept. 22, 2008, at the James A. Haley VA Hospital, Tampa, FL. from organ failure.
He was born Feb. 6, 1924 in Dunkirk, New York, the son of Robert G. Suggett and Elma (Holcomb) Suggett . He moved to Lakeland, FL in 1958.
He was predeceased by his wife Cecelia Ann Parker; son, Larry Suggett ; grandson, Paul Suggett ; and granddaughter, Melissa Suggett . He is survived by his children: Richard Suggett (Arenda), Davenport, FL, Clayton Suggett (Libby), Lakeland, FL, Sharon Styles (Tommy), Albany, GA, Edwin Suggett , Wauchula, FL and Wade Suggett (Letha), Lakeland, FL; sister, Alice Straight, Fredonia, NY; seven grandchildren; twelve great-grandchildren; several nieces and nephews.
Mr. Suggett was a first class gunners mate and served on twenty-one Atlantic patrol ships and nine merchantmen in Armed Guard duty. Five of those ships were sunk by submarines and mines. He was blown off a ship into the sea off Dakar. He was awarded the Silver and Bronze Stars for rescuing two shipmates and assisting the ship's doctor. He received the Purple Heart with two Encores. He spent eight months in a German prisoner of war camp after being shot down at Messina while a gunner on a PBY seaplane. He was then awarded the Veterans of Foreign Wars Medal with the Maltese Cross. He was one of three Navy veterans in the State of New York to receive a 'Battle Wagon' discharge in WWII, and was awarded the New York Conspicuous Service Award.
Mr. Suggett loved children and volunteered for many years with Toys for Tots. He also played Santa Claus for hospitals, schools and other groups.
Mr. Suggett was a member of First Baptist Church of Plant City. Interment will be at a later date in Versailles, New York. In lieu of flowers, memorials may be made to Disabled American Veterans Chapter 28, 303 S Veterans Ave, Lakeland, FL 33815, for support of their services to disabled veterans.
The Ledger, Lakeland, FL, 25 September 2008

Elizabeth Ann Sullivan Bern "Betsy", a longtime resident of New Rochelle, died on July 9, 2016. She was 84.
Betsy was born on February 25, 1932 in Bridgeport, CT to the late John and Ann (Larkin) Sullivan and was raised by her loving aunt Katherine Larkin.
Betsy was a teacher at Blessed Sacrament High School for over 22 years, where she was the first female member of the faculty and initiated the Senior Religion Service Project. She volunteered at Sound Shore Medical Center for over 15 years.
She is survived by her husband Raymond Bern whom she married on June 24, 1954; her daughter Cathryn Frances Bern-Smith and her husband Steven Smith; her son Paul Henry Bern and his wife Kim Parr; her granddaughter Naomi Parr Bern, her sister Rosemary Howard, her sisters-in-law Shirley Sullivan and Barbara Gordon, and her brother-in-law James Howard. She was pre-deceased by her brothers, Bob and Jack Sullivan, her sister-in-law Eleanor Sullivan and brother-in-law Gerald Gordon, her nephews Terry and Barry Sullivan and her niece Lisa Howard Spillane. She is survived by a very large extended family of cousins, nieces and nephews. Her best friend since the sixth grade, Diane Smith Drugge and husband Everett Drugge, also survive her.
Betsy was involved throughout her lifetime in many worthwhile community and church activities. At Blessed Sacrament Church, she was a member of the Parish Council when it was first formed after the Second Vatican Council, the Marian Chorale, Blessed Motherʼs Sodality, and in 1969 was the founder and director of Blessed Sacrament Folk Sounds, the first parish contemporary music group that played at the 10:00 Mass for many years.
Betsy organized a group of children at 60 and 70 Locust Avenue in the early 1960ʼs to form a choir to sing Christmas Carols throughout the two-building complex. The choir collected money and all of the proceeds were donated to the New Rochelle Campership Fund. A picture of the choir was published in the Standard Star.
She was also very involved in local Democratic politics and worked as a poll watcher for many elections. She proudly voted for Hillary Clinton in the New York State Presidential Primary.
One of her most enjoyable community activities was as a member of the cast of several musical theatre productions in the Blessed Sacrament Community Theater under the direction of Joe Martini. She played Mrs. Harcourt in Anything Goes, Sue in Bells Are Ringing, Mrs. Upton in Mame, and Mrs. Peterson in Bye Bye Birdie. Her crowning theatrical achievement was as Bloody Mary in South Pacific.
