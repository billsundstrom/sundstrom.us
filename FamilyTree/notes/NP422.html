Evelyn Nevada PERRY

[I045327]
PEABODY - Evelyn N. "Bunnie" Lynch , 74, of Peabody, died on Tuesday, March 15, 2011 at the Kaplan Family House in Danvers with her family at her side.
Bunnie was born in Boston on Jan. 7, 1937, the daughter of Grace E. (Trefy) Perry and the late Francis F. Perry . She was raised and educated in Beverly. Her family then moved to Boxford where she resided before moving to Danvers, and 12 years ago moving to Peabody.
Many families will remember Bunnie as being their private duty caregiver on the North Shore and in Florida until her retirement in 2001. She was also employed for many years as a sales clerk at Ann & Hope in Danvers. Bunnie was a loving mother who devoted her life to creating a family atmosphere for her children and all of their friends, and who later was so proud of her grandchildren and their accomplishments. She also cherished her great-grandchildren. She enjoyed her companion dog Madden, enjoyed nature, tropical fish and crocheting as hobbies.
In addition to her mother, Bunnie is survived by her three daughters, Karen M. Festo and her husband Joseph of North Andover, Kim Elizabeth Mitchell of Cotuit, and Holly A. Lynch and her companion Karl Salvato of Peabody; her beloved daughter-in-law, Mary Lynch of Brooksville, Fla.; 17 grandchildren, three great-grandchildren; her siblings and their spouses, Fred and Cheryl Perry of Brookfield, N.H., Daniel and Carol Perry of Seminole, Fla., Carol and Phil Lemire of Topsfield, and Robin and Jim Lynch of Effingham, N.H.; a close friend Joyce Tampone of Peabody; many nieces, nephews. Her son Daniel F. Lynch predeceased her.
ARRANGEMENTS: Her funeral service will be held on Friday, March 18, 2011 at 11 a.m. at the Calvary Christian Church, 47 Grove St., Lynnfield. The family will receive family and friends on Thursday, March 17, 2011 from 5 to 8 p.m. at the Conway, Cahill-Brodeur Funeral, 82 Lynn St., Peabody. The family requested that in lieu of flowers donations be made in Bunnie's memory to the North Shore Elder Services, www.nselder.org , 152 Sylvan St., Danvers, MA 01923, or to Hospice of the North Shore, www.hns.org, 75 Sylvan St., Suite B-102, Danvers, MA 01923. Scott PESCARA

[I066771]
A former Albion man faces up to 20 years in prison after he was convicted Friday of shooting a Monroe County sheriffʼs deputy after he went berserk following an argument with his wife.
Scott Pescara, 42, was found guilty of attempted aggravated assault on a police officer, second-degree assault and first-degree reckless endangerment.
Pescara was acquitted on charges of second-degree assault and attempted first-degree assault in the shooting of his neighbor, Nellie Marshall, who was hit in the shoulder from a round fired by Pescara.
According to the Democrat and Chronicle, jurors supported the claim that Pescara had no way of knowing Marshall was in the house because she was behind closed blinds.
Pescara fired more than two dozen rounds from a handgun from inside his house on Route 31A in the town of Sweden on Jan. 8, 2010. The shooting happened after an argument with his wife, Jeanine, who was not home at the time of the shooting.
One of those rounds hit Marshall. The other struck Deputy Jonathan Strong in the leg.
Pescara, who moved from Albion to Sweden about five years ago, had been free on $250,000 bail posted last fall.
The judge in his case revoked bail after the jury returned its verdict after more than a day of deliberating.
The trial lasted four weeks. Pescara was represented by John Parrinello. Assistant District Attorney Timothy Prosperi prosecuted.
Judge James J. Piampiano set sentencing for Sept. 29. Pescara faces seven to 20 years in prison.

ROCHESTER -- A father of two has been sentenced to 16 years in prison for shooting a sheriff's deputy in upstate New York while high on alcohol and drugs.
Scott Pescara was convicted last month of attempted aggravated assault on a police officer and related charges. At sentencing Friday, the 44-year-old said he was "completely remorseful."
Authorities say Pescara randomly fired dozens of rounds from his house in the Rochester suburb of Sweden in January 2010.
A woman across the street was hit in the shoulder and a responding officer, Monroe County Deputy Jonathan Strong, was wounded in the leg. Neither was seriously injured.
Pescara blamed his estranged wife for driving him to drink gin and take prescription pills. The defense argued he was too intoxicated to intentionally shoot anyone.

William D. PETERS

[I109109]
MAYVILLE - William D. Peters, 89, formerly of Mayville, died Tuesday (December 27, 2016) at Heritage Village Rehab and SN in Gerry, NY.
He was born July 27, 1927 in Conewango, NY to Mannie and Lucy Peters. Volunteered for the Merchant Marine in 1943 and served in the North Atlantic, including a trip to Mermansk, Russia. He then joined the US Navy, where he served until 1947. He was awarded the Great Patriotic War Medal by President Boris Yeltsin in 1985.
He married Jean Hammond of Little Valley, NY in 1955 and they eventually settled in Mayville. In 1956 he started at the Chautauqua County Sherriffʼs Department, where he worked as a patrolman, an investigator and as the lieutenant in charge of patrolmen, retiring in 1983. He then worked as a security officer for the Chautauqua County courts until 1998.
Bill was an expert carpenter and built two houses in Mayville, including a log house built with logs that he cut himself.
He was an avid hunter and fisherman and was active in the Boy Scouts, the Masons, the Veterans of Foreign Wars and the American Legion.
He is survived by his wife, Jean, his sons, Jeff (Terry) Peters of Pittsburgh, PA and Don (Karen) Peters of Palmer, Alaska; and his grandchildren, Emmali, Nora, Will, Ben and Asa.
He was preceded in death by his brother, Mannie Peters.
The funeral service will be held at 2:00 pm Friday in the Freay Funeral Home in Mayville. Rev. Daniel Bender will officiate. Burial will be in the Villenova Cemetery.
