FREDEGUND

[I010066]
Fredegund, or Frédégonde, (d. 597) was the Queen consort of Chilperic I, the Merovingian Frankish king of Soissons.

Originally a servant, Fredegund became Chilperic's mistress after he had murdered his wife and queen, Galswintha (c. 568). But Galswintha's sister, Brunhilda, in revenge against Chilperic, began a feud which lasted more than 40 years.

Fredegund is said to be responsible for the assassination of Sigebert I in 575 and made attempts on the lives of Guntram (her brother-in-law and the king of Burgundy), Childebert II (Sigebert's son), and Brunhilda.

After the mysterious assassination of Chilperic (584), Fredegund seized his riches and took refuge in the cathedral at Paris. Both she and her surviving son, Clotaire II, were protected by Guntram until he died in 592.

Said to be ruthlessly murderous and sadistically cruel, Fredegund perhaps has few rivals in monstrousness. And although she did not live to see it, her son's execution of Brunhilda bore the mark of Fredegund's hatred: Clotaire had the old queen, now in her sixties, stretched in agony upon the rack for three entire days, then watched her meet her death chained between four horses that were goaded to the four points of the compass, tearing her body asunder.

Thomas Reed FREEBURG

[I076094]
Thomas Reed Freeburg, age 83 and longtime resident of Plummer, ID, passed away on July 9, 2017 at his home near Plummer. Tom was born in Bloomfield, NE to Ivan and Ione (Kingsley) Freeburg on September 8, 1933. When he was three years old, the family moved to the "Stump Ranch" in Sanders, ID. They lived on a self-sustaining farm. Tom attended school at Sacred Heart Mission in DeSmet and the Tensed Elementary School. He then attended high school in Plummer, ID and graduated from Plummer High School with the class of 1951.
While in high school, he met the love of his life, Sandra Storms. They went together for three years before they were married in June of 1952. They made their home in the Plummer area where they raised their children.
Throughout Tom's lifetime, he worked for various farmers in the area. He also worked for the Plummer-Gateway Highway District for over 14 years. In 1966 Tom started his own farm and farmed until he retired in 2005. In his retirement, Tom continued to harvest hay and take care of animals.
Tom is survived by his wife Sandra of 65 years at the family home; his daughters Sue Millhorn of Plummer, ID and Molly (Kevin) Griffin of Sammamish, WA; son Mike (Paulina) Freeburg of Plummer, ID; eight grandchildren, 12 great-grandchildren; and a sister Beverly Melusky of Olympia, WA.
He is preceded in death by his parents and his brother Bill Freeburg.
A visitation was held at HODGE FUNERAL HOME in ST. MARIES, ID on Wednesday, July 12, 2017 at 5:00 pm - 8:00 pm. A Graveside service will be Thursday, July 13, 2017 at 11:00 am at Evergreen Cemetery in Plummer, ID. A gathering for family and friends to share a meal will follow at the Plummer Bible Church. Larry Raymond FREEMAN

[I040799]
Larry Raymond Freeman, 69, went to the Lord on January 8, 2016.
Larry was born on Nov. 22, 1946 in Sturgis, SD to Raymond E. and Jesse "June" Freeman. He spent his childhood in the Union Center/White Owl area and attended the local grade school. He attended high school in New Underwood, SD and joined the U.S. Army after graduation. He served his country with honor and returned home to study architecture at S.D.S.M. & T.
On April 20, 1968 he married Lindell J. Carstensen and they moved to Minnesota in 1970. They returned to Rapid City in 1971 and lived there until 1973. Larry and Lindell began their ranching life in 1973 just south of Enning, SD, where they raised two children, Heath and Kara. In 1991, the family moved to Owanka, SD. Larry was a rancher at heart but also did trucking, construction, and maintenance.
Larry was a dedicated, loving father and husband who always enjoyed teaching and coaching the community kids. He was a humble, smart, creative man who loved to tell stories and make people laugh. His determination and desire to work hard were obvious to anyone who met him. He was always willing to lend a hand and never turned down an opportunity to make a friend. His laugh, ability to fix anything, and unique culinary skills, among a million other characteristics, will be missed more than words can express. He is at peace now and resting in the hands of the Lord, for that we are thankful. He was a true blessing to his family and friends who will continue to cherish the good times, special memories, and the lessons he taught us all.
He was preceded in death by his father, Raymond E. Freeman, and his brother, Clifford J. Freeman.
Larry is survived by his wife, Lindell J. Freeman; son, Heath A. (Colleen) Freeman; daughter, Kara A. Freeman; mother, Jesse "June" Mickelson; half-brother, Wayne (Sharon) Mickelson; and his grandchildren, Avery L. Freeman and August R. Freeman.
Rapid City Journal, 12 January 2016
