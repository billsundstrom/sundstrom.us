John Daniel TRUDE

[I106103]
Services for John Daniel Trude, 73, Concordia, will be at 11 a.m. Tuesday at the Baptist Church in Concordia with the Revs. James Autrey and Rachel Hanson McFall officiating.
Masonic graveside services and commandry escort will be at Pleasant Hill Cemetery in Concordia.
Mr. Trude died Saturday, Nov. 16, 1991, at a Concordia hospital.
He was born Oct. 23, 1918, in Concordia, the son of Rachel Frances Walker and Frank Alton Trude.
Mr. Trude was a lifetime resident of the Concordia area. He worked as an owner-manager of Trude Insurance Agency before retiring in 1986.
He was a Master Mason, a member of the York Rite Chapter No. 45 RAM, Hirim Council, Concordia Council No. 42, and in 1977 became a Knight of York Cross of Honor.
He also was affiliated with many other masonic organizations including ISIS Shrine and Scottish Rite in Salina.
Mr. Trude was a member of the Concordia Kiwanis Club, the American Legion Post No. 76, Veteran of Foreign Wars Post No. 588, and Benevolent and Protective Order of Elks, all in Concordia.
He was a member of the Baptist Church in Concordia.
He married Ruth Chesebro on Jan. 7, 1943, in Concordia. She survives of the home.
Other survivors include two daughters, Katherine Sue Schumock, Lawrence, and Mary Elizabeth Hinkle, Washington; a brother, Carl Rogers Trude, Jamestown; four grandchildren; and five great-grandchildren.
He was preceded in death by a grandchild.

Sharon K. TUBBS

[I002306]
On November 10, 2019, Our Father and Grandfather Francis McElwee "Mac" was freed from Alzheimer's. Although, he will be greatly missed by many we find peace in knowing he is now free.
Mac was born on February 2, 1938, in Edgerton Wisconsin. He was an Army Reservist. Mac moved to Florida where he worked at Kennedy Space Center from 1967 to 1973. Mac worked in Communications at the Center and was on the team that launched Apollo 11, on July 16, 1969. Mac loved to tell stories of how all the communications operators would all be out watching the launches and his supervisor would order them back to work.
After his years at the space Center Mac moved to Port Charlotte where he worked at Sprint until he retired in 1998. He then dedicated many years as a volunteer at the Peace River Wildlife Center. He was well known as the Pelican Guy for his care of the Pelicans as well as much other wildlife. In addition to caring for the birds and animals he conducted educational seminars for the public to better understand about the wildlife.
Mac is survived by his devoted daughter Tracey Williams, his sons Bryan and Jeffery McElwee and his loyal caregiver and friend Robert Allen. Mac also has three Grandchildren and six Great-Grandchildren.
Mac's loving wife Sharon McElwee preceded him in death.
Sun Newspapers, 21 November 2019

[I034350]
Rocklin, Ca.- Josephine M. Apa, formerly of the Olean area, died Tuesday (December 24, 2002) in the Folson Convalescent Hospital in Folson, California.
Born December 8, 1910 in Serra San Bruno, Calabria, Italy she was the daughter of Bruno and Rose Catroppa Tucci. On April 3, 1937 in Johnsonburg, Pa. she married Ralph Anthony Apa, Sr. who predeceased her November 28, 1971.
Mrs. Apa was raised in Johnsonburg, Pa. and has been a resident of the Olean area since her marriage in 1937. She had been employed for 25 years for Acme Electric Co. in Allegany prior to her retirement in 1976.
She was very active for over 20 years as the local chairperson for the Mother's March of Dimes. She was a former member of St. John's Church in Olean and had attended St. Bonaventure Church in Allegany for the past 18 years. In 1997 she moved to Rocklin, California to reside at the home of her daughter.
Surviving are 3 sons Ralph Anthony (Terry) Apa, Jr. of Liverpool, Oh., Fred Apa of Bradford, Pa. and Michael Apa of West Chicago, Il; 1 daughter Frances Apa of Rocklin, Ca. and 1 adopted daughter Victoria Alonzo of Rocklin, Ca.; 10 grandchildren; 2 great grandchildren; 2 sisters Louise Servidea of St. Mary's Pa. and Theresa Caruso of Phoenix, Az; 1 brother Floyd (Florence) L Tucci of St. Mary's Pa. and several nieces and nephews.
In addition to her husband she was predeceased by 1 sister Mary Caruso on December 13, 2002 and 2 brothers Michael Tucci in 1997 and Peter Tucci who died in Tunisia, Africa in WWII.
