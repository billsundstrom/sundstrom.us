Ernest Near JAKWAY

[I024978]
The death of Ernest Near Jakway occurred at the Auburn City Hospital Saturday, Oct. 16, 1921, of lockjaw, after only a few days illness, which resulted from a fall sustained in his slaughter house S few weeks ago.
The deceased was 47 years old, born in Cato and a son of John I. and the late Lueila Jakway. He leaves beside his father, a wife and family of eight children, also five sisters and one brother to mourn his loss.
Mr. Jakway was a meat man of great ability, recognized throughout the country as such and owned a thriving meat market on Clark St., Auburn.
The funeral was weld from the Rolling Funeral Home, Auburn, the Rev. Dr. MacPhail of the First Presbyterian Church officiating. The burial took place at Soule Cemetery.
The large attendance at the funeral showed the esteem in which Mr. Jakway was held as did the beautiful bower of flowers. The bearers were H. N. Jakway, J. Ira Jakway, H. B. Carr, Paul Ross, H. L. Westcott, and Martin T. Barry.
The Cato Citizen and Tri-County Leader, 20 October 1921

John Rodney JAMIESON

[I003651]
Jamieson, John Rodney age 85, longtime resident of Prospect Park, died October 23rd, 2008 of complications of a stroke. Formerly of Madison and Poynette, WI, he graduated from the the U of WI and U of MN with degrees in Mechanical Engineering. He was a 1st Lieutenant in the U.S. Air Force in Italy from 1943-1945. His career began as an engineer for Honeywell, Inc. followed by Commissioner of Highways for the State of MN, Deputy Federal Highway Administrator, Washington D.C., Director of Transit Development, St. Paul, and Deputy Commissioner, N.J. Dept. of Transportation. John will long be remembered for his involvement and commitment to the Prospect Park East River Road Neighborhood. After retiring his interests extended to evaluating the role of public transit in support of initiatives such as energy conservation, future petroleum availability, and global climate change. He enjoyed discussing local politics around the pot belly stove at Schneider's Drug with friends. To his family and friends John will be remembered for his love of curling, sailing, camping trips, and family gatherings at the cabin on Long Lake. John is preceded in death by his wife of 50 years, Ruth (Sundstrom) Jamieson; son, Andrew H. Sundstrom, Jr.; and brother, Tom Jamieson. He will be dearly missed by son, Peter Sundstrom; daughters, Mary Sundstrom Gramer and Liz Jamieson; grandchildren, Denna Riley, Andrew Sundstrom, Ilsa, Anders and Anna Gramer, Hilary Dittmore and Grant Burnham; and great-grandchildren, Dustin and Kylara Dittmore. John will also be missed by sisters, Peg Winkler and Maryanne Meigs; sister-in-law, Helen Jamieson; brother-in-law, Howard Winkler; and several nieces and nephews. Memorials may be sent to the Alzheimer's Research Center, 640 Jackson St., St. Paul, MN 55101.
