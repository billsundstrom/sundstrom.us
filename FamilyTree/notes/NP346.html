SOLOMON WILSON, PIONEER RESIDENT OF WABASH, IS DEAD
WAS FATHER OF E.M. WILSON.
Solomon Wilson, a pioneer resident of Wabsah and the father of Edward M. Wilson of this city, died at his home in Wabash at 6 o'clock Wednesday evening at the advanced age of 74 years.
Mr. Wilson was in earlier life a manufacturer, but during his later years had been in the insurance business. He had long been very ill, and friends had realized for some time that he could not recover. A few days ago the patient lapsed into unconsciousness.
Edward M. Wilson, who has made frequent visits to his father during his illness, reached his bedside for the last time shortly before death intervened.
Mr. Wilson is survived by the widow, three sons and one daughter.
Weekly Sentinel, Fort Wayne, 28 February 1906

Frederick James MARSH

[I020374]
CHATHAM - Fred J. Marsh of Chatham, MA and Vero Beach, FL passed away peacefully at home with his wife and family by his side on Nov. 14, 2014.
He was born July 30, 1920 in Adams, MA to the late Fred and Gertrude Marsh .
He graduated from Drury High School in North Adams, MA, Class of 1939, and went into the Army Air Corps during World War II where he was assigned to the bombardier squadron as an aerial gunner. After the service, he worked at James Hunter Machine Company before finding his calling in the early 1950s as a meticulous craftsman, homebuilder and developer in the Berkshires and on Cape Cod.
A consummate family man, Fred was also a hard-worker with an engaging sense of humor and a firm handshake. He didn't put down his hammer in retirement, but kept active with projects including building the bandstand for the annual Taste of Chatham.
Throughout his life Fred enjoyed skiing, clamming, fishing, golf, and tennis, especially in the warm Florida sunshine. His passions included John Wayne films, Peyton Manning audibles and his 1981 green truck. He was most passionate about his family. He was a lifelong fan of the Yankees, the New York Giants and Notre Dame football.
Fred himself played football for the Army Air Corps and later went semi-pro for the Crusaders in the Berkshires.
His family's still not sure whether he was happier when the Red Sox lost or when the Yankees won. When Fred wasn't watching sports, he played the organ and the harmonica in a band, wowed his children and grandchildren with magic tricks and traveled to the Mediterranean, South America and Mexico. He is now on a new adventure.
He was a past member of organizations including Monomoy Yacht Club, Vero Beach Country Club, and a corporator of the North Adams Hoosac Savings Bank.
He leaves his wife of 68 years, Betty, who remembers him as "always a gentleman, but also a gentle man."
Beside his spouse, other survivors are his four boys, Bill Marsh of Chatham, MA, his partner Debbi Otto and his children John, Ketchum, Julia and her husband Bruce; Jim Marsh of Andover, MA, his wife Cynthia and their children Alex and Lauren; Tom Marsh of San Antonio, TX, his partner Janise Saul and his children Aaron and Debra; John Marsh and his wife Anne of Sun Valley, ID and their son Colby; three great-grandchildren, Kaylee, Billy and Stryker and special family members Pam Marsh and Mary Ann Harwood. He also leaves two sisters, Lucille Kulp of Stuart, FL and Barbara Sprague of North Adams, MA, and sister-in-law Bernice Marsh of Sebastian, FL. Fred was predeceased by two brothers, Donald Marsh and William Marsh .
A memorial service will be held Saturday, Nov. 29 at 12:30 p.m. at the Nickerson Funeral Home in Chatham.
In lieu of flowers, please consider donating to the Visiting Nurse Association of the Treasure Coast at 1110 35th Lane, Vero Beach, FL 32960 and the VNA of Cape Cod at 67 Terrace Heun Drive, Falmouth, MA 02540.
Cape Cod Times, 19 November 2014

Alexander MARSHALL

[I070233]
Alexander Marshall , an international marketer for McDonnell Douglas Corp. and community volunteer, died Friday (Oct. 19, 2007) at his home in Creve Coeur after a struggle with lymphoma and leukemia. He was 78.
Born in Pawtucket, R.I., Mr. Marshall earned a bachelor's degree from Brown University. A wrestler and member of ROTC, he served in the Navy from 1950 to 1953 and nurtured a passion for photography while stationed aboard the Colonial, a landing ship dock.
Mr. Marshall joined McDonnell Douglas in 1953 and in 1960 took a post as an international representative in Ottawa, Canada. Five years later, he moved back to St. Louis, becoming director of international marketing in 1970 and vice president of international marketing in 1983. He traveled often to meet with representatives of such countries as England, Australia and Israel.
Fairly early in his 38-year career at McDonnell Douglas, he also met Elizabeth Ann Wood, a company secretary.
"We weren't madly in love immediately, but we noticed each other," she said. "He was a salesman and I was a farmer's daughter."
They married, had three sons and were together for 47 years.
Mr. Marshall retired from McDonnell Douglas in 1991 but stayed active in the community. He sang in the choir and served as chairman of the administrative board at Salem in Ladue United Methodist Church.
He also volunteered with the Literacy Council of St. Louis and tutored twice a week at Epworth Children and Family Services. His work garnered Commerce Bank's Community Service Award in 2003. For 10 years, Mr. Marshall sat on the Board of Curators at Central Methodist University, which this year awarded him an honorary doctorate.
In addition to his wife, survivors include three sons, Douglas Marshall of Arlington, Mass., David Marshall of Ft. Lauderdale, Fla., and Daniel Marshall of Middlebury, Ind.; a sister, Sarah M. Fell of Sayre, Pa.; and three grandchildren.
Visitation will be 4 to 8 p.m. Monday at Kriegshauser West Mortuary, 9450 Olive Boulevard, Olivette. A funeral service will be at 10:30 a.m. Tuesday at Salem in Ladue United Methodist Church, 1200 South Lindbergh Boulevard, Ladue, followed by burial in Caledonia Cemetery in Sparta, Ill.
Memorial contributions may be made to Central Methodist University, 411 Central Methodist Square, Fayette, Mo. 65248, or Epworth Children and Family Services, 110 North Elm Avenue, St. Louis, Mo. 63119.
St. Louis Post-Dispatch, 22 October 2007
