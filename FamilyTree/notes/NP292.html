Agnes "Aggie" Merrill. 80, of Kennewick, died Tuesday, April 15, 2003, at Kadlec Medical Center. She was born April 17, 1922 in Malden, Massachusetts and had lived in the Tri Cities since 1948.

She is survived by son and daughter-in-law Raymond and Jennie Merrill of Oak Harbor, Wa.; daughter Pat Davis of Richland; grandchildren , Tony, Michael, Kevin of Oak Harbor and Shelly of Georgia, and Amy Davis of Richland; great grandchildren Jacob, Justin and Christina of Georgia; sister-in-law and brother-in-law Shirley and Bob Tate of Beverly MA.
She was preceded in death by her husband, Francis Merrill and her brother James Kerr.

Nellie Wibbels, age 99, of Mahnomen, MN, died Saturday, October 25, 2014, at the Mahnomen Health Center in Mahnomen.
Upon emigrating from the Netherlands and settling in Gregory Township, near Bejou, MN, William and Henrietta (Kruisdyke) Kerssen began their family. Their second child of seven, Nellie Henrietta, was born September 21, 1915, at home. She grew up in Gregory Township and attended country school near Bejou. Nellie cared for her younger siblings while helping her parents with the day to day farming operations. On June 24, 1941, Nellie was united in marriage to Edgar Wibbels. The couple made their home and farmed east of Bejou for 37 years, raising livestock and grain. They retired from farming and purchased Haavenʼs Rest Home in McIntosh, MN. After five years of operation, Nellie and Edgar sold and moved into Mahnomen. Edgar passed away in September of 1999 and Nellie remained in their home until moving to Dilworth, MN, to be closer to her daughter. Only recently had Nellie moved back to Mahnomen.
Wanting to be productive, Nellie was always active and kept herself busy. She loved to knit and crochet and go fishing with Edgar. Nellie was an active member of First Lutheran Church in Mahnomen and its Ladies Aid. She also enjoyed the time she was able to spend with her family and friends.
Nellie is survived by a daughter, Ruth (David) Soderstrom, Moorhead, MN; a son Roger (Gina) Wibbels, Atwater, CA; four grandchildren; eight great-grandchildren; two great-great grandchildren; one brother, Henry Kerssen, Washington State; one sister, Alieda VanDyke, Richfield, MN; and several nieces and nephews. She was preceded in death by her parents; husband; granddaughter, Cindy; three brothers and one sister.
Funeral services will be held on Saturday, November 1, 2014, at 10 a.m. at First Lutheran Church in Mahnomen. A visitation will be held on Friday, October 31st from 5-7 p.m., at the Anderson Family Funeral Home in Mahnomen. Interment will be held in Bejou City Cemetery, Bejou, MN.

Malcolm Edward Kersteen, 92, of Simsbury, husband of Julia (St. John) Kersteen for 67 years, died February 16, 2008 at McLean. Malcolm was born October 14, 1915 in Wilkes-Barre, PA, son of the late Ralph and Olga (Pleiskatt) Kersteen and he became the valedictorian of his high school class. He was married in St. Louis, subsequently moved to Cleveland, OH and in 1950 transferred to West Hartford. After retirement he moved to Farmington and in 1998 to the McLean Village in Simsbury. He was a graduate of Columbia University, Class of 1937 and attended Columbia Graduate School of Business. Malcolm was a member of the Phi Gamma Delta fraternity and served as treasurer and president. He was an officer of the Travelers Insurance Co. and retired in 1979 after 41 years of service. Mr. Kersteen was a veteran of World War II in the U.S. Army Air Force. He was a long time member of St. James Episcopal Church, West Hartford, having served on the vestry. He was a member of the Golf Club of Avon, and active in the West Hartford Squires. Besides his wife, Julia, he is survived by sons Glenn Kersteen of Cape Elizabeth, ME, Gary Kersteen and wife Hilary of Wellfleet, MA, and was predeceased by his daughter, Gail Frothingham who lived in Topsham, ME. He also leaves behind eight grand daughters: Julia, Rebecca, Jane, Mary, Jessica, Elizabeth, Amelia, and Gwendolyn. He was proud to have seven great grandchildren. His pleasant, kind demeanor and sense of humor will be missed by many. A memorial service will be celebrated Saturday, February 23, 12 NOON in the Carling Chapel at McLean, 75 Great Pond Road, Simsbury with the Reverend Robert C. Hooper, III officiating. Private burial will be in Fairview Cemetery, West Hartford.

[I063222]
NORTHBRIDGE, Mass. - Delmar R. Kidd, 90, of 125 Sheryl Drive, died Thursday, March 1, at Radius Healthcare in Worcester. His wife, Marion L. Kidd, died in 2007.
He is survived by his six children, Susanne McKeon, Sheryl Corsello, Gary Kidd, Delmar Kidd II, Jon Kidd, and Robin Kidd; nine grandchildren, and many great-grandchildren.
Born in Gas City, KS on June 1, 1921, the son of Charles and Beatrice (Steele) Kidd, his parents died when he was very young and he lived with relatives in many states until he joined the Civilian Conservation Corps at 16 years old. He joined the Navy when he turned 18 and served as a first class radioman for the next six years traveling all over the world until his discharge in 1946.
He met his future wife, Marion, in Providence while on leave and they settled in Whitinsville where he started Kidd Laundry at Plummer's Corner. In 1953, he saw a need for affordable housing for veterans and working people and became a dealer for the National Homes Corporation selling prefabricated homes. He dedicated his life to building and built many roads and homes throughout southeastern Massachusetts. He was known for his fairness and his ready smile. He will be missed.
There will be a graveside funeral service at Riverdale Cemetery in Whitinsville on Monday, March 5, 2012 at 2 p.m. All are welcome.
Buma Funeral Home, Whitinsville is directing arrangements. Condolences and remembrances may be left at www.bumafuneralhome.com.

Gerald Earl KIEFFER

[I091733]
Gerald Kieffer passed away on Friday, Feb. 24 at St. Clare Meadows Care Center in Baraboo. Jerry, or Pee Wee as his family knew him, was born in Easton, Pa. on Oct. 5, 1929 to William B. and Julia M. (Brown) Kieffer. He moved with his family to Muskegon, Mich. in 1932. When in the fourth grade Jerry was stricken with rheumatic fever and spent the next year in bed. He saved all the cards from his classmates and friends and never forgot their love and support. He graduated from Muskegon High School in 1948 having completed a vocational printing course. During his senior year he was co-foreman of the print shop. After graduation Jerry worked in the display advertising department of the Muskegon Chronicle.
In August of 1949 Jerry moved to Baraboo to help his father start Industrial Coils. It was there that he met his father's new secretary, Audrey Meisel who became his wife in 1951. In 1973 he left Industrial Coils to become executive secretary of Thunderbird Lanes, Inc., which he, along with his brother Jim, later purchased.
Jerry was founding secretary of the Four City Safety Council where he served as president and director. He served as a director and president of the Baraboo Bowling Association. Thanks to his work and the support of bowlers for various fund raisers Special Olympics of Wisconsin selected Thunderbird Lanes as Business of the Year in 1980. In 1981 Jerry received the Special Olympic Volunteer of the Year award. In 2001 he received his greatest honor by being selected as a member of the Baraboo Bowling Association Hall of Fame.
Jerry was a member of the Baraboo Elks Lodge for over 50 years and served on the bowling committee. He was a charter member of the Baraboo Country Club and served 14 years on the Board of Directors serving as president for two years. He spent many hours working to make the course and club a better place for all members. He was a member of St. Paul's Lutheran Church in Baraboo.
Throughout his life Jerry felt his greatest assets were his family and friends and he cherished the time spent with them.
He is survived by his wife Audrey of Baraboo; brother James (Shirley) of Baraboo; brothers-in-law George Meisel of Madison, Don (Barb) Meisel of McFarland, Al Meisel of Beloit and John (Norma) Meisel of Platteville; and his sister-in-law Dolores (Bill) Mossman of Baraboo; his step-sister Suzanne (Vincent) Neuhaus of Waco, Texas; and step-brother Robert (Janet) Persson of Tulsa, Okla.; many nephews and nieces and other relatives and friends.
Jerry was preceded in death by his parents; brothers, Robert and William; step-mother Mae Wrobke; and step-brother Marshall Persson.
Visitation for Jerry will be at Redlin Funeral Home, 401 Madison Ave. in Baraboo on Monday, Feb. 27 from 4 until 7 p.m. Services will be held on Tuesday, Feb. 28 at 11 a.m. at St. Paul's Lutheran Church, 727 Eighth St. in Baraboo, with Pastors Deb and Steve Ohrtman officiating. Anne of KIEV

[I007747]
From Wikipedia

Princess Anne of Kiev (1024 - 1075), daughter of Yaroslav I, was Queen of France, as the wife of King Henri I. After the death of his first wife, Mathilde, King Henri had searched the courts of Europe for a suitable bride, but could not locate a princess who was not related to him within illegal degrees of kinship. At last he sent an embassy to distant Kiev, who returned with Anne (also called Agnes or Anna). Anne and Henri were married at the cathedral of Reims, May 19, 1051. They had three sons:
* Philippe I, King of France (May 23, 1052 - July 30, 1108)
* Hugues ("Magnus"), later Count of Crépi (1057 - October 18, 1102), who married the heiress of Vermandois and died on crusade in Tarsus, Cilicia.
* Robert (ca 1055 - ca 1060)

For six years after his death in 1060, she served as regent for their son, Philip, who was 7 at the time of his father's death. She was the first queen of France to serve as regent. Her co-regent was Count Baldwin V of Flanders. Anne was known as a literate woman, rare for the time, but there was some opposition to her as Regent on the grounds that her mastery of French was less than fluent.

A year after the King's death, Anne, acting as Regent took a passionate fancy for Count Raoul III of Valois and of the Vexin, a man whose political ambition encouraged him to repudiate his wife to marry Anne in 1062. Accused of adultery, Raoul's wife appealed to Pope Alexander II, who excommunicated Raoul and Anne. The young king Philippe forgave his mother, which was just as well, since he was to find himself in a very similar predicament in the 1090s. Raoul died in September 1074, at which time Anne returned to the French court. She died in 1079, and her obits were celebrated on September 5.
