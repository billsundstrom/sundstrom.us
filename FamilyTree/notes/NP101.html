Richard of CORNWALL

[I015002]
From Wikipedia

Richard (5 January 1209 - 2 April 1272) was Count of Poitou (bef. 1225), Earl of Cornwall (from 1227) and King of the (German) Holy Roman Empire (formally "King of the Romans") (from 1257).

He was the second son of King John "Lackland" and Isabella of Angouleme, and thus, the younger brother of King Henry III; although all other mediaeval lords of Cornwall have been known as "Earl" (or, later, "Duke"), as he is most known to history through continental accounts his version of that title has come down to us in a French-derived rendering ("Count," as opposed to Earl).

In 1231 he married Isabel Marshal, the widow of the Earl of Gloucester, much to the displeasure of his brother King Henry, who had been arranging a more advantageous match for Richard. Isabel and Richard had four children, of whom only their son, Henry of Almain, survived to adulthood. When Isabel was on her deathbed in 1240, she asked to be buried next to her first husband at Tewkesbury, but Richard had her interred at Beaulieu Abbey instead. As a pious gesture, however, he sent her heart to Tewkesbury. Later that year Richard joined the Sixth Crusade and departed for the Holy Land.

Richard opposed Simon de Montfort, and rose in rebellion in 1238 to protest the marriage of his sister, Eleanor, to Simon de Montfort.

In 1257, he was elected by three German Electoral Princes known as the "English party" (Cologne, Mainz and Palatinate) as King of Germany.

On April 2, 1272, Richard died at Berkhamstead Castle in Hertfordshire. He was buried at Hayles Abbey, which he had founded.

He married three times:
* On 30 March 1231 to Isabel Marshal, widow of Gilbert de Clare, Earl of Gloucester and Hertford, and daughter of William Marshal, 1st Earl of Pembroke. She died in childbed 17 January 1240.
* On 23 November 1243 to Sanchia of Provence, daughter of Raymond Berenger IV, count of Provence. She died 9 November 1261.
* On 16 June 1269 to Beatrice of Falkenburg, daughter of Dietrich I, Count of Falconburg. There were no children. She was aged about sixteen to Richard's sixty, and was said to be one of the most beautiful women of her time. Beatrice died October 17, 1277 and was buried at Friars Minor in Oxford.

Issue
Isabel bore him four children, all of whom died in the cradle, except Henry of Almain (1235-1271), Richard's heir apparent. Henry was the victim of the famous murder at Viterbo, when he was cut down while praying in a church by his cousins, Simon the younger de Montfort and Guy de Montfort, Count of Nola. Richard's successor was his son by Sanchia, Edmund, Earl of Cornwall (1249-1300) but he too died childless.

Richard had the reputation of being a womanizer, and indeed his only descendants are found among his illegitimate children. His mistress, Joan de Valletort, was certainly the mother of two of his children. Their daughter Joan de Cornwall married Richard Champernowne, and their son Richard de Cornwall died at the Battle of Berwick in 1297. An illegitimate son, Philip de Cornwall, was a cleric in 1248. Another illegitimate son, Walter de Cornwall, was granted lands by his half-brother Edmund, and died in 1313.

Josephine B. COSTELLO

[I044860]
Josephine B. (Costello) Small, 96, formerly of East Braintree, died on Jan. 10, 2006 at Quincy Medical Center after a brief illness. She was the beloved wife of the late Irving C. Small, Lt. Col, USA Ret.
Born and raised in Boston, Josephine was the only child of the late James F. and Johanna (Crowley) Costello, who were the last family to live on Rainsford Island in Boston Harbor. Mrs. Costello witnessed the Great Boston Molasses Flood in January 1919.
She was a 1929 graduate of Boston Teacher's College, now UMass Boston. She began her teaching career at the Gaston School in East Boston.
After raising her family, she taught second grade at Sacred Heart School in Weymouth for 11 years. Mrs. Small was active in the Braintree Point Women's Club, Braintree Philergians, Archbishop Williams Parents Guild, Puritan Bridge Club and East Braintree Seniors Bowling League.
A parishioner of Sacred Heart Church for 68 years she was a past-prefect of Sacred Heart's Ladies Sodality and a member of Sacred Heart School's Mothers Club.
Mrs. Small and her husband traveled extensively throughout Canada and the United States, including Alaska, and to Mexico and Guatemala with the Wally Byam Club of Airstream owners.
After her husband's death Mrs. Small continued her travels to Europe and the Caribbean.
She leaves sons, Irving C. Small, Jr. and Robert F. Small, both of Hanson, and four daughters, Josephine B. Donahue and her husband, Joseph of Scituate, Mary C. Frazier and her husband, Ronald of Braintree, Elizabeth A. Pesci and her husband, Edward of Greensburg, PA and Virginia M. Cookson and her husband, Edward of Hamilton, MA., thirteen grandchildren and twenty-five great-grandchildren. Arrangements by the Clancy- Lucid Funeral Home Weymouth Landing.
A Mass celebrating Mrs. Small's life was held at St. Francis of Assisi Church, with burial in the military section of Blue Hill Cemetery.
Contributions in her memory may be made to the Sacred Heart Church Building Fund, Commercial St, Weymouth, MA 02188.
Braintree Forum, 18 January 2006

Anabelle Anne COTTINGHAM

BELLE ANNE COTTINGHAM was born at Queensville, Jennings County, Indiana, on Monday at I I o'clock A. M., January 23, 1854. Died at Ontario, San Bernardino County, California, on Thursday, at I I o'clock PM., February 11, 1926. She is buried in Bellevue Cemetery. Ontario. She was five feet six inches in height, weighed about one hundred thirty pounds, slender figure, sprightly step, alert, erect, graceful, brown eyes, black hair, fair complexion, small mouth and ears, small aquiline nose, impulsive, intense, masterful, fearless, sensitive, proud-spirited, sympathetic, strong-willed, had musical and oratorical abilities, industrious and thrifty. She was a beautiful woman. In young womanhood she had rosy cheeks. She was a devoted, self-sacrificing mother and a pure hearted woman. She did not grow old in mind and in phyical appearance. On the day that she died, she arose from her sick bed, dressed and sat up in a chair for about an hour. She died bravely with a smile upon her lips. On October 5. 1872, at Cordova, Minnesota, she married Peter Orison Dillon. Rev. Wallace Hopkins, Christian minister, performed the marriage ceremony. Mr. Dillon died at Manitou Springs, Colorado, December 2, 1879. She married Thomas Cassadv at Ellensburg, Washington, in 1889. Mr. Cassady organized the State Bank of Hensel, Hensel, North Dakota. He died in Seattle in May, 1912. She was a member of the Christian Church, having joined this denomination when a young girl. She was a member of the Pomona, California Chapter of the Daughters of the American Revolution. She was also a member of May Queen Rebekah Lodge No. 177, Independent Order of Oddfellows of Ontario, California. She had no children by her second husband. Had one child by her first husband, the compiler of this genealogy Arthur Orison Dillon.

William H. COUGHLIN

[I020376]
William C. Coughlin , 68, of Naples died Tuesday, July 6, 1999 at his residence.

He was born in North Adams, Mass. in 1931 to William and Dorthy Coughlin .

Mr. Coughlin served as a corporal in the United States Army during the Korean War from 1951 to 1953. In 1956, he earned his bachelor of science degree from Fairfield University, and from 1959 to 1962, he attended the University of Connecticut as a Ph.D candidate. He worked as a teacher and a counselor in the Massachusetts and Connecticut areas before becoming the assistant superintendent of schools for Trumbull, Conn. In 1968, he moved to Naples and became the assistant superintendent of schools for Collier County, serving there until 1971. In 1971, he opened and managed the Garden Exchange, Inc. in Naples. In 1985, he became coowner of S&H Metal Fabricators in Naples and in 1992 he formed Intelligent Pest Management.

As a master gardener, Mr. Coughlin was called upon numerous times for his expertise. He taught Horticulture Adult Education courses in Collier County as well as teaching state horticulture courses.

Mr. Coughlin is survived by his sons, Kerry Coughlin of Sugar Hill, Ga., Kevin Coughlin of Connecticut, Timothy Coughlin of Trubhill, Conn., and William Coughlin of Alexandria, Va.; his daughter, Lisa Hayes of West Palm Beach; brother, Donald (Roma) Coughlin of Tennessee; sister, Dorothy (Richard)Way of Naples; and four grandchildren. He will also be missed by his ex-wife and good friends, Judy Quinn of Naples, and his step-children, Dr. James Goldman and AnneMarie Goldman, both of Naples.

A Memorial Mass was held Monday, July 12 at St. John the Evangelist Catholic Church in Naples. An inurnment followed at St. John's Memorial Gardens.
The Banner, Bonita Springs, FL, 14 July 1999
