Dora Zepherine LAFERRIERE

[I014486]
Dora Crowell passed away peacefully on May 21, 2020. She was born Dora Zepherine Laferriere on August 14, 1919, in Silver Plains, Manitoba Canada and spent most of her youth in Winnipeg. Growing up in a French Canadian family, Dora also learned English as a second language when her family moved to the "City" at age 10.
Dora met Idaho native Paul J. Crowell after WWII and left home for the first time at age 28. They were married in Weiser, Idaho, and moved to a small acreage in "south" Boise - the Bench above now Bishop Kelly, where Dora lived for 73 years. In 1947, they carved out the family homestead from alfalfa & sagebrush. Many weddings were held in the backyard -- with a commanding view of downtown and Bogus Basin -- for both sons, grandson, granddaughter and friends. Dora was able to stay in her beloved home until Easter this year, which was her greatest wish and source of pride.
Dora was preceded in death by her husband of 57 years, and her 15 siblings. She was the last of the generation. Her legacy continues through her sons, Brian (Barbara) and Gary (Buffy), grandchildren, Russ, Jennifer and Andrew, and 4 great-grandchildren - who brought much joy in her life.
Dora's 100th birthday celebration was the only memorial she wanted. Mom will be held in our hearts for eternity, leaving us a legacy of love.
Idaho Statesman, 7 June 2020

Joseph Frederick LAGUNDO

[I090142]
A memorial Mass for Joseph Frederick Lagundo Sr., 51, of Edgewater and formerly of Churchton, will be said at 11 a.m. tomorrow at Our Lady of Perpetual Help Catholic Church, 515 Loch Haven Road, Edgewater.
Mr. Lagundo is presumed drowned after falling off his boat near Thomas Point in the Chesapeake Bay on Nov. 28.
Born Aug. 9, 1942, in Washington, D.C., he attended Our Lady Queen of Peace Elementary School there and graduated in 1968 from Bishop McNamara High School in Forestville.
A disabled Vietnam veteran, he served in the Navy as a petty officer first class and radarman on the USS Gyatt and the USS Bon Homme Richard between 1968 and 1970.
Mr. Lagundo was an operating engineer, handling heating, ventilation and air-conditioning and other electrical work. He was employed from 1971 to 1979 by the Smithsonian Institution and from 1979 to 1997 by the Government Printing Office.
In his youth he was an altar boy in the Our Lady of Peace Parish. He was a Fourth Degree member of the St. Pius X Council 4076 of the Knights of Columbus and a union shop steward in Local 121 in the Brotherhood of Electrical Engineers. He also belonged to the American Legion and the Fleet Reserve Association.
His interests included hockey, baseball, football, playing the guitar, hunting and fishing. He caught and checked in a 51-inch, 41-pound rock fish on May 2, 1993. He also enjoyed growing Japanese red maple trees.
He was formerly married to Regina Tucker of Laurel.

Earl John LAING

[I121182]
Murray Hill psychiatrist, decorated Vietnam veteran, 70
It is with great sadness that we announce the death of Earl Laing, M.D., a beloved local psychiatrist with a long-standing practice in Murray Hill. Dr. Laing, 70, died Saturday, Dec. 20, 2008, at Compassionate Care Hospice, Dover.
Visitation will be held at the Doyle Funeral Home, 106 Maple Ave., Morristown, on Monday from 2 to 4 and 7 to 9 p.m. Interment is private.
A native of Asbury Park, Dr. Laing received his medical degree from the University of Iowa. He did his internship at Los Angeles County Hospital and his residency at the Menninger Clinic in Topeka, Kan.
From 1966 to 1968 he served as a medical officer in the US Army First Infantry Division, spending much of that time in Vietnam. He earned two Bronze Stars, one for valor under fire, and numerous other medals of which he was very proud. Dr. Laing loved the practice of medicine and left a great legacy of patients he has helped. He served on the staffs of Overlook Hospital, Summit, and Muhlenberg Regional Medical Center, Plainfield, for 30 years. He had many interests and loved to travel.
He is survived by his wife, Ruth; his three children, Catherine Dietze and her husband, William; John Laing, and Patricia Nacamuli and her husband, Robert. His five grandchildren, Nicholas, Alexandra, Jared, Miranda, and Neil, were especially treasured by him. He is also survived by a large extended family and many dear friends and colleagues.
The Star-Ledger, Newark, 21 December 2008
