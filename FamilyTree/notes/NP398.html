Lenore Ann NORELIUS

[I003642]
Lenore Ann "Lee" Chatterton, 87, of Albany died Wednesday at Albany Care Center.
She was born in Vancouver, Wash., to John and Anna (Anderson) Norelius. She grew up in Vancouver and Corvallis and graduated from Corvallis High School. She attended Oregon State University.
She married Robert "Chip" Chatterton on Aug. 11, 1944, in Seattle. The Chattertons lived in Washington, Alaska and Oregon and moved to Albany in the 1960s. Mr. Chatterton died May 30, 1970.
Mrs. Chatterton worked as an office manager and administrative assistant, and in accounting. She retired from Cominco American Inc. of Harrisburg in the late 1970s.
A member of the Albany First Church of God, she also did volunteer work and enjoyed traveling in the United States and Europe, playing pinochle, bowling and reading.
Survivors include nieces Mary Ann Sanker, Judy Peterson, Joan Moore and Roberta Eudaly; and nephews John, Webber, George and Michael Doughton.
Sister Elida Doughton died before she did, and brother John Norelius died in infancy.
Mrs. Chatterton's funeral will begin at 2 p.m. Monday at the First Church of God in Albany, with Pastor Bill Lane officiating. Burial will be at the Lebanon IOOF Cemetery.
Contributions in her name may be made to the First Church of God Building Fund, in care Fisher Funeral Home, P.O. Box 156, Albany, Ore. 97321.
Albany Democrat-Herald, 4 May 2002

Leo William NORGAARD

[I096587]
Sioux Falls - Leo Norgaard, 87, died Monday, July 7, 2014, at the Sanford Hospital. Funeral service will begin at 10:30 AM on Friday, July 11, 2014, at George Boom Funeral Home in Sioux Falls with burial to follow at Hills of Rest Memorial Park. The family will be present to greet friends for visitation from 6:00 to 8:00 PM on Thursday at the funeral home.
Leo William Norgaard, son of William and Elsie (Henricksen) Norgaard, was born May 12, 1927, in Arlington, SD. He grew up in Arlington where he graduated from high school in 1945. On September 16, 1950, Leo enlisted in the United States Army and served honorably during the Korean War. He was honorably discharged on August 9, 1952. Following his military service, Leo moved back to Sioux Falls where he began working for John Morrell. Shortly after his return, Leo married Ferne Edwards in Luverne, MN. The couple made their home in Sioux Falls where Leo continued to work for Morrell's and they stared a family. Over the years, he also farmed near Lyons, SD. In 1980, Leo married Bernes Dornebush in Sioux Falls. Leo retired from John Morrell in 1987 after 34 years of hard work. Bernes passed away in 1993.
Leo loved to go dancing and was known for his hard work ethic.
Grateful for having shared his life are his children, Kenneth (Renee) Norgaard, Rib Lake, WI, Bonnie (Myron) Rosheim, Sioux Falls, Bill Norgaard, Renner, SD, and Jim (Holly) Norgaard, Crooks, SD; grandchildren, Stephanie Norgaard, Justin Norgaard, Chelsea Norgaard, Jessica Rosehim, Christine (Travis) Petty, Cassidy Norgaard, and Carson Norgaard; great-granddaughter, Maci Petty; and brothers, Daryl (Phyllis) Norgaard, Badger, SD, and Orlan (Gloria) Norgaard, Sioux Falls.
Leo was preceded in death by is parents, William and Elsie; son, Garry; second wife, Bernes; brother, Ray; and sister, Doris.

Philip Jones NORRIS

[I020425]
Philip J. Norris, 75, of 25 South Road, a longtime town official and area agricultural leader, died Monday at home.
He served on the School Committee for 21 years, 16 as chairman and representative to the Hampshire Regional High School, which he also chaired for many years. He served on the town's Finance Committee for 21 years and on the Board of Assessors for 12 years.
He also was a cemetery commissioner, a charter member of the Volunteer Fire Department and belonged to the Westhampton Firemen's Association.
He was the owner of Philip J. Norris & Sons Dairy and Maple Products.
He was a director of the Massachusetts Farm Bureau, a past president of the Hampshire County Farm Bureau, a region delegate for Agrimark and chairman of the Local Farmer-Member Advisory Committee for Agway Inc. Associated with the Department of Agriculture, he served in the Hampshire-Hampden Farm Service Agency, was on the Eastern Artificial Insemination Advisory Council and worked with the Three-County Fair Association.
Born in Northampton, he was a 1942 graduate of Northampton High School and a longtime resident of Westhampton. He was a 62-year member of the Congregational Church, where he was a deacon, deacon-emeritus and member of the senior and men's choirs.
He and his wife of 56 years, the former Jean Warner, formerly of Williambsurg, belonged to the Franklin-Hampshire Harvest Club and the former Calico Couples Square Dancing Club.
Besides his wife; he leaves two sons, Charles of Westhampton and Philip Jr. of Littleton, Colo.; three daughters, Eileen Taylor of Great Exuma, Bahamas, Luanne Clark of Westhampton and Phyllis N. Huntley of Northampton; a brother, Robert W. of Bridport, Vt.; two sisters, Jeanette Adams of Burlington and Elizabeth Nova of South Windsor, Conn.; nine grandchildren; and two great-grandchildren.
The funeral will be Friday morning at the church, with burial in Center Cemetery. Calling hours are Thursday afternoon and evening at Mitchell Funeral Home of Easthampton.
Memorial contributions may be made to the Hospice of Hampshire County, 7 Denniston Place, Northampton, MA 01060, the Westhampton Congregational Church Music Fund, North Road, Westhampton, MA 01027, or the Westhampton Elementary School, in care of the Philip J. Norris Citizenship Award, Westhampton, MA 01027.
Union-News (Springfield, MA)

Dorothy Emiline NORSTEDT

[I104228]
Dorothy Hovda Corpron, 86, passed away peacefully on July 13, 2016 surrounded by her loving family at the Mountrail Bethel Home in Stanley, ND. Her memorial service will be Saturday, July 16, 2016 at the Sybil Center in Stanley. Committal service is in Fairview Cemetery, Stanley.
Dorothy was born April 16, 1930 to Rueben and Gladys Norstedt in White Earth, ND. She attended schools in White Earth and Stanley. One of six children, her siblings include Doris Walhaug (deceased), Marge Ostdahl, Loretta Joern, Larry Norstedt and Allan Norstedt.
She married Clayton Hovda on July 23, 1947 and raised three children (Ric, Pam and Craig) while living in Stanley, Williston, and Minot. Clayton passed away in 1984. On November 29, 1988, she married Robert Corpron. Robert passed away in 1991.
In her early years, Dorothy enjoyed spending summers with her grandparents, Sam and Emma, on their farm near Powers Lake as she assisted her grandmother with both farm and kitchen chores. Once moving to Stanley as a teenager, she fondly remembered working at Dakota Drug and ironing/babysitting for local families. She attributed her strong work ethic and sense of duty to these formative experiences.
Dorothy was active in her community. She was a room mother at the school, member of the PTA, a Sunday school teacher, Bible study coordinator, and church council member. In addition, she served as president of the VFW and president and secretary of the American Legion. She worked for the Minot Commission on Aging for over 13 years and for many years as Coordinator of Seniorʼs Services for Stanley, Plaza, Parshall and New Town. In recognition of this work, on October 19, 2002, she was selected for The Outstanding Woman of North Dakota Award by the North Dakota Women of Today "For her dedication in promoting state causes and for being a living example of courage, integrity, and commitment".
Dorothy was a loving sister, mother, mother-in-law, grandmother, great- grandmother and great-great grandmother to: daughter, Pam; sons, Ric and Craig; sons-in-law, Lonnie Fladeland and Kevin McGrew; daughter-in-law, Shellie Weitz; seven grandchildren, twelve great-grandchildren and one great-great grandchild; siblings, Doris Walhaug (deceased), Marge Ostdahl, Loretta Joern (Charles), Larry Norstedt and Allan Norstedt (Jane); and to the families of Jon Corpron (Cammy), Jim Corpron, Karen (John) Anderson, and Teri (Gene) Behr (deceased).
