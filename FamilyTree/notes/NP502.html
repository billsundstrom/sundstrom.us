Thomas Christopher SKLUZACEK

[I022502]
Thomas C. Skluzacek was born June 2, 1911, in LeCenter, Minnesota, to Jacob and Mary (Zelenka) Skluzacek. He was raised on a farm in Hazelwood and was baptized & confirmed at Annunciation Catholic Church. He married Olive Rosanna Schmitz on June 10, 1935, at St. Lawrence Catholic Church in Faribault. After marriage, they bought a farm in Waterford Township, Dakota County. Together for over 60 years, Tom and Olive farmed and raised a variety of livestock and fowl. From the time he was young Tom loved horses. He always had horses on the farm - quite often including a Percheron working team. Olive passed away on November 17, 2001, after 66 years of marriage. Tom remained on the farm until moving to Northfield in August 2006.
Tom was a member of the Church of St. Dominic and a few years ago was recognized as being the oldest living member of the Knights of Columbus. He also served the church as an usher for over 40 years. For many years Tom taught Square Dancing and he and Olive used to spend every Saturday night dancing. He loved to play cards - especially 500. He was known to have taught all of his grandchildren to play (and maybe even to cheat!) He enjoyed spending his time driving around the country roads and always thought that "Home was the best place to be." He was friendly, never complained, and always and a smile on his face and a sparkle in his eyes.
Survivors include his children and their spouses: Lora and Roger Tutewohl of Cannon Falls, Audrey and Ron Olinger of Randolph, Gerald and Jane of Northfield, Nancy Lane of Janesville, Wisconsin, Joanne and Richard Olson of Punta Gorda, Florida, Janet Rahman of Carthage, Illinois; his daughter-in-law, Laura Skluzacek of Randolph; 19 grandchildren, numerous great & great-great-grandchildren; 2 brothers: Stanley and wife Helen of Northfield, Dan and wife Emma of Faribault; his brother-in-law, Ed (Susan) Schmitz of Faribault; many nieces, nephews, and other relatives and friends.
He was preceded in death by his wife, Olive; his son, Robert; 3 grandsons: Jeff Lane, Dale Skluzacek and Rudy Tutewohl; 2 sons-in-law, Keith Rahman and Mark Lane; his parents; his sister, Agnes Tralle; and 5 brothers: Charlie, Leo, Jacob, John and Willie.
Thomas passed away Friday evening, January 30, 2009, at the Northfield Hospital, at the age of 97.
Mass of Christian Burial will be 10AM Saturday, February 7, 2009, at the Church of St. Dominic in Northfield with Fr. Dennis Dempsey officiating. Music will be provided by Marlys Mouser. Interment will be in Red Rose Cemetery, Waterford Township. Casket bearers will be his grandchildren. Visitation will be 4-7PM Friday, February 6, 2009, at the Benson & Langehough Funeral Home in Northfield. Visitation will continue at the church on Saturday one hour prior to the funeral.
In lieu of flowers, memorials are preferred to the Northfield Senior Center or St. Dominic's Catholic Church.

Town of Morehouse - Jane K. Kelley, 77, of Lewiston, ME and formerly of Hoffmeister, NY, died on February 10, 2013 at the Marshwood Nursing Care Center in Lewiston, ME.
Jane was born on May 15, 1935 in Syracuse, a daughter of the late Frank and Marguerite (Hamlin) Slater. She graduated from North Syracuse High School and was affiliated with the family business, Kelley Marine, both formerly of Syracuse and Morehouse. For several years Jane owned and operated Tamarack, a restaurant and bar business formerly located in the Town of Morehouse. She served as Town Clerk in Morehouse for over ten years until her retirement in 2004. Jane resided in Morehouse for over 35 years until moving to Maine in 2007.
She was a member of the Morehouse Volunteer Fire Company Auxiliary and as a certified E.M.T. served with the Company's Ambulance. Jane was a former member of a Lions Club in Syracuse and was of the Methodist faith.
Survivors include one daughter, Debra Merchant of North Syracuse; two sons, Seaborn Kelley, Jr. and his wife, Melissa, of Binghamton and John Kelley of Hoffmeister; and eight grandchildren. She also leaves her friend and companion of over 30 years, Stewart Farber of North Syracuse.
A graveside service will take place at Morehouse Cemetery in the spring.
