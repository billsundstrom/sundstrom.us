Benjamin Franklin PEIRSON

[I097493]
Benjamin F. Peirson, III, 90, died April 4, 2012 at the VA Hospice Center, Coatesville, Pa.
Frank was the beloved husband of Doris C. Peirson, with whom he shared 66 years of marriage.
Born in Philadelphia, he was the son of the late Benjamin F. Jr. and Emma Esherick Peirson.
After graduation from West Philadelphia High School, he attended Drexel University and then served for three years with the 13th Army Air Depot during the Asiatic and Pacific Theaters of War during WWII. After leaving the service he was employed as an electrician for many years in the Coatesville area. Later in life he became a realtor and was a part owner in Hoopes/ Peirson Realty in West Chester when he retired in 1978. During the 60's, 70's and 80's Frank was involved with the Cub Scouts and Boy Scouts of Chester County. He was an assistant scout master at Hopewell Pack and Troop 8 and later served as a District Commissioner. He guided his son and grandsons through the scouting experience. He was present when they earned their Order of Arrow awards and Eagle Scout Awards. In addition he was a Brotherhood life member of the Order of Arrow and a member of the Presbyterian Church for over 60 years. Frank enjoyed restoring his vacation homes in New Jersey and Maine and until recently he vacationed with his family and friends spending many happy days boating on the coast of Maine and visiting his cabin in Mansfield, Pa. as well as trips to the Elk River in Maryland.
In addition to his wife, he is survived by his children, Barbara, wife of Nathaniel Carter of Downingtown; his son Chris, wife Donna of North East, Md.; his grandsons, Stephen MacCall-Carter, wife Kelly, their children Megan and Henry, of Horsham and Jonathan Carter, wife Kristina, their children Nathaniel and Abigail, of Honey Brook and beloved friends.
A memorial service will be celebrated Saturday April 14, 2012, at 11 a.m. at the Forks of Brandywine E. Presbyterian Church, 1648 Horseshoe Pike, Glenmoore, PA 19343. In lieu of flowers, contributions in his memory may be made to one of the following: Department of Veterans Affairs Hospice Unit, Vol Service CMAMC, 1400 Black Horse Pike Coatesville, PA 19320 or Forks of the Brandywine EPC, Music Department Fund, 1648 Horseshoe Pike, Glenmoore, PA 19343.

Funeral Services For John Pelican --
John Pelican, 82, died Sunday at the Central City Hospital where he had been taken for an operation. Funeral services, conducted by Rev. Robert Ditterline were held at Palmer Methodist church Tuesday, March 26 at 2:00 p.m. and burial made in Rose Hill Cemetery.
Casket bearers were Edward Santin, Fred Santin, Archie Snodgrass, Henry Santin, Jr., Frank Santin and Joseph Santin. Singers were Mrs. Richard Lemmerman and Mrs. Thomas Condell with Mrs. William Kucera, organist.
Mr. Pelican was born in Czechoslovakia January 31, 1881, to John and Frances Pelican. The family came to America when John was nine, settling in Saline County, Nebraska.
John came to Nance County to begin work on an early irrigation project which was started in the Loup Valley below Palmer. He had fallen in love with America and Nebraska and began farming here. He and Anna John were married October 7, 1907. They farmed in Nance County until retiring and moving to Palmer.
He was preceded in death by his parents, his wife, one sister, 2 grandchildren and one great-grandchild.
He leaves to cherish his memory: one daughter, Mrs. Helen Grosch of Grand Island; four sons, Louis of Chapman, Lumer, Leonard and Stanley of Palmer; two brothers, Anton of Palmer and Frank of Wilber, Nebraska; nine grandchildren and three great-grandchildren, and a host of friends. Stanley PELICAN

[I046890]
PALMER, Stanley Pelican , 90, of Grand Island, formerly of Palmer, died Friday, April 11, 2008, at the Grand Island Veterans Home. A memorial service will be held at 10:30 a.m. Tuesday at the United Methodist Church in Palmer. The Rev. Martin Leeper will officiate. Burial will be in the Rose Hill Cemetery with military rites by Burton Beyer Post 120. Visitation will be one hour prior to service time at the church. Palmer Funeral Home in Palmer is in charge of arrangements. Stanley Pelican was born in Nance County in the Loup River Valley near Palmer on Dec. 18, 1917, to John and Anna John Pelican . He was the youngest of five children. Survivors include his wife of 65 years, Irene; his children and their spouses, Linda and Virgil Berney of Grand Island, Marlene and Robert McCammon of Lincoln and John and Shirley Pelican of Palmer; and his brother and sister-in-law, Leonard and Ada Pelican of Palmer. Also left to cherish his memory are seven grandchildren, Sheri Pfeil, Angie Owen, Scott and Chad McCammon and Stacy, Adam and Sara Pelican ; three great-grandchildren, Erick and Anna Owen and Jack McCammon; several nieces and nephews, and many friends. Stanley grew up in the Palmer area, graduating from Palmer High School in 1935. He also graduated from sheet metal school at Frye Aircraft in Omaha. He farmed with his father and brothers until entering the Air Force in May 1942, receiving training as an aircraft avionics specialist at Chanute Field in Rantoul, Ill., and Anderson Field in Walterboro, S.C. He was sent overseas in April 1943 and served in Africa, Italy and India, servicing planes that flew Òthe Hump. He contracted malaria and was hospitalized in North Africa for several weeks. He received his discharge in November 1945. He was united in marriage with Irene Paulsen on Aug. 15, 1942, at Champaign, Ill. Following his military service, he farmed near Palmer for 60 years. In 2002 they retired to Grand Island. Because of failing health he entered the Grand Island Veterans Home five years ago. In addition to his love of farming and raising livestock, Stanley played the button accordion by ear all his life, often playing Czech polkas and waltzes for neighborhood parties and community events. He enjoyed bowling, watching professional baseball, spending time with his grandchildren and traveling. He was a charter member of the VFW, 63-year member of the American Legion Post 120, and served on the firing squad in Palmer for many years. He also belonged to the Palmer United Methodist Church, Platt-Duetsche, and Eagles. He served as a Nance County supervisor and was on the school board of Palmer when the new school was built. He was preceded in death by his brothers Lumer and Louis Pelican and his sister Helen Grosch. Memorials are suggested to the American Legion Post 120 and Auxiliary or the Palmer United Methodist Church. Sign the guest book at theindependent.com/obits.
Grand Island Independent, The (NE)
Date: April 13, 2008

Henry PERCY

[I007569]
From Wikipedia

Henry Percy, 3rd Earl of Northumberland, (25 July 1421 - 29 March 1461) was the son of Henry Percy, 2nd Earl of Northumberland and Lady Eleanor Neville, daughter of Ralph Neville, 1st Earl of Westmorland and his second wife Joan Beaufort.

His maternal uncles included Richard Neville, 5th Earl of Salisbury. His maternal aunts included Cecily Neville. Percy was first cousin to (among others) Edward IV of England, Margaret of Burgundy, George, Duke of Clarence and Richard III of England. He was thus closely related to the House of York.

Percy however followed his father in swearing allegiance to the House of Lancaster. On December 30, 1460, Percy is known to have fought on the Lancastrian side at the Battle of Wakefield. He commanded the Lancastrian van at the Battle of Towton, where he was killed.

He was married to Eleanor Poynings , daughter of Richard Poynings, Lord Poynings. They had two children:
* Henry Percy, 4th Earl of Northumberland (d. 28 April 1489).
* Margaret Percy (born c. 1447). She was married to a younger Sir William Gasgoigne.
