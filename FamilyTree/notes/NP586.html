Ralph Truman WICKER

[I100951]
Ralph Trueman Wicker, age 93, died July 4, 2015, in Buffalo. Ralph was a nearly lifelong resident of Cokato. He was born there on Jan. 29, 1922, and after wedding Vivian Stein on April 10, 1948, was married for 67 years and raised three children there.
After serving in the Marines and being stationed in Hawaii and Japan during and after World War II, Ralph was employed by local companies Johnson Produce and Dura Supreme for many years. One of his true passions, however, was gardening. Many family members and friends got to share in the beauty of Ralph’s flowers and the bounty of his vegetable garden. He also loved reading, traveling, spending time with his family, stamp collecting and serving the Cokato Evangelical Lutheran Church in a variety of roles, including as a church council member and Sunday School teacher.
Ralph is survived by his wife, Vivian, of Monticello; daughters Ann McDonald of Mashpee, Mass., and Kay Wicker of Minneapolis; son Brian (Robin) of St. Michael; and grandchildren Kristin (Tom) Maynard of Mulberry, Fla., and Matt McDonald of Hyannis, Mass. He was preceded in death by his parents, Timothy and Esther Wicker, brother Dennis and sister Donna.
A memorial service will be held Monday, July 13, at 1PM. at Cokato Evangelical Lutheran Church. Visitation will be 4-9 PM Sunday at Swanson-Peterson Funeral Home and Cremation Service of Cokato. In lieu of flowers, memorials can be given in his name to the church.

Richard S. WICKLINE

[I083702]
MONTAGUE - Richard S. Wickline , 72, of 19 Laurel Lane, died Saturday, March 17, 2012, at home, surrounded by his loving family.
He was born in Camden-on-Gawley, West Virginia, Feb. 10, 1940, the son of William and Molly (Foster) Wickline . He was educated in West Virginia schools and later married Marie Paciorek in Greenfield. They celebrated their 48th wedding anniversary on March 11, 2012. All of their married life was in Deerfield, moving to Montague four years ago.
In early years, he worked at Oxford Pickle Co., division of John E. Cain and later he worked as a locomotive electrician for B&M Railroad, East Deerfield, retiring in 1990.
He was a volunteer fireman for Deerfield and a EMT for Deerfield Rescue Vehicle.
Besides his wife Marie of Montague, he leaves two sons, Richard Wickline and his wife Kim of Montague and Michael Wickline and his companion Aimee Gochinski of Deerfield; a daughter, Denise Baranoski and her husband Roger of Guilford, Vt.; four brothers, David and his wife Louella of Milton, Fla., Robert and his wife Glorianne of Turners Falls, Larry and his wife Ann of Georgia and James of Virginia Beach; eight grandchildren, William and Matt Baranoski of Guilford, Kaeli Wickline of Greenfield, Molly and Ethan Wickline of South Deerfield, Josh Hastings of South Deerfield, Justin Hastings of Greenfield and Jaimee Stafford of Montague; two great-grandchildren, Carter Malovin and Carson Stafford.
A brother, Danny, and a sister, Carol, predeceased him.
Calling hours will be Thursday from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. in the Wrisley Funeral Home Chapel with a religious service at 1 with the Rev. Jeremiah Rhood, pastor of the South Deerfield Congregational Church officiating.
Donations in his memory are suggested to Baystate Franklin Medical Oncology Patient Needs Fund, 164 High St., Greenfield, MA 01301.
The Recorder, Greenfield, 20 March 2012
