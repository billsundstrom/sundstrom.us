Mary Janice MCCOY

[I033804]
Mary Janice "Jan" McCarthy fought a long battle with cancer. But that did not keep her from going to see her son Brendan run his first marathon. He raised more than $2,500 in pledges on behalf of the American Cancer Society when he ran in the 1993 Revco-Cleveland Marathon.
"I thought what he was doing was really neat. It's even more neat that he was doing it for me," she said from her bed in a van.
Mrs. McCarthy, 54, of Rocky River, died Wednesday at her home.
She was a member of St. Christopher Catholic Church. Over the 12-year course of her illness, Mrs. McCarthy, a devout Catholic, made friends through frequent at-home Masses.
People who attended the Masses said they came not so much to comfort Mrs. McCarthy, but for what they learned from her courage, humor and acceptance of her situation.
Mrs. McCarthy was born in Bradford, Pa., and graduated from Villa Maria College in Erie, Pa.
She was a substitute teacher in Lakewood and Rocky River schools, and a real estate agent for HGM Hilltop Realty. She also was a real estate investor.
Mrs. McCarthy is survived by her husband, Terrance B.; daughters Colleen S. of Raleigh, N.C., and Megan A. of Rocky River; sons Brendan T. of Denver, and Daniel M. of Orlando, Fla.; mother Dorothea McCoy of Bradford, Pa.; two sisters; and two brothers.
Services will be at 9:30 a.m. today at St. Christopher Catholic Church, 20141 Detroit Rd., Rocky River. McGorray Bros. Funeral Home in Lakewood is handling arrangements.
The Plain Dealer, Cleveland, 1 June 1996

Laura Evelyn MCCREARY

[I052964]
Laura Evelyn Lebak died on November 15, 2016, at the Missouri Slope Lutheran Care Center in Bismarck, North Dakota.
Evelyn, as she was known by her relatives and friends, was born July 19, 1924, in Portage, Cambria County, Pennsylvania, to Wilson and Laura (Knee) McCreary.
She graduated in 1942 from high school in Portage and moved to Harrisburg, PA, to work as a quality control checker in a military clothing factory. During this time, she met her future husband, Henry I. Lebak, who was on leave prior to deployment to Germany. Their courtship consisted mainly of letters written to each other.
Upon Henryʼs discharge from the Army, they were married on October 14, 1945, in Portage. Following the marriage, Evelyn and Henry moved to the family farm seven miles south of Alexander. They farmed with Henryʼs father, John Lebak. John taught his Pennsylvania Dutch daughter-in-law the art of Norwegian cooking, which she excelled.
She was a member of the Highland Lutheran Church until they moved to Alexander in 1980 when they joined Trinity Lutheran Church. She was extremely involved in the community (American Legion Auxiliary, PTA, Trinity Quilters, Alexander Senior Center and a local whist club). She served as Curator of the Lewis and Clark Trail Museum in Alexander for more than twenty-five years. Henry died in 1999. Due to health reasons, she moved to Bismarck in 2002.
She is survived by her son, Henry J. Lebak and his wife, Margaret, of Bismarck, three grandchildren, Laura Colby (Jamie) of Arlington Heights, Illinois, Erik Lebak of Fargo, North Dakota, and Rebecca Sweet (Nick) of Knoxville, Tennessee, two great-grandchildren, Erika and James Colby, one nephew, Daniel Greenaway, Uinta, Utah, and her special friends Rae and Larry Olson and Kay and Rachel Power.
She was preceded in death by her husband; a son who died at birth; her parents; her sister, Margaret Greenaway; and her niece Marlene Richards.
Per her wishes, there will be no service. A family memorial get-together and the inurnment of her cremated remains next to her husband at Highland Cemetery southwest of Alexander will be held at a later date.
Her family thanks the staff at Missouri Slope Lutheran Care Center for all of their wonderful services provided to Laura.
