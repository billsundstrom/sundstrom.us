William Francis MCINARNAY

[I031172]
William F. McInarnay, Sr., 90, of Jacksonville, affectionately known as Papa to his large and loving family passed away March 25, 2013 in the Hospice unit of St. Vincent's Hospital.
He was the youngest son of the late Dan and Mary Elizabeth (Peterson) McInarnay. He lived his entire life in Jacksonville, Florida. He was a member of Cedar Creek Baptist Church and served in the US Army in the Pacific during WWII, where he was awarded the Silver Star, Purple Heart and numerous other citations. His memberships and activities include the American Legion and Veterans of Foreign Wars. He was a respected member of the business and political community of Jacksonville throughout his life, establishing Jax Plumbing and Septic Co. early in his career. He was a founder and charter member of F.O.W.A. a statewide organization representing his industry.
His wife of 59 years, Esther McInarnary, preceded him in death in 2002. Survivors include his sons, William F. McInarnay Jr. (Lana), Gerald C. McInarnay (Elaine), Michael McInarnay (Tammy), daughter, Donna J. Peacock (Danny), 10 grandchildren, 15 great grandchildren and 1 great great grandchild and numerous nieces and nephews.
Funeral Services will be held on Saturday, March 30, 2013 at 11:00 AM at Cedar Creek Baptist Church with Dr. John Montgomery officiating. Interment with military honors will follow at Riverside Memorial Park. The family will receive friends on Friday, March 29, 2013 from 5:00 until 8:00 PM at FRASER FUNERAL HOME, 8168 Normandy Boulevard.
Florida Times-Union, 28 March 2013

David Glenn MCKAY

[I034811]
David passed peacefully in Phoenix on February 10, 2011. He was born in Gadsden, AL on February 24, 1939. Soon after, his family moved to Ajo, AZ where he spent his youth growing up with his three brothers Jim, John and Bill. He graduated from Ajo High School in 1957 and went on to play quarterback as a student athlete for the Gila Monsters of Eastern Arizona College. McKay received both his bachelor's and master's degrees from Northern Arizona University (Arizona State College). He retired after 35 years of service to the Flagstaff Unified School District where he served as principal of Thomas Knoles Elementary School (1987-98), Principal of Christensen Elementary School (1982-87), assistant Principal of Christensen and Killip Elementary Schools (1976-82), title III coordinator (1973-76), assistant Principal of Mt. Elden Elementary School (1968-73), Flagstaff Schools first elementary guidance counselor (1966-68) and science teacher for Flagstaff Junior High School (1963-66). David proudly founded Big Brothers of Northern Arizona in 1968 and served as its executive director for 40 years. In 2000, Governor Jane Dee Hull appointed McKay to the Citizens Clean Election Commission where he served out his five year term. In addition, McKay served as a member of the investigative committee for the Arizona Dental Board during the same time period. David is survived by his wife of 46 years, Kay; son Gibson and daughter-in-law Rebecca; son Paul and daughter-in-law Heather; and five grandsons Jack, Finn, Sam, Charlie and Zach. Services will be held at 1:00 PM on Saturday, February 19th at the Little America Grand Ball Room (2515 E. Butler Ave., Flagstaff). Directly following the service, a ceremony and tree planting will be conducted at Thomas Knoles Elementary School (4005 E. Butler Ave., Flagstaff). In lieu of flowers, the family requests a donation be made to Big Brothers and Big Sisters of Northern Arizona (1338 W. Forest Meadow Rd. #130, Flagstaff, AZ 86001). Family and friends are encouraged to share their memories of David by visiting the online obituary Guest Book at www.ALMoore-Grimshaw.com or www.mem.com. Arrangements under the direction of A.L. Moore-Grimshaw Bethany Chapel, Phoenix, AZ.

John Bruce MCKAY

[I034807]
John "Johnny" Bruce McKay, 77, passed away at Reche Canyon Rehabilitation Center on Wednesday, Aug. 15.
He had been a resident of Fontana for 52 years, the last 46 years in the same home.
A native of Alabama, John was born on May 29, 1935 to Paul and Elizabeth McKay. John served in the U.S. Army for two terms, concluding with the rank of SP/4. After attending some college in North Dakota, John came to Fontana to work at Kaiser Steel. He worked long after the mill closed and retired from California Steel Industries as a pipefitter with a total of approximately 35 years at the two mills. John was an honest, hard-working man who enjoyed spending time with his family. He was a true patriot and flew the American flag at his home every day.
He was preceded in death by his parents.
John is survived by his loving wife of 31 years, Jadelle "Judy," of Fontana. He also leaves behind daughters Diana of Ontario, Cindy of Massachusetts, Marilyn of Long Beach, Shelley of Victorville and Kelly of Florida. Also mourning his passing are sons Bruce of Massachusetts and Billy of Ontario. Two brothers are Jim and Bill McKay, both of Arizona. Grandchildren are Misty Brett, John, Bobby, Christen, Jacqueline, Alex, Michael, Christopher, Jack, William, Lauren, Jared, Karlis, Jeffery, Joseph, Brandon, Anthony, Kody, Kaitlyn, Brookelyn, Hannah, Brittney, Kevin and Audry. Great-grandchildren are Randy, Michael B. Howard Jr., Catelyn, Phoenix, Malika, and Alexis. He will be deeply missed by his many friends and relatives. He was loved by all who knew him.
John was laid to rest with military honors at Riverside National Cemetery in Riverside on Wednesday, Aug. 22. Dickey Mortuary, Fontana, was in charge of arrangements.
