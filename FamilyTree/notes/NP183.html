Max William Fleming, 64, Ellendale, formerly of Route 1, Owatonna, died Friday, June 19, at Veterans Hospital, Minneapolis. Services were at 1 p.m. today at First Lutheran Church of Ellendale, with the Rev. Richard Spande officiating. Burial will be in Groveland Cemetery, Dundas. He married Margaret Hatfield on July 6, 1950, in Northfield. He was employed by the VA Hospital, Owatonna Human Relations Center from 1960 to 1980. He was a foster parent to many children. He was a veteran of World War II, serving as a member of the U.S. Army Paratroopers when he was wounded in the Battle of the Bulge, for which he received a Purple Heart. Survivors include: his father, Dr. Paul D. Fleming of Beaver City, Neb.; his wife; five sons, Pierce of Lexington, Ky., Stephen of Owatonna, Jeffrey of Spring Valley, Ralph of Minneapolis and Keith of Des Moines, Iowa; two daughters, Mary Harris of Waseca and Mrs. Dale (Rita) Seath of North Mankato; one brother, Paul Fleming of Beaver City, Neb.; and 13 grandchildren. Carl Lessley FLETCHER

[I067864]
One of the last Humboldt County Railroaders has begun his final journey, Carl Lessley Fletcher passed away on May 26, 2012. He is now at peace after a long eleven year illness. Carl was born on June 11, 1931 in Eureka, CA to Royal Sheldon Fletcher and Edna Alice (Purdy) Fletcher. He was one of eight children. Carl graduated from South Fork High School in 1950 and started his railroad career soon after at South Fork. On June 7, 1952 he married the love of his life Patricia Ann Still; they will celebrate 60 years of marriage this year. Together they had three children, Richard Fletcher of Redcrest, Linda (Rodney) Hill of Lincoln, NE and Steven (Fawn) Fletcher of Redding. Nine grandchildren and spouses, Ryan (Racheal) Henry of Fortuna, Richard (Eliza) Fletcher, US Army, Germany, Michael (Sarah) Henry of Mitchell, NE, Ann Marie (Casey) Andrews of Reno, NV, Trevor (Amber) Fletcher of Redding, Adam (Janell) Gordon, US Marines, Camp Pendleton, CA, Tracee Fletcher of Redding, Ashley Gordon of Blair, NE and Travis Fletcher of Redding. Great grandchildren, Katrina, Gabriel, Alyssa, Austin and Lane Fletcher, Presley, Kyra, Michaela and Andrew Henry, Megan and Joshua Gordon, Isabella Erickson, Katelyn & Allison Andrews and Jessi Shinn. He is survived by his sisters, Marilyn Milligan of Fortuna, Evelyn Smith of Eureka, Phyllis (Johnny) Stark of Eureka, brother in-law and sister in-law, Skip and Clara Littlepage of Redding, Lynn and Pat Still of Scotts Valley and numerous "favorite" nieces and nephews. He was preceded in death by his brothers Edward and Newell Fletcher and his sisters Jane Philp and Virginia Yegge, brother in-laws Teke Philp and Merle Smith. Along with his love of anything to do with trains, he enjoyed his hunting trips with family and friends and our yearly RV camping trips to the coast to abalone dive. After 40 plus years, he retired from the railroad; his first vacation was a month long trip to Alaska to visit his friends Willie and Billie Machado on Prince of Wales Island. His retirement hobby was his love of gardening, from spring through summer we could find him in his garden with each year it getting larger. Family and friends never left without loads of fresh produce. Graveside services will be held on Saturday June 2, 2012, 11:00 am at Sunset Memorial Cemetery in Eureka, CA. A Celebration of Life and luncheon will follow at Redwood Grange Hall in Redcrest, CA. Please sign the guest book at www.Times-Standard.com, click obits.
Eureka Times-Standard, 31 May 2012

Mildred Lee Darnall
Nov. 11, 1902 - Nov. 9, 2001
Mildred Lee Darnall, 99 of Vale died Nov. 9, 2001, in a Vale Care Center. Funeral services will be Wednesday, at 11 a.m. at the Vale Christian Church. Interment will be at 2 p.m. in Parkview Cemetery, New Plymouth. Services under the direction of Lienkaemper Chapel.
Mildred L. Darnall was born Nov. 11, 1902, to Leighton and Bessie Flock in Sumner, Neb. She was reared and educated there until 1912, when her family moved to Idaho.
She married Roy W. Darnall Oct. 26, 1922, in Fruitland, Idaho and they made their first home in Glenns Ferry, Idaho. They later homesteaded a ranch at Harper, moving to Los Angeles, Calif., in 1942 and returning in 1947. From that time they ranched and owned and operated the Harper General Store until, retiring in 1967 in Ontario. She was a member of the Ontario Church of Christ.
Survivors include her daughter and son-in-law, Lois and Lloyd Palmer of Vale, three grandsons; Steve Palmer and his wife, Carol, Jerome, Darnall and Nathan Darnall, five great grandchildren, two step great-grandchildren; five great-great-grandchildren, seven step great great grandchildren; and several nieces and nephews. She was preceded in death by her husband, a son, three brothers and a sister.
The Argus Observer,

Opal Elaine FLUGUM

[I027745]
Opal Elaine Bergerson was born February 4, 1929, to Oscar I. and Emma Davidson Flugum in Albert Lea. She was a lifelong resident of Freeborn County and attended Albert Lea and Ellendale schools, graduating from Ellendale High School in 1947. Opal graduated from the University of Minnesota School of Agriculture in 1949. On August 26, 1956, she was united in marriage to LaVerne A. Bergerson in Geneva. Opal worked for several grocery businesses in the area - Leonard Johnsrud in Albert Lea, Mayfield, Bob's Food Store in Clarks Grove, and Wintergreen Food Co-op in Albert Lea for 22 years. She was a member of the Community Lutheran Church in Geneva and the Geneva Senior Citizens. Opal enjoyed music and handiwork. She had tatted many crosses for the baptismal towels for the Church. One of the highlights of Opal's life was waiting on Louis Armstrong in 1952 when she was working at Johnsrud's Grocery in Albert Lea. Survivors include her husband, LaVerne of Clarks Grove; sons: Peter Bergerson of Clarks Grove, Aaron and wife, Connie Bergerson of Albert Lea, and Mark and wife, April Bergerson, also of Albert Lea; grandchildren: Chris, Ashley, Tracy, Travis, Danielle, Sara, Cheyenne, Leah, and Amanda; 2 great-grandchildren; sisters, Fern Wahlers of Geneva and Irene and husband, Shirly Sletten of Owatonna; brothers: Virgil O. and wife, Nancy of Castle Rock, CO, Robert and wife, Barbara of Dallas, OR, and Rolland and wife, Cindy of Aiea, HI; and many nieces and nephews. William FOBES

[I025297]
Lieut. William Fobes ran a ferry from Little Compton across the Sakonnet River to Newport, what is today part of Middletown, R.I. He lived on the Great West Road on Lot # 4 of the 'great lots' as designated by the founders, near Windmill Hill.
He first married (about 1679) Elizabeth Southworth, daughter of Constant and Elizabeth (Collier) Southworth, who died in 1681. He married second Martha (Pabodie) Seabury, widow of Samuel Seabury and daughter of William and Elizabeth (Alden) Pabodie, born in Duxbury 24 Feb. 1650, died 25 Jan. 1712 in Little Compton.
