Albert John ETCHANDY

[I061673]
Albert J. Etchandy , 72, a pioneer Orange County citrus farmer and co-owner of the once-popular Etchandy Brothers Farms, died Friday.
Mr. Etchandy , of Anaheim, died of natural causes at Canyon General Kaiser Foundation Hospital, said his nephew, Robert Etchandy Jr.
The Etchandy brothers -- Albert, Robert and Dominic Jr. -- cultivated citrus and strawberry crops. The brothers' farms were some of the largest strawberry-producing farms in the county, Robert Etchandy Jr. said.
The Etchandy Brothers Farms operated for 35 years. The brothers ended their farming partnership in 1983.
The largest of the strawberry farms was located at the corner of Lakeview Avenue and La Palma Avenue in Anaheim, next to the Santa Ana River. It closed in 1987, and the Etchandy Commerce Center now stands in its place.
Mr. Etchandy was a citrus and strawberry farmer until his death. He leased farmland near Disneyland and owned farming property in Ontario and Corona.
The Etchandy family, including the brothers' father, Dominic, and grandfather, Martin, has owned and operated citrus farms since 1872. Some of the farms are still operated by family members.
He was born in 1917 in Yorba Linda and had lived in Orange County all of his life.
Mr. Etchandy is survived by his wife, Lois of Anaheim; three sons, Ronald and Gary of Anaheim and Van of Corona; two brothers, Robert of Lake Matthews and Dominic Jr. of Anaheim; and seven
grandchildren.
Rosary will be recited at 7:30 p.m. Sunday at Backs-Kaulbars Baggott & Schacht Anaheim Mortuary. Mass will be said at 10 a.m. Monday at San Antonio de Padua Catholic Church, Anaheim Hills. Burial will follow at Holy Sepulcher Cemetery, Orange.
The Orange County Register, 19 August 1989

Jacob Frederick ETZEL

[I004442]
When Jacob was 16, he enlisted in the German army as a Pioneer in the Elite Royal Regiment, 8th Wurttembergers Infantry Regiment #126. Jacob was mustered out at age 18*. Jacob joined the military so he could claim his inheritance from his mother, Anna Magdelena Etzel. US Census records indicate Jacob immigrated to the United States in 1891-1892. Jacob settled in Fort Dodge, Iowa following other immigrates from Weilheim an der Teck, Esslingen, Baden-Württemberg, Germany who had settled in this area. After Jacob arrived in Fort Dodge, his first job was with the railroad. After that job, he went to work at the Fort Dodge Planing Mill as a foreman. The planing mill was destroyed by fire and Jacob no longer had a job. That is when he decided to start his own business as a carpenter and contractor. Jacob built several homes (including his,his son, Herman and his son-in-law, Jacob Brown) and several business buildings in Fort Dodge, Iowa.

EUPHROSYNE

[I015164]
Empress Euphrosyne (died ca. 1210) was the wife of Alexius III Angelus who secured the election of her husband to the throne by wholesale bribery. She virtually took the government into her hands, working through the minister Vatatzes and temporarily restored the waning influence of the monarchy over the nobles. Her reputation for profligate extravagance undercut the dynasty's popularity, however, in spite of her talent for government. In the sack of Constantinople in 1203 by the crusaders she was abandoned by her husband but joined him in exile and died in Epirus.
