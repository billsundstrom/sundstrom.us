James Harold STACEY

[I083136]
Vermont House Speaker. Known as J. Harold Stacey, he grew up in Royalton, Vermont and attended Dartmouth College and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. He was a Navy veteran of World War I, receiving an Ensign's commission and completing training as a pilot. He settled in Windsor, where he operated a business dealing in ice, building materials and home heating fuel. A Republican, he served in local offices including village trustee. He was also active in other businesses and trade associations, including board of directors member for the Vermont Railway and President of the Northeastern Retail Lumber Association. Stacey was active in the American Legion, and served as Vermont's commander. He was also active in Masonic groups, and served as Grand Commander of Vermont's Knights Templar, Grand Master of Vermont's Masonic Grand Lodge, and Grand High Priest of Vermont's Royal Arch Masons. In 1942 he was elected to the Vermont House of Representatives, and he served four terms, 1943 to 1951. From 1949 to 1951 Stacey was Speaker of the House. In 1950 he lost the Republican primary for Governor. He later served on the state Fish and Game Commission. In 1954 he won a seat in the Vermont State Senate, and he served one term, 1955 to 1957. In June, 1956 Stacey was appointed to head the Vermont Development Commission.

Ralph STAFFORD

[I008964]
Sir Ralph Stafford (24 September 1301 - 31 August 1372, also Ralph de Stafford) was an English soldier and nobleman, and became a founding Knight of the Garter in 1348.

Stafford was summoned to Parliament as the 2nd Baron Stafford from 1337 to 1350. He was created 1st Earl of Stafford in 1351. He served as a military leader under King Edward II, fighting in campaigns in Scotland, then in Brittany, France, where he was captured during the Siege of Nantes. He died in 1372 at Tunbridge, Kent.
