Betty Jane ALDEN

[I086869]
Betty Jane Alden was the daughter of Grace Isadora Taggart and Alfred Edward Fruin. Her mother remarried in the spring of 1921, and Betty was adopted by Minor Smith Alden, around that time. He was the only father she ever knew.
Betty Jane married Jack Harvey Johnson on June 18, 1938 in Ohio.They started their family in Jackson, Michigan and had one daughter and three sons (one son is still living).
In 1951 Jack, Betty, and the kids moved to Texas in search of work and warmer temperatures. They settled in Beaumont, Texas and all of the children graduated from French High School. After their children moved out away from home, Betty and Jack chose to end their marriage and remarried other people. She married W.C. Gardner in Jefferson County, and they lived in Beaumont. She later moved to the nearby city of Vidor.
Betty worked as an assembly clerk for Rogers Enterprise in Beaumont. Her favorite color was orange, and she liked collecting rooster decor. She also enjoyed going to Jefferson Theatre to see concerts, plays, and other such performances and has a seat dedicated in her name.
At the time of Betty's passing from cancer, she was being cared for by the staff at Lindberg Heath Center in Beaumont. Her memorial took place at Memorial Funeral Home in Vidor. She chose to donate her body to science with the hope that others may have a better future.
She loved all of her family and friends dearly, and enjoyed hosting the family reunions and gatherings for everything from holidays to birthdays. She had an entire wall of her home set aside for photos of her children and their families, which she added to frequently. She also regularly kept in touch with her 3 half-sisters: Frances June (Brown) Askew, Marjorie "Marjie" (Brown) Ten Brink, and Priscilla "Jean" (Alden) Mathews. She will always be loved and missed by her family and friends.

Cynthia Louisa ALDEN

[I003936]
Mrs. Cynthia L. Griffin, the aged mother of James F. Griffin, 1127 Monroe street, met her death on Saturday evening about 9 oʼclock by falling down the cellar stairs.
She had gone from her room through the dining room which was partly dark into the kitchen, unobserved by the family who were at the front of the house. In returning through the dining room she evidently missed the hall door and opened the one leading to the cellar instead. She was missed a short time later, and after a search, was found dead at the foot of the stairs with a deep cut upon her head and her neck was probably broken, so that death must have been nearly or quite instantaneous.
Mrs. Griffin lived in Topeka 32 years, and had three sons, James F. Griffin of Topeka; Joseph A. Griffin of Chicago, and Rev. P. M. Griffin, Matfield, Mass., and all will be present at the funeral, which will take place this afternoon at 5 oʼclock.
Mrs. Griffin, whose maiden name was Cynthia L. Alden, was born 88 years ago in Claremont, N. H., and was a lineal descendant of sixth generation of John Alden, of the Mayflower.
She was a member of the First Congregational church, and in years past was active in temperance and relief work.
Topeka Daily Capital, 23 July 1901, 3:3.

Judy Kay ALDEN

[I077165]
Judy Kay Robinson, 67 VISTA -- Judy Kay Robinson died peacefully at her home in Vista, California on December 29, 2010. She is survived by her former husband, Ernest Robinson of Vista; daughter Julie Lynne (Robinson) Calfin, son-in-law, Matthew Calfin, and grandson, Carter Calfin of Carlsbad; and sister-in-law, Elissa Alden of Jackson, Mich. Judy Kay is remembered fondly for her generosity, strength of character, and love of learning. Judy Kay was born to Richard and Mildred Alden of Michigan Center, Mich., on March 16, 1943. From an early age, Judy Kay had an affinity for books and learning. Upon graduating from Michigan Center High School, she was the only member of the class of 1961 to attend university. Judy Kay loved Michigan State University, especially living in a dormitory, studying art history, and making ceramic pots. She graduated in 1965 and began the first chapter of her adult life as an Art Teacher in Jackson, Mich. On a trip to Orange County, Calif., she met her husband-to-be, Ernest Robinson, and the two were married in 1967. After making their home in Buena Park, Calif., she became a Kindergarten teacher and immediately loved her new job. She would often say with a laugh, "I can't believe they pay me to do this!" In 1973, she and her husband relocated to Vista, Calif., and she began the motherhood chapter of her life. She was a loving and devoted mother who delighted in her daughter. She later returned to the classroom in the Vista Unified School District where she served as the School Improvement Program resource teacher at Beaumont Elementary School and later the Gifted and Talented Education teacher at Olive and Beaumont Elementary Schools. Soon, she settled into her calling as a Kindergarten teacher at Casita Center for Technology, Science and Math where she taught among her friends for 20 years. After her divorce in 1992, Judy Kay began a new chapter of her life by buying a home and traveling the world with her friend, Cheryl Vinson. When in far-off places like China, Russia, or Australia, Judy Kay always said, "Can you believe a girl from Michigan Center is here?" Upon retirement in 2006, Judy Kay missed her students but thoroughly enjoyed being able to "do what I want to do when I want to do it." She spent her time reading, traveling, going to movies, meeting friends for a beer, and enjoying her one and only grandchild, Carter. Perhaps the greatest joy of her later years was being "Gram" to "the cutest and smartest child on the planet," as she would often describe Carter. In the final chapter of her life, she faced cancer with courage, grace, and no regrets. She died the way she lived---filled with love and concern for others. Her legacy as a mother, grandmother, and teacher is a testament to a life well lived. Judy Kay was preceded in death by her parents and her brother, Michael Alden of Jackson, Mich. Judy Kay will be interred on April 9, 2011 in Grass Lake, Mich. A private celebration of her life will be held in Carlsbad, Calif., and in Jackson, Mich. To honor her love of books, her family suggests a donation to the library at Casita Center for Technology, Science and Math. Please make checks payable to "Casita Center" and send them to: 260 Cedar Road, Vista, Calif. 92084. Sign the Guest Book online obits.nctimes.com
The San Diego Union Tribune, 9 January 2011

William Rockwood ALDEN

William A. ALDEN, 55, of 163 Upton avenue, a lifelong resident of Battle Creek and active in the cityʼs business circles for the last 32 years, passed away at 2:30 oʼclock Friday afternoon at a local hospital, death being due to heart trouble. Mr. ALDEN had been in falling health for the last two years, but his condition did not become serious until two months ago.
For the last five years of his life Mr. ALDEN conducted the ALDEN grocery, at 572 Maple street, and prior to that time was associated with his brother, Horace G. ALDEN, in the Alden Bros. Lumber company.
Mr. ALDEN was born in Calhoun county, just outside the city limits of Battle Creek. He was educated in the public schools of this city and was a member of the graduating
class of 1897 at the Battle Creek High school. In the spring of 1898, he entered the lumber business with his brother. The firm engaged in the purchase of standing timber
throughout Michigan and Northern Indiana and one of the first contracts after Mr. ALDEN joined his brother was that of furnishing 25,000 ties for the electric railway which was being constructed in 1898 between South Bend and Goshen, Ind.
About seven years ago Mr. ALDEN severed his connection with the Alden Bros. Lumber company and for a short time was associated with the Montgomery Motor Sales.
Five years ago he purchased a grocery store at 572 Maple street which he operated up to the time of his death.
Mr. ALDEN was married 27 years ago to Miss Jessie ATT, of Bristol, Ind., who survives him. He also leaves a daughter, Miss Norma ALDEN, at home; a brother, Horace G. ALDEN, of 304 Main street; and several nieces and nephews.
The deceased was a past Exaited Ruler of the Battle Creek B. P.O.E., No. 131, a member of the Knights of Pythias, the Quality Service Grocers association and the United Commercial Travelers.
Funeral services will be held at 1:30 oʼclock Sunday afternoon at Pendillʼs Funeral Home, 129, Main street. The Rev. Carleton Brooks MILLER, pastor of the First
Congregational church, will officiate and burial will be made in Memorial Park cemetery.

Wilma Irene ALDEN

[I076999]
Wilma Irene Alden was born July 2, 1927 to Homer J. Alden and Ethel May Lindal Alden, at Vandercook Lake, Summit Township, Jackson County, Michigan. She attended public schools in Jackson, Michigan, and worked in an early television and electronics assembly plant in Jackson.
She married William Stuart in 1947 and from that union had a son and daughter, Danny and Connie. After Mr. Stuart's accidental death, she came to California, met and married Joseph Powell of Fresno.
In this union she excelled as a dedicated and loving mother and wife as well as being involved in activities outside the home. She was an active church worker teaching Sunday School, VBS, girls' and women's groups and church wide activities. She studied Early Childhood Education at Mount San Antonio College, and became director of two different preschools. She then returned to her electronics work, building component parts for defense and aerospace companies. She helped build power supplies for the Hubble Telescope.
Wilma was the 8th great granddaughter of John Alden, the youngest signer of the Mayflower Compact and resident of the Plymouth Colony, of which she was very proud in her earlier years, and was a member of the Mayflower Society.
Upon moving to Atwater in 1988, she continued to be involved in church activities as well as partnering with her husband in a retail/service business in Merced. Upon retirement, she and Joseph focused their time and attention to service to their church.
Wilma suffered long-term disability related to common dementia and spent her final months in the care of Hinds Hospice. She was preceded in death by her parents, brother, Virgil Alden, and eldest grandson, Nathaniel Skai Stuart.
A Celebration of Life will be held at 10:00 a.m., Thursday, May 15, 2014 at First Baptist Church, 7264 W. Myrtle Avenue in Winton. The burial will follow at Winton District Cemetery in Winton.
In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to Hinds Hospice of Merced, or First Baptist Church of Winton.
