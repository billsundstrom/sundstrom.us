Edwin William KEIGHTLEY

[I068158]
Edwin William Keightley (August 7, 1843 - May 4, 1926) was a politician from the U.S. state of Michigan.
Keightley was born on a farm near Scott in Lagrange County, Indiana. His parents, Peter L. and Elizabeth (Winter) Keightly, emigrated from Lincolnshire, England; the former in 1831, and the latter in 1828. They settled on the farm in Van Buren township in 1836, and soon after they were married. Keightley attended the local common schools, Lagrange Academy, and Valparaiso Collegiate Institute. He was graduated from the law department of the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor in 1865 and was admitted to the bar. He commenced practice at White Pigeon, Michigan, several miles northwest of his family home in Scott. While practicing law, he was also editor of the White Pigeon Republican, a staunch advocate of Republican Party views and policy.
In 1867, Keightley moved to Constantine, Michigan, and entered into a law partnership with Judge S. C. Coffinberry, which ended in 1869, on amicable terms. He was married on July 14, 1868, to Mary Mitchell, the daughter of Thomas Mitchell, a long-time resident of Constantine, and closely identified with its manufacturing and material interests.
Keightley's former residence (left), located in the Logan Circle neighborhood of Washington, D.C.
In 1872, he was elected prosecuting attorney of St. Joseph County, serving until January 21, 1874 when he was appointed by Michigan Governor John J. Bagley to be judge of the 15th circuit court of Michigan, comprising the counties of St. Joseph and Branch County. He was elected to the position in the spring of 1875.
In 1876, he was elected as a Republican from Michigan's 4th congressional district to the 45th United States Congress, serving from March 4, 1877 to March 3, 1879.
Keightley was appointed by U.S. President Rutherford B. Hayes as the Third Auditor of the United States Treasury Department and served from April 30, 1879 to April 30, 1885, when he resigned. He resumed the practice of his profession in Chicago, Illinois. He moved to Constantine, Michigan, in 1899 and engaged in agricultural pursuits until his death there. He was interred in Constantine Cemetery.

Jacob Peter KELDSEN

[I047081]
Keldsen Rites Here Saturday -
Former Resident Died at Wood River -
Jacob Peter Keldsen was born September 9, 1874 at Astrup, Denmark. At the age of 18 years he came to the United States.
As a baby he was baptized in the Lutheran church in Denmark and later confirmed in the same church, and he remained a Lutheran all his life.
The first few years after coming to this country he worked on farms, after which he moved to Dannebrog and for seventeen yeas was a partner with Fred Jorgensen in the dray and livery business.
On December 28, 1899, he was united in marriage to Miss Alma E. Isaacson. Three children were born to this union: Ulric G., who passed away February 20, 1943, at the age of 42 years; Lloyd F., who at present is in the U. S. Navy, and Jack K. of Cheyenne, Wyoming.
In 1913 the family {moved} to a farm near St. Paul, Nebr., and in 1918 moved to Lake Andes, South Dakota. Returning to St. Paul and near Cushing in 1922 where they farmed until in 1929, when they bought a farm near Wood River, Nebr., where Mr. Keldsen passed away November 2, 1943, at the age of 69 years, 1 month and 23 days. He had been a loving and devoted husband and father, a true friend and neighbor, and upright in all his dealings.
Besides his wife, two sons, three daughters-in-law and four grandchildren, Mr. Keldsen leaves three sisters, Mrs. P. S. Petersen of Dannebrog, Mrs. T. J. Nelsen of St. Paul, and Mrs. Jens Beck in Canada, and one brother, Kjeld Keldsen in Denmark. One brother and one sister died in infancy in Denmark. There are also a number of other relatives and many friends.
Among the relatives from a distance who attended the funeral were: Mrs. Lloyd Keldsen of San Francisco, Calif., Mrs. Lela Keldsen and Mr. and Mrs. Jack Keldsen of Cheyenne, Wyo., Mr. and Mrs. T. J. Nelsen and three daughters of St. Paul, Ray Paulsen of Lake Andes, So. Dak., Mr. and Mrs. Glenn Paulsen and son, Burton of Palmer, Mr. and Mrs. Emerald Nielsen of Upland, John Isaacson and Mr. and Mrs. Leon Bartunek of Rockville, Mr. and Mrs. Anton Woton and daughters, Mr. and Mrs. George Woton and son, and Mrs. Molly Woton, all of Shelton, and Mr. and Mrs. V. E. Evans of Grand Island, and many friends.
The pallbearers were Cecil McKaig, John Schwartz, Ralph Miller, Carl Johnson, Irvin Crosser and Harry Glenn, all neighbors and friends of Mr. Keldsen from near Wood River.
After the funeral the Royal Neighbors served lunch and coffee at the P. S. Petersen home to the relataives and friends who had came a distance and from other towns.
The funeral was held Saturday afternoon from the Lutheran church in Dannebrog with Rev. L. C. Hansen in charge of the services. Interment was in the family plot in Oak Ridge cemetery. Jacobsen Mortuary was in charge of the funeral arrangements. Many relatives and friends attended the funeral as a last respect to the memory of the deceased.
The Dannebrog News, 11 November 1943
