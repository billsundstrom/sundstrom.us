Gertrude Enola HALE

[I089564]
It is with great sorrow that on October, 9th we bid good-by to our Mother, Grandmother and Friend, Gertrude Enola Doubleday , a woman of great beauty both inside and out. The youngest of nine children, Gertrude was born in Suttercreek, California on October, 15, 1918 to Annie and Harvey Hale. She was a highly spirited child who found joy in everything around her. Gertrude was a person who didn't worry about tomorrow but let life unravel before her with all the adventure and excitement that it had to offer. At sixteen, Gertrude went to live in Oakland, California with her older sister Mabel to finish high school and learn some social graces. Aunt Mabel shared with us that the task was difficult but the results were fabulous. Dental technician school was next for Gertrude and after graduating she began working in San Francisco. In 1940 she met the dashing Eugene Doubleday at a San Francisco dance. They were married in 1941, a union that lasted 49 years and produced three children, Laurena (1943) Carol (1945) and Fred (1951). The war years resulted in Eugene deciding to make the military his career. Gertrude traveled extensively with her husband for the next 25 years encountering many adventures along the way. She witnessed the General Tojo trial in Japan after WWII attended Alaska's Constitutional Convention hearings and in 1958, traveled down the Alcan Highway with 3 children and a dog that had just given birth to puppies. Retirement took Gertrude and Eugene to Monterey, California were the lived until Eugene's death in 1990 at which time Gertrude moved to Idaho to be near her son and daughter. Adventures abounded in Gertrude 's life but she is best remembered for her kindness to all living things, her deep faith in god and the love and unselfishness she showed to her family and friends. We will miss you Mom. Gertrude is survived by Laurena and John Christianson of Boise, ID Carol and Victor Klukkert of Salome, AZ, Fred and Barbara Doubleday of Boise, ID, grandchildren Kevin Mark and Alyssa Doubleday , Heather Littlefield Amy Dydynski, six great-grandchildren and countless friends. The family wishes to thank Aarenbrooke Assisted Living and Heart 'N Home Hospice for their wonderful care of Gertrude.
The Idaho Statesman, Boise, 12 October 2010

Eystein HALFDANSSON

[I008108]
Eystein Halfdansson (Old Norse: Eysteinn Hálfdansson) was the son of Halfdan Hvitbeinn of the House of Yngling according to Heimskringla. He lived around 730, and inherited the throne of Romerike and Vestfold.

His wife was Hild, the daughter of the king of Vestfold, Erik Agnarsson. Erik had no son so Eystein inherited Vestfold.

Eystein went to Varna with some ships to pillage and carried away all livestock and other valuables. However, the king of Varna was king Skjöld who was a great warlock. Skjöld arrived at the beach and saw the sails of Eystein's ships. He waved his cloak and blew into it which caused a boom of one ship to swing and hit Eystein so that he fell overboard and drowned. His body was salvaged and buried in a mound.

Eystein was inherited by his son Halfdan the Mild.

Gudrød HALFDANSSON

[I007654]
From Wikipedia

Gudrød the Hunter (Old Norse: Guđröđr veiđikonung, Modern Icelandic: Guđröđur veiđikonung, Norwegian: Gudrød Veidekonge) was the son of Halfdan the Mild of the House of Yngling and of Liv Dagsdotter of Vestmar, according to Heimskringla, a collection of sagas from thirteenth century Iceland.

Gudröd married Alfhild, a daughter of Alfarin the king of Alfheim (Bohuslän), which was the name of the area between Glomma and Göta älv, and inherited half the province of Vingulmark. They had the son Olaf Gudrødsson.

When Alfhild died, Gudröd sent his warriors to Agder and its king Harald to propose a marriage with his daughter Åsa. However, Harald declined so Gudröd decided to take his daughter by force.

They arrived at night time and when Harald realised that he was attacked he assembled his men and fought well, but died together with his son Gyrd. Gudröd carried away Åsa and married her. He raped her and she gave him a son named Halfdan who would be called Halfdan the Black.

In the fall when Halfdan was a year old, Gudröd was having at a feast in Stiflesund. He was very drunk and in the evening as he was walking on the gangway to leave the ship, an assassin thrust a spear through Gudröd and he was killed. Gudröd's men instantly killed the assassin who turned out to be Åsa's page-boy. Åsa admitted that the page-boy had acted on her behalf.

Hroar HALFDANSSON

[I008580]
Hrođgar (Proto-Norse *Hro-igaizaz, Hrothgar, Hróar, Ro), legendary Danish king.

According to the epic poem Beowulf, he ruled Denmark when the Geatish hero Beowulf arrived to defeat the monster Grendel and he was married to Wealheow. He was supposed to have built a famous hall called Heorot, and according to Saxo Grammaticus in Gesta Danorum, he (Roar or Ro) founded the town Roskilde. His brother Halga (Helgi) who ruled before him had a famous son, Hrothulf (Hrólf Kraki).

Ivar HALFDANSSON

[I008570]
From Wikipedia

Ivar Vidfamne. Ívarr inn vı́đfađmi or Ivar Vidfamne was a semi-legendary king of Sweden c. 655- c.695, and according to the Heimskringla and the Hervarar saga also the king of Norway, Denmark, Saxony and parts of England. He began as king of Scania and conquered Sweden by defeating Ingjald Illråde. He is then said to have conquered all of Scandinavia and parts of England. Because of his harsh rule, many Swedes fled west and populated Wermelandia under its king Olof Trätälja. His last campaign was in Russia where he died, defeated by Odin in disguise. (Another source claims he took his life by drowning himself in the Gulf of Finland.)

His homeland was Scania, but according to the Heimskringla and the Sögubrot, he had to flee Scania when his uncle Gudröd had slain his father Halfdan the Mild. Snorri Sturluson, Historia Norwegiae, Hervarar saga and Upplendinga Konungum tell that Ivar conquered Sweden after Ingjald's suicide, and later returned to take Denmark.

According to Hversu Noregr byggdist, he was the son of Halfdan the Kind (also given as his father in the Heimskringla and the Hervarar saga), son of Harald the Old, son of Valdar, son of Roar (Hrođgar) of the house of Skjöldung (Scylding). According to Hversu, the Lay of Hyndla and Sögubrot, Ivar had a daughter named Aud.

Sögubrot relates that when Ivar was the king of Sweden, he gave his daughter Aud to king Rörek of Zealand, in spite of the fact that she wanted to marry Rörek's brother Helgi. Rörek and Aud had the son Harald Hildetand. Ivar made Rörek kill his brother Helgi, and after this, he attacked and killed Rörek. However, Aud arrived with the Zealand army and chased her father Ivar back to Sweden. The following year, Aud went to Gardariki with her son Harald and many powerful men and married its king Radbart. This was the opportunity for Ivar to conquer Zealand. However, when he learnt that his daughter had married without his permission, he marshalled a great leidang from Denmark and Sweden and went to Gardariki. He was very old at the time. However, when they had arrived at the borders of Radbart's kingdom, Karelia (Karjálabotnar), he threw himself overboard. Harald then returned to Scania to become its ruler. In the Lay of Hyndla, Ivar, Aud, Rörek and Harald appear. Radbart also appears, but there is no information about his relationship with them.

However, according to the Hervarar saga, his daughter was named Alfhild and she was married to Valdar whom Ivar made king of Denmark.

Howard Brackett HALL

[I029026]
CENTERVILLE - Howard Brackett Hall, 84, of Centerville, MA died peacefully on Sunday, December 30 after a brief period of declining health due to congestive heart failure. Son of Howard B. Hall and Katherine (Brown) Hall, Brackett was born on April 8, 1928 in Milton, MA.
Brackett lived a full and active life. He graduated from Tabor Academy in 1945 where he was the president of his class. He attended Bowdoin College before entering the his family's business, Hall & Cole Inc., a wholesale fruit and produce business started in Faneuil Hall, the Quincy Market in Boston and later moved to the NE Produce Center in Chelsea, MA. Together with his brother Stacy and later his son Howard they continued to build this hundred-year-old business until as president he sold it in 2004. In 1962 he purchased Three Hills Orchard in Limerick, ME, a 350-acre apple farm, which was developed as a second business and a lovely home for his mother, Katherine.
Brackett enjoyed the ocean. His family summered in Centerville on Long Beach and later he and his wife Nancy lived for many years in Hyannisport, MA. He enjoyed sailing for most of his life, and raced Wianno Seniors in Hyannisport. He loved to fish at the Horseshoe Shoals. Picnics in the boat with his family were a regular activity in both Hingham Harbor and Hyannisport.
Golfing was a passion for Brackett that started when he was a caddy at Eastward Ho golf course at a young age. He was a longtime member at Cohassett Golf Course in Cohassett, MA and the Hyannisport Club where he played until the end of his life. Sailing and golfing brought Brackett many friendships that he held dear.
Brackett was always involved with his family. He married his wife Nancy (Sturges) on September 23, 1950. Together they raised their family of two children, Howard B. Hall 3rd and Carolyn Hall Schulten of Dover, NH. They had 40 happy years at 15 Forest Lane in Hingham, MA, and at 55 Longwood Ave., Hyannisport, MA, where they summered for many years before making it their permanent home upon retirement.
Brackett was an involved grandfather who took keen interest in the activities of his four granddaughters.
He leaves behind his wife Nancy (Sturges), his son Howard and his wife, Maureen Hurney, daughter Carolyn and her husband, Steve, four granddaughters; Elizabeth, Katherine, Jennifer and Alison and three great-granddaughters.
Visiting hours will be from 3-5 p.m. on Friday, January 4, 2012, at the Doane, Beal and Ames Funeral Home, 160 West Main Street in Hyannis.
Immediate funeral services are private with a memorial service for friends to be announced in the spring.
Donations in Brackett's memory may be made to the Francis Ouimet Scholarship Fund, 300 Arnold Palmer Boulevard, Norton, MA 02766, or the charity of your choice.
Cape Cod Times
