Doris Marie WAGNER

[I018928]
Doris M. Roeder, 76, Rosholt, SD, died Saturday, August 18, 2012, at Sanford Hospital in Fargo, ND.
Funeral services were Wednesday, August 22, at 11:00 a.m. at Bethania Lutheran Church in Rosholt with Rev. Nancy Quatier officiating. Music was provided by organist Lana Sand.
Burial was in the Hillside Cemetery, Rosholt. Honorary pallbearers were all of her close friends. Active pallbearers were Tom Wagner, Bob Thompson, Dale Voss, Tony Oster, Dennis Nelson, and Curtis Wagner.
Visitation was Tuesday from 5:00 to 7:00 p.m. followed by a prayer service at 7:00 p.m. at the Eggers Funeral Home in Rosholt.
Doris Marie was born May 15, 1936, in rural Rosholt to Anton "Tony" and Erna K. (Hanson) Wagner. Doris attended school in New Effington. After graduating from New Effington High School in 1954, Doris worked at a bank in Browns Valley.
On October 28, 1954, Doris married Roger Roeder in New Effington. Following their marriage, she worked as a cook at the Rosholt School and for Flossie Larsen at the Midway Café. She later operated the Midway Café on her own for a time. Doris also worked at the Glacial Lakes Rest Area for several years.
Doris was a volunteer member of the Rosholt Ambulance for several years, serving as president. She also served on the Rosholt Housing Committee, now HRC, and was instrumental in getting the new townhomes in Rosholt built. Doris and Roger were the first tenants in 2001.
Doris enjoyed playing cards and Scrabble. One of Dorisʼs most exciting events was winning a car at the casino. Above all, Doris enjoyed spending time with family.
Survivors include her husband, Roger of Rosholt; daughter, Paulette (Keith) Schmitz of Wheaton, MN; son, Jon (Gayle) Roeder of Rosholt; a granddaughter, Tracey Schmitz (Adam Boyer) of Carlos, MN; son-in-law, Dave Reineke of Delano, MN; and two great-grandchildren, Mackenzie and Alex Hornseth of Rosholt.
Doris was preceded in death by her parents; daughter, Becky; and grandson, Chris Schmitz.
Eggers Funeral Home

SOUTH YARMOUTH - Elsbeth Gertrude (Wagner) Rambelje , 77, of South Yarmouth, formerly of Arlington, died on March 22, 2008. She was the wife of the late Harry Rambelje .
Born, raised, and educated in Germany, Mrs. Rambelje became a citizen of the United States in 1951. She made her home in Arlington and worked as a salesclerk for Helen's Pastry Shop until 1983, when she moved to South Yarmouth. While living on the Cape, she worked as a salesclerk for the former Bradlees Department Store in Hyannis for 17 years, retiring in 2000. Mrs. Rambelje enjoyed gardening, traveling to Europe, and spending time with her family. She was a communicant of St. Pius X Church.
She is survived by one son, Andrew Rambelje of Webster, and three grandchildren, Abigail of North Brookfield and William and Sean of Webster. She was the sister of Walter Wagner of Germany and is also survived by three nieces and five nephews.
A funeral will be held Wednesday, April 2, at 9 a.m., from the Hallett Funeral Home, 273 Station Ave., South Yarmouth, followed by a funeral Mass at 10 a.m., in St. Pius X Church, Station Ave., South Yarmouth. Edwin was born and raised in Slate River, where at the young age of 6 he began working on the family farm. As a young man he painted houses along with his brother- in- law John Erickson. After working for 37 years at Great Lakes Paper Edwin retired in 1976 as Forman Painter. He also enjoyed hunting, fishing, camping and travelling with his grandchildren. Edwin was an excellent craftsman and woodworking was his area of expertise. His talent in cabinetry and house building will always be cherished by family who were fortunate to be the recipient of his handy work.

The family of Weldon Renus Walberg is greatly saddened by his passing on October 18, 2014, in Progreso, Yucatan, Mexico.Weldon was born July 7, 1938, in Fort William, Ontario, and grew up in Slate River and Fort William. After high school he realized his childhood dream of becoming a bush pilot. He obtained his commercial pilot license and aircraft engineering certification. Weldon began his aviation career with Superior Airways and later joined Laurentian Airlines in Ottawa. He then went to British Columbia flying for Transprovincial Airlines out of Terrace and Prince Rupert to destinations involving the mining, forestry and tourism industries. After a few years he became General Manager of the airline. He ended his career in aviation as President of Air B.C. in Vancouver.After he retired from his aviation career, the family moved to Port Hope, Ontario, where Weldon joined Triple E as the company's representative from Manitoba to the East Coast, often driving recreational vehicles across the country to their buyers.Weldon loved the outdoors, and enjoyed great salmon fishing in Northern B.C. When he flew there he always kept a fishing rod in his plane so a few casts could be made when time allowed.Canoeing and fishing with his two boys in his beloved cedar-strip canoe were great joys for the three of them. They often caught speckled trout, and when up north, Arctic char.Living in Vancouver allowed the family to frequently visit their Whistler retreat where a lot of skiing was done. Hiking, fishing, canoeing, and golf were enjoyed when skiing was over for the season.Weldon was a soft-spoken kind, giving man. If he became your friend, he was your friend for life. He will be greatly missed by family, friends, and anyone who knew him.Weldon is survived by his wife Mary, his daughters Anne (Shane Matthews), Cheryl (Brian Boyd); his sons Matthew (Amber), Michael; his grandchildren Robert, Nicholas, and Daniel Matthews, Chloe and Oliver Boyd; his brother Ronald (Margot), and his sister Diane (David Harri). He is also survived by nieces, nephews, and cousins.Weldon was predeceased by his parents Renus and Edith (Lindberg) Walberg, and his nephew Daniel Fuglesang.
