Donald Lee PATTERSON

[I105276]
Donald "Don" Lee Patterson, 87 of Twin Falls, Idaho passed away at his home on Feb. 11th, 2019 surrounded by his family. He was born Sept. 5th, 1931 to Joseph and Emrett Patterson. He had 4 brothers and 4 sisters. Don joined the Airforce in 1952. He was a first class airman. He married Mary Ann Griffin on June 16th, 1958 and they had 3 children. Don is survived by his wife Mary of 61 years; their 3 children Don (Maritta), Dale and Cindy; one brother Phil and two sisters Darlene and Peach. He was preceded in death by his mother, father, 3 brothers, 2 sisters and 1 great granddaughter. Don is also survived by his 10 grandchildren and numerous great grandchildren, nieces and nephews. Before his passing you could see his glow and excitement when any of them came to visit. He was loved by everyone that knew him. His saying "I'm a lover not a fighter" really resonated to those around him. When he returned from the airforce he worked for the City of Twin Falls as a mechanic. He was willing to lend a hand to anybody that needed it. Don loved to spend time with his family, if he wasn't teaching them how to change their own oil, he was teaching them how to hunt and fish. At family gatherings you could always find him drinking a cold one and playing a game of horse shoes. He loved to play pinnacle. Don was truly a great role model to others. His great legacy will live on through stories told by future generations. A celebration of his life will be held at The Moose Lodge, 835 Falls Ave. Twin Falls on Saturday Feb. 23rd, 2019 at 2pm.
MvMagicValley.com, 16 February 2019

Milton Roscoe PATTERSON

[I035280]
AUBURN -- Milton R. ''Pat'' Patterson , 84, of Pakachoag Village, a retired music teacher, died Sunday, July 20, in UMass Memorial Medical Center -- Memorial Campus, Worcester, after an illness.
He leaves his wife of 61 years, Mary (Milleison) Patterson ; a son, Ronald Patterson of Millbury; seven grandchildren; and five great-grandchildren. A daughter, Mary Jean Demazure, and a sister, Helen Elliott, predeceased him. He was born in Beverly, son of Milton A. and Edna (Roscoe) Patterson , and lived in Oxford and Auburn for many years. He graduated from Beverly High School and attended New York University and Boston University School of Music. He was an Army veteran of World War II, serving in Europe.
Mr. Patterson was a music teacher for the Auburn School Department and later for the Quaboag School District before retiring. He then worked as an oil burner technician for C.K. Smith Oil Co. in Worcester for 10 years, retiring in 1989. He also was choir director for the Oxford and Quinsigamond United Methodist churches. An accomplished musician, he played the trombone and drums. Before the war, he played with many bands, including one of Tommy Dorsey's. He was a member of Pakachoag Church.
The memorial service will be held at 11:15 a.m. Thursday, July 24, in Pakachoag Church, 203 Pakachoag St. After cremation, scattering of ashes will be private. A calling hour will be held from 10 to 11 a.m. Thursday, July 24, in the church.
Worcester Telegram & Gazette, 22 July 2003

Burton PAULSEN

ST. PAUL - Burton W. "Burt" Paulsen, 101, of Palmer died Saturday, Nov. 14, 2015, at Brookefield Park in St. Paul.
Services will be held at 11 a.m. Tuesday at the United Methodist Church in Palmer. The Rev. Martin Leeper will officiate. Burial will be in Rose Hill Cemetery in Palmer.
Visitation will be from 5 to 7 p.m. Monday at the church in Palmer.
Greenway Funeral Home in Palmer is in charge of arrangements
Mr. Paulsen was born Jan. 18, 1914, in St. Paul to Glen and Viola (Hanisch) Paulsen.
Burt attended and graduated from Cushing Consolidated School in 1934.
He was baptized May 2, 1943, at St. Mark ºs Lutheran Church in Cushing.
Burt was united in marriage to Alvana Helser on Dec. 1, 1939, in Grand Island.
As a young man, he worked on the Fred Forbes farm and then at Western Land Roller Co. in Hastings from 1939 until 1942.
In 1942, the couple moved to a farm north of Palmer. He was involved and lived on the farm until moving into Brookefield Park in St. Paul in January 2013 where he lived until the time of his death.
The couple enjoyed traveling. They traveled to all 50 states, driving to all of the lower 48 states. He loved playing cards, fishing and hunting.
For many years on Tuesdays the family got together to play cards and eat pizza.
Burt and Alvana attended many dances at the Platt-Duetsche in Grand Island.
He was a member of the United Methodist Church in Palmer and the Platt-Duetsche in Grand Island.
Survivors include his wife, Alvana, living at Brookefield Park in St. Paul; five sons and daughters-in-law, James and Deniece Paulsen of Central City, Robert and Jette Paulsen of Port Orchard, Wash., Alan and Melodee Paulsen of Grand Island, Larry and Diane Paulsen of Palmer and Jack and Cindy Paulsen of St. Paul; 15 grandchildren and their spouses, Todd and Jill Paulsen, Debbie and Jay Meyer, Eric and Julie Paulsen, Chris Paulsen, John and Megan Paulsen, Kiley and Joseph Palensky, Andrew and Mindy Paulsen, Ryan and Erin Paulsen, Shane Paulsen, Jared and Heather Paulsen, Courtney and Dave Sullivan, Heather and Loren Pearson, C.J. Chris PAULSEN

[I046876]
A Resident Here For 46 Years Passes Away--
Christoffer Paulsen was born in Vilslev, Denmark, on September 24, 1857 and died October 23, 1924, living 67 years and 1 month.
He landed in America in the fall of 1878 and located in Howard County, Nebraska, where he has lived ever since. On January 19, 1881 he was united in marriage to Miss Hanna Isaacson and to their union were born six children: Paul and Rose, who died at the age of 12 years, Glenn Paulsen, living near Dannebrog, Ray Paulsen at Lake Andes, So. Dakota, Mrs. Max Wilcox, San Francisco, California and Mrs. V. E. Evans of Grand Island.
Mr. Paulsen's illness, became afflicted with stomach trouble about 25 years ago and withstood his illness bravely until 2 months ago, when he was confined to his bed. Everything that his loved ones could do for him was done until his demise. He leaves to mourn his death his wife and the above named children and also 3 sisters and 1 brother: Mrs. Mary Laverentz, Cheyenne, Wyoming, Mrs. Hans Petersen, Boelus, Mrs. Pauline Jones, Los Angeles, California, Peter Paulsen, Grand Island, and many other relatives and a host of friends. Mr. Paulsen was a good husband and father, a kind neighbor, honest and upright in all his dealings with men. He was a patient sufferer and always of a cheerful disposition. His old friend Rev. J. Chr. Pedersen, conducted the funeral service which was held from the home in St. Paul, Saturday afternoon, where a very large number of friends were gathered to pay their last respect to Mr. Paulsen. The body was taken to Dannebrog, where a short service was held at the grave. He was laid to rest in the beautiful Oak Ridge cemetery at Dannebrog. May the Lord bless and comfort our sorrowing friends.
The Phonograph desires to add a few words to the above obituary which was prepared by our friend, Rev. Pedersen, a life-long friend of the deceased and a man who has been close to him during the 46 years he was a resident of our fair county. Mr. Paulsen was a very fine gentleman, an honor to the country he selected for his permanent home, a credit to the community that he selected to rear a family and bring them all up God-fearing, God-loving and self-respecting men and women. Our heart goes out to them in their hour of grief and sorrow, especially to Mrs. Paulsen who is thus bereft of her companion of more than 43 years time. Truly may it be said that a good man has been called to his reward.
St. Paul Phonograph, 29 October 1924
