Marion Ruth SHEPARD

[I081087]
TRAVERSE CITY - Marion Ruth Cox, age 93, passed away on March 8, 2016 at the Pavilions in Traverse City. Marion was born on Nov. 18, 1922 in Detroit to James and Myrtle (Hoisington) Shepard. She had two sisters, Barbara Vreeland and Madelyn Hyde, and a brother, James Shepard.
She attended Cooley High School and graduated from Wayne State University with a Bachelor of Arts degree in psychology.
She was offered an internship at the Traverse City State Hospital in Traverse City. It was there that she met Jack H. Cox and they married on Oct. 11, 1947 in Detroit at the All Saints Episcopal Church. They made their home in Traverse City.
Marion spent her 22-year career working in the Social Services Department of the Traverse City State Hospital. She worked in many areas of the hospital with the last five years at the Arnell Ingstrom School for Children. She served on the boards of the Northwest Michigan Child Guidance Clinic and the Michigan Association for Retarded Children. She enjoyed her co-workers and made friendships that lasted a lifetime.
Marion and Jack had three daughters, Jacque (Ed) Schmitt, Carole (Bill) Dwyer and Bev (Mike) Gore. Grandchildren include Chris (Jacqui) McGrew, Doug (Tonya) McGrew, Megan (Lars) Kelto, Adam (Erica) Dwyer, James Dwyer, Zach (Anne) Gore, Danielle Gore, Erin (Nathan) Atterberry and Alex Gore. Marion was very proud of all of her grandchildren and their accomplishments. She was the great-grandmother to 10 children. She is survived by a sister-in-law, Joan Walters, and numerous devoted nieces and nephews.
Marion was preceded in death by her husband, Jack in 2010.
Marionʼs interests included knitting, music, decorating her home and enjoying many occasions with family at her home on East Bay. It was a rare Christmas that mom didnʼt make slippers for everyone, sweaters for the kids or afghans for the teens. She enjoyed bridge games with close friends for many years.
She was a member of the First Congregational Church.
Her memorial service will be held on Saturday, March 19, at 2 p.m., at the First Congregational Church, 6105 Center Road, Traverse City. The Rev. Dr. Gary Hogue will officiate.
Record-Eagle, Traverse City

Paul A. SHEPARD

[I029329]
Paul A. Shepard 64, formerly of Acton passed away in San Jose, Costa Rica, Central America on January 30, 2012. He is survived by his wife of twenty-five years, Marta, their daughter Suzana, who will receive a MA in Latin American Studies from Georgetown University in Washington D.C. in May and by their son Charles, a student at the Berkley College of Music in Boston. He is also survived by his parents May and Bert Shepard of Acton, three sisters, Suzanne Beale of Bedford, Patricia Follett of Watertown and Bonnie Moretti of Montague, and two brothers, Bruce of Acton, and Richard of Medway and by numerous nieces and nephews. He was born in Ware, Massachusetts on July 30, 1947 while his parents were still living on the Shepard Farm in Warren. He was the first of the ninth generation of the Shepard family to live on the farm and was always very proud of his family heritage. Educated in the Acton Public Schools, he graduated from Acton-Boxboro Regional High School in 1965. He attended Antioch College in Yellow Springs, Ohio and in 1995 he graduated from the International University of the Americas in San Jose, Costa Rica where he majored in English. After living a few years in Canada he lived most of his adult life in Cost Rica. There he taught in several private schools. He had been a teacher and computer specialist at the Country Day School in San Jose for twenty years when he retired as Technology Coordinator in 2006. Paul will be remembered for his enthusiasm and interest in the world around him. He made a study of local fireworks producers in Central America and spent time in Guatemala using his computer skills to help U.S. Peace Corps. Workers improve lives there. He was devoted to his family and very proud of their achievements. He was a ardent sports enthusiast and in spite of his long absence from New England remained a Boston Red Sox fan. He never missed an opportunity to attend a game at Fenway Park when he was in the area during baseball season. When he was a member of Acton's Troop 1, Boy Scouts of America in the late 1950's the troop pioneered in the retracing of the Minuteman Trail to the North Bridge at Concord. He was always very proud of that activity and for several years he told the story of the beginning of the American Revolutionary War to successive classes in the schools where he taught in Costa Rica A Funeral Mass was said in San Jose after his death and a Memorial Service will be held at the Shepherd Farm in Warren, MA in September.
The Boxborough Beacon, 15 May 2012

John J. SHERMAN

[I076556]
Sherman , John J. 81 06/14/1928 01/27/2010 John J. Sherman passed away into the arms of Jesus, surrounded by his family at home, Wednesday, Jan. 27, 2010. He was born to John F. and Leona E. (Vetter) Brinckerhoff in Bassett, Neb., June 14, 1928, joining a sister, Leona Marie, and brother, Rodney. Because his mother passed away following his childbirth, he was raised and adopted by his aunt and uncle, Mary C. and Charles F. Sherman , joining three older brothers, Charlie, Howard and Don. In the early 1930s their family moved to the Pacific Northwest and eventually settled in Milwaukie. He was a graduate of Milwaukie High School. In 1949, he married Vera I. Sebade at Immanuel Lutheran Church in Sellwood. It was a special relationship of love, devotion and friendship that spanned over 60 years. He was a longtime member of the church, serving God in many ways and as elder and congregational president. His professional career included sales and terminal management positions in the transportation industry. In 1945 he began with Consolidated Freightways and was also with United Buckingham, Ringsby United and Willamette Valley Transfer. In 1985 he purchased Ball Food Distributing Co. and he and Vera managed it until their retirement in 1993. They moved to the Oregon coast in 1994 and enjoyed over 12 happy years of beautiful sunsets and wild coastal storms. They made many friends and were involved in the Coronado Shores community and St. Peter the Fisherman Lutheran Church in Lincoln City. In 2006, as John 's health began declining, they moved to Silverton and closer to family. He enjoyed this harvest community and serving the Lord as a member of Trinity Lutheran Church in Mount Angel. John is survived by his loving wife, Vera ; son, Michael (Kirby), and granddaughter, Mallory; daughters, Patty Cormack (Doug) and Kathy Perry (Steve) and their families; sister, Leona Marie Thornton; aunts Ruth Haller (Yngol) and Neva Vetter; and his loved nieces and nephews. He was preceded in death by his parents and brothers. He will be remembered for his kind and humorous spirit, his faith in God and serving others; his love and devotion to his family and his respect for others. John 's service will be held at 11 a.m. Tuesday, Feb. 2, 2010, in Immanuel Lutheran Church (Sellwood), 7810 S.E. 15th Ave., Portland, OR. 97202. A lunch will follow his service. In lieu of flowers, the family would appreciate any loving contributions in his memory be made to Immanuel Social Ministries.
The Oregonian, 31 January 2010
