Scott K. SUNDSTROM

[I001302]
A lifetime pursuit came to fruition recently for singer/songwriter Scott "Sonny" Sundstrom of Alcester, S.D. The 43-year-old released his first CD, a collection of 18 original songs amassed over a 20-year music hobby.
More is on the way.
"Creative Collection," available now at Uncle John and Backstage with Ray, is the first of a three-volume set Sundstrom is production. Volumes II and III, he said, should be available next month.
All told, there are 42 original songs in Sundstrom's collection. The musical styles vary from rock to country to blues and folk.
"I sing and play keyboard on the CDs," said Sundstrom, who works in an office supply firm in Sioux Falls by day. "I've added other tracks like percussion and bass. Everything was done in the studio."
Sundstrom's music passion was fed at an early age. The son of Burdette and Lois Sundstrom of Alcester grew up watching his mother sing at various church and community functions. Sonny was soon to follow. And for much of the past two decades he's been going from site to site around Siouxland offering his sound.
"One of our bands that played a lot around Sioux City was called Full Circle," said Sundstrom. "I know we had two different two-year runs with that band. And there were others, or I'd just go myself to places around Sioux Falls and Sioux City. Over the years, I've done a lot of it."
Sundstrom's first collection revolves around personal experiences. Titles are things like "Still a Family Man," "Four Generations," "Friends of Life," and a tribute to his daughter Julia, now 12, at the time of her birth.
"I wrote 'Little One' right after she was born and it means a lot," said Sundstrom. "In fact, Julia's now getting into some music herself.
"Each song has a special place," he added.
Another "special place" of sorts is The Bread Basket at 1101 Fourth St., Sioux City, where Sundstrom performs at 8 p.m. Saturday. He'll follow on March 28 with another 8 p.m. show at the restaurant's new downtown location.
Sioux City Journal, 14 March 2003, Tim Gallagher, Journal staff writer

Wesley John SUNDSTROM

[I003285]
Wesley Sundstrom was born November 11, 1926 in Beresford, South Dakota to Richard and Ruth (Sanford) Sundstrom. He died March 4, 2009 at Good Samaritan Society in Canton, SD at the age of 82 years, three months and 23 days.
Wesley was raised and educated in Beresford. He served in the US Army in the South Pacific during WWII. Following his discharge he returned to Beresford. He worked with his father custom combining. April 2, 1955 he married Aileen Johnson. Wesley and Aileen lived on their farm for 53 years. He also did electrical work with a friend in the area for a few years. Wesley was a past commander and life member of the VFW.
Survivors include his wife Aileen Sundstrom, Beresford; two sons, Dwight Sundstrom and David Sundstrom of Beresford; grandson, Nicholas Sundstrom of Sioux Falls; brother, Harrison Sundstrom of Concord, CA; sister-in-law, Verna Sundstrom and several nieces and nephews.
He was preceded in death by his parents, sister, Ruth, brother, Richard and grandson, Dustin.
