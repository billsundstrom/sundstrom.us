Robert Ellsworth OFFERDAHL

[I043375]
Robert E. Offerdahl, age 76, of rural Clitherall, died Saturday, June 1, 2002 at MeritCare Hospital in Fargo. Services will be Thursday, June 6 at 1 p.m. at Eagle Lake Lutheran Church of rural Battle Lake with Rev. Don Peterson officiating.
Visitation will be Wednesday, June 5, 4:00-8:00 p.m. with a prayer service at 7:00 p.m. at the funeral home in Battle Lake and one hour prior to the service at the church.
Organist will be Margaret Nelson and soloist is Gloria Flaten who will sing "On Eagles Wings" and "How Great Thou Art". Congregational hymns will be "Softly and Tenderly" and "Rock of Ages".
Casketbearers will be Ronald Boe, Bryan Spille, David Pederson, Steven Boe, Gary Offerdahl and Dean Thompson. Interment will be in the church cemetery.
He was born September 14, 1925, in Hatton, ND to Alfred G. and Alma M. (Hanson) Offerdahl. He was baptized and confirmed in Washington Prairie Lutheran Church of rural Hatton. He attended Washington Township Country School through the eighth grade and graduated from the Hatton High School in 1943.
October 28, 1948, he married Curtiselle Thompson in Grand Forks. He was employed with the Olson-Hegge-Peterson Hardware Company in Hatton. In 1952 they moved to Minneapolis and in 1960 built a home in Coon Rapids. He worked for Williams Steel for six years and then the T.S.I. Inc. in Shoreview for 28 years. He retired in 1989 and in 1994 they moved to Eagle Lake of rural Battle Lake, where they lived until moving to their present home in Leaf Mountain Township in 1997.
He was a member of Eagle Lake Lutheran Church. He was an avid reader and enjoyed fishing, gardening and his family especially his grandchildren. His great-grandchildren were a big plus to him.
He is survived by his wife, Curtiselle; son, Michael (Sue) Offerdahl of Monticello; daughters, Jane (Ollie Backman) Ohlind of Lakeville and Cathy Hauschildt of Maple Grove; five grandchildren and five great grandchildren, Laura (Scott) French, Treighton and Josie of Rosemount, Tracy (Eric) Hathaway, Claudia and Isabelle of Buffalo, Michael Jr. (Angela) Offerdahl and JosceLyn of Monticello, Erica Ohland of Eagan and Mitch Hauschildt of Maple Grover; two sisters, Margaret (Bud) Anderson and Audrey Spille, both of Bloomington; sister-in-law, Yvonne Offerdahl of Hatton, ND; brother-in-law, Peter Boe of Portland. ND; nieces, nephews, relatives and friends.
He was preceded in death by his parents; a brother, Arland Offerdahl and a sister, Ruth Boe.
Glende-Nilson Funeral Home of Battle Lake is in charge of arrangements.

Halfdan OLAFSSON

[I008112]
From Wikipedia

Halfdan Hvitbeinn (Old Norse: Hálfdan hvı́tbeinn) was the son of Olof Trätälja of the House of Yngling according to Heimskringla. He lived around A.D. 710.

His father was sacrificed to Odin by the Swedish settlers in Värmland because of a famine. Some Swedes, however, realised that the famine was brought by overpopulation and not by the fact that the king had been neglecting his religious duties.

Consequently, they resolved to cross the Ed Forest and settle in Norway and happened to end up in Soleyar where they killed king Sölve and took Halfdan prisoner. The Swedish expatriates elected Halfdan king as he was the son of their old king, Olof. Halfdan subjugated all of Soleyar and took his army into Romerike and subjugated that province as well.

Halfdan was to become a great king, who married Åsa, the daughter of king Eystein, the ruler of Oppland and Hedmark. They had two sons, Öystein Halfdansson and Gudröd.

Halfdan conquered a large part of Hedemark, Toten, Hadeland and a part of Vestfold. When his brother Ingjald Olofsson died, he inherited Wermelandia. Halfdan died of old age in Toten and was transported to Vestfold where he was buried under a mound.

Rognvald OLAFSSON

[I004830]
Ragnvald the Mountain-High was a petty king of Westfold in what is today Norway. He was the son of Olaf Geirstad-Alf and the cousin of Harald Fairhair.

His greatest contribution to posterity was that he asked the skald jóđólfr of Hvinir to compose a poem about his ancestry. This poem is known as Ynglingatal and is not only one of the oldest, but also one of the most famous and debated of the Old Norse poems.

jóđólfr ended the poem with these lines:
Under the heaven's blue dome, a name
I never knew more true to fame
Than Rognvald bore; whose skilful hand
Could tame the scorners of the land, --
Rognvald, who knew so well to guide
The wild sea-horses through the tide:
The "Mountain-high" was the proud name
By which the king was known to fame.

LeRoy Erling OLAUSON

[I045841]
LeRoy Erling Olauson, 65, passed away March 8, 2013, surrounded by his loving family after a courageous battle with lung cancer. Services will be held at 11:30 a.m. Tuesday, March 12, at First Lutheran Church, Mandan. Graveside services will be held at the North Dakota Veterans Cemetery, south of Mandan, immediately following the service.
A viewing will be held from 4 to 7 p.m. today at Bismarck Funeral Home, followed by a prayer service at 7 p.m.
LeRoy Olauson was born Oct. 17, 1947, to Erling and Bessie (Jackman) Olauson. LeRoy was raised in Driscoll and graduated from Steele High School in 1966. LeRoy attended Bismarck Junior College in 1966, before enlisting in the U.S. Army in June 1967.
LeRoy was assigned to the 311th Evacuation Hospital Unit in the U.S. Army. He enlisted in the U.S. Army Reserves in 1973 and continued his nursing education. LeRoy obtained his LPN degree in 1981. In 1983, LeRoy was promoted to sergeant first class. He was awarded the Armed Forces Reserve Medal, Army Service Ribbon, NCO Professional Development Ribbon "1", Army Reserve Component Achievement Medal (third award) and Sharpshooter (M-16 rifle). He honorably retired from the U.S. Army Reserves in 2007.
In November 1973, he married Donna (Jangula) Hagel and was blessed with three daughters, Tammy (Terry) Boone, Bismarck, Lynn (Jeff) Schaffer, Bismarck, and Amy (Jim) Martens, Mandan. In 2006, LeRoy married Marlys (Hogue) and welcomed three stepdaughters, Tracy (Faron) Reich, Hazelton, Kim (Jeff) Luptak, Bismarck, and Kari (Brett) Charvat, Mandan. LeRoy was blessed with 13 grandchildren, Tyler, Mackenzie, Wyatt and Jordan Boone, Madison and Makenna Reich, Ashlee, Hanna and Hailee Luptak and Taylor, Ryann, Brodie and Avery Charvat. LeRoy was known by everyone as Papa.
LeRoy worked as an LPN and respiratory therapist at St. Alexius Medical Center for 30 years and found joy in taking care of others. In 1990, he was honored to receive the St. Alexius Higher Standard Award for his outstanding patient care. He enjoyed working with the 3CC staff and greatly appreciated all the love and support over the past six months. He had a love for gardening and carpentry and you could always find him working on projects around the house or for others. LeRoy enjoyed bowling and had gained many friendships throughout the years. He had a special place in his heart for his friends, Vince and Diane Swenson. He enjoyed spending time with his coffee friends and saving big money at Menards (with rebates).
What LeRoy enjoyed most was spending time with his family and grandkids. He spent countless hours at many basketball, volleyball, baseball, softball, soccer, football games, track meets, choir and band concerts and dance competitions. He rarely missed an event. LeRoy was also a devoted Minnesota Vikings and Minnesota Twins fan. He could always keep you up-to-date on the score, whenever they were playing.
LeRoy is survived by his wife, Marlys; six daughters and their spouses; 13 grandkids; his sister, Elva Schultz, Renton, Wash.; and a brother, Marvin, Ogden, Utah.
He was preceded in death by his parents, Erling and Bessie Olauson; and infant daughter, Kathy.
Those wishing to sign the online register book or leave a message of condolence please got to www.bismarckfuneralhome.com.
The Bismarck Tribune, 11 March 2013
