Robert Ira SEELEY

[I065709]
Robert Ira Seeley, age 91, of Campbell, NY passed away on Monday, April 25, 2016 at home surrounded by his family. Robert was born August 23, 1924 in Lewisville, PA to the late Ira I. and Katherine E. (Heath) Seeley . Robert was endearingly known by many as "Bobby". He married his beloved Marion A. Allen on August 26, 1960.
Robert served in the Army during World War II from 1944 to 1946 as a paratrooper and was a Purple Heart recipient. After returning from the war, Robert farmed, and attended night classes at Corning Community College obtaining two Associate Degrees. He also was employed over 30 years as a welder and in maintenance at the Polly-O Dairy in Campbell, NY and retired in 1986. Robert was a wonderfully devoted husband, father, and grandfather. He is survived by his wife: Marion (Allen) Seeley ; two daughters: Mary Katherine Savercool of Philadelphia, PA and Jo Sue (Gary) Nash of Painted Post, NY; three sons: Raymond (Connie) Seeley of Campbell, NY, Dale (Kaye) Seeley of Campbell, NY and Joseph (Michelle) Seeley of Campbell, NY; and his grandchildren: Melissa, Zachariah, Augustus, Jessica, Katherine, Alicia, Leilani, Karin, Stephania and Christopher. He was predeceased by a grandson: Isaiah.
Robert is survived by his brother, Charles (Nikie) Seeley of FL and several nieces and nephews. He was predeceased by his three sisters and brothers and their spouses: Edith (Leroy)Wood, Josephine (Phillip) Ruocco, Margaret (John)Thompson, Lyndon(Reta) Seeley , and William(Georgie) Seeley .
Robert was life time member of the Campbell American Legion, VFW, and the AMVETS FL44. He attended Curtis Baptist Bible Church of Campbell NY. Robert was an avid reader, photographer, and enjoyed hunting, fishing and restoring houses.
The Leader, Corning, NY, 29 April 2016

Missing man found dead
The search for a Butte man who had been missing since last week ended Friday morning when he was discovered dead in the mountains between Basin and Elk Park.
Searchers found Daniel "Danny" Segna, 51, the victim of an apparent suicide by hanging, according to Jefferson County Sheriff Craig Doolittle.
Family members reported Segna missing Oct. 15.
His pickup truck was found Monday abandoned near the Basin Creek campground between Basin and Elk Park, north of Butte. Police initially searched the area, but found no sign of the missing man.
The missing man始s sister, Debbie Segna, told The Montana Standard Thursday that family and friends also searched the area, but turned up nothing.
Doolittle said teams with search dogs resumed the search about 6:30 a.m. Friday, and one of the teams found Segna始s body about a half mile away from his truck.
Investigators haven始t determined when Segna died.
By John Grant Emeigh, The Montana Standard, 22 October 2011
