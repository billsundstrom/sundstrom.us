Julia Jo SMITHWICK

Judy (Julia) Smithwick Smith, age 85, passed away October 4, 2016 in Pullman, WA.
Judy was born November 18, 1930 in Midvale, ID to Malvern H and Bessie M Smithwick. Judy graduated from Wapato High School with the class of 1948. She attended the University of Washington where she married Dean C Smith. After Dean graduated from law school, they moved to Yakima where Dean began his practice with his father in Wapato.
In 1956 they were blessed with twins Tremaine (Trim) and Jeanine. The family moved to Spokane in 1969 where Dean was appointed as US Attorney for Eastern Washington by President Richard Nixon. Judy became a gracious host to many dignitaries which led her to her next career as being a House Mother (Director) for Kappa Alpha Theta Sorority at Washington State University. Judy dedicated 30 years to the sorority, retiring in 2009. Judy loved her job and the thousands of girls (and their families) she worked with. In 1996 she received the National Kappa Alpha Theta Directorʼs Award (best House Mother). Upon retiring she was initiated as an honorary member of the sorority.
Judy worked tirelessly at the local thrift store in Pullman. She loved to golf, play bridge and was an active member of PEO. She was an avid junker (antiquer).
Judy is survived by her children, son Tremaine and Lindy Smith, Olympia, WA, daughter Jeanine Smith, Pullman, WA, grandchildren Stephanie Smith, Corey and Lindsey Smith, and Ben Smith, and a great grandson, Kyle Smith. Judy is also survived by her sister-in-law, Ann Smithwick, West Seattle, WA, and numerous nieces and nephews.
Judy was preceded in death by her parents, sisters Pauline Westover, Mary Ann Raykovich, and Mable Faucher, and brother Harry (Bus) Smithwick.
A graveside service will be held Saturday, October 8, 2016 at 10:30 am at Reservation Community Memorial Park, 5161 West Wapato Rd., Wapato, WA.

OBITUARY: Mrs. Alice M. Porter
Kentville - Mrs. Alice Marie Porter, 59, of Lakeville, Kings County, widow
of Mansor Porter, passed away at her home in that community early Saturday.
While her health had not been good for several years, her sudden passing
was unexpected. Born in Parrsboro, Mrs. Porter was the daughter of the late
Mr. and Mrs. William Smythe. Following her marriage in 1913, she lived at
Glenmont, where her husband was a well known farmer, and later resided at
Marden, where Mr. Porter died in 1937. Mrs. Porter, until her health failed,
was a prominent church worker in the Baptist Churches in communities where
she had resised, and was also interested in other women's organizations and
community work generally. Surviving are twelve children, five sons, Luther
Porter, Berwick; Leonard, with the Black Watch Regiment, Aldershot Camp;
LeRoy, Sault Ste. Marie, Ont.; Harry, Picton, Ont.; and Leander Porter,
Lakeville; seven daughters, Irene (Mrs. Harry Porter), Berwick; Reta Porter,
Granville Ferry; Esther (Mrs. Cecil Sanford and Etta (Mrs. Joseph Dempsey),
of Marden; Eulirua (Mrs. Harry MacIntosh), Avonport; Florence (Mrs. Jack
Bowser), Hantsport; Freda (Mrs. Harold Grover), of Lakeville. The body, now
resting at the H.C. Lindsay Funeral Home, Kentville, will be taken to the
Lakeville Baptist Church for funeral services at three o'clock, Tuesday
afternoon. The pastor, Rev. Hoyt Fenwick will officiate. Interment will be
in Lakeville Cemetery.

Source: Dave Winter
