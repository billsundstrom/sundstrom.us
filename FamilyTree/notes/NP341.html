Keith Malcolm MACKEEN

MacKEEN, M. Keith - 86, Medford Beach, Kings Co., passed away on Monday, June 28, 2004, in Valley Regional Hospital, Kentville. Born in Delhaven, Kings Co., he was a son of the late Lawrence and Ellen (Strong) MacKeen. Mr. MacKeen was a veteran of the Second World War and after the war he continued to work with aircrafts as a maintenance engineer, with Maritime Central Airways, Wheeler Air Lines, Hollinger Ungava, Timmins Aviation, then retiring from Quebecair in 1983 to his home in Medford Beach, Kings Co. He was a member of the Royal Canadian Legion, Habitant Branch 73. Mr. MacKeen loved to fish and hunt, he enjoyed the great outdoors. He is survived by his daughter, Heather Hickman, Medford; grandchildren, Sarah and Andrew, both of Toronto; sister, Katherine Palmeter, Medford; special niece and nephew, Andrea and Eugene Rogers; many more relatives and friends. He was predeceased by his wife, Ella (Greene) MacKeen; sisters, Frances Hatchard, Margaret Clarke; brothers, Cyril, Eldon and Charles. There will be no visitation by request. A memorial service will be held at a later date. Funeral arrangements are in the care of H.C. Lindsay Funeral Home, 5 Leverett Ave., Kentville 902-678-2151. On-line condolences can be sent via e-mail to: lindsayfunerals@@ns.aliantzinc.ca Donations can be made to Heart and Stroke Foundation of Nova Scotia or a charity of choice.

Al was born and raised in Goshen, NS and was the only son of the late Robert and Bertha MacNaughton. He was the beloved husband of Lynn (Thomas) MacNaughton. Their special relationship was truly a model of love and companionship. Al graduated from Nova Scotia Normal College and St. Francis Xavier University, celebrating 50 years as an alumnus just two weeks ago. He taught in Goshen, then at St. Mary's Elementary School in Aylesford for 33 years. Al was a favorite teacher of numerous students, including his own children. He was extremely community-minded, volunteering for over 50 years with the Aylesford United Church and was very proud of being the first baby Lion of the Aylesford & District Lions Club. Al also delivered Meals on Wheels for over 20 years. His quick wit and dedication to his family, work, and volunteer commitments will be remembered by his many friends in the Valley and beyond. Al loved sports, and was a long-time member of both the Berwick Curling Club and Paragon Golf and Country Club. He was also a dedicated Montreal Canadiens and Los Angeles Dodger fan and he was delighted that his final vote helped his favorite "red" team sweep the country. Al and Lynn enjoyed many winters in Indian Harbour Beach, Florida.
