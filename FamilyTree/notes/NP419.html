Glen Werner PAULSEN

[I046877]
Funeral Services Held Tuesday for Glenn W. Paulsen--
Funeral services were held on Tuesday afternoon for Glenn Werner Paulsen, 71, retired Cushing farmer.
He died late Saturday evening at a hospital in Omaha. He had been in the hospital the past 10 weeks.
The services were held at St. John's Lutheran church in Cushing with Rev. Eugene Wekander the pastor, officiating.
Pallbearers were Forrest Butter, Marius Mickelsen, Clarence Adams, James Petersen, Alfred Schubert and George Anderson. Pianist was Mrs. Dean R. Armstrong. Burial was in Elmwood cemetery, St. Paul under the direction of McIntyre-Jacobsen Funeral Home.
He was born on January 12, 1884 at Rockville, Nebraska to Christopher and Hannah Paulsen. He married Viola Hanisch on April 27, 1909 at Grand Island. Survivors include his wife, Viola, 2 sons, Ray of St. Paul and Burton of Palmer, 2 daughters, Mrs. James Pitcher of Sterling, Colo, and Mrs. Stanley Pelican of Palmer; a brother Ray of Lake Andes, So. Dak, 2 sisters, Mrs. V. E. Evans of Grand Island and Mrs. Max Wilcox of San Francisco; and 13 grandchildren. He was preceded in death by his parents and 1 brother.
Obituary from St. Paul Phonograph, 20 April 1955

Irma M. PAULSEN

[I046888]
Irma M. Pitcher, 89, of Grand Island, died Saturday, April 24, 2010, at St. Francis Medical Center. Mrs. Pitcher was born Aug. 4, 1920, on a farm near Cushing to Glenn and Viola (Hanisch) Paulsen.
She married Jim Pitcher on Dec. 27, 1952 in Alliance. He preceded her in death in 1990.
Survivors of the immediate family include sons and daughters-in-law, Michael and Audrey Pitcher, Richard and Diana Pitcher, Ken Pitcher, all of Grand Island and David and Mary Pitcher of Kearney; daughter, Loretta Corcoran of Clovis, Calif.; brother and sister-in-law, Burt and Alvana Paulsen; twin sister, Irene Pelican; sister-in-law, Ellen Paulsen; grandchildren and their spouses, Mark and Julie Pitcher, Cathy and Luke Gard, Andrew Pitcher, Ryan and Kelly Pitcher, Cindy and Courtney Harris, Mandy and Lee Dobish, Katie and Chris Lyon, Tina and Matt Mitrione, Aimee Gong and Curtis Pitcher, plus 15 great-grandchildren and a host of friends and relatives.
Irma graduated from Cushing High School in 1938 and St. Paul Business College in 1940. She worked at several offices including the Hastings Tribune, Ordnance Plant, Lockhead Aircraft, Hodges Chevrolet in Lexington, Minden Beef, Hall County Election and Dramco Tool Company.
She enjoyed working puzzles, some television sports and playing cards with family and friends. She enjoyed volunteering at the Grand Generation Center. Irma was a member of Trinity United Methodist Church.
The Grand Island Independent, 27 April 2010

Lida C. PAULSEN

[I046880]
Mrs. Lida Evans, 91, of 1823 W. Koenig and a member of a pioneer Hall County family, died Thursday afternoon at a local hospital after a short illness.
Services will be Saturday at 2 PM at Livingston-Sondermann Funeral Home. The Rev. Charles Olson will officiate. Burial will be in Grand Island Cemetery.
There will be no visitation. Memorials are suggested to the First Presbyterian Church.
Mrs. Evans was born July 1, 1886 at Dannebrog, the daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Christopher Paulsen. She attended school and grew up in Dannebrog.
On Sept 2, 1905, she married Virden Evans at St. Paul. They lived in St. Paul and later in Cushing, where Mr. Evans was a banker. In 1911 they moved to Grand Island where the Evans family was active in auto sales, banking and insurance. Mr. Evans died Aug 8, 1967.
Mrs. Evans was a member of the First Presbyterian Church. She no longer maintained social affiliations.
Survivors include a son, Gordon of Grand Island, 4 grandchildren, and 13 great-grandchildren.
She was preceded in death by a son, Clayton, 3 brothers and 2 sisters.

Raymond E. PAULSEN

[I046886]
Raymond E. Paulsen, 92, of St. Paul died Saturday, January 15, 2005 at the Howard County Community Hospital in St. Paul.
Services will be held at 2 pm at Jacobsen-Greenway Funeral Home in St. Paul. Reverend Loedy will officiate. Burial will be in the Elmwood Cemetery in St. Paul with military honors by the Carl Mogensen American Legion Post #119 of St. Paul.
Mr. Paulsen was born on Feb. 13, 1912 on the family farm near St. Paul to Glenn and Viola (Hanisch) Paulsen.
Survivors include his wife, Ellen of St. Paul; a daughter and son-in-law, Elaine and James Powell of Phillips; and a son, Glenn Paulsen of Grand Island.
Other survivors include: two granddaughters and their husbands, Rebecca and David Renter of Edmonton, Alberta, Canada and Tina and T. J. Gill of Denver; one great-grandson, Calvin Renter; one brother and sister-in-law, Burton and Alvana Paulsen of Palmer; two sisters and one brother-in-law, Irma Pitcher and Irene and Stanley Pelican, all of Grand Island.
He attended Cushing schools where he graduated from high school in 1930. He attended the former St. Paul Business College for a year. After his schooling, he farmed with his family.
He entered the US Army in May 1942 and served in the south Pacific. He received his discharge on Dec. 31, 1945. He then returned to St. Paul.
Raymond was united in marriage to Ellen Mortensen on February 8, 1946 in St. Paul. They first farmed near Midway west of St. Paul for three years. They then purchased a farm south of St. Paul where he farmed and was a cattle feeder first and then he raised cattle. Ray was well known throughout the cattle industry and sale barns in central Nebraska. He retired from farming and raising cattle in the fall of 2004.
Raymond enjoyed fishing and hunting.
He was a member of the First Presbyterian Church of St. Paul and the Carl Mogensen American Legion Post #119 of St. Paul.
He was preceded in death by his parents.
Grand Island Independent

Last Rites Held for R. E. Paulsen Monday, October 17
Funeral services for Ray E. Paulsen, 71, who died in his sleep Friday, October 14, were held at the Presbyterian church Monday, October 17. The Rev. Leslie Lewis officiated and burial was made in the Lakeview cemetery with Stedronsky Funeral Home in charge of arrangements. Masonic rites were conducted at the cemetery. Pall bearers were: George Padrnos, George Shekel, Wm. Wuest, John Engelland, Seymour Hedeen and C. H. Edwards.
Mrs. Hazel Danks, Mrs. Wayne Nelsen, Alex Williamson and Henry Williamson sang, accompanied by Mrs. Alex Williamson.
Mr. Paulsen was born at Dannebrog, Nebraska, June 18, 1889 and came to Lake Andes in 1919 where he farmed before moving to town where he operated a café.
He later managed a bowling alley until retiring a few years ago. At the time of his death he was Justice of Peace. He was past master of the Lake Andes Masonic Lodge and past grand of the I.O.O.F.
He is survived by his widow, Boline; two sons, Rex of Norwalk, California, and Max of Waterloo, Iowa; four grandchildren and two sisters, Mrs. V. E. Evans of Grand Island, Nebraska, and Mrs. Max Wilcox of San Francisco, California.

She was born in Longmont, Colorado July 21, 1922 to John Ramey and Pauline Elizabeth Payne. She attended high school in Wheatridge, Colorado. Anna and George Strobeck were married in Denver, Colorado on August 11, 1940. They shared 64 years of memories together.
George and Ruth moved to Bainbridge Island in 1941, then for a short time to Philadelphia, finally returning to Bainbridge. Anna worked 20 yrs at the Island Bazaar and 8 yrs for the Bainbridge School District in food service. She was a member of the Amelia Hungerford Orthopedic Guild for Childrenʼs Hospital and a 20 yr volunteer for the Chuckwagon Senior Nutrition Program. She served as a leader for Bluebirds, Campfire Girls, and Cub Scouts. Anna enjoyed walking, golf, cooking, and camping. Her pets were beloved companions.
She was preceded in death by her parents, brothers John and Edward Payne, sister Rachael Nugent, nephews Tom Payne, Wes and Bill Nugent, husband George, and grandson Donald Schwartz.

Eugene William PEABODY

[I064151]
Eugene W. Peabody, well known wholesale grocer who was active in church and fraternal circles, died suddenly yesterday morning at his Coolidge Avenue home, Sycaway. A native of this city, Mr. Peabody was a lifelong Trojan.
Mr. Peabody was a prominent member of the Fifth Avenue-State Street Methodist Church and served on the official board of the church for several years. Before the Fifth Avenue Church merged with the State Street Church, he was secretary of the Fifth Avenue Church Sunday school and he continued his Interest in the church school in later years.
Mr. Peabody was active in both the Modern Woodmen of America and the United Commercial Travelers, filling various offices in these organizations.
He is survived by his wife, the former Jessie F. Darling, and three sisters, Mrs. George H. Brewer of Schenectady, Mrs. Paul E. White of Dorset, Vt, and Mrs. John Aird of Troy. The funeral will be held Wednesday afternoon at 2 o'clock from the residence. Interment will be in Oakwood Cemetery.
The Times Record, Troy, NY, 17 July 1939

Carl Ellsworth PEACH

[I039907]
PEACH, Carl Ellsworth - It is with great sadness that the Peach family announces the death of Carl Ellsworth Peach, November 16, 2005, in Dykeland Lodge, Windsor, where he resided for the past five years. Born in Mount Denson, he was a son of the late Arthur and Wilhelmina (Porter) Peach. He was a veteran of the Second World War, serving on the Corvette Kamsack. Following the war he was employed by Gypsum Transportation Co. from where he retired in 1985 after 43 years of service. Carl was Past Master of Poyntz Lodge No. 44 and Past President and life member of Lucknow Branch 109 Royal Canadian Legion, Hantsport. A lifelong member of St. James United Church, where he served and acted on many committees; fishing, hunting and gardening were some of his passions His family also brought him great joy and love throughout his life. An avid curler, he enjoyed the friendship of his fellow curlers. He will be sadly missed by all who knew and loved him and whose lives he touched. Surviving are his loving wife of 59 years, Barbara (Morris), Hantsport; daughter, Claudia (Greg) Coldwell, Port Williams; grandsons, Michael (Vonda) and Dana, Alberta; great-grandchildren, Jessica, Tara, Monte, Ashleigh, Alberta; sister, Lillian (Fred) Wilkins, Windsor. He was predeceased by his brothers, Eugene and Erlston; sisters, Lena, Estella, and Nina. Cremation has taken place and there will be no visitation by request. Masonic service of Poyntz Lodge No. 44, as well as a Lucknow Royal Canadian Legion memorial service will be held Sunday, November 20, at 2 p.m. in Serenity Funeral Home & Crematorium, 1212 Parkway Dr., Port Williams (542-4656). A memorial service will be held Monday, November 21, at 2 p.m. in St. James United Church, Hantsport, Rev. Glennis Smith will officiate, with a private family burial in Riverbank Cemetery. Reception to follow in St. James United Church. Family flowers only. Donations in memory may be made to the Parkinson Society Canada, Maritime Region, Friends of Dykeland or a charity of one's choice. "Our heartfelt thanks goes out to Dykeland Lodge for the care and compassion shown to Carl during the past five years; also, to all friends and neighbours, for all their help and attention during Carl's illness."
Halifax Herald, 19 November 2005
