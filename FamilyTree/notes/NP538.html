Richard Francis TAYLOR

[I071042]
Richard F. Taylor, 92, of Middlefield, a lifelong resident of Geauga County, passed away Friday, July 13, 2018 at Briar Hill Care Center.
He was born April 22, 1926 in Bainbridge to the late Virgil and Caroline (Yaxley) Taylor. Richard, Dick to his family and friends, graduated from Bainbridge High School. He enlisted in the Army at the age of 17 and was a veteran of WWII.
After receiving his Bachelor's Degree in Animal Science from Ohio State University, he worked in the agribusiness field before returning to Geauga County in 1968. He managed the Burton office of Warren Production Credit Association, until he and his wife, Roma, became full time antique dealers and promoters of antique shows. Together, they managed the Antique Market at the Geauga County fairgrounds for 35 years.
Richard was a member of the International Farm Youth Exchange, a former Maple Festival Chairman, and former Treasurer and life member of the Geauga Fair Board. In his younger years he was a member of the Geauga County Fair Band. His love for antiques led to his active involvement in the Geauga County Historical Society & Century Village Museum. In recent years, Richard spent time at the Middlefield Senior Center, where he became interested in Rug Hooking. He found a new talent and creativity in rug hooking and enjoyed making rugs and wall hangings for his family and friends, which he often displayed at the Geauga County Fair.
He will be sadly missed by his children, Julie (Bruce) Ewing of Auburn, Jon (Susan) Taylor of Burton, Jamie Taylor of Aurora; grandchildren, Caitlin Ewing, Patrick Ewing, TJ Ewing, Daniel (Stephanie) Taylor, Kimberly Taylor, and Emily Taylor; great-grandchildren, Henry and Archer Taylor. He is survived by his sisters, Marion Howard and Carol Malz.
In addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his wife, Roma Taylor, who passed away December 11, 2007.
Memorial Calling hours will be held Friday, July 20, 2018 from 5:00 to 8:00 p.m. at Russell-Sly Family Funeral Home, 15670 W High St, Middlefield, OH 44062. A Private burial will take place at a later date in Restland Cemetery, Bainbridge, Ohio.
In addition to flowers, memorial donation can be made to the Middlefield Senior Center, 15820 Ridgewood Dr, Middlefield, OH 44062.
Memories and condolences can be shared with the family at www.russellslyfh.com.
The News-Herald, 18 July 2018

William Clarence TAYLOR

W.C. (Bill) Taylor, 90, died Wednesday, July 25, 2001, at Stillwater Medical Center. Services, with Rev. Rodney James officiating, will be Friday at 10:30 a.m. at the First Baptist Church in Stillwater. Interment will follow at Sunset Memorial Gardens. Strode Funeral Home is directing the arrangements.
He was born in Maple Nov. 19, 1910, to Atha (Ross) and James A. Taylor. He grew up in Sallisaw where he married Reba Berniece Cheek. They had been married 69 years when she died in December 1999.
For several years, he owned a dry cleaning business in Sallisaw. He later moved to Stillwater where he worked as an electrician. During World War II he moved to Texas and worked on the battleships used in the war.
He returned to Stillwater and began working as an electrician for Oklahoma State University. Later, he became the building engineer at the then new Edmon Low Library. He operated the OSU football scoreboard until he retired.
He was a high school football, basketball and baseball official for many years. He also coached "Murphy Mites" little league baseball and basketball teams.
After he retired, he and his wife traveled and enjoyed the winters in south Texas and Arizona. In the summers,they spent time in New Mexico and Colorado. They loved to meet there with friends and family and loved trout fishing and hunting.
He was a member of the First Baptist Church and served as a deacon and Sunday school teacher for many years. In his younger years, he sang with a Southern Gospel Quartet.
In addition to his wife, he was predeceased by his parents, stepmother, two brothers, four sisters, three step-brothers and one grandson, Brad Durham.
Survivors include three children, Shirley James and her husband Jerry, Lanna Carter and her husband Ken, all of Stillwater, and Jim Taylor and his wife Connie of Norman; six grandchildren, Rodney James and his wife Dana of Tulsa, Bryan James of Stillwater, Scott Durham and his wife Sonia of Boise, Ida., Kristin Baumann and her husband,Tom, Lindsey Taylor and Michael Taylor, all of Norman and five great-grandchildren.

Arthur Cecil TEDFORD

[I085585]
Arthur C. Tedford was a man for all seasons. He was a doctor, a civic booster, a sportsman and a cattle rancher.
Tedford of Melbourne, who practiced medicine in Brevard County for 52 years, died of kidney failure Saturday at his summer home in Franklin, N.C. He was 84.
He was still practicing medicine when he died. Tedford grew up in Orlando and graduated from Orlando High School in 1932 before earning degrees from the University of Florida School of Pharmacy and, later, from Tulane University School of Medicine in 1941.
He served as a Navy flight surgeon during World War II and opened a general practice in Melbourne in 1947 after serving at the former Banana Naval Air Station. It's now Patrick Air Force Base.
''I have never met a physician who was more able to practice medicine in a more comprehensive way,'' said Melba Tedford , his wife of 26 years. ''He loved to learn and to do.''
In 1958, he started the Brevard Crippled Children's Clinic, now the Easter Seal Rehabilitation Center.
Tedford also was an accomplished sailor and pilot. He docked his boat at the Melbourne Yacht Club, where he was a former commodore. He loved to fly and owned his own plane, which he used to commute to the Osceola County cattle ranch he bought in 1964. The ranch had its own landing strip.
He and his wife, who had moved to the ranch 20 years ago, sold the property and moved back to Melbourne two months ago. He was starting to slow down, his wife said.
Tedford also is survived by sons, Arthur III of Dove Canyon, Calif., and David of Port Orange; daughter, Barbara Tedford -DeNeve of Texas; and four grandchildren.
Brownlie and Maxwell Funeral Home in Melbourne is handling arrangements.
The Orlando Sentinel, 12 October 1999
