Jane J. Geelhoed HALL, 79, of Gulfport, died unexpectedly on Saturday, March 12th at Palms of Pasadena Hospital in St. Petersburg. Born July 21, 1931 in Lewiston, ME. She was the only child of the late Philip and Dorothy Johnson. She attended Cushing Academy and graduated from Freeport, ME, class of 1949. Jane was married in 1951 and eventually settled in Freeport, ME to raise her family. She was predeceased by her first husband, Daniel Geelhoed, in 1986 and by her second husband, Stacy Hall , in 2007. Jane was employed by L.L. Bean in Freeport for 19 years, starting in customer service and later retired in 1990 as advertising coordinator. In earlier years, she was a familiar sight at Johnson's Rexall Pharmacy in Freeport, ME, which was owned by her father. She loved to entertain and enjoyed hosting both her friends and her children's friends in her home. She was an avid golfer and won several club championships and couple's league championships at Sunny Meadows Golf Course in Freeport. She loved to read, complete crossword puzzles and never missed an evening episode of Jeopardy. She was a wonderful cook and loved making big family meals. She was a great lover of animals. She enjoyed spending winters in Florida and summers in Limerick and Frye Island, ME, and at the homes of her children in more recent years. Survivors include her three children; and ten grandchildren; daughter, Tracie Ezzio of Pepperell, MA, her husband Louis and children Kyle, Larissa, Ross, Deanna and Keegan; son, Philip Geelhoed of Poland, ME, his wife Christine and children Andrew and Sophia, and daughter Tara Gendron of Greenland, NH, her husband Barry and children Blake, Pierce and Cole; as well as several cousins. A memorial service will be held at a later date in the Freeport area. If desired, donations may be made in her memory to the ASPCA Tributes, 520 8th Ave, 7th Floor, New York, NY 10018 or by visiting www.aspca.org/donate, or preferably to your local SPCA or animal shelter.

Kenneth Albert JOHNSON

[I067047]
Amity-based state police said Kenneth Johnson , 84, of Belmont died today after a 3-car accident at the intersection of Plum Bottom Road and State Route 19 in Amity, just outisde of Scio.
Troopers said Johnson was driving South on Route 19 toward Scio when he took a left hand turn onto Plum Bottom Road and struck a marked State Police Tahoe driven by Trooper Rodney Perry.
Troopers said Johnson failed to yield to northbound traffic resulting in a collision between the two vehicles. A third vehicle, driven by Patricia Rogers of Scio, was then struck by Johnson 's vehicle. Rogers was stopped at the stop sign on Plumbottom Road, troopers said.
A 14-year-old passenger, Johnson's great-grandson, was taken to Buffalo Women and Children's Hospital by ambulance.
Johnson was pronounced dead at Jones Memorial Hospital in Wellsville.
The accident took place at 4:37 p.m. and State Route 19 was closed until 10:30 p.m. Monday night.
The investigation is continuing.

Marian Jeanette JOHNSON

[I017125]
Marian Jeanette Errington, 92, rural Northfield, died Monday morning, April 12, 2010, at Three Links Reflections Care Center in Northfield.
Marian was born August 21, 1917, in a farmhouse near the village of White Rock. Her parents, Roy G. Johnson and Emma G. (Stromme) Johnson raised her and her younger brother Willard, surrounded by aunts, uncles, cousins, grandparents and great grandparents on neighboring farms. Marian was baptized into the Lutheran faith at Spring Garden Evangelical Lutheran Church in May of 1931. She attended country school in White Rock.
Marian met Harold Robert Errington at the Cannon Valley Fairgrounds in 1939. They were married at the Spring Garden Lutheran Church parsonage on June 21, 1940, and took up residence at the Errington family farm in Sciota Township. She lived the life of a farm wife during the golden age of the American small farmer.
Marian was an avid seamstress and gardener and especially enjoyed her flower gardens. In later years she loved to reminisce about life on the farm, feeding threshing crews, grand picnics with family and relatives, family trips out west, summer fishing trips up north and the joys of her Scandinavian childhood in Belle Creek.
During recent years her life continued to flourish as she lived at Wellstone Commons apartments in Northfield, surrounded by new dear friends and helpers. In her life she worked hard, loved much and saw humor in everything. She lived a long and very healthy life interrupted only at its end by a short illness.
During much of her life she cared lovingly for her husband Harold during his long struggle with arthritis. Her ceaseless dedication to him was characteristic of her devotion to the many loved ones in her life.
Surviving are son Allan and daughter-in-law Sheryl, rural Sciota Township; son Donald, Pollock Pines, CA; son Gary and daughter-in-law Nina (Thielbar), rural Lonsdale; and daughter-in-law Lois Errington. She also has granddaughters: Angela of Northfield; Lindsay of Lonsdale; and Andrea Botto of Lafayette, CA; grandson Christopher of Bend, OR; and great-granddaughters Piper and Avery Botto; and great grandson Cedar Roy Errington. She is survived by dozens of nieces and nephews.
Preceding Marian in death were her parents, her brother Willard, her husband Harold, and her daughter-in-law Cyndy Duchscherer.
Funeral services will be 11 a.m. Friday, April 16 at St. Ansgar's Lutheran Church in Cannon Falls, with burial at Oaklawn Cemetery, Northfield beside her beloved husband, Harold.
Visitation will be Thursday from 4-8 p.m. at the Bierman Funeral Home in Northfield, and at the Church on Friday, one hour prior to services.
Arrangements by the Bierman Funeral Home of Northfield.
