Geraldine Marie HARPOLD

[I060165]
HILLSBORO, Wis. -- Geraldine M. Stanek , 63, of Hillsboro, passed away Sunday, Oct. 24, 1993, in her residence following a courageous battle with cancer.
She was born December 21, 1929 to Richard and Luella (Durkee) Harpold in Hillsboro. She grew up in the Hillsboro area where she was united in marriage to George E. Stanek on October 21, 1950. He preceded her in death Sept. 4, 1993. She was a homemaker and also assisted in owning and operating the Stanek 's Refrigeration and Appliance Store for 40 years, and also the Piggly Wiggly Bakery Department the past few years. She was also a member of St. Aloysius Catholic Church.
She is survived by two daughters, Patricia (Steve) Cox of Colorado Springs, Colo., and Jeanette (John) Sake of La Crosse ; five sons, Steven of Hillsboro, Alan (Sue) of Hillsboro, Greg (Carol) of Colorado Springs, Stanley (Claire) of La Crescent, and John of La Crescent; eight grandchildren, Jerry Buchal, Tina Buchal, Cole Stanek , April Stanek , Aaron Stanek , Nicholas Stanek , Samanatha Stanek , and Allison Stanek ; one brother, Leonard (Sherry) Harpold of Des Moines, Iowa; and three sisters, Marlene (Virgil) Vrbsky of Chetek, Wis., Dorothy Garner of Barneveld, Wis., and Bernita (Paul) Schroeder of Madison. Besides her husband, she was preceded in death by her parents; and a brother, Richard Harpold Jr.
Funeral services will be held at 10:30 a.m. on Tuesday, Oct. 26, from St. Aloysius Catholic Church, Hillsboro. Father James Ennis will officiate. Burial will be in St. John's Catholic Cemetery, Dilly. Friends may call from 4 to 9 p.m. tonight at Picha Funeral Home, Hillsboro. A 4 p.m. rosary by the Ladies of the Church, and a 8 p.m. Bible vigil followed by a K.C. rosary, are scheduled.
La Crosse Tribune, 25 October 1993

Ralph Alvin HARRIS

[I081787]
Ralph Alvin "Bunky" Harris passed away peacefully at his home in Bullhead City, AZ after a short bout with cancer on December 12, 2017. Ralph was born September 23, 1935 in Lincoln, Nebraska, the first of three sons born to Floyd and Dorothy Harris. He graduated in 1953 from Lincoln Northeast High School and proudly served in the Nebraska Air National Guard for six years. He and Darlene (Wade) Harris united in marriage May 5th, 1954. They enjoyed 54 years resulting in four wonderful children and a lifetime of memories when Darlene passed away from heart disease. His hobbies were watching, talking and cheering for the Nebraska Cornhusker football team with family, metal detecting, fishing and visiting with family at the Kohn cabin in Minnesota, cheering the family fencers, soccer players, band members, science experimentersyou name it, if we LOVED it--He LOVED it and learned all he could about our current interests. He worked in the soils testing division of the Nebraska Department of Roads for over 40 years as a dedicated and insightful member of that team, employing a brain that could recall details accurately no matter how much time had elapsed since the learning of them. After retirement he and Darlene moved from their lifelong home of Lincoln, Nebraska to the warm, dry climate of Bullhead City, AZ where they enjoyed spending time together late at night gambling side by side at the casinos. He will be deeply missed, but leaves us the example of making our first focus the good in others and ourselves and to be content with what we have. Preceded in death by parents Floyd and Dorothy Harris and wife Darlene. Survived by son Michael Harris and wife Katie of Lakeside, TX, daughter Cynthia Kohn and husband Rick of Lincoln, daughter Rebecca Renken and husband Mark of Lincoln, daughter Jennifer Wright and husband Marshall of Stanardsville, VA, ten grandchildren, two great-grandchildren (with two more on the way when he passed), brother Rodger Harris and wife Scarlet of Loveland, Colorado, brother Jeff Harris of Lincoln and many more relatives. A memorial service will be held Saturday, June 9th, 2018, 10am at Lincoln Memorial Funeral Home in Lincoln, Nebraska.

Ross C. HARRIS

[I042818]
Ross Harris of Bellevue, Washington died at age 81, Saturday, October 23, 2004 at Overlake Hospital of respiratory failure following recent surgery. A long time resident of San Diego, Ross and his wife, Pat Harris, recently moved to Washington to be near their family. Born on June 11, 1923 in Independence, Missouri, Ross, with his parents Roscoe Conkling Harris Sr. and Eleanor McCoy Harris, moved to Los Angeles when he was one year old. He attended school in Los Angeles, life guarding in the summers and was a member of the University of Southern California's water polo team. He joined the Navy during World War II to teach swimming to pilots. Ross married Pat (Shirley) Patterson on August 21, 1944 and raised their three children in Pasadena before moving to Point Loma in 1960. Ross spent 35 years working in the glass container division of Owens Illinois retiring in 1985. An avid sailor and former commodore of the Mission Bay Yacht Club, he is credited for helping establish the Snipe sailing fleet and bringing the first Snipe national regatta to San Diego. Ross devoted many years as a member of the Country Club of Rancho Bernardo enjoying the sport on a daily basis. Ross is survived by his wife, Pat (Shirley) Harris; sons, Greg Harris (Linda) and Kendall Harris (Penny Grace) of Seattle, Wash.; daughter Wendy Coulson (Kent) of Hayward, Calif.; and sister, Shirley King, of Costa Mesa, Calif. and grandchildren: Aaron and wife Jennie Harris, Eliot and wife Corinna Harris, Morgan and Mason Harris, Kevin, Ryan and Erik Coulson. Interment at Pacific View Memorial Park, Corona Del Mar, Saturday, 11 a.m. Oct. 30, 2004. Please sign the guest book at obituaries.uniontrib.com
The San Diego Union-Tribune, 29 October 2004
