Mary C. KOUBA

[I060213]
Mary C. Stanek, age 83 years, of Hillsboro, Wisconsin, passed away on Tuesday, October 8, 1991 at her home.
She was born on June 22, 1908 in the Township of Union, Vernon County, Wisconsin to Joseph and Mary (Sebranek) Kouba. She grew to adulthood in the Town of Union and attended the Champion Valley School. She was united in marriage on May 10, 1922 to Stanley Stanek in Hillsboro. He preceded her in death on January 24, 1989.
They lived at Dilly all their married lives. They were the honorary king and queen of the Hillsboro Cesky Den in 1987. She moved to Hillsboro in October of 1990. She was a member of the St. John's Catholic Church at Dilly until moving to Hillsboro and then became a member of the St. Aloysius Catholic Church. She was a member of the St. Ann's Altar Society of both parishes.
She is survived by four daughters, Angela (Jim) Marshall of LaFarge, Mildred Steckel of Waunakee, Mary Ellen (James) Bahan of Reno, Nevada, and Monica Nevins of Reedsburg; a son, George (Geraldine) Stanek of Hillsboro; thirty grandchildren; sixteen great-grandchildren; and a sister, Julia Havlik, of Hillsboro.
In addition to her husband, she was preceded in death by her parents; two sons, Clarence and James; three grandchildren; two sisters, Martha Stanek and Anna Pesik; and three brothers, John, Martin and James.
Funeral services will be held on Friday, October 11, 1991 at 11:00 a.m. at St. John's Catholic Church at Dilly, with Father James Ennis officiating. Burial is in the St. John's Catholic Cemetery. Friends may call at the Picha Funeral Home on Thursday from 4 to 9 p.m., with a Rosary Service by St. Ann's Altar Society of Dilly and Hillsboro at 4:15 p.m., and a Bible Vigil at 8:00 p.m.

Charles M. KOWING

[I019456]
ROCKFORD - Charles M. "Chuck" Kowing, 86, of Rockford died at 10 p.m. Tuesday, May 4, 2004, in Rockford Memorial Hospital. He was born Sept. 8, 1917, in Madison, Wis., to Rose Esther O'Brien and Frank P. Kowing. Charles graduated from Madison Central High School in 1936. He began an apprenticeship in carpentry with J.H. Findorff & Co., where he did mill work for Frank Lloyd Wright during the late 1930s and early 1940s. Charles married Shirley Oler on Nov. 20, 1940, his wife of nearly 54 years. He lived 46 years in Rockford, coming here from Madison. Member of the U.S. Army Air Corps and received basic training in Missouri and flight training at Texas Tech in Lubbock. He served in World War II in Algeria, North Africa. Charles was a self-employed carpenter and partner of Trussoni & Kowing Construction Co. from 1960 to 1974. He was a volunteer fireman for the North West Fire Protection from 1963 to 1997, during which time he became president of the board of trustees. After retiring, he and Shirley worked at The Loft Antique Shop. On Jan. 1, 1997, Charles married Mary Rubey in Chicago. He was a member of St. Chad's Episcopal Church and Forest City Woodcrafters. For the last years of his life, Charles and Mary were a host family to foreign-exchange students through the American Field Service. He was a host father to Kwabena "Murphy" Ofori of Ghana, Rodrigo Paz of Bolivia, Ryo Yasue of Japan and Jose Zapata of Peru. Survivors include his wife, Mary; brothers, Gordon (Carol) Kowing and Loren (Janet) Kowing, both of Madison; several nieces and nephews; and many dear friends. He was predeceased by his former wife, Shirley Kowing; brothers, Dallas, Warren and Richard; and sister, Lorraine.
Rockford Register Star (IL)
Date: May 8, 2004

Marjorie C. KRAFT

[I039026]
Spitfire Marjorie Cathlene Pellicciotti, formerly known as Marjorie Kraft, born on August 12th, 1927 has gone home to be with the Lord after bravely fighting a long battle with old age. Marjorie (Margy) attended deaf school in Manhattan, N.Y. and also the Rome School for the Deaf. There she met Carmen Pellicciotti whom she later married. They celebrated 58 years of marriage before he passed away in 2008. Margy worked at a sewing factory in Philmont N.Y. before her marriage and was a very talented seamstress. She utilized her superior sewing skills throughout her life to decorate her and her children ºs homes, make dolls and quilts, and to dress her daughters beautifully. She bred dogs as a girl, collected dolls, built fabulous doll houses, and also knew how to figure out a carpentry or building project, because she was so smart. She enjoyed a good card game, a steamy romance novel, and a cup of tea ( no sugar, please).
She was often underestimated because of her deafness but quickly those who got to know her were " put in their place" by her cleverness, gutsiness, bit of temper, unabashed honesty, and racy sense of humor. She is survived by her two daughters: Carmelita DeCicco, engaged to Paul Dietershagen and Tammy Barbour, married to William Barbour; four grandchildren: Brian and Nicoletta DeCicco, and Nathan and Bianca Barbour; nieces: Carole(Rick) Robbins, Bonnie Kraft and nephew Daniel Kraft. She is predeceased by her husband, Carmen Pellicciotti, her parents; William and Hattie Kraft, sister Nellie( Frank) Sarkozy, and brother John Kraft and son-in-law Ben DeCicco.
Though her soul is set free, she will be greatly missed by those of us left behind ,as no one could have had such a sassy and fiery mother. Marjorie's daughters would like to publicly thank her Columbia County Department of Aging case manager, Interim Health Care staff, Columbia County Meals on Wheels staff and volunteers, and her neighbor, Toni Merchant who all went above and beyond the call of duty and made it possible for Marjorie to remain safely in her home for so long. Also , a heartfelt thank you to the Pines Nursing Home staff that cared for her so compassionately in her final days. Your service to the elderly is precious and Godly.
Memorial services are to be held Tuedsay , May 29th ,2018 at Bates and Anderson-Redmond and Keeler Funeral Home 110 Green Street, Hudson, N.Y. with calling hours 11:00 am to 1 :00 pm and funeral service to follow at 1:30 pm at Ghent First Reformed Church on Co. Rt. 22 Ghent, N.Y.. In lieu of flowers, donations to the Pines in Catskill or Columbia County Meals on Wheels would be appreciated in memory of Marjorie.

Janet (Kraiger) Manspeaker Foster of Mishawaka, Ind., passed away peacefully Wednesday, Aug. 20, 2014.
Janet was born March 14, 1934, in Lawrence to Charles and Leona Kraiger, who preceded her in death. Left to cherish her memory is her husband of 11 years, David Foster . David and Janet married later in life and shared many wonderful years together. They enjoyed golfing, wintering in Florida with friends and spending time with family.
Janet was preceded in death by her first husband of 45 years, David Manspeaker, who died in 1996. Together, they had two children: Debbie (Jim) Bolinger and David (Coleen) Manspeaker. Also surviving are two stepdaughters, Patricia Foster and Hillary Foster ; a stepson, Steven Foster ; five grandchildren: Jay (Olga) Bolinger of Largo, Fla.; Becky (Michael) Dundek of Nice, France; and Trevor, Kaley and Trace Manspeaker of Mishawaka; three step-grandchildren: Leo, Max and Stephen Foster ; one sister, Ardith (Ken) Russell of Illinois; and a brother, Kirk (Charlotte) Kraiger of Arkansas. A brother, Kenneth (Joyce) Kraiger, preceded her in death.
Janet was a member of the Republican Women of Mishawaka and the St. Joseph Hospital Auxiliary, Mishawaka. She believed strongly in the value of mentoring. For 17 years, she mentored young adults and children at Grissom Middle School, Bonita Springs Elementary and recently at Pennway Alternative. One of her final wishes was for others to share their wisdom and experiences with those who are in need of guidance, support and love through mentoring.
Janet was a woman of strong Christian faith. Those who knew her have no doubt she is walking with the Lord surrounded by her loved ones. Her family will miss her presence forever, but will rejoice in memories of her until they meet again. A visitation will be from 3-5 p.m. Tuesday, Aug. 26, at River Valley Community Church, Mishawaka. Frances Augusta KRAMER

Aitkin, Minn. - Frances Augusta Ackerman , 92, of Aitkin, Minn., formerly of Aberdeen, S.D., was called to be with Lord on Saturday morning, March 8, 2003, from the Riverwood Extended Health Care, Aitkin, Minn.
She was born December 12, 1910, at Fort Pierre, S.D. to Fred and Augusta (Goltz) Kramer. The family moved and lived on a number of farms on the N.D./S.D. border near Havana, N.D. She went to school and graduated from Havana, N.D. Attended business college in Aberdeen, S.D., studied law, and worked as a legal secretary.
Frances was united in marriage to Russell R. Abersoll on September 19, 1931, Aberdeen S.D. To this Union were born two sons Russell Junior (Ivy) Abersoll of Aitkin, Minn. and Ronald F. (Gill) Abersoll of Duncan, Okla. Russell R. died of cancer September 25, 1943.
In 1949 she married Frank J. Ackerman and had one son, Patrick F. (Gaye) Ackerman of Helena, Mont. On March 31, 1989 Frank died of a stroke. Also are surviving 8 grandchildren; 13 great grandchildren; 2 great-great grandchildren; nieces, nephews and other relatives. Preceded in death by parents, brothers, William and Robert, 1 sister, Viola Lein, 1 granddaughter, Kristie Jane Abersoll.
Frances moved to Aitkin, August of 1996 and resided and enjoyed Maryhill Manor making the move to Riverwood Extended Care in September of 2000. She was a member of the Christian Temperance Union, Royal Neighbor Fraternity and Faith United Methodist Church, Aberdeen, S.D., being very active in UMW's.
Funeral Services will be held 2 p.m. Monday, March 17, 2003, at Faith United Methodist Church, Aberdeen, S.D., with Pastor John Baskins officiating.
Visitation will be 1 hour prior to services at the church.
The Organist is Donna Seaton. Singing by Frances ' granddaughters. Casketbearers are grandchildren Mark Abersoll, Cherie Couture, Tara Hallberg, Tate Abersoll, Jeremy Ackerman and Brea Ackerman .
Interment will follow at Riverside Memorial Park Cemetery. The family has provided a time of fellowship and lunch following committal services at the church.
