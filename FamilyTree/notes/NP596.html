Robert Conrad WOLF

[I040929]
Robert (Bob) Conrad Wolf, 83, of Meridian, ID, passed away July 24, 2012, peacefully at home. Bob was born January 31, 1929, in Dillon, MT, to John and Josephine (Findling) Wolf. He was the youngest of two sisters and 3 brothers. He attended school in Dillon and graduated from Beaverhead County High School, received a Bachelorʼs Degree in Education from Montana State College in Bozeman and a Masterʼs Degree in Education from Western Montana College in Dillon. He married Bernadine Kay Whitworth on May 5, 1951, in Dillon. There were three boys and two girls born to this union. He served in the Montana Army National Guard for 37 consecutive years and rose to the rank of Major. He was branch qualified in armor, artillery, chemical warfare, and military police. He and his wife, along with two other couples, organized the Dillon Little League Baseball. He was very involved in American Legion Baseball and served many years as chairman of Class A baseball in Montana and southern Alberta Canada. He served for 30 years as a member of the American Legion, acting as commander of Post 20 in Montana for 3 years and 1 year as commander of Post 113 in Idaho. Bob received the Outstanding Legionnaire of the Year citation for the years 2008-2009. He was an active member of the LDS Church, serving in many capacities over the years. He and his wife served 4 missions. Bob taught Math, Industrial Arts, and Drivers Training at Beaverhead County High School for 30 years. He was a contractor and was a gifted wood worker. Those who knew Bob will remember his patriotism and that he lived to volunteer and be of service to others. He is preceded in death by his parents, 2 older brothers, and 2 sisters. He is survived by his wife Kay, sons Dr. David Wolf (Janet) of Carmichael, CA; Dale Wolf (Jan) of Great Falls, MT; Eric Wolf (Kimberly) of Meridian, ID; and daughters Cheri Blomquist (Kevin) of Twin Falls, ID; and Shelli Schuett (Dr. David) of Dillon, MT; one brother Ray of Stoughton, WI; 17 grandchildren; 13 great grandchildren; and numerous nieces and nephews. Services will be at Meridian West Stake Center, 1985 Black Cat Rd, on Saturday, July 28, at 11 a.m. The family will greet friends from 9:30-10:30. Graveside services will be at Idaho Veterans Cemetery on Monday, July 30, at 11 a.m. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the Boys Basketball Program at Rocky Mountain High School (RMHS Boys Basketball, 3917 Bryce Canyon Place, Meridian ID 83646).

Berna Oatway passed away at the Red Deer Regional Hospital on December 18, 2000 at the age of 89 years. Berna was born in Elstow, Saskatchewan on June 15, 1911. Berna was raised on a dairy farm near Guernsey, Saskatchewan and received her education at Guernsey and Moose Jaw Normal School where she received her teaching degree. Berna taught school during the depression of the 1930s and had many stories of those trying times. Berna married Norton Oatway in 1938 whom she met while teaching at Nottawa, Saskatchewan. They farmed near Unity, Saskatchewan until 1947 when they bought a farm in the Clive area, raising purebred Hereford cattle. Berna moved to Lacombe in 1991 and had been in the West Park Lodge in Red Deer since November 2000. Berna was predeceased by her husband Norton in 1991, sisters; Ellen and Marion and grandson Craig. Berna is survived by her sons Donald (Barbara) of Calgary, Grant (Lois) of Clive, Gerry (Bonnie) of Cochrane, five grandchildren; Nola, Brenda, Ward, Jason and Shannon, three great-grandchildren; Evan, Reed and Ezri. Ernest Gordon WOOD

[I031666]
Gordon E. "Woody" Wood passed away of natural causes on March 17, 2015 at home in Tumwater, Washington, at the age of 85. He was born to Ernest and Gertrude Wood on September 24, 1929 in Britain, South Dakota. He was raised in Chehalis, Washington, and became a longtime resident. He received diplomas from WF West High School in Chehalis and Centralia College, and also attended the University of Washington.
Gordon served in the U.S. Air Force (1950-1954) during the Korean War, achieving the rank of Sergeant. He was stationed in South Carolina at Shaw Air Force Base, and in Alaska. He played on the All-Star U.S. Air Force basketball team.
Professionally, Gordon was an insurance agent with Allstate, specializing in recreational sales. He also was a member of the Eagles in Centralia.
A resident of Tumwater for the last 17 years, Gordon was a good-natured, fun-loving and caring person. He enjoyed traveling, camping, spending time in the outdoors, playing cards, following horseracing and many other sports. He was a huge UW Huskies fan.
Gordon is survived by his beloved constant companion of many fun years, Patty Pastornicky of Tumwater; daughter, Ramona Diaz, her husband, Jim, and their children, Ray and Ava, all of Lafayette, California; sister, Phyllis Morgan of San Clemente, California; brothers, Deryl Wood and his wife Nyla of Olympia, Lowell Wood and his wife Jan of Jacksonville, Florida, and Glenn Wood and his wife Cheri of Glendale, California; and numerous nephews and nieces. He was preceded in death by his parents; brother, Clarence; and sister, Eunice.

She is survived by her children and their families: Jan and Stewart Bell, and son, Jesse, of Midlothian; Betty and Ned Cleland, and daughter, Jennifer, of Winchester, and son, John, of Los Angeles, Calif.; Robert and Julia Flagler, and children, Emily and Avery, of Hanover; sister, Elizabeth Ingram of Hopewell; cousins, Marie Frith and Katherine Crouch, both of Mechanicsville; sisters-in-law, Louise Stark of Hopewell and Joyce Butsch of Broomall, Pa; and other extended families.

She graduated from Chester (now Thomas Dale) High School and Montreat College. She married her sweetheart after he returned from service in WWII. Together they raised their boomer generation children, camped, fished, bowled, and played cards. They were members of the VFW Local 1426 and Ladies' Auxiliary in Midlothian.
Frances retired after a long career as a clerk with the Virginia State Board of Nurses in Richmond. After John's death, Frances lived with her daughter's family and attended Central Baptist Church.
The family would like to thank the local chapters of the American Parkinson's Disease Association (APDA) and Alzheimer's Association for their support. Gratitude is extended to Frances' church group, the Willing Workers, for their friendship.
