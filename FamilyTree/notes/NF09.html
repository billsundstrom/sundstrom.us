Cassadaga - Miss Joan Eileen Rath, daughter of Mrs. Florence D. Rath, 37 Leroy Avenue, Buffalo, and Everett Howard Webster, son of Mr. and Mrs. Russell E. Webster, were united in marriage at Park Baptist Church, Buffalo, on June 9 in the presence of 250 guests. Rev. John Neubert, pastor and Rev. Harold Snitker of Jerusalem Evangelical and Reform Church, Buffalo, officiated at the ceremony which, took place before an altar decorated with white snapdragon sprays. Miss Ruth Johnson was soloist.
Miss Kathleen Herniman, Buffalo, was maid of honor. She wore a blue organdy gown with a picture hat and carried yellow carnations arranged in colonial style. Bridesmaids were Mrs. Donald Neurevter of Buffalo, 5owned in yellow organdy, and liss Jane Evans of Kenmore In pink organdy. Both wore picture hats and carried colonial bouquets of carnations.
Groomsmen included Robert Holtz, Allegany; Richard Jerome, Jamestown and Donald Stellrecht, Buffalo.
The bride, given in marriage by her brother, Robert C. Rath, was gowned in white chiffon over white satin. The gown was fashioned with full draped skirt with short train, drop-shoulder neckline, fitted bodice, and full, gathered sleeves of chiffon. Her rayon tulle veil, finger-tip length, was attached to a halo hat of satin. The bridal bouquet was a cascade of white carnations.
The mother of the bride was gowned in mauve lace and crepe with matching hat. She wore a corsage of pink roses. Mrs. Webster wore black sheer chiffon with white accessories and a corsage of yellow roses. Following the ceremony,. a reception was held at the Forty and Eight Club, Buffalo. Following a wedding trip to Williamsburg, V., and Washington, D.C, Mr. and Mrs. Webster will be at home at 160 Woodell Avenue, Buffalo. The bride wore brown sheer rayon with yellow accessories for traveling.
In July 19, Mr. Webster will report to active duty at Sampson Air Force Base, Geneva. He has recently been commissioned a 2nd lieutenant. A graduate of Buffalo State Teachers' College, he spent two years la the Army Air Force during World War II. He is presently a member of the Air Force Reserves. He is a member of Phi Delta Kappa at University of Buffalo, and a member of the faculty at Cheektowaga Cleveland Hill School, Buffalo.
Prenuptial affairs were given in Buffalo by the Misses Jane Evans and Kathleen Herniman and Mrs. Isabelle Stellrecht, and in Cassadaga by Mrs. Wesley Webster, Mrs. Clifford Webster, Mrs. Elmer Nutt, Mrs. Roman Miller, Mrs. Alberta Snyder, Mrs. LaVerne Webster.
Out-of-town guests were present from Cassadaga, Dunkirk, Fredonia, Jamestown, Sinclairville, Oak Harbor, Ohio and Rochester.
Jamestown Post-Journal, 27 June 1951

Thursday evening, August 17, it was my privilege to officiate at a quiet wedding ceremony held in our home here at South Lancaster. Enar Gottfred Hallgren, formerly of Gardner, but more recently of Northampton, Mass., and Miss Eloise Cynthia Hoxie, of Northampton were united in marriage. The bridal party consisted of the parents and near relatives of the groom, the brother of the bride and a few close friends. Mr. and Mrs. Hallgren will make their home at Northampton, where Brother Hallgren is engaged in farming. Both are members of the Northampton church. We wish them a happy experience as they build their new home.
