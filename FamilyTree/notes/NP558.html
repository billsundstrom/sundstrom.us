John C. VANDYKE

[I094334]
John C. Van Dyke Passed Away at His Home in Pequaming Tuesday Night.
The community was surprised Wednesday morning upon learning of the sudden death of John C. Van Dyke, which occurred at hjs home in Pequaming Tuesday night. Mr. Van Dyke had not been in good health for some time, but was able to be around and attend to his duties connected with the hotel in Pequaming, of which he had charge, and Tuesday he drove to Baraga.
Upon his return home about 5 o'clock he appeared as usual, and ate a hearty supper, and in the evening retired at his usual hour. About 11:30 o'clock his son Harry heard him breathing heavily, and, accompanied by Mrs. Van Dyke, went to his bed and tried to arouse him, but Mr. Van Dyke passed away in a few moments. The cause of death was heart failure.
Deceased was born in Middlebury, Scoharie county, New York, October 4, 1834, and was therefore 70 years, one month and eleven days old.
He served in the civil war, and received a wound, from which he never fully recovered.
He was united in marriage October 8, 1876, to Miss Jennie C. Wright, at West De Pere, Wis. About two years ago Mr. Van Dyke and family located in Pequaming, and took charge of the hotel, and during their residence made many warm friends.
Mr. Van Dyke was a member of the G. A. R., and held in high esteem by all who knew him.
Besides the bereaved widow, four children, Jesse, of Chicago, Leslie, of New Richmond, Wis., and Harry and Genevieve, residing at home, are left to mourn his departure.
The funeral was held from the late home in Pequaming, Friday morning at 8 o'clock, Rev. Horace N. Aldrich, pastor of the M. E. church, conducting the service, and also accompanied the family to Ishpeming, where the burial took place.

Paul Irvin VANKEUREN

[I097221]
Paul Irving VanKeuren , 83, of Charlevoix, died Oct. 29, 2003, at Boulder Park Terrace Nursing Center.
He was born May 8, 1920, in Lansing, the son of James I. and Margaret (Kennedy) VanKeuren . He grew up in Lansing and was a graduate of Cranbrook School in Bloomfield Hills.
In 1942, he married his childhood sweetheart, Mary Lou Caulkins, at St. Paul's Episcopal Church in Lansing. He and his wife sang in St. Paul's choir for more than 30 years.
Mr. VanKeuren served two years in Tokyo, Japan, with Gen. MacArthur's staff in the Army of Occupation.
Following his retirement as manager of Ingham County Title and Abstract Company, he and his wife spent part of the year in East Lansing and the remainder in their home on St. Croix in the U.S. Virgin Islands. They moved to Charlevoix in 1990.
He was preceded in death by his parents; his wife, Mary Lou; and a brother, Stewart. He is survived by a niece, Maritje VanKeuren -Geroux, of Venice, Fla.; and a nephew, James I. VanKeuren III, of Washington, D.C.
A memorial service will be held at 10 a.m. Saturday, Nov. 1, at Christ Episcopal Church in Charlevoix. Cremation has taken place and burial will be in St. Paul's Episcopal Church Memorial Garden in Lansing.
Petoskey News-Review, 30 October 2003
