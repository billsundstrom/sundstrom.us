RANDOLPH - Monica Jean Rice and Wade Anthony Manning were married June 26, 2004, in a double-ring ceremony performed by Justice of the Peace Martha Lawrence at the couple's home in Randolph. The bride is the daughter of Patricia I. and G. Dave Eaton of Montpelier and Allen and Kathy Fogarty-Rice of Belmont, N.H. The groom is the son of Elmora Manning of Randolph and the late Chester R. Manning. The groom and the daughter of the bride, Katelyn Jean Rice, escorted the bride. Alice Cloud was the maid of honor. Stacey Manning, niece of the groom, Shavone Baker and Tamara Race were the bridal attendants. Jack Manning, brother of the groom, was the best man. Timothy Hutchinson and Calvin Manning, nephews of the groom, and Allen Montgomery were the ushers. Katelyn Jean Rice was the flower girl. Gualberto Manning, a relative, was the ring bearer. A reception was held at the couple's home. They took a honeymoon trip to Mount Washington in New Hampshire. The bride graduated from Twinfield Union High School in Marshfield. She is a licensed nursing assistant at Upper Valley Services in Randolph, Gifford Hospital in Randolph and Mayo Nursing Home in Northfield. The groom graduated from Randolph High School in Randolph and Vermont Technical College in Randolph. He is a dispatcher at Central Vermont Public Service in Rutland.
The Barre Montpelier Times-Argus, 25 July 2004

CLYDE - Wedding vows were exchanged by Miss Dorothy Lynn Wentworth and Steven F. Chilson at 2 p.m., Saturday, Sept. 27 at the Tyre Reformed Church on Rt. 414. The Rev. David W. Ayers officiated a Line ceremony, and the church was decorated with oval baskets of daisy pompoms, cattails and wheat. Mrs. Ruth Smith was the organist. Mr. and Mrs. Benning Lewis Wentworth Jr. of RD 2, Clyde, are the bride's parents. Mr. Chilson is the son of the late Mr. and Mrs. Howard Chilson of the town of Galen.
Mr. Wentworth escorted his daughter to the altar. The floor-length wedding gown, made by the bride, was of embroidered voile fashioned with a gathered skirt, empire waist encircled with daisies and a high Peter Pan collar. The long chiffon sleeves were gathered to snap-closed sleeves. A gold heirloom pin from her Wentworth ancestors was worn in the center of the collar. The elbow length veil was caught to a bow of the same material as the dress. The bride carried cascade bouquet of daisy pompoms and green ivy.
Mrs. Charles Schade of Lyons was the matron of honor Roger A. Chilson of Geneva was his brother's best man. Two other brothers, Howard R. Chilson of Waterloo and Ronald E. Chilson of Seneca Falls, were the ushers.
A reception at the Junius Fire Hall was attended by guests from Savannah, Lyons, Seneca Falls, Newark, Geneva, Waterloo, Massachusetts, Vermont and Missouri. Following a wedding trip through the Adirondacks, the couple will be at home on Benning Rd., Clyde.
The bride is a graduate of Clyde Central School and of Auburn Community College where she majored in art, music and science. She is employed at Sembl Inc. at Clyde. Her husband is a graduate of Waterloo Central School and is employed a t Seneca Falls Machine Co.
THE Geneva Times, 1 October 1969

The marriage of Miss Barbara Ann Saunders, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. O. G. Saunders of Troy. O., to Lamont Bruce Webster, son of Mr. and Mrs. Clifford A. Webster of 232 Temple Street, was solemnized at 3:30 o'clock Saturday afternoon at the First Congregational Church in Troy. The Rev. Arthur C. Decker officiated at the ceremony.
The bride wore a gown of Chantilly lace and tulle over white bridal satin fashioned with a strapless lace bodice topped by a fitted Spencer jacket having a tiny Peter Pan collar and long lace sleeves. The bouffant ballerina skirt was embellished with an insert of lace. Her two-tiered French illusion veil, of fingertip length, fell from a Juliet cap of lace with pearl trim. She carried a bouquet of roses and baby orchids.
Miss Patricia Limber, of Toledo. Ohio, as: maid of honor, wore a ballerina length light blue antique silk gown and carried a bouquet of yellow roses.
Jack Van Scoter of the Van Buren Road was best man and Mac Evarts of Temple Street the usher.
For her daughter's wedding Mrs. Saunders chose a navy blue dress. The bridegroom's mother favored a Dior blue silk shantung dress with black accessories.
Following the-ceremony, a reception was held for 100 guests at the Alturian Club in Troy. Upon their return from a short wedding trip, the bridal couple will reside at 1002 Eighth Street, Bowling Green, Ohio, where the bridegroom is a student -at Bowling Green State University.
Mrs. Webster is a graduate of Tipp City High School and Bowling Green. University. Mr. Webster, who served three years in the United States Army, is a graduate of the Fredonia Hih School and Bryant and Stratton Business College in Buffalo.
The Fredonia Censor, 9 September 1954

SHINGLEHOUSE, PA - Miss Cora Audine Murray, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Fred Murray, became the bride of Charles Edward Hawkes, Sunday night at the home of her parents.
The ceremony was performed by the Rev. George F. Sickenberger, pastor of the Christian and Missionary Alliance Church. Attending the couple were Miss Doris Murray, sister of the bride, and John Green, the bride's brother-in-law.
The bride wore a dress of blue satin with red and white trim. Her corsage was of sweet peas, red roses and babies-breath. The bridesmaid selected a blue and white dress with corsage of pink roses, sweet peas and babies-breath.
Immediately following the ceremony a reception was held for nineteen guests.
The groom has been employed by Ben Price. The couple will reside on the farm of the bride's grandfather, Carmon Freer, Plank Road.

LITTLE GENESEE - On July 14, 1996, Charles and Cora Hawkes of Little Genesee were honored with an open house at the Ceres Community Center to celebrate their golden wedding anniversary.
The couple observed their anniversary on March 10, 1996, while at their winter home in Florida.
Mr. and Mrs. Hawkes received about 60 guests at their open house fron NY, PA, ME, OH, and AL attending.
An anniversary quilt made by Marie Hawkes, daughter-in-law of Mr. and Mrs. Hawkes, served as a guest registrar.
Mr. and Mrs. Hawkes were married at the home of the bride's parents, Mr. and Mrs. Fred Murray of Shinglehouse, PA. Robert (John) Green, brother-in-law of the bride, was the best man and Doris Hawkes, sister of the bride, was the maid of honor.
The couple have four children, Mrs. James (Thelma) Burrows of Portville; Larry Hawkes of Bath, ME; Willard Hawkes of Atmore, AL; and Bruce Hawkes of Ceres. They also have 10 grandchildren and four great-grandchilden. In 1987, Mr. Hawkes retired from Dexter/Hysol after 17 years of service.
Mrs. Hawkes retired from AVX in 1991 after 26 years of service.
