Ellen Sanger RICHARDSON

[I098872]
MRS. HUNTOON AT REST.
A Pioneer Who Came to Kansas in 1857
Mrs. Ellen S. Huntoon, whose funeral took place on Wednesday afternoon, was one of the first settlers of Topeka, having come here with her husband, Colonel Joel Huntoon, forty-one years ago.
Mrs. Huntoon whose maiden name was Ellen S. Richardson, was born at Alstead, N. H., December 2, 1823, and was married at the same place to Mr. Joel Huntoon, May 22, 1845, and moved the same year to Lawrenceville, Pa., where they lived until April 1857, when they came to Kansas.
Their journey from Kansas City, Mo., to Topeka was made in a lumber wagon. Soon after arriving in Topeka, Colonel Huntoon preempted eighty acres of land which now forms part of the city of Topeka, and upon which the family home now stands.
There were seven children, one of whom died in infancy, and another, Joel Huntoon, Jr., who was well known here, died in 1895. Of those now living there were present at the funeral, Loudean P., now of Fort Wayne, Ind.; Fred R., living near Topeka; Eva J., wife of Rev. P. M. Griffin, now of Brockton, Mass.; Nellie H., wife of C. Sim, now of Wichita. Another son, Fritz A., now of San Jose, Cal., was not present at the funeral, but there were present several grandchildren and great grandchildren. The sons acted as pallbearers.
Mrs. Huntoon was a member of the Episcopal church and her quiet and lovable disposition was manifest to all about her, and she was especially known and esteemed by all the earlier settlers of Topeka. Her life was one of loving devotion to her children and her love of flowers and little children was her strong characteristic. Her bereaved husband and children have the sympathy of a large circle of friends.
Topeka State Journal, 17 December 1898, 2:5.

George Washington RICHARDSON

[I065523]
Mr. George W. Richardson, one of the old settlers of DeKalb county, died at his home, two and a half miles southeast of Genoa, on Wednesday last. Mr. Richardson came from New York and settled in this county about 1838. He was about 60 years of age, and by honesty and industry had accumulated a competency, being the owner of one of the finest 160 acre farms in the county. Four children - two sons and two daughters, and a faithful wife, are left to mourn his loss. The funeral services at the M.E. church in Genoa yesterday at 11 o'clcok were attended by a large number of mourning friends.

Sarah Joan RICHARDSON

[I065529]
Sally Stillinger peacefully passed away in her home at Hillcrest in La Verne early Friday, November 16, 2007. She was born in 1922 in Sioux City Iowa and grew up there as an only child. She earned a BS degree from Iowa State University where she met Clark Stillinger. They married in 1945 and moved to Pomona where Clark opened a veterinary practice and they raised three boys. Sally was a terrific mother dedicating herself and her time to her sons, their schools and their sports teams, She was active in the Assistance League and was an avid sports fan. In her free time she enjoyed golf, bridge and gourmet cooking. Once all her boys were off to college she embarked on a second "career" teaching home economics at Emerson Junior High in Pomona and later in Fallbrook, CA. After 20 years she retired and she and Clark traveled the western states in their RV before settling in Sedona, AZ for several years prior to returning to La Verne. Sally is survived by her sons; Rich, Scott and Ron and four grandchildren; Doug, Kelsey, Ryan and Matt. Donations in her memory can be made to the American Cancer Society.
Inland Valley Daily Bulletin, 24 November 2007
