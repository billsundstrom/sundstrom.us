Isabelle E. MOSS

[I099231]
MUSKEGON -- A 96-year-old Muskegon woman was found dead in the kitchen of her home after firefighters forced entry Thursday night as smoke poured from the structure.
A family member identified the victim as Isabella Flickema , who lived alone. The fire was reported around 9:53 p.m.
Fire Marshal Major Metcalf said Friday that Flickema , who was sight and hearing-handicapped, was found just inside the entryway from the dining room to the kitchen.
"Right now, it appears that the fire started in the northeast corner of a kitchen wall, and it appears to have been electrical," he said, adding that the wiring in the home was old.
A neighborhood couple leaving the gas station across the street saw smoke coming from the one-story house. They said it looked like too much smoke to be from a chimney.
"We thought we should drive by," said Michelle LeMieux, 31. She and Christopher Place, 30, saw the house was dark. They stopped their vehicle and he began pounding on the front door.
"It sounded like a TV was going inside," Place said, but no one answered the door. He said he knocked on the windows and went to the back door.
"The paint on the back door was starting to pucker and a window was gone from the heat," he said. "I was hoping no one was inside."
Mark Kincaid, deputy public safety director for fire services, said when firefighters arrived they were told an elderly woman lived there and that no one knew if she was home. A primary search was conducted and the victim, dressed in a nightgown and slippers, was discovered.
Metcalf said it looked as if she "rolled her bedcovers back to get up and walked to the kitchen," where she probably inhaled smoke and superheated gases.
The fire marshal estimated from the amount of damage and the fire patterns coming from the area where the blaze started that the fire could have been burning in the wall for some time. "By the time she got to the kitchen, the fire had broken through the wall," he said.
Although fire officials at first believed there were no smoke detectors in the home, the victim's son indicated there was an alarm in the dining room area.
Metcalf said the woman had a lifeline-type alarm that is activated by pushing a button and she also had a police scanner. While the television set was off when he arrived, Metcalf said the noise the couple heard could have come from either the scanner or someone on the other end of the help line.
The Grand Rapids Press, 23 February 23, 2008

Francis H. MOTTLEY

[I102148]
From the January 19, 2008 edition of the Dubuque Telegraph Herald

DARLINGTON, Wis. -- Francis H. "Chub" Mottley, 81, of Darlington, died Thursday, Jan. 17, 2008, at Upland Hills Health in Dodgeville.

Services will be at 11:30 a.m. Tuesday at Holy Rosary Catholic Church, Darlington, with the Rev. Randy Budnar officiating. Burial will be in the church cemetery. Friends may call from 3 to 8 p.m. Monday at Steil Camancho Funeral Home, 206 E. Harriet Street, Darlington, where a parish and Knights of Columbus scripture service will be held at 3 p.m.
He was born on June 29, 1926, in Willow Springs Township, son of Curtis and Margaret (Hendrickson) Mottley. He graduated from Darlington High School in 1944. Then, in 1947, with his heart set on playing football, he enrolled at UW-Madison, where he spent a semester until a knee injury forced him off the field. Chub then attended business school in Madison for accounting before returning to Lafayette County and landing a job at Lafayette County Co-op Oil.
On Sept. 17, 1949, he married Mary Jo Martin at Holy Rosary Catholic Church. For the next 48 years, he delivered oil and got involved with the city's recreation program as a coach, spending the next 15 or so years coaching baseball. He spent 40 years as a school bus driver. He retired in 1991.
One constant in Chub's life was his commitment to his family and community. He was a lifetime member of Holy Rosary Catholic Church and its Monsignor Doyle Knights of Columbus Council No. 1080. He was a member of the Darlington Country Club. Chub was chairman of the committee that headed up the building of the swimming pool in Darlington in 1958, and went on to manage the facility for 15 years. He was a volunteer and honorary member of Darlington Volunteer Fire Dept., and spent 50 years as captain of the "chain gang" on the DHS football field beginning the year he got out of high school. He also served on the committee when the new Darlington High School was built in 1965, and was one of the originators of the Darlington Canoe Festival. From 1968 until 1984, he served on the Lafayette County Board of Supervisors and during that time was chairman of the committee that oversaw the remodeling of the courthouse and addition of the ag building and jail.
Surviving are his wife, Mary Jo; his children, Kathy (Rick) Hempel, of Madison, Kristy (Mike) Hutchens, of Lakeland, Fla., Mary Pat (Bob) Vosberg, of Platteville, Theresa (Garon) Reinke, of Madison, Terry (Connie) Mottley, of Park Ridge, Ill.; 10 grandchildren, Michelle, Patricia, Ryan, Troy, Rachel, Sarah, Brian, Kevin, Elise, and Luke; a sister, Eunice Arnold, of Arcadia; and other relatives and friends.
He was preceded in death by his parents; two brothers, Richard in infancy, and Bob Mottley; and two sisters, Dorothy Ehrich and Catherine Mottley.
