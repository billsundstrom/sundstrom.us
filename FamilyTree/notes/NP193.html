Howard D. Fuller
Hortonville, May 29 - Throughout the province and particularly in Halifax, where he had many friends, there will be deep sorrow at the news of the passing today, at his home here, of Howard D. Fuller. Mr. Fuller, who was in his 69th year, was a life-long resident, and well known farmer of Hortonville. The funeral service will be from the residence at 2:30 o'clock Thursday.

Hugh Jameson FULLER

[I062193]
Train Crash Kills Elderly Jesup Man
JESUP - Every morning Hughie J. Fuller, 84, would load his cans of milk in the family car and take them to the creamery.
Almost every morning he would take the same route crossing the Illinois Central Railroad tracks on 20th St.
Saturday morning was no different. Fuller loaded the milk in the car and set off for the creamery.
He crossed the tracks at 7:25 AM but Fuller apparently failed to see a passenger train speeding east. It rammed his auto broadside and threw him out of the car, killing him outright.
The car was pushed 266 feet and was a total loss. There are no warning lights at the crossing.
Engineer of the train was R. J. Bradfield of Cedar Falls.
Two years ago, on Aug 15, 1964, a few blocks east, a train car collision killed George Damme of Casper, Wyo., his wife and daughter. This fatality prompted the installation of automatic grade crossing signals at that intersection.
Funeral services for Fuller will be 2 PM tomorrow at the First Presbyterian church here with burial in the Cedar Crest Cemetery. The Parker Funeral Home is in charge.
The retired farmer is survived by his widow; two sons, Walter of Dubuque and Harold of Emmestburg; a daughter, Mrs. Gertrude Widdel of Waterloo; three brothers, Elmer of Jesup, Earl of Clear Lake and George of California and a sister, Mrs. A. C. Debacca of Los Angeles.
Waterloo Courier, 31 July 1966

On January 16, 1973, he was united in marriage to Francella Guinn in St. James, Virginia. The couple made their home in Estherville and Ray worked at NAPA for 20 years, retiring in 2012. For hobbies, he enjoyed old western movies, NASCAR, auto racing, fishing, Green Bay Packers, bingo, wood crafts and plastic canvas.
He was a lifetime member of the VFW Post 3388 in Estherville and a past member of American Legion Post 162 of Graettinger.
