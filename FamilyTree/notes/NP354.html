Kathleen Carol MCCANN

[I006833]
PROVIDENCE -- Kathleen C. McCann, 57, of Bettinson Street, East Taunton, Mass., died Sunday, July 20, 2003, at Women and Infants Hospital. She was the daughter of the late James L. and Artemise (Pohl) McCann.
Born in Saint Cloud, Minn., on June 16, 1946, she graduated from John Marshall High School in Minneapolis.
After graduation, Ms. McCann moved to Atlanta where she trained as a flight attendant for Eastern Airlines for several years. She then moved to Westport, Mass., and worked as an administrator at Hanover House Retirement Home in Fall River, Mass.
While working she attended night classes and studied for 10 years at the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth to earn a bachelor of science in accounting in 1985. She began working for A.B. Dick Co. in North Dartmouth, Mass., in the early 1980s and then went on to work for Delphax Systems Inc. in Canton, Mass.
She later worked as a financial accounting supervisor at Teledyne Rodney Metals in New Bedford, Mass., and as an accountant for Hallsmith-Sysco Food Services in Norton, Mass.
Ms. McCann was a member of the Institute of Management Accountants, Fall River, New Bedford chapter. She was also a member of the Order of Saint Luke's the Physician and would minister the sick with spirituality for healing. She attended conferences in Washington, D.C. and Toronto, Ontario.
She regularly attended service at Episcopal churches in Barrington and Portsmouth and for the last several years was a member of the First Congregational Church in Fall River.
She enjoyed spending time with her family, painting, flowers and plants, watching movies, music, cooking, and traveling.
Survivors include a daughter, Andrea Greenwood Syron of Westport; a son, Eric Greenwood of Westport; two sisters, Mary Jo Carlson of Elk River, Minn. and Collen A. McCann of Minneapolis; five brothers, James McCann of Las Vegas, Dennis M. McCann of Columbus, Ohio, Steven J. McCann of Forest Lake, Minn., Kevin A. McCann of Maplegrove, Minn., and Brian D. McCann of Resole, Minn.; two grandchildren; and many nieces and nephews.
Her funeral will be at 10 a.m. Friday in the First Congregational Church, 282 Rock St., Fall River. Burial will follow in the Assonet Burial Ground in Assonet, Mass.
Arrangements are by Hathaway Home for Funerals, 1813 Robeson St., Fall River.

Clarabell Charlotte MCCAULL

[I023460]
Clara Benson passed away peacefully with family by her side on Monday March 14, 2016 at the age of 83.
Clara was born on November 7, 1932 in Parson BC. Clara was the sixth child of Janet and Calvin McCaull. She grew up on the family farm in Parson with six sisters and one brother surrounded by a very large extended family.
Clara met Garth Benson in the early 1950's; they married on June 18, 1955. They moved frequently between Creston and Golden. They moved back to the valley in 1965. After living in town for many years Clara and Garth moved to a piece of property, it is bordered by the Blaeberry and Columbia Rivers. This "piece of heaven that my husband made for me" as she described it, became a place where family and friends were welcomed with Claraʼs particular brand of hospitality.
Claraʼs world revolved around her family, both immediate and extended. For those that knew and loved her there is a familiar theme of memories of what she loved: cooking, playing cards, camping, fishing and picking berries, gardening and her personal relationships with cows and a goose named 'Honk'. Clara was never too busy to listen, to make people laugh with her quick wit. People of all ages and backgrounds enjoyed spending time with her.
Clara cherished her children, grandchildren, and great grandchildren. Clara was predeceased by Garth on January 17, 2013. They enjoyed 57 years together. Clara will be lovingly remembered and deeply missed by her children Mark, Leonard, Susan, and Penny (Keith), grandchildren, and great grandchildren.
At a later date, Clara's wishes for her and Garth's cremated remains to be spread together on the farm will be honoured.
The Golden Special, 14 March 2016

Edith Mary MCCAULL

[I000149]
It is with deep sadness that we, the family, announce the passing of Edith Mary Johnson, a beloved mother, grandmother and great grandmother at Durand Manor, Golden B.C. on November 8th, 2014. She was born in Parson B.C. on February 4th, 1927 to Calvin and Janet McCaull, the 4th of 8 children - 7girls and 1 boy. She spent her childhood in Parson and finished school at 13 years old with a grade 9 education. She worked at various jobs, cooking in logging camps and cafes. In the early 40?s she worked at the Cranbrook hospital and then at the hospital in New Westminster, B.C. as a nurses aid where she preformed some of the duties of an R.N., wearing the nurses uniform and cap. In 1946 she nursed at the Revelstoke hospital. Nursing was her passion! She married Clayton Johnson in 1948 and they raised 5 children. She did family genealogy for 60 years, before it was a trend. She held the position of postmistress at the Parson P.O. for 27 years. She had the H&R Block franchise in Golden B.C. for 29 years. She planted trees for 2 seasons and for a few years she was a beekeeper and sold honey. She loved to spend quality time with her grandchildren and great grandchildren. She loved to dance, quilt, knit, take pictures, and had a love for the outdoors - fishing, hunting, berry picking and snow machining. At age 70 she went tandem paragliding from Mount 7. She is survived by her husband Clayton, daughter Jeni (Garry) Brock and 2 sons, Jerrie (Nancy) Johnson and Lyle (Alaina) Johnson. Predeceased by her son Ralph in 1970, her son Ron in 2010, her parents Calvin and Janet McCaull, sister Bertha Doumnoff and her brother Bruce McCaull.
