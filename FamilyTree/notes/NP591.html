Margaret Williams "Peg" Linstroth
Linstroth Margaret Williams "Peg" Linstroth, 89, passed away peacefully on November 7, 2004. Born on May 21, 1915, she was the daughter of the late James T. and Louise H. Williams. Her father, a pioneer in the macaroni manufacturing industry, was the founder of the Minneapolis based Creamette Company. More than 60 years ago, Mr. Williams placed Peg's lovely face and reassuring smile on the familiar green box of the company's flagship line of elbow macaroni known to pasta aficionados as "Creamettes." Peg's visage continues to smile on consumers of Creamette pasta brands today. Peg served as a member of the Creamette Company Board of Directors and as a Trustee for the J. T. Williams Family Trust. Peg was a long-time member of Basilica of St. Mary in Minneapolis where she was baptized, received her first communion, was confirmed and married. Peg attended Basilica Grade School; St. Margaret's Academy and graduated Phi Beta Kappa from The College of St. Catherine in St. Paul. Until the end of her life, Peg had remained active in the alumnae associations of her beloved "schools." In addition, Peg supported numerous local charities including the Poor Clare Sisters. Peg was preceded in death by her beloved husband of 62 years, John H. Linstroth; and is survived by her six cherished children: son, John P. Linstroth of West Palm Beach, Florida; daughter, Catherine L. Stewart of Alexandria, Minnesota; daughter, Margaret L. Francis and her husband Dr. Gary Francis of Bratenahl, Ohio; son, J. Thomas Linstroth and his wife Shirley Linstroth of Columbia Heights, Minnesota; son, Peter M. Linstroth of Edina Minnesota; and son, Paul J. Linstroth of Edina, Minnesota. Peg expressed a special devotion and loving interest in her 18 grandchildren: John, Molly (Joseph Del Re) and Michael (Robyn Poarch) Linstroth; Tim, Maggie (Michael Weinkauf) and Patrick Stewart; Gary Jr. (Kimberley), Amy (Kevin Johnson) and Katie Francis; Sarah (Blair Reinhard), Rachel, Jesse and Martha Linstroth; Jennifer and Ryan Linstroth; and, Joseph, Nicholas and Daniel Linstroth. Peg loved and cherished her 12 great grandchildren: Alexandra and Peyton Del Re; Robin Michael Linstroth (deceased); Greta, Benjamin and William Weinkauf; Mallorie, Alexander and Ella Francis; and Andrew, Maxwell and Joseph Johnson. Mass of Christian Burial will be celebrated Saturday November 13, Noon at the Basilica of St. Mary (co-cathedral) 88 N 17th St. with The Very Reverend Michael O' Connell as Celebrant. The family will receive friends and loved ones two hours prior to Mass. Interment St. Mary's Cemetery. Those planning an expression of sympathy, in lieu of flowers, are asked to consider making a contribution in memory of Peg Linstroth to the Poor Clare Monastery, 8650 Russell Ave S., Bloomington, MN 55431, or a charity of your choice.
Star Tribune, 11 November 2004

EMMETT WLLMARTH CIVIL WAR VETERAN
Good Citizen of This County For Many Years Passed To His Final Reward At His Home In Sycamore, Saturday Night, Sept. 9.
Emmett Willmarth, veteran of the Civil war and a resident of DeKalb county for over half a century, passed from this life at his home, 203 East Elm street, Sycamore, at about 11 o clock on Saturday night, Sept. 9, after a period of failing health extending over four years, and after being confined to the house for two months.
Emmett Willmarth was born in Willett, Courtland county, N.Y. on August 1, 1847, a son of Galon and Charlotte (Osgood) Willmarth. The family resided in Chenango county, that state, for about six years, and when the son was ten years of age, they came to Illinois, and resided in Kendall and Will counties, this state. The son Emmett enlisted on his country's call and became corporal in the 147th Illinois Volunteer Infantry, and served in the Civil war with credit during that great conflict. After his discharge he resided for a year in LaSalle county and then came to DeKalb county and had always since made his home in Cortland township and Sycamore. For many years he was engaged in farming. He was united in marriage on May 28, 1873, to Miss Mary L. Scott of Cortland. She passed from this life October 21, 1905. To them were born four children of whom there are surviving Mesdames Willard Elliott of Elburn, Calvin Royce of Joliet and Arthur Kenyon of Elgin.
In 1910 Mr. Willmarth was married to Mrs. Lottie Garbutt, and after a few years they moved from the farm east of this city to Sycamore, where they had since resided.
He was a member of the Baptist church, Woodmen and Royal Neighbors.
Besides the wife and daughters he leaves surviving seven grandchildren.
Mr. Willmarth was a kindly agreeable citizen, the soul of honor, and performed conscientiously and to the best of his ability every duty to himself and his fellow men. He had a wide circle of friends. During his last illness, while confined to his home, he was an example of cheerfulness and patience and never complained. His death was sudden; he had been up and around his rooms only a short time before he sank back on his pillows in his last sleep; just as he had often wished he might go.
There was a large attendance of friends and relatives at the funeral services which were held at the home this Tuesday afternoon, Rev. James O'May, pastor of the Methodist church, and Rev. J. W. Patterson, pastor of the Baptist church officiating. The interment was in the family lot in Ohio Grove cemetery.
The True Republican, 13 September 1922

Alex Wilson (Aleksander Wyszotrawka), age 94, longtime resident of Dumont, MN, passed away Monday, March 17, 2014 at the Traverse Care Center in Wheaton.
A Funeral Mass will be held Saturday, March 22, 2014 at 11:00 a.m. at Ave Maria Catholic Church in Wheaton, with Fr. Stanley Wieser, Celebrant. Organist will be Cindy Sanasack with special music provided by Tressa Lanter. Pallbearers: Billy Grell, Shamus Wilson, Aaron Wilson, Joe Wilson, Riley Wilson, Isaac Wilson. Honorary Pallbearers: Willie Rinke, Garner Schmidt, Donald Conroy, Ronald Peickert, the late Gene Fridgen, and the entire Dumont/Wheaton Communities. Visitation will be held Friday evening, March 21st, from 5:00 p.m. until 7:00 p.m. at Ave Maria Catholic Church. Visitation will continue Saturday morning from 10:00 a.m. until service time. Interment will be in St. Peter's Catholic Cemetery, Dumont, MN.
Alex was born August 26, 1919 in Il'nyk, Poland, to Adam and Maria Wyszotrawka. He attended school off and on through grade four and worked on his family's small farm. In 1939 he joined the Polish Military Cadets. His military time ultimately took him to Germany where he met and married Marion Schmid and daughter Wanda was born.
Alex and Marion were sponsored by Leo and Katherine Yaggie of Breckenridge, MN, through Catholic Charities in 1949 and immigrated to the United States. After working for the Yaggie family for a short period, Alex went to work for Schuler Grain of Breckenridge for approximately four and a half years. Mike and Tony were born while the family was in Breckenridge. He then purchased a grain elevator in Dumont, MN, and the family moved. Tom, Bruce and Doug were born while in Dumont. Alex owned and operated Dumont Grain Company for twenty-seven years and retired in 1982.
Alex and Marion spent their retirement years between Dumont and Tucson, AZ, thoroughly enjoying their winter home.
Alex enjoyed dancing, golfing, fishing and hunting. He especially loved playing cards and watching his sons and grandchildren play sports. Alex was a die-hard Twins fan. He was always very engaging with people and in good spirits! He was very proud to be an American!
He will be dearly missed by his family and all who knew him.
Alex is survived by his wife Marion of Wheaton; daughter Wanda (Art) Grell of Parker, CO; Sons: Michael (Vicki) Wilson of Prior Lake, MN; Anthony Wilson of Dumont, MN; Thomas (Robin) Wilson of Detroit Lakes, MN; Bruce Wilson of Burnsville, MN and Douglas (Monica) Wilson of Wheaton, MN; grandchildren: Andrea (Josue') Sabillon, Billy (Erin) Grell, Leah (Jeremy) Dahlback, Joe Wilson, Suzanne (Rob) Hansen, Heather Wilson, Kara Wilson, Shamus Wilson, Christin Wilson, Haley (John) Benjamin, Jordan Wilson, Aaron (Tiffany) Wilson, Riley Wilson and Isaac Wilson; great-grandchildren: Isabela Sabillon, Cecilia Sabillon, Lola Dahlback, Max Dahlback, Cecelia Wilson and Brenna Wilson.
Alex is preceded in death by his parents Adam and Maria Wyszotrawka; brothers Nikolai, Jakob and Ivan; sister Ludvinia; and daughter-in-law Jennifer (Bruce) Wilson.
