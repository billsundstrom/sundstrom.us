Ranulf the MONEYER

[I016276]
Ranulf the Moneyer, whose antecedents are unknown, frist appears in 1035 when, Robert I of Normandy having died on his way home from Jerusalem, the Abbot of Le Mont St Michel sold to Ranulf the mill of Vains which the Duke had given to the Abbey. At a later but uncertain date he witnessed, as Ranulf the Moneyer, with his son Osbern, a charter of Roger, son of Hugh, bishop of Coutances, for the Abbey of St. Amand, Rouen. He was dead in 1061. He had 4 sons, whose order of birth is doubtful. [Complete Peerage XII/2:268-9. (transcribed by Dave Utzinger)]

Alan Dale MONNIER

[I023993]
A. Dale Monnier, age 77, of Toronto, SD passed away Thursday, August 16, 2012 at his home in Toronto. Funeral services will be 10:30 a.m. on Tuesday, August 21, 2012 at the Leganger Lutheran Church in Toronto.
Burial will be in the Toronto Cemetery, Toronto. Visitation will be from 6 - 8 p.m. on Monday, August 20th at the Houseman Funeral Home in Toronto and will resume Tuesday one hour prior to the service at the church.
Allan Dale Monnier was born on December 12, 1934 to Allen and Mildred "Brown" Monnier on the family farm in Blom Township near Toronto, SD. He was baptized and confirmed in the Lutheran Faith and attended and graduated from Clear Lake High School. Following his education he worked on the farm.
He was united in marriage to Janis Bendt on June 6, 1954. Following their marriage they lived and farmed near Brandt, SD for 26 years. He then began trucking where worked for Jack B. Kelley, INC in Amarillo, TX. Dale and Janis enjoyed over the road trucking for 13 years together. In 2002 they moved back to Toronto where they enjoyed their retirement. On August 29, 2011, Janis passed away. Dale remained in Toronto until he passed away on Thursday, August 16, 2012 at his home in Toronto at the age of 77 years, 8 months and 4 days.
Dale was a member of Leganger Lutheran Church in Toronto where he served on the church council and also as a Sunday school teacher. He loved to watch Westerns, read, travel, play cards and take Sunday drives.
Dale is lovingly remembered by his six children: Debbie Stevens, Oskaloosa, IA, Kathy Monnier (Doug Torgerson), Brookings, SD, Patricia (Tom) Gilman, Lakeville, MN, Kevin (Linda) Monnier, Ionia, IA, Alan (Thelma) Monnier, Toronto, SD and Bryon (Sheila) Monnier, Brandt, SD; 21 grandchildren; 10 great grandchildren; one brother Richard (Bonnie) Monnier, Council Bluffs, IA; two brothers-in-law: Lloyd Bendt, Sioux Falls, SD and Larry (Joyce) Bendt, Sioux Falls, SD; four sisters-in-law: Betty Hawley, Sioux Falls, SD, Shirley Kjenstad, Brandt, SD; Sandra Bendt, Clear Lake, SD and Verna Bendt, Brandon, SD; and many nieces and nephews.
Dale was preceded in death by his wife Janis and parents. Blessed be his memory.

Frank Bridgman MONTAGUE

[I020447]
WESTHAMPTON Frank B. Montague, 73, of 49 Montague Road, a longtime town official, died Tuesday at home.
He was a dairy farmer until 1956, when he sold his herd of Guernsey cows and went into the trucking business. He also worked for Omasta Brothers Construction Co. and was a custodian for the Hampshire Regional School District from 1971 until his retirement in 1988.
Born and schooled here, he was a 1943 graduate of Smith Vocational High School in Northampton.
He was a selectman and served on the Board of Health, the Board of Public Welfare, the Finance Committee and the Board of Assessors. He was a member of the Congregational Church for more than 50 years, a town constable for more than 40 years and a member of the Volunteer Fire Department for 42 years.
He leaves his wife of 53 years, the former Marion Warner; a son, Peter E. of Westhampton; three daughters, Sandra M. Sluman of Lake Worth, Fla., Deborah E. Montague of Guilford, N.H., and Sylvia A. Montague of Olympia, Wash.; two brothers, Edward A. Jr. and Sidney A., both of Westhampton; a sister, Ruth E. Wentworth of South Amherst; five grandchildren; and a great-granddaughter.
The memorial service will be Saturday afternoon at the Congregational Church, with burial at the convenience of the family in Center Cemetery. There are no calling hours.
Pease Funeral Home is in charge. Memorial contributions may be made to the Hospice of Hampshire County, P.O. Box 1087, Northampton, MA 01061, or the Congregational Church Organ Fund, Westhampton, MA 01027.
Union-News (Springfield, MA)
Date: March 11, 1999

Roger Watson MONTANYE

[I103903]
DEFOREST - Roger W. Montanye, age 71, of Windsor Township, died unexpectedly on Wednesday, April 14, 1993, at St. Mary's Hospital in Madison following a heart attack. Roger Watson Montanye was born on December 13, 1921 at Reedsburg to Royce and Sarah (Watson) Montayne. He attended Butterfield Grade School and was a graduate of Reedsburg High School. He served his country with the Army during World War II where he served in the European Theatre as a paratrooper. On October 18, 1947 he was united in marriage with Norma Dummer in a double ring ceremony with their close friends Wally 'Bill' and Norma 'Pete' Clavadatcher. In 1970, he and his wife built their home near DeForest where he resided until his death. Mr. Montanye was employed by Ohio Chemical and Oscar Meyer and in 1980 retired after working at the Madison Post Office for many years. They spent summers in their trailer home at Woodruff. Roger enjoyed talking with friends and relatives on his HAM radio. Mr. Montanye was preceded in death by his parents and his wife, Norma Montanye, who died on September 5, 1992. He is survived by a brother-in-law, Donald (Joyce) Dummer of Baraboo; a sister-in-law. Tootie Ender of Baraboo; close friends, Wally and Norma Clavadatcher of Sauk City and their daughters and grandchildren and Ed and Jean McFarlane of Reedsburg; and many cousins, nieces, nephews and other relatives and friends. Funeral services will be held on Friday, April 16, 1993, at 7:00 p.m. at the EDWARDSON-AXTELL FUNERAL HOME in DeForest with the Reverend Dennis Wels officiating. Entombment will be in the Roselawn Memorial Park in Monona.
The Capital Times, 16 April 1993

Robert William MONTGOMERY

[I093174]
Robert Wm. "Bob" Montgomery, 82, of Terre Haute, passed away in Union Hospital at 3:16 p.m. on Monday, Sept. 13, 2010. He was born in Detroit, Mich., on July 11, 1928, to Dr. James and Charlotte Montgomery. Soon after graduating from Casstech High School, Bob reported for duty for the United States Army. After serving in World War II, he then attended Eastmond School of Music in New York. Bob taught in Birmingham, Ala., and was principal cellist in the orchestra. He later taught at Lamar Technical College Beaumont, Texas, and where he also sat first chair in the symphony. Then in 1964, he came to Indiana State University as associate professor and played in several quartets and was first chair cellist for the Terre Haute Symphony many years. He retired from ISU. in 1998 after 34 years. He was a loving husband, father and grandfather.
He is survived by his wife, Mary (Switzer) Montgomery; children, Katherine Meissner and husband Volker of Germany, Susan Keser and husband Huseyin of New York, Belinda Danner and husband Eddy of Terre Haute, Ricky Sebastian and wife Annette of Trafalgar, Ind., Robert Gess of Beanblossom, Ind., Danny Gess and wife Debby of Columbus, Ind. Also surviving are 10 grandchildren; two great-grandchildren; and many nieces and nephews. He was preceded in death by his parents; his first wife, Shirley; and one son, Robert David.
Funeral services will be in DeBaun Springhill Chapel, 85 E. Springhill Drive, Saturday, Sept. 18, 2010, at 1 p.m. Burial will be in Grandview Cemetery. Visitation is today from 4 until 8 p.m. and Saturday from 11 a.m. until 1 p.m., in the funeral home. The family requests memorial contributions be made to either the Alzheimer ºs Association, the American Cancer Society or American Heart Association, in his memory.

Ronald William MOOBERRY

[I002052]
DAVENPORT - Ron Mooberry, 90, of Davenport, passed away Saturday, Nov. 7, 2015, in Bettendorf. Funeral services celebrating his life will be 10 a.m. Thursday, Nov. 12, at Weerts Funeral Home, Kimberly and Jersey Ridge Road, Davenport. Burial will follow in the Rock Island National Cemetery, Arsenal Island. Visitation is 4-7 p.m. Wednesday, Nov. 11, at the funeral home. In lieu of other expressions of sympathy, memorials may be made to Bethel Assembly of God Missions in Rock Island.
Ronald William was born in 1925 to Grover and Elizabeth Mooberry in Osceola, Iowa. He was united in marriage to Erma Scarr on April 18, 1953, at the Little Brown Church in the Vale in Nashua, Iowa. In his earlier years, he was a Seaman First Class in the U.S. Navy and then later worked for the Jasper County Soil Conservation Service in Newton, Iowa. In May of 1963, he accepted Jesus Christ as his Savior and soon thereafter felt God's call to pastoral ministry. He then moved his family to Minneapolis so he could attend North Central Bible College. Upon his graduation from NCBC and ordination as an Assemblies of God Minister, he began what would become a lifetime of ministry - sharing God's love and forgiveness with all he met. Known as "Pastor Moo," he pastored at the Conrad Assembly of God Church in Conrad, Iowa, and later served on the pastoral staff at Westside Assembly of God Church in Davenport. He loved to laugh and loved to bring joy to others. He loved to travel, enjoyed collecting rocks and was a huge fan of his beloved Hawkeyes. Ron loved the Lord and was firm in his commitment to serve Him. He will be deeply missed by all those who knew and loved him.
Survivors include daughters, Ronda (David) McConnell and Jody Smith, both of Davenport; son, Mark (Jill) Mooberry, Springdale, Ark.; grandchildren, Kristin (Ben) Froeschle and Luke, Logan and Colton Mooberry; great-grandsons, Brody Smith and another baby boy on the way; brother, James Mooberry; and numerous nieces and nephews and foreign exchange daughter, Elinor Levinsen (Kaare) Lovdal, Tonsberg, Norway. Ronald was preceded in death by his wife of 62 years, Erma Scarr Mooberry; his parents; sister, Dorothy Kendall; and brothers, Darwyn Mooberry and Merryll Mooberry.
Quad City Times, 10 November 2015)
