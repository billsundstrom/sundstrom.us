John HOWARD

[I095504]
John was the son of John Howard and Joan Cornwall, the daughter of Richard de Cornwall, (a son of King of the Romans Robert Plantagenet (Cornwall) and his Queen Sanchia of Provence), and Joan Saint Owen.
Sir John Howard knight was constituted in the ninth year of King Edward III admiral and captain of the king's navy in the North and in the following year had an assignation of 1537 for the wages of himself with his men at arms in that service. And that same year having affairs to transact beyond the seas the king granted him his protection dated April 24, 1337 to be in force till the August 1, 1337.
Sir John was sheriff of Norfolk in 1345.
He married before October 11, 1338 Alice de Bois, daughter of Robert de Bois, Knt., of Fersfield and Garboldisham, Norfolk, and Brokes (in Ipswich), Suffolk, by Christian, daughter of William le Latimer, Knt. They had two sons, Robert, Knight., and John, and one daughter, Anne (wife of Robert Pashley, Knight.). Alice was heiress in 1333 to her brother, Robert de Bois, Knt. Sir John Howard was living November 1347, when he was granted license for a weekly market and yearly fair at Wiggenhall, Norfolk. His widow, Alice, died September 6, 1372.

Charles Stanley HOWE

[I098493]
Charles Stanley Howe, 92, of Horse Shoe passed away Friday evening, June 13, 2008.
He was born in Marblehead, Mass., a son of the late D.P. Howe and Stella Tupper Howe and had resided in Gamboa in the Panama Canal Zone prior to moving to Henderson County in 1968. He earned his B.S. in forestry from Purdue University and was an instructor at Fort Sill and West Point Military Academies during World War II. He was employed by the Panama Canal Company and retired in 1970 from his civil service as assistant chief of dredging after 32 years.
He proudly served his country during World War II and retired from the Army as a lieutenant colonel. He was a member of the American Military Society, a charter member of the U.S. A.A. Insurance Company and a member of the Panama Canal Society of Florida, Inc.
He was preceded in death by a daughter, Stella Marina Howe, in 1950.
He is survived by his loving wife, Carmen Joudrey Howe; five children, Marion Elaine Howe of Columbia, S.C., Shirley Howe McHenry of Hendersonville, Charles William Howe and wife, Ruth, of Mills River, Alice Howe-Bonne of Port Orange, Fla., and Dan Paul Howe and wife Betty of Franklin, Va.; 10 grandchildren; 14 great-grandchildren; and one brother, David Grant Howe and wife, Linda, of Plant City, Fla.
A service will be conducted out of town at a later date.
Memorial contributions can be made to the Panama Canal Museum, in care of Panama Canal Society, Inc., 7985 113th St., Suite 100, Seminole, Fla. 33772.

David Grant HOWE

[I098492]
David G. Howe, 89, of Plant City, Florida, born in Wilmington, Massachusetts on August 11, 1928, went to be with the Lord on March 14, 2018. He grew up during the Great Depression in the Panama Canal Zone. He graduated Castle Heights Military Academy in Lebanon, Tennessee in 1946, and attended Caltech and Emory Universities. He was a veteran of the United States Air Force, having served during the Korean War. He was a flight engineer for Eastern Airlines; in 1960 he joined Boeing as a telemetry engineer, and in 1963 joined Wichita as an engineer for Beechcraft, working on the first turbo prop aircraft. He came back home to Florida in 1969, and moved to Plant City in 1974. As an inventor, pilot, and engineer, he earned many accolades, including the prestigious Charles Taylor award presented by the FAA. He retired from Cypress Aviation in Lakeland where he designed repairs and modifications for aircraft. His passions included woodworking, like designing cabinets, tinkering in the barn, helping friends and neighbors fix things, watching western movies. He is survived by his wife of 54 years, Linda R. Howe; son, Matthew Howe of Plant City; and many nieces, nephews, and cousins. He was preceded in death by his daughter, Terri Howe; and siblings, Charles, Roger, Blanch, Muriel, and Dolly. A Celebration of Life will be held Saturday, March 17 at 11:00 A.M. at Hopewell Funeral Home, 6005 S County Road 39, Plant City, where the family will receive friends beginning at 10:00 A.M. Committal to follow at Hopewell Memorial Gardens, Plant City. Reception to follow at Hopewell Funeral Home. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the American Heart Association, heart.org, and/or the American Lung Association.

Kathrina HOWZE

[I072358]
Kathrina Howze (Mrs. Robert L.) Maclellan , a member of a prominent Chattanooga family, went home to be with the Lord at her home on Lookout Mountain on Monday Sept. 26, 2011. She was 99.
She was a devoted and loving wife, mother, grandmother, great-grandmother and faithful community leader. She was a woman of devout Christian faith, who served humbly and gave thanks to Jesus Christ.
Mrs. Maclellan was preceded in death in 1971 by her husband, Robert Llewellyn Maclellan , chairman of the board and chief executive officer of Provident Life and Accident Insurance Co.; and in 1978 by her daughter, Anne Maclellan Munford. She was the daughter of the late Kathrina Richardson Howze and Gerald Howze, of Duluth, Minn.
She was a graduate of Sweet Briar College and a resident of Lookout Mountain for over 70 years. She was an active and devoted member of the Lookout Mountain Presbyterian Church and served in several capacities on the Board of Women in Church since 1947.
Mrs. Maclellan was an active contributor to her community and served on several boards as well as held membership in many different organizations. Several include: Daughters of the American Revolution, Junior League of Chattanooga, the National Society of Colonial Dames of America, Children's Home, YWCA, Team Evaluation Center and Review Club. She served on the boards of trustees of Senior Neighbors, Garden Club of Lookout Mountain, King College, Helen M. Tipton Charitable Trust, Christian Education Charitable Trust and the Maclellan Foundation Inc.
She also served on the board of associates of Covenant College.
After her husband's death, Mrs. Maclellan founded the Robert L. and Kathrina H. Maclellan Foundation. Many Chattanooga schools and charities are benefactors of this generous work. The foundation seeks to fulfill Bob's and her wish that their estates be used "for the benefit of our community and the needs of its people." Mrs. Maclellan sought to continue in the traditions of her husband and her own heart for building the Kingdom of Christ here in the greater Chattanooga area.
In a Chattanooga Times article dated Jan. 13, 1971, Bill Casteel quoted Bob Maclellan as he spoke of his wife by saying, "She was always at my side, in good and bad times." She supported the work of her husband in attending Provident meetings and insurance association meetings. During World War II, she moved with her husband to Washington, D.C., where he was instrumental in establishing the National Service Insurance Co. that insured the lives then and now of our military. The work of Mrs. Maclellan and her legacy will continue on through the Robert L. and Kathrina H. Maclellan Foundation, but her presence and true Christian spirit will be greatly missed.
She is survived by one son, Robert Howze "Scott" Maclellan and wife, Lisa; two granddaughters, Lara M. Munford and Heather H. Maclellan ; three grandsons Ian, Reid, and Lee Maclellan ; and five greatgrandchildren, Tyler, Haydon, Kyle, Landon and Lundon.
Visitation will be at Wann Funeral Home from 1 until 2 p.m. Thursday, Sept. 29, followed by a graveside service at Forest Hills Cemetery with Ben Haden presiding.
In lieu of flowers, the family request that donations be made to the Children's Home, Hospice of Chattanooga, Lookout Mountain Presbyterian Church or a charity of your choice.
Arrangements by Wann Funeral Home.
Chattanooga Times Free Press, 28 September 2011
