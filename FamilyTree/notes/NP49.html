John K. BOYLE

[I074581]
John K. Boyle , a Triton College faculty member and Mayfair area community leader, died Tuesday. He was 59.
Mr. Boyle died of cancer in Rush Presbyterian-St. Luke's Medical Center.
The professor taught English and developmental reading at the River Grove college for the last 24 years.
During his tenure, he served as president of the Triton College Faculty Association. He was instrumental in affiliating the organization with the Cook County Teachers Union while in that post. In 1983, he was at the center of a well-publicized fight to prevent the re-election of a board chairman.
Mr. Boyle was a community activist in his Northwest Side neighborhood. He founded the Mayfair Civic Association, one of many small neighborhood groups that sprung up in the early 1970s to oppose the construction of the Crosstown Expy. As the association's first president, he later fought for various community improvements, including the creation of a number of small area parks. He also was vice president of the North River Commission, an umbrella group that provides support to small community organizations.
Mr. Boyle, a graduate of St. Gregory High School, served in the Army before attending college. He earned his bachelor's and master's degrees from Loyola University.
His spare time was devoted to his grandchildren and the Chicago White Sox.
"He often called his grandchildren the 'stars in grandpa's heaven,' " said his son, John Jr.
Besides his son, Mr. Boyle leaves his wife, Margaret "Peggy"; a daughter, Margaret "Peggy" Marren; two more sons, James and Jeremiah; six brothers, J. Neil, James, Dennis, Richard, Terrence and Patrick; five sisters, Veronica Zogman, Patricia Flynn, Paula Cody, Jane and Miriam, and three grandchildren.
Visitation will be from 3 to 9 p.m. today and Friday at Ryan-Parke Funeral Home, 120 S. Northwest Hwy., Park Ridge. Services will be at 9 a.m. Saturday at the funeral home chapel. Mass will follow at 10 a.m. in St. Edward Catholic Church, 4350 W. Sunnyside. Burial will be in All Saints Cemetery, 700 N. River Rd., Des Plaines.
Chicago Sun-Times, 11 November 1993

Maynard BOYNTON

[I044108]
BOYNTON, MAYNARD ALTON "PAPPY", Died Monday, September 15, 2008, in Chandler, AZ. He was 87 years of age. Pappy was born in Millinocket, ME on November 11, 1920, one of two sons born to Lottie and Wyman Boynton. He grew up in South China, ME and was graduated from Erskine Academy in 1938. Shortly after Pearl Harbor, Pappy joined the Army Air Corps and later continued to serve in the newly formed Air Force. He served during WWII and in Korea and was awarded the Bronze Star. He retired in 1965 as a Master Sergeant with 22 years of service. After retiring from the Air Force, he worked for more than twenty-one years at the Kennedy Space Center for NASA Secur-ity. Pappy met Alice Jacqueline Gamble at a USO dance during WWII. They married on October 2, 1942 and remained married until her death in June 1, 2007. Pappy is survived by their children: Neil Boynton (and Laura) of Rockledge, FL; Kathleen Emeott of Mesa, AZ; Kevin Boynton (and Kitty) of Casselberry, FL, and Elizabeth Graham (and Mark) of Chandler, AZ. They have six grandchildren, David Emeott, Richard Boynton, Matthew Emeott, Philip Emeott, Duncan Graham, and Sean Graham, and one great-granddaughter, Allison Emeott. Pappy and his family resided in Titusville, Florida from 1965 until he and Alice moved to Austin, TX in 2001 and later to Chandler, AZ. While in Titusville, Pappy was a very active member of St. Luke's Presbyterian Church and cooked a pancake breakfast every Thanksgiving morning for the congregation. He was known and loved by everyone as a patriotic, generous, humorous, kind, and loving man whose devotion to his wife and family was unmatched. No matter what the circumstances, everyone who came near him was met by his warm and open welcome. Our world has lost a wonderful father, grandfather, and friend. There will be a gathering of friends on Saturday, October 4, 2008, from 4-6PM at North Brevard Funeral Home Funeral Home. A memorial service will be held at St. Luke's Presbyterian Church, 1255 Knox McRae Dr., Titusville, FL on Sunday, October 5, 2008 at 2PM. In lieu of flowers, the family suggests donations to the Alzheimer's Association or the Hospice Organization of your choice. Please sign Maynard's guest book at: northbrevardfuneralhome.com
