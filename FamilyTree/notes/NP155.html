Bernice Alma DURKEE

[I037116]
Bernice "Bea" Scott, age 88, of Rhinelander died Sunday, April 22, 2007, at Taylor Park Nursing Home. She was born on October 27, 1918, in Hillsboro, WI to Edward and Emma (Winchell) Durkee. Bea graduated from high school as her class valedictorian. She married Raymond Scott on November 24, 1937, in Rhinelander and together they raised their family of four children and three foster children. Bea loved antiques and operated her own antique shop in the 50's and 60's. She and Ray also lived on a hobby farm where they raised various animals. Music played a major role in Bea's life and she was a member of several bands over the years including "Bea and The Boys" and the "Durkee Generation" and in later years, she led the "Racketeers", a group that played at functions and events throughout the area. She worked as a musical therapist in several nursing homes and aided Dr. Cline in establishing several self help groups that are still active today. Bea was a faithful member of Zion Ev. Lutheran Church. Bea is survived by her daughter, Emmaline (Wesley) Wolfgram of Niagara, North Dakota; two sons, Randy (Marge) Scott of Ladysmith and Dennis (Barb) Scott of Richfield, Minnesota; a daughter-in-law, Jacqueline Scott of Rhinelander; three foster daughters, Rose, Marty, and Lydia; ten grandchildren; and fourteen great grandchildren. She was preceded in death by her parents; her husband, Ray in 1981; a son, Dewaine Scott in 2004; a sister, Violet; and two brothers, Oscar and Almon. Funeral services for Bea will be at 11 a.m. on Friday, April 27, 2007, at Zion Ev. Lutheran Churh with Rev. Peter Korthals officiating. Visitation will be from 4 to 7 p.m. on Thursday at the Carlson Funeral Home and from 10 a.m. until the time of services on Friday at the church. Interment will be in Northland Memorial Park.

Edwin George DURKEE

[I059621]
Edwin G. Durkee, age 76, of Rhinelander, died on Sunday, September 21, 2008. He was born in the town of Cassian on September 29, 1931 to Almon and Bertha (Gerbig) Durkee.
He was baptized on October 22, 1931 and confirmed at Faith Lutheran Church on June 26, 1949. He was a life-long member of Trinity Lutheran Church in Rhinelander.
Ed was employed at Rhinelander Paper Company. He spent his last day of work at the mill in style, dressed in formal attire. Ed, who was a towmotor driver, arrived in full regalia, causing quite a stir around the mill, to say nothing of attracting a lot of attention. At the end of the day, he was picked up by a chauffeured limousine and the local media were on hand as he symbolically smashed his alarm clock. Ed spent 40 years with the Rhinelander Paper Company. He built many homes in the Rhinelander area. Ed was also a great sportsman; hunting and fishing were his passion.
Ed is survived by his wife, Judith; his sons, Edwin (Diane) of Shawano, Gary of Harshaw, and Mark (Laura) of Rhinelander; his daughter, Julie (Tom) Shelton of Rhinelander; his sisters, Beulah Lang, Betty Mayer, and Emily (Dave) Garvey, all of Rhinelander; his step-children, Glenn (Petra) Durkee of Eagle River, Alaska, Holly (Bob) Nedden of Ringle, Wisconsin, Jennifer (Dan) Dart of Blaine, Minnesota, and Dr. Russell (Binu) Durkee of Eau Claire, Wisconsin; his grandchildren and step-grandchildren, Elizabeth Durkee, Lilian Durkee, Mikayla Durkee, Luke Durkee, Kendra Durkee, Caleb Nedden, Eli Nedden, Lily Durkee, Gabriel Durkee, Evelyn Durkee, Jayden Dart, and Isaac Durkee; four great-grandchildren; and fifteen nieces and nephews.
He was preceded in death by his parents, his wife, Lois, and his step-father, Ted Peschek.
Funeral services for Ed will be held on Wednesday, September 24, 2008 at 3:00 P.M. at Trinity Lutheran Church with Pastors Timothy and Kari Vadis officiating. Interment will follow at Northland Memorial Park Cemetery.

Harvey DURKEE

[I037230]
Harvey was the son of Benjamin Durkee and Susanna Clark.
Harvey married Sarah Hurd, 28 Apr 1794. In 1797, they lived in New Petersburg, Herkimer Co., NY.
Baptismal records at the Augusta Congregational Church in Oneida Co., NY show that their children included: Justus, Abraham, Clorenda, Samuel, Eunice, James Harvey, Asahel, Sarah Florilla & (George) Washington. Sarah Hurd Durkee died in 1819 in Augusta.
When the 1830 census was taken, Harvey lived in Pomfret, Chautauqua Co., NY. He was also enumerated there in 1840.
At the time of the 1850 and 1860 censuses, Harvey lived with son James Harvey and his family, in Conneaut, Ashtabula Co., OH.
A fallen tree nearly hit Harvey's tombstone. The tombstone to Harvey's right is that of his son, James Harvey Durkee, and to James' right is James' wife, Huldah. There is space for several (now unmarked) graves to the right of Huldah. The next tombstone is that of James & Huldah's daughter, Juliett.
