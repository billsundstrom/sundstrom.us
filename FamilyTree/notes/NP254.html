Norval Leon HOLT

[I014506]
Born April 7, 1927 and died January 26, 2011 Norval was born the sixth and youngest child to Hylas and Lorena Holt. He grew up in Selma California, but spent many wonderful days with his maternal family the McNarys in Kingsburg. In 1948 after a tour of duty in the US Navy he married E. Jeannette Brown of Fowler, who had only been a friend, until they were both attendants in the wedding of his brother and sister in law, Charles and Faye Holt of Selma. He was a dedicated and loving husband for 62 years. They had four children: Rodney Holt, who is married to Kristi; Randall Holt; Jerry Holt who is married to Sherian; and Carol Holt. In 1955 they built a home and moved to Kingsburg.
Norval was a meat cutter working as the meat manager for the grocery store chain known as Food Banks, located on the corner of Draper and California. In 1963, he realized a life long dream of owning his own business when he bought an interest in Park & Save Market. In 1974, he and mom bought the Olson Market on Draper Street and later remodeled it into The Butcher and Baker Market.
Norval lived for his wife, his family and his relationship with Jesus Christ. Nothing brought greater joy to him than spending time with his 8 grandchildren: Loreen Holt, Erin Lunde and Carrie Yokota of Kingsburg; Jonathon Holt, Joel Holt and Jeannette Holt of Twin Falls, Idaho; Joshua Holt of Edmonton Canada, and Jessie Holt of Richmond, Washington; and 17 great grandchildren.
He struggled with his health for the last three years of his life and his family takes great joy and comfort in knowing he is now in the presence of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. He requested that any memorials be given to the First Baptist Church of Dinuba.
A graveside service was held at Fowler Cemetery on Monday, January 31st.
Kingsburg Recorder, 2 February 2011

He served his country in the United States Army during World War II in Germany and France.
After the war, he came home and farmed.
In 1953, he worked with the Wayne County Sheriff's Department.
In 1955, he became Wayne County's first animal control officer, and retired in 1983 after 30 years of service to Wayne County.
He was an avid outdoorsman, and enjoyed fishing and hunting. He was known throughout the United States and Canada for his quality black and tan coon hounds and Warfield red beagles.
He was a member of Selah Christian Church, and funeral services will be held there Sunday, Jan. 11, 2015, at 3 p.m., with visitation beginning at 2 p.m. in the church fellowship hall. Burial will follow at Falling Creek Baptist Church Cemetery with military honors.
At other times, the family will receive friends at the home.

Paul H. HOOVER

[I038801]
Longtime educator, Monticello Empire League commissioner and Napa resident Paul H. Hoover died Feb. 11, 2003. He was 75. Paul was born Feb. 1, 1928, in Pike County, Ill.
Paul spent five years as a hospital corpsman in the U.S. Navy attached to the U.S. Marine Corps 1st Division during World War II and the Korean War. He and his wife, Juanita, arrived in San Francisco via Alaska in the late 1950s. They relocated to Napa when Paul accepted his first teaching position. He received his bachelor's degree and credential, California general secondary, and his master's degree in education from University of San Francisco.
Paul's education career with the Napa Valley Unified School District began in 1963 as a teacher at Silverado Junior High. In 1971, he became a counselor at Silverado, and in 1982, he returned to the classroom as a teacher at Napa High School, retiring in 1991.
He was the founding commissioner of the Monticello Empire League at its creation in 1976 and continued as commissioner until the time of his death. Paul shaped MEL into one of Northern California's premiere high school sports programs. His contribution to education and high school sports also included positions as a coach, official and administrator.
Paul enjoyed his "retirement job" at Napa Wooden Box Company, where he worked as a member of the sales team focusing on the wine industry.
He was a 35-year member, and past President and Parish Council member of St. Apollinaris Catholic Church. An avid golfer, he was a member of the Napa Valley Country Club. He was also a member of numerous local, state and national educators association.
Paul is survived by his wife, Juanita E. Hoover of Napa; his daughter, Kathee Hoover, and son-in law, Eric LaBuse of Honolulu, Hawaii; daughter Nancy Kaiser, and son-in-law Malcolm Kaiser of Brentwood; daughter Vickie Metcalfe, and son-in-law Robert Metcalfe of Everett, Wash.; daughter Jennifer Hoover, and son-in-law Ed Flaherty of Santiago, Chile; and foster son, Lester Clayton of Alaska. He is also survived by his grandchildren: Scott Hoover Kaiser, Stacey Kaiser, Cameron Metcalfe, Carson Metcalfe, Benjamin Flaherty and Sean Flaherty; and his sister, Frances Snider, and brother-in-law James Snider of St. Louis, Mo. He was preceded in death by his brothers, Rex, Gail, Hal and Don Hoover; and his sister, Mary Jo McGann.
A memorial service will be held Monday, Feb. 17, at 4 p.m. at St. Apollinaris Catholic Church, 3700 Lassen St., Napa, with a reception immediately following in the Parish Hall.
In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Paul Hoover Scholarship, c/o Napa High School, 2475 Jefferson St., Napa, CA 94558, attention: Janette Mulgrew.
Napa Valley Register, 13 February 2003
