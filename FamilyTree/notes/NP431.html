Theodore POMEROY

[I015723]
Dr. Theodore Pomeroy, who came from Cooperstown, was born at Southampton, Mass., March 14, 1785, was graduated at Yale College in 1808, studied medicine with Dr. James, of Albany and afterward of Utica, and also with Dr. Chester, of Hudson, attended lectures at Pittsfield, and had made a successful stand in Cooperstown.
His new venture in Utica was a fortunate one, for he soon fell into a large and profitable business, and for ten or a dozen years at least was the medical adviser of some of the best families of the place. His pleasant manner and his engaging exterior attracted admirers who were changed to friends when they realized the purity and uprightness of his life. He loved his profession as an art more than as a science. He cared less for brilliant exploits in surgery than to relieve the common ailments of humanity, and win the rewards of his exertion as well as the gratitude of those on whom he waited.
Other pursuits akin to medicine into which he was drawn tended to lead him more and more from his chosen calling and eventually took him wholly out of it. With Thomas R. Walker he had become interested in an oilcloth factory from their having loaned money to J. D. Edwards, its founder, and which the latter was unable to reimburse them. They assumed the charge of the factory and for many years continued its management. After Mr. Walker withdrew, about the year 1854, Dr. Pomeroy kept on in the concern in company with his son Theodore. As a citizen, neighbor, and friend the rank of Dr. Pomeroy was among the liberal, the useful, and the trusted.
He died at St. Anthony, June 26, 1860. His first wife and the mother of three of his children was Mary, daughter of Dr. Thomas Fuller, of Cooperstown. His second was Miss Cornelia Voorhees, of New Brunswick, N. J.

Charles O. PONTIOUS

[I098907]
Charles O. Pontious, known as "Chuck" Pontious, age 84 of Grant, went to the Lord on Saturday, October 16, 2010 at his home in Grant. He was born August 2, 1926 in Alpine, Michigan the son of Mary Elizabeth (Showers) and Halleck Pontious who have preceded in death.
He graduated Shelby High School and enlisted in the US Navy serving in the Pacific Theater. He later attended Muskegon School of Business. He married Marjory Louise Roberts on June 12, 1953 in Grant. Charles spent many years in the insurance business, opening the Pontious Insurance Agency in 1969. Over the years he worked for the Grant School System as custodian and bus driver, served as Treasurer for the City of Grant and in 1995 retired from Clarke Memorial Home.
After his retirement Charles served as Newaygo County Chaplains. Charles was active in Grant United (Community) Church of Christ serving in may capacities over the years. He was ordained as a lay-minister and had an active jail ministry and ministered to others throughout the community. He was an active member and sponsor of Alcoholics Anonymous, live member of the VFW, Boy Scouts of America Commissioner, Jaycee, and City of Grant Council member.
He was survived by his wife Marjory Pontious, his children Halleck & Shirley Jankuski of Jackson, Hollie & William Ricards of Sanford, Todd & Carol Pontious of Maryville, TN, Kimberlee Harp of Coffeyville, KS, Tracey & Michael Krauss of Spring, TX, 13 grandchildren, 13 great-grandchildren, his brother Bill Pontious of Florida.
He was also preceded in death by brothers Halleck, Jerry (Doris), Steve (Margaret), sisters Pauline (Norman), Beatrice (Ben), and son-in-law Norman Harp (Kim).

MRS. WM. W. (MARGARET POORMAN) SEARS
Word was received hereon Monday announcing the death of Mrs. William W. Sears, which occurred at Webster, S. Dak. , on Sunday. While her health had been very poor for many years, it was not known to her friends here that her condition was critical. In fact, she had been in her usual health until two days previous to her death, having been about town on Friday. Margaret Poorman was born In New York, Dec. 8, 1832. She was united in marriage with William W. Sears on September 27,1855, and they came to Illinois in 1867 and located at El Paso, Woodford county. They resided in Woodford and McLean counties until 1865 when they moved to Chatsworth. They made this city their home from that time until last spring, when they moved to Webster, S. Dak., with the hope that the change or climate would be beneficial to Mrs. Sears' failing health, but her physical forces could not be rejuvenated, and her death occurred as above stated. Mr. And Mrs. Sears were the parents of ten children, three of whom survive, namely Judge Frank Sears, of Webster, S. Dak.; Grace, wife of Jay Baker, of Kansas City, Mo.; and Miss Frances who is unmarried and has made her home with her parents. The remains arrived her Wednesday afternoon and the funeral was held at the home of Mr. and Mrs. J.F. Sullivan at 2 o'clock, Pastor Rev. Eldridge of the Baptist church, officiating and the remains were laid to rest in the Chatsworth cemetery.
Chatsworth Plaindealer, 6 October 1905

Allan Bertrand POPP

[I090735]
Allan B. Popp of Sherwood died Aug. 25. He was 84 years old.
He was born in Portland on Sept. 30, 1926, to Alexander Popp and Pauline Schnell Popp and attended Jefferson High School.
At the age of 17 he joined the Navy and served in the Navy Armed Guard during World War II and in the Korean war.
After leaving the Navy, he attended Lewis and Clark College and worked at various sales jobs. He retired after working for more than 30 years at Doughty's Appliance in Tigard.
Mr. Popp 's many volunteer activities included being a Boy Scout Scoutmaster, president of the Tigard Kiwanis Club, board member of Tigard Community Youth Services and several years on the Tigard Planning Commission. In 1981 he was honored as Tigard's First Citizen.
Survivors include children Pamela Gunderson, Michael Popp , Sharon Pasero and John Popp ; grandchildren Renee Waller, Michelle Reeves and Nick Pasero, and great-grandchildren Allison, Evan and Levi Reeves.
Preceding him in death were his brother Wilbert (Bill) Popp and sisters Elizabeth (Betty) Widlits and Eleanor Pitzer.
A memorial service will be held at 10 a.m. on Saturday. Sept. 17, in Metzger Park Hall, 8400 S.W. Hemlock St., followed by a service with military honors on Sept. 30 at Willamette National Cemetery.
The family of Allan B. Popp suggests donations in his memory to Sherwood Loaves and Fishes.

Blanchard Leadlo PORTER

Blanchard L. Porter was born in Halls Harbor, Nova Scotia, on March 25th, 1860, and came with his parents to the vicinity of Peru, Ill., when about six years of age. The Porter family moved after three years to Minnesota, locating in Dundas and later on the farm now occupied by the Jon Van Orsow family. Mr. Porter received his early schooling in the Forest school district No. 29 and except for a time spent in Bird Island and the Dakotas has spent his entire life here.
Mr. Porter was married to Annie Canedy on February 4, 1886, the young couple locating on the same farm where they have lived for 48 years. Beside the widow, there are three children left to mourn Mr. Porter's death on Friday, September 21, following a short illness. There are two sons, Clarence Wilbur of Bismarck, ND, and Floyd of Little Prairie and Mrs. Irving N. Taylor of Union Lake. A daughter, Mrs. Cynthia Thielbar and infant son, Lloyd, preceded their father in death.
There are also 17 grandchildren, one great grandchild and three sisters, Mrs. Sarah Van Selus of Stockton, New York, Mrs. James Lucas of Bird Island and Mrs. Milton Van Selus of Shell Lake, Wis. Funeral services in charge of Rev. A. S. Earl were conducted on Monday afternoon from the Little Prairie church of which Mr. Porter was a member. He was also a member of the Yeomen and I.O.O.F. lodges, the latter of which took charge of the interment service ant the Dundas cemetery. Pallbearers were E. M. and R. R. Hoover, J. H. Finger, G. E.
