Shirley Ann LARSON

Shirley was born at 1:47am at Saint Barnahas Hospital, Minneapolis, Minnesota. She graduated from Faribault High School in 1948. Shirley and Dave were classmates in high school. They started dating in college and were married when they were juniors. She married just prior to her 21st birthday. When Shirley was a freshman in college she became ill and went to the Mayo Clinic in Rochester, Minnesota. She was operated on for a mass in her chest. The doctors believed that she was a twin at conception and the mass was from the other embryo. She returned to Faribault to recover and missed half of the school year. And yet, she graduated on time, in only 3 1/2 years. On 2 June 1952, she graduated from Hamline University in St. Paul, Minnesota with a Bachelor of Arts, major English, magna cum laude. She taught high school English at Dodge Center, Minnesota for 2 years after she graduated from college. Dave went to Air Force basic training and then on to administrative technical school, while Shirley was teaching. She enjoyed dancing and the card game "bridge". She enjoyed participating in the Officer's Wives Club and oversees life. During their assignments to Japan and Germany, she traveled extensively. Dave says she really enjoyed the military and was "the perfect officer's wife". She died just prior to Dave being promoted to full colonel. Unfortunately, she never knew it, because it would have met so much to her. She was diagnosed with breast cancer at Wright-Patterson AFB, Ohio in 1966. After a mastectomy, doctors gave her 5 years to live. She lived 7 years after the diagnosis. During their assignment in Germany, Air Force doctors asked her to go to Wilford Hall, Texas for experimental surgery to remove her pituitary gland. The surgery did not stop the cancer from spreading. On 28 Aug 1973 she went into the Kirtland Air Force Base, New Mexico hospital and started to fail very quickly.

After working on the farm, he went to Detroit, Ml for Auto Diesel and Aviation Mechanics School. He returned home and worked several mechanical jobs in Cambridge and the Twin Cities.
In 1941, he enlisted in the U.S. Navy going to North Africa, and the Pacific in Okinawa. He returned to the states in 1946 moving back to Ogilvie.
While working at Tinker and Johnson he met Phyllis Hansen at the Deluxe Cafe, and they were married June 21, 1947, at Siloa Lutheran Church. To this marriage 3 children were born.
Wally and Phyllis purchased the Montgomery Ward Catalog Agency in the mid 60ʼs and worked side by side for over 10 years, retiring in 1978 at the ages of 59 & 50. Later he returned to part-time work at the Coast-to-Coast Hardware as he loved working with the public, and staying busy.
They became winter Texans for over 25 years where they enjoyed entertaining many family and friends who came to visit. They also loved traveling the US visiting with family and friends.
He was very active in the First Presbyterian Church, the American Legion, 40/8 and was a WWII veteran. He was truly very loyal to God and his country.
Wally was an amazing father, grandfather, great-grandfather, brother and friend, and will be greatly missed by all who knew and loved him.
He was preceded in death by parents, Otto and Anna Larson; his wife, Phyllis; sister, Myra; and son, Darwin.
Wally is survived by his daughters, Debbie and Jeff Johnson of Page, AZ, Doreen and Randy Anderson of Modesto, CA, and adopted daughter Diana and Maynard Bauer of Mesa, AZ. Theodore I LASCARIS

[I008422]
Theodore Lascaris (d. 1222), emperor of Nicaea, was born of a noble Byzantine family.

He became the son-in-law of the Byzantine Emperor Alexius III and distinguished himself during the sieges of Constantinople by the Latins (1203-1204). After the capture of the city he gathered a band of fugitives in Bithynia and established himself in the town of Nicaea, which became the chief rallying-point for his countrymen.

Relieved of the danger of invasion by a Latin force which had defeated him in 1204 but was recalled to Europe by a Bulgarian invasion, he set to work to form a new Byzantine state in Asia Minor, and in 1206 assumed the title of emperor.

During the next years Theodore was beset by enemies surrounding his fledgling state. He maintained himself stubbornly in defensive campaigns against the Latin emperor Henry of Flanders, defeated his rival Alexius I, emperor of Trebizond, and carried out a successful counter-attack upon Kay Khusrau I, the sultan of Rüm (also called the sultan of Iconium or Konya), who had been instigated to war by the deposed Alexius III.

Theodore's crowning victory was gained in 1210, when in a battle near Pisidian Antioch he captured Alexius and wrested the town itself from the Turks.

At the end of his reign he ruled over a territory roughly conterminous with the old Roman provinces of Asia and Bithynia. Though there is no proof of higher qualities of statesmanship in him, by his courage and military skill he enabled the Byzantine nation not merely to survive, but ultimately to beat back the Latin invasion.

Family
By his first wife, Anna Komnena Angelina, the daughter of the Emperor Alexius III, Theodore Lascaris had two daughters: Eirene Laskarina (married John III Ducas Vatatzes) and Maria Laskarina (married King Bela IV of Hungary).

After Anna Angelina died in 1212, Theodore Lascaris remarried to Philippa of Armenia, the daughter of King Ruben III of Armenia. This marriage was annuled a year later, and the son born to them, Constantine, was disinherited.

Theodore Lascaris married thirdly Marie de Courtney, the daughter of Peter of Courtenay and Yolanda of Flanders, but they had no children.

Albert W. LASH

[I064891]
Albert W. Lash, 99, a lifelong resident of Big Rock, Ill., passed away Saturday, June 9, 2007, at Hillside Rehab and Care Center in Yorkville.
Born Dec. 31, 1907, in Big Rock, he was the son of Louis and Mamie (Cate) Lash. Albert was united in marriage to Gladys Sherwin on April 4, 1931, in Aurora; they spent the next 53 happy years of their lives together until her passing on Oct. 19, 1984.
He was a loving husband, father, grandfather, great-grandfather, brother and uncle who will be deeply missed by his family and friends.
Mr. Lash was a member of First Baptist Church of Big Rock and English Congregational United Church of Christ in Big Rock. He was employed for many years at the post office in Big Rock until his retirement. Albert was the manager of Big Rock Grain Co-op for 25 years. He also was employed for 12 years by Anchor Brush in Montgomery. Albert was a devoted 20-year member of Big Rock Lions Club and long will be remembered for all the Easter lilies and poinsettias he sold to raise money for them. He loved nothing more than to go fishing with his family and his many friends. Up until just a few months ago, Albert could be seen driving around in his little aqua car smiling and waving at everyone he passed. He will be dearly missed by so many.
Albert is survived by two children, Adelbert (Janet) Lash of Aurora and Barbara Williams of DeKalb; five grandchildren, Debra Gabriel and Kevin (Mary) Williams, both of DeKalb, Karen (Bill) Yoder of Big Rock, Sheri (Chris) Hernandez of Aurora and Laurie (Tim) Kipper of Sandwich; seven great-grandchildren, Christine and Kaylee Williams, Jordanna and Karissa Marsh, David and Megan Yoder and Bradley Kipper; and many nieces, nephews, great-nieces and great-nephews.
He was preceded in death by his parents; his wife, Gladys Lash; three brothers; and four sisters.
The funeral service will be held at 10 a.m. Thursday, June 14, at First Baptist Church of Big Rock, 201 Lincoln Ave., with the Rev. Dr. Eugene Frost officiating. Entombment will follow at Greenwood Cemetery in Hinckley. Friends may call from 3-7 p.m. Wednesday, June 13, at Nash-Nelson Memorial Chapel, 141 N. Maple St. in Hinckley.
Memorials in Albert's name can be directed to First Baptist Church of Big Rock, the Lions Club of Big Rock or the Big Rock Fire and EMS.
