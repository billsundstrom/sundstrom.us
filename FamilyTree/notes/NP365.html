Jerrold Edward MERRILL

[I110807]
Jerrold Edward Merrill was born to Claude Richard and Nellie Bowlden Merrill, the youngest of seven siblings.
Though of humble circumstance, he recollected a happy childhood. As was his nature, he remembered difficult times as fond memories. A natural and gifted athlete, he was remarkably humble. It was only through others that we learned of his state championship tennis title in high school, won with a borrowed racket. Any sport he played, he quickly and effortlessly became expert at, all 6'7" of him beautiful, lithe, and smooth. His long fingers and hands appeared those of an artist, not reflecting the difficult work of his every day in the painting business, in support of his family.
Dad met his beautiful sweetheart Beth Eldredge in 1956, they married July 26, 1957. Their marriage was solemnized in the Salt Lake LDS Temple in 1958. They were blessed with seven children; he saw us all as bright lights with limitless potential, he was full of praise and fatherly pride. He loved his wife dearly and spoke of her with respect and amazement, always expressing disbelief that she chose him! We fondly remember family vacations, nothing compared with being held by him and "wave jumping" high above the crests of the ocean. Dad never met a stranger! He saw God in everyone, he quietly loved and sought to serve others.
He is survived by children: Sydna (Mike) Freeman; Shauna (Mike) Brockbank; Kurt (Tammy) Merrill; Kent (Carol) Merrill; Salli (Kent) Brown; Angie (Shawn) Rynearson; 30 grandchildren and three great-grandchildren; by his sister Gloria and brothers William and Richard. He is also survived by his wife Beth, who tenderly cared for him throughout his six month battle with Gastric cancer.
He was preceded in death by beloved son Jay; brothers James and Joseph; sister Patricia. We learned much about dignity and bearing adversity well as we watched our husband and father suffer afflictions without complaint. We will try to honor his good name by emulating his gracious acceptance of difficulties and his ability to see goodness and beauty in all about him. We wish to express gratitude for his caregivers at Utah Cancer Specialists, Dr.'s Miller and Corcoran, and sweet nurse Nora; Symbii Hospice, Rochelle, Megan, Mike and Libby; Nancy Aiken, P.A. at the Veteran's Hospital. Thanks to his dear friends at the Mt. Olympus Senior Citizen Center, he loved them all.
Visitation at Memorial Estates Chapel 3115 East 7800 South on Saturday, September 25 at 10:00 a.m., with services to follow at 11:00 a.m. In lieu of flowers, donations to the Utah Cancer Specialists would be appreciated. Good-Bye, our gentle giant, until we meet again.

Lee MERRILL

[I068121]
Lee Merrill Byrd, novelist and publisher, was born and raised in New Jersey but has spent most of her life in the Southwest. In 1985, with her husband, poet Bobby Byrd, she founded Cinco Puntos Press, a publishing company named after their neighborhood in El Paso, Texas. Cinco Puntos-a publisher of non-fiction, fiction, poetry and childrenʼs literature-is recognized for bringing the multicultural literatures of the American Southwest, the U.S./Mexico border region and Mexico to a national audience. Lee and Bobby have three children and five grandchildren.
Leeʼs collection of short stories, My Sister Disappears, was published by Southern Methodist University Press in 1993. It received a Southwest Book Award and the Stephen F. Turner Award from the Texas Institute of Letters for the best first work of fiction in 1993. In 1997, she was the recipient of the Dobie-Paisano Fellowship. In 2005, she and her husband received Cultural Freedom Fellowships from the Lannan Foundation.
In October 2003, Cinco Puntos published Leeʼs first picture book for children, The Treasure on Gold Street, A Neighborhood Story in Spanish and English. It received a Skipping Stones Honor Book Award, a Southwest Book Award, a Paterson Poetry Center Prize, and a Teddy Award from the Texas Writers League.
Cinco Puntos published Lover Boy, A Bilingual Counting Book, in the Fall of 2005. In the Spring of 2006, Algonquin Books published Leeʼs first novel, Riley's Fire. People Magazine chose Riley's Fire as one of the top 10 books of 2006.

Jacob Daniel MERRIMAN

[I004039]
June 9, 2003 - it was the day of the my greatest sorrow. It was the day that saw the passing of my wife, Tristen. She was 25 years old, and we had so many hopes and dreams for our future together that never came to pass. She was gone, and I remained. God, however, desired for me to do more than just remain. After first sustaining me with His love, He persuaded me that my life would only be full if I invested in people. Instead of allowing my pain to build up protective walls from others, I was being called to comfort others with the comfort that I, myself, had been comforted with (2 Cor. 1:4).
I knew that God was calling me to seminary, but I also knew that it was not time for me to move away from the support of my family and friends. The decision to enroll at NABS (now Sioux Falls Seminary) was primarily due to its location. I soon discovered, however, that there were many more reasons to stay. From my first class, I felt God affirming my call and equipping me specifically for pastoral ministry. I have also been greatly blessed by the faculty and community that I have come to know at Sioux Falls Seminary. The diversity of both the professors and the students has challenged me along the way, and has given be a broad base for ministry.
December 30, 2006 - it was a great day of rejoicing. It was the day that I married Caroline, a second precious daughter that the Lord has blessed me with. I've now graduated, and working in a pastoral position. When I lost Tristen, my life felt so void of purpose. But since then, God has been refilling my cup.

If you feel God telling you that it is time to pursue His calling for your life, don't wait any longer. He wants to do amazing things in and through you.

Robert MERRITT

[I086094]
MERRITT, Robert Robert Merritt, 98, of Glastonbury, died Monday, (November 19, 2007) at Hartford Hospital after a brief illness. He was predeceased by his wife of 57 years, Mary (McAleer) Merritt, a son, Peter Charles, and a sister, Priscilla Dow. He is survived by his daughters, Priscilla Ann and her husband Louis Montalvo of Colchester, Cynthia Elizabeth of Glastonbury, and son, Robert Nicholas and his wife Susan Lennon of Hartford, and grandchildren Katherine Wang of Philadelphia and Stephen Wang of Glastonbury. He is also survived by his sister, Barbara Nason of Concord, NH, and twelve nieces and nephews. Bob was born August 29, 1909, in South Hadley Falls, MA, the son of Harry T. and Ethel (Moore) Merritt and was raised in Wolfeboro, NH. He graduated from Harvard College in 1930, Magna Cum Laude and Phi Beta Kappa. He attended his 75th class reunion in Cambridge two years ago. During his years in Wolfeboro, he worked in the Western Union telegraph office and ran a printing shop with his father. He worked in Boston and New York before coming to Hartford in 1945. He worked for Connecticut Savings Bank Life Insurance and the State Insurance Department before joining Phoenix Mutual Life, where he worked for most of his career. He was a Fellow of the Society of Actuaries and, after retiring, consulted at The Phoenix until the age of 93. Bob was active in Glastonbury town government, serving on its first Town Council and later as chair of the Board of Education. He was also involved in the local Democratic Party for many years, including as a writer and performer in the annual Democratic skits that were a popular town event from the late '50s to the early '70s. He ran for State Representative from Glastonbury in 1976. With his quick wit, he was frequently called upon to compose and recite doggerel for birthdays and other special occasions, and was known to come up with spontaneous limericks. He and Mary enjoyed classical music and opera, and they sang together in the Hartford Chorale for nearly 20 years. They enjoyed spending time on Martha's Vineyard and in Colrain, MA, and traveled widely in their later years, particularly to Italy, their favorite. Bob's family would like to offer its heartfelt appreciation to Mary Hough-Scholl and Barbara Cooper, who provided loving and attentive care to Bob and made it possible for him to continue living in his home after Mary's death. A Memorial Mass will be held at St. Patrick-St. Anthony Church, 185 Church St, Hartford, on Saturday, December 15, at 11 a.m. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Second Century Fund of the Welles-Turner Memorial Library; the House of Bread; or the Jimmy Fund. Glastonbury Funeral Home has charge of the arrangements.
The Hartford Courant, 21 November 2007

MRS. MAX KRUKOW, TERRIL RESIDENT, DIES ON TUESDAY
Mrs. Max Krukow of Terril, who has been a resident of Dickinson county most of her life, passed away at the Spencer hospital Tuesday morning, Sept. 18 at the age of 52 years and nine months, after an illness of several months. However, Mrs. Krukow had been a patient at the hospital only a day, and her death was sudden and unexpected.
Mary Mertens Krukow, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Henry Mertens, Sr., was born Dec. 5, 1892, at the farm home of her parents southwest of Milford, where she lived until the time of her marriage on Nov. 8, 1911. She was united in marriage to Max Krukow at the Lutheran church in Terril, and with her husband has lived at various homes near Graettinger, Ruthven, Fostoria, Milford and Terril.
Mr. and Mrs. Krukow are the parents of eight children, five sons and three daughters, who with Mr. Krukow survive. The children are: Julius of Dickens; Orville of Ruthven; Art, Donald, and Kenneth at home; Mrs. Walter Stukey and Mrs. Arthur Peterson of Spencer and Mrs. Roy Johnson of Sutherland. There are 12 grandchildren surviving.
Her parents preceded her in death, but she leaves three sisters and three brothers. They are: Mrs. Oscar Hoisett and Mrs. John Danielson of Minnesota; Mrs. George Foster of Montana; Mrs. Marinus Johnson and J. M. Mertens of Milford; and Henry Mertens of Lake Park.
Mrs. Krukow was a wonderful mother and her home and family were her life.
Funeral services will be held Friday, Sept. 21, at the Cobb Funeral Home in Spencer. The Rev. G. D. Serril of the Christian Church will preach the service at 2 o'clock. Interment will be in North Lawn cemetery at Spencer. Pallbearers will be Ed Metcalf, O. L. Stukey, Jack Griffin, R. A. Long, Roy Brown and Walter Bart.
The Milford Mail, 20 September 1945. pg 1
