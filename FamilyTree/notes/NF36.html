Janet Arlene Nitzel Is Bride of Freeman Keith Bennett
MR. AND MRS. FREEMAN KEITH BENNETT
Miss Janet Arlene Nitzel, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Theodore Nitzel, Rte. 2, became the bride of Freeman Keith Bennett, son of Mr. and Mrs. William Bennett, Rle. 2, in a 2 p.m. ceremony last Saturday In the First Congregational Church. Mixed bouquets of gladioli, snapdragons, iris and cosmos decorated the church. Mrs. S. E, Ekman presented a half hour prelude of organ music concluded with Lohengrin's Wedding March, as the bride appeared on the arm of her father. Mendelssohn's wedding march was played at the close of the double ring ceremony read by the Rev. Ward J. Fellows.
The former Miss Nitzel fashioned her own wedding gown of white lace over satin. Her fingertip veil, edged with a wide border of lace, was fastened to a crown traced with seed pearls and rhinestones. She carried a bouquet of red roses tied with white satin streamers, and wore the groom's gift of necklace and earrings in gold leaf design set with blue stones. Charlotte Nitzel, sister of the bride, was maid of honor; Victoria Bennett, sister of the groom and Beverly Durkee, cousin of the groom, were bridesmaids. They were gowned in yellow, blue and pink ankle length dresses of net over taffeta, which featured full all-around peplums, and stoics over strapless bodices. Their flowers were carnations and iris and their gifts from the bride were necklaces to match their gowns. Attendants of the groom were best man Dewaine Scott, cousin of the groom; James Piasecki and Theodore Pollock, cousins of the bride. Performing ushering duties were Raymond Miller, Jr. and Jay Miller, friends of bride and groom. The bride's mother wore a blue print dress with navy accessories; the groom's mother, blue satin with black accessories. Both had red rose corsages.
Dinner for the immediate families was served in Sally's Hilltop, a reception and dance were held later in Waverly Beach. Out of town guests included: Mr, and Mrs. Paul Crupi and daughter, Paula, Milwaukee; Mr. and Mrs. William Durkee and Mr. and Mrs. Sylvester Kiefer and son, Florian, Little .Chute; Mr. and Mrs. Leo Lentzwitz, Charlotte and Barbara, Mr. and Mrs. Harry Adamski, Henry Bennett and Mr. and Mrs. Julius Duberstien, Mosinee; Mr. and Mrs. William Britton, Wausau; Mr. and Mrs. George Kabak, William Johnson and Otto Johnson, Kenosha; Fred Connat, Chicago and Mr. and Mrs. Elto Arnot and Mrs. Bell Schuckhart, Tomahawk. Both Mr. and Mrs. Freeman Keith Bennett attended Rhinelander High School and the groom is employed by Musson Brothers, Inc. The couple will be at home on Rte. 2.
THE Rhinelander Daily News, 10 April 1956

Miss Joan M. Coffey Weds Clayton Ryder

Schenectady Girl Became Bride Saturday of Son of Dr. and Mrs. Morton Ryder, of Rye

Saturday, April 23, 1949, Miss Joan Maureen Coffey, daughter of Mrs. Joseph Matthew Coffey of Schenectady and the late Mr. Coffey, was married to Clayton Ryder, son of Dr. and Mrs. Morton Ryder of Rye. The ceremony was performed in the rectory of St. Paul the Apostle church in Schenectady at 2:00 p. m. Following the ceremony a reception was held at the Mohawk Golf club. Both Mr. and Mrs. Ryder were graduated from Cornell University. During the war Mr. Ryder served with the Army Air Forces overseas as a pilot and at the time of his discharge held the rank of first lieutenant. The couple will make their home in Rye. Mr. Ryder is a grandson of Clayton Ryder of this village.
The Putnam County Courier, Carmel, NY, 26 April 1949

Joan Maureen Coffey Is Bride
In Church Ceremony Saturday
In a ceremony held Saturday afternoon at 2 o'clock at the Church of St. Paul the Apostle, Miss Joan Maureen Coffey, daughter of Mrs. Joseph Mathew Coffey and the late Mr. Coffey of Albany street, became the bride of Clayton Ryder 2nd, son of Dr. and Mrs. Morton Ryder of Rye. Rev. Robert Cronin officiated at the marriage.
Traditional White Satin
The bride was given in marriage her brother-in-law, John H. Reynolds Jr. of this city. Her gown was of traditional white satin, designed with off-the-shoulder neckline, a yoke of illusion, long sleeves, a fitted bodice and long train. She wore a bonnet of Val lace and her fingertip veil of French illusion was caught with stephanotis. She carried a bridal cascade bouquet of white orchids, white roses and stephanotis.
The bride's sister, Mrs. John R. Reynolds Jr., was her matron of honor. Mrs. Reynolds was in a gown of pale yellow marquisette, which had a tucked fischu with ruffled edge and tiered skirt. She carried a Colonial shower bouquet of violets and wore a half halo of violets.
The bridesmaids, Miss Doris M. Coffey of Albany, a sister of the bride, and Miss Constance Ryder of Rye, sister of the bridegroom, were in identical gowns of aqua marquisette made with off-the-shoulder neckline, fitted bodice and full skirt. Each carried a Colonial shower bouquet of feathered yellow carnations and violets, and wore a half halo matching the bouquet.
Clifton Corley Jr. of Harrison was best man.
A reception was held at the Mohawk Golf club immediately following the ceremony. The bride's mother wore a formal gown of pastel green crepe, an orchid color flowered hat and corsage of orchids. The mother of the bridegroom chose a gown of pastel yellow lace with a black hat and corsage of Talisman roses.
Following their wedding trip to Bermuda, the couple will reside at Rye. The bride's traveling ensemble was a beige suit, with matching cloche hat and accessories of brown. She wore a corsage of white orchids.
Mrs. Ryder is a graduate of Nott Terrace High school and Cornell University where she was affiliated with Kappa Alpha Theta sorority. Mr. Ryder is a graduate of Loomls School, Windsor, Conn., and Cornell University, where he was a member of Chi Psi fraternity. During the war he served as pilot of the 15th AF in Italy, with rank of first lieutenant. He is now associated with the General Electric Co. in New York City.
Gazette, Schenectady
