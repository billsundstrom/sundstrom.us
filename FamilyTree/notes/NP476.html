Dora was born on February 20, 1911 to Frank and Alvina (Berneking) Schmidt on a farm near Nashua, MN. She was baptized on May 14, 1911 at Zion Lutheran Church in Parnell Township. Dora was confirmed on Palm Sunday, March 28, 1926 by Rev. Gierke at St. John Lutheran Church in Dumont, MN. Dora attended rural School District No. 6 southwest of Wheaton. She worked for eight months for room and board so she could attend daily confirmation classes. Dora also worked for neighbors earning $1.00 to $1.50 in three weeks. She shocked grain, milked and herded cattle and in the hay bucker worked with horses. In 1926 the family moved to a farm by Mohall, ND. On February 7, 1928 she married Henry Tobeck of Tolley, ND and they moved to Milwaukee, WI.
In September of 1928 they moved to a farm southwest of Wheaton and started farming with Henry's brother John. Dora helped with the farm work. She always had a garden and did a lot of canning and sewed all the clothes for the family. In the fall of 1944 they moved a mile east of Wheaton and they continued to farm. She was a circle member of St. John Lutheran Church where she helped to mend sheets and gowns for the Wheaton Hospital. Dora enjoyed taking the grandchildren swimming in Lake Traverse which they loved, along with her homemade potato bread with strawberry jam. In the fall of 1966 they moved into Wheaton where Dora worked at a café and the high school cafeteria. Henry and Dora always enjoyed the grandchildren coming to see them. Henry died in 1975. Dora enjoyed playing solitaire, embroidery and cheering for the Minnesota Twins. Dora stayed in her home until March of 2012 when she entered the Traverse Care Center due to health problems. Dora passed away on June 14, 2012 at the Traverse Care Center in Wheaton.
Dora is survived by four children: Mrs. Milton (Ione) Schmidt of Wheaton, MN; Mrs. Kenneth (Eunice) Schmitz of Wheaton, MN; VerDean Tobeck of Annandale, MN; Sandra Sager of Wheaton, MN; daughter-in-law, Joyce Skoltke; 18 grand children, 31 great grandchildren and 24 great-great grandchildren.

John F. SCHMIDT

[I076441]
Among the early settlers of the Bloomfield community wereJohn and Dorathea (Koch) Schmidt both having immigrated to this country from the old country. John was born December 20, 1856 at Luhrsdorf, Holstein, Germany and came to the United States while a young man and settled in Clinton County, Iowa. Dorathea was born April 12, 1855 1t Messsunde, Holstein, Germany and immigrated to this country in 1882 and married John at Maquoketa, Iowa that same year. They farmed in several states before coming and settling northeast of Bloomfield in 1902. John and Dorathea retired to Bloomfield in the ear 1918. They were the parents of eight children: Carl, Albert, Bertha, Amanda, Frieda, Ella, Henry and John.
Mr. and Mrs. Schmidt experienced many of the hardships of the pioneer days. The walking plow and cultivator were familiar pieces of equipment during those days of non-motorized equipment. A team of horses provided the power necessary to farm and also provided transportation for the family by being hitched to a wagon, buggy or sled. While farming in Kansas prior to their moving to Nebraska, the Schmidt family endured a severe Blizzard and in order to save their lives of the team of horses for lack of a adequate barn , it was necessary to bring them into the house. When relating this event many times later, they started that apparently the horses sensed the seriousness of the storm and remained very calm and tranquil.
Entertainment included visiting the neighbors as well as with friends at church. Mrs. Schmidt often told how during the long winter months, she would push the dinning room table to the wall and while her husband played the accordion, she would take turns dancing with the children. Occasionally Square dances in the neighborhood homes also provided entertainment and a chance to socialize. Due to financial circumstances and raising a large family, Mr. and Mrs. Schmidt were never able to return to Germany to visit the relatives there.
While residing in Bloomfield Mr. and Mrs. Schmidt were involved in community affairs and were members of the First Trinity Lutheran Church. John passed away July 27, 1924 at the age of 67 years, Dorathea passed away December 1, 1929 at the age 74 years
