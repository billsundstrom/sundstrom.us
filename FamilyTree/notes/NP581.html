Edward Ernest WESTOVER

[I065391]
Edward E. Westover, 97, of 1000 9th Avenue S. Clear Lake, Iowa, Jan. 7 at a Mason City Hospital.
He was born March 1, 1885 in Genoa, Ill., the son of Fred and Mary Ann Westover. At the age of 4 he moved with his family to a farm near Swaledale. He attended rural school and farmed in the area most of his life. On Jan. 30, 1907 he married Geneva Bisgrove at Rockwell. He was a member of St. Patrick's Catholic Church.
Survivors include his three sons, Glenn and George of Clear Lake and Edward Gene of Garner; two daughters, Ellen Mae (Mrs. Leonard) Ouverson of Clear Lake, and Mrs. Gertrude Marion of Ionia; two sisters, Mrs. Lucille Koplau of Aurora, Colo. And Mrs. Nellie Bestel of St. Cloud, Minn.; 12 grandchildren; nine great-grandchildren; and four great-great-grandchildren.
Preceding him in death were his parents, his wife, one grandson, one great-grandson, two sisters and two brothers.
Services for Mr. Westover were held Jan. 11 at St. Patrick's Catholic Church, Clear Lake, with Rev. James Duster officiating. Organist was Marie Faber. A parish wake was held Jan. 10 at the Ward-Van Slyke Colonial Chapel. Interment was in Clear Lake Cemetery. The Ward-Van Slyke Colonial Chapel is in charge of arrangements.
Pallbearers were Marshall Friest, Shelby Pierce, Robert Nichols, Wilbur Schmoll, Orville Stinehart and Roger Flaten.

Edward Eugene WESTOVER

[I065402]
MOBERLY, Mo. - Edward Eugene Westover, 78, formerly from Clear Lake, died on Saturday (May 10, 2003) at his home in Moberly, Mo.
Mr. Westover was born on Dec. 13,1924, at Clear Lake, Iowa, the son of Edward E. and Geneva (Bisgrove) Westover.
He served in the U.S. Navy from 1942-1946.
In 1947, he married Maxine Prescott and she survives at the home in Moberly, Mo.
Mr. Westover farmed and worked in Iowa until his retirement and moved to Clark, Mo., at that time.
Survivors include a son, Larry Westover of Clear Lake, Iowa; two daughters, Judy Anderson and husband, David, of Clear Lake, and Sharon Deling and husband Dan of Moberly, Mo.; three grandchildren, Nicole Lyon and husband, Mike of Sherburn, Minn., Travis Anderson, of Clear Lake, and Tanya Deling of Moberly, Mo.; three great-grandchildren, Ashley and Katelyn of Sherburn, Minn., and Amber of Moberly, Mo.; and a sister, Ellen Mae Ouverson of Clear Lake.
He was preceded in death by his parents; a son, Garry; two brothers, Glenn and George Westover; and one sister, Gertrude Marion.
Ed or Gene we never knew which name to call him. When he farmed and worked at the sugar beet plant he was known as Gene. When he worked at Winnebago and IMT he was known as Ed. But to the family he was known as a loving and caring husband, father, grand-father, and great grandfather.
In our eyes he achieved raising us with many interests, from hunting, trail riding to putting up with 4-H projects with his children and grandchildren.
His most enjoyment was his wood working projects, especially the buggy seats of which more than one has made it to different states. So if you see a buggy seat, then possibly you will see a small piece of what he enjoyed doing the most in his retirement years.
His body will be cremated and no service will be performed as were his wishes. He wanted people to remember him as he was.
Parker Funeral Home Service & Crematory, (573) 449-4153.

George Tibutis WESTOVER

[I065396]
Former Glenwood Boy is Drowned
The many friends of George Westover, son of Mr. and Mrs. Fred Westover of Glenwood, were grieved to learn last week that he had lost his life at the Nelson Reservoir near Malta, Montana. The information received is that he was out hunting and was trying retrieve a goose when the accident happened which took his life. The body was recovered on Friday, October 22nd, the day after the accident occurred. George Tibutis Westover was born on April 14, 1900, in Swaledale, Iowa. At the age of eight, he came to Glenwood Township, Pope County, with his parents and attended the country school there. In October, 1905, he moved with his parents to Glenwood. He graduated from Glenwood High School with the Class of 1919. He was employed at the Swanson Bakery for several years and was also employed with the Soo Line Railroad. Later on, he went to Glasgow, Montana, where he became a brakeman with the Great Northern Railroad. In 1929, he was married to Miss Geraldine Gould of Spokane, Washington. Besides his wife, he leaves to mourn his parents, Mr. and Mrs. Fred Westover, and three brothers and three sisters: Edward of Clear Lake, Iowa; John William of Tomah, Wisconsin; Fred B. of Billings, Montana; Nellie, Mrs. Orie Bestel of Alexandria; Lucille Koplau of Burbank, California; and Bertha of Glenwood. The last visit of George Westover to Glenwood was last March, when he visited his family and friends here. The news of his tragic death came as a sad blow to many that knew him here. Through his friendly ways, his pleasant personality, and fine manly qualities, he had the ability to make lasting friends wherever he went.The body was taken to Spokane, the home of his wife, for burial, where funeral services were held on Monday October 25th.
Pope County Tribune, 28 October 1937

Glenn Ernest WESTOVER

[I065401]
Glenn E. Westover, 83, of Clear Lake, Iowa, died Tuesday, May 27, 1997 at his home in Clear Lake.
Funeral services were held Friday, May 30, at the First Congregational Church, Clear Lake, with the Rev. Thomas Healey officiating. Internment was in the Clear Lake Cemetery.
Glenn was born in rural Burchinal, Iowa, the son of Ed and Geneva (Bisgrove) Westover on October 22, 1913. He attended rural county school.
Glenn worked for Sam Kennedy after school. He married Lucile Crozier on Feb. 24, 1935 at the Congregational Parsonage in Clear Lake.
Glenn stared working for Farmers Co-op Elevator, Clear Lake, in March 1942 and became manager in 1948. He continued as manager until his retirement on Jan. 1, 1977.
He loved doing gardening; raise flowers, watch rodeos, the Iowa Hawkeye's and the Chicago Bulls. He was a member of the First Congregational Church, Clear Lake and the 100F Lodge in Clear Lake.
He is survived by his wife, Lucile Westover, Clear Lake; two sons, Roger Westover, Clear Lake, and Allan Westover and his wife Bonnie, Brooklyn Park Minn.; one sister, Ellen Mae Ouverson and her husband Leonard, Clear Lake; one brother, Gene Westover and his wife Maxine, Clark, MO., three grandchildren, Tammy Boeshart and her husband, Jeffrey, Clear Lake, Jonas Westover and Nathaniel Westover of Brooklyn Park.
He was preceded in death by his parents, his daughter-in-law, Marilyn Westover; one brother George Westover; and one sister, Gertrude Marion.
Organist Pat Treloar and accompanied soloist Paul Langholz.
Honorary pallbearers were Shelby Pierce, Fred Banks, Robert Baker, Wilbur Schmoll, Marion Warren, Dick Nickerson, and Ray Christensen.
Pallbearers were Glen Brady, Jerry Scott, Jay Ouverson, Ray Grell, Jerry Millard, and Ron Huntely.
Clear Lake Reporter, 4 June 1997

Jack Lane WESTOVER

[I065479]
Jack L. Westover, 65, of 1702 Winfield Avenue, Bremerton, a retired supervisor at Puget Sound Naval Shipyard, died Monday at Harrison Memorial Hospital.
He was born Oct. 10, 1920, in Mason City, Iowa and moved to this area in 1940 from Tomah, Wis.
Mr. Westover served in the U.S. Navy during World War II.
He married Juanita Wagner July 19, 1963, in Bremerton.
Mr. Westover was a supervisor coppersmith at PSNS and retired in 1975 after 30 years.
He was twice past post commander of American Legion Post 149, Bremerton and a member of the National Association of Retired Federal Employees 40 and 8. Mr. Westover enjoyed rock and gem collecting, hunting, fishing, and horticulture.
In addition to his wife, survivors include two daughters, Sharon (Mrs. John) Klinkert of Port Orchard and Teresa (Mrs. Kim) Schaefer-Falconer of Lynnwood; a son Richard Schaefer of Kent, nine grandchildren and two great-great grandchildren.
Visitation will be during the day Thursday at Miller-Woodlawn Funeral Home, with services Friday at 1pm at the funeral home.
Internment will be in the Miller-Woodlawn Memorial Park.
Memorials may be made to the Intensive Care Unit at Harrison Memorial Hospital, Bremerton 9310.
The Sun Bremerton Washington, 21 February 1985

John William WESTOVER

[I065392]
Longtime area resident John William Westover, 97, died Saturday at Harrison Memorial Hospital as a result of flu complications.
He was born July 7, 1887 in Genoa, Ill., and served in the Navy from 1909 to 1913. While in the Navy, he served in the Nicaraguan Rebellion and aboard the coal-fired cruiser USS California.
Following discharge, he lived in Iowa, North Dakota, and moved to Belfair area prior to World War II. He lived in Belfair for many years, while employed at Puget Sound Naval Shipyard.
Mr. Westover retired from PSNS, and also had been employed by a railroad. He moved to Bremerton in 1980.
His wife died in 1957. Survivors include his son and daughter-in-law, Mr. and Mrs. Jack Westover of Bremerton; a sister, Lucille Koplau of Fort Collins, Colo., three grandchildren, nine great-grandchildren, and two great-great-grandchildren, all of the Puget Sound area.
At his request, there will be no services.
The Sun, Bremerton, WA, 21 February 1985
