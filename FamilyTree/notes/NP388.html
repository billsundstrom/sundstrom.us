Gloria Theresa (Nardiello) Zuchegna, 89, of Winsted, formerly of New York, NY, passed away peacefully at Hartford Hospital with family by her side on February 8, 2013. She was the daughter of the late Giuseppe and Asunta Nardiello. Born on June 27, 1923, she was the youngest of the first American generation of Italian immigrants in New York City. She married George Zuchegna in 1956. Upon arriving in Connecticut in 1972, she never looked back, as she grew to love Winsted and the many loyal friends she made here. She worked at Highland Acres Convalescent Home as bookkeeper and administrative assistant for 19 years, where she made many dear, life-long friends. Upon retiring in 1993, she then devoted herself to her favorite hobby: reading, and later to her newly arriving grandchildren. She was a member of the Mystery Book Club at the Beardsley Library right up until her passing, and was a Volunteer Mentor at Hinsdale School for several years, spending lunchtime reading to children. Gloria was a devout Catholic, faithfully saying her Rosary, attending Sunday Mass, and watching daily Mass on television. She was predeceased by a son, Peter, and by her six siblings (Guido, Danny, Vincent, Antoinette, Linda, and Lucia). She had the most wonderful sense of humor, and her smile brightened every room she entered. She leaves behind her loving family-son Alan Joseph Zuchegna of Winsted, daughter Teresa Lovejoy and Son-in-law John Lovejoy of New Hartford, and grandchildren Jacqueline, Elijah, and William Lovejoy of New Hartford. We all miss her so much already.

Toda of NAVARRE

[I030929]
Toda Aznárez, also Teuda de Larraun or Tota (c. 885-aft. 970), was the queen-consort of Pamplona through her marriage to Sancho I, who reigned 905-925, and was regent of Pamplona, 931-934.
She was the daughter of Aznar Sánchez, lord of Larraun, paternal grandson of king Garcı́a Íñiguez of Pamplona, while her mother Onneca Fortúnez was a daughter of king Fortún Garcés. Thus, Toda's children were also descendants of the Arista dynasty of Navarrese monarchs. She was sister of Sancha Aznárez, wife of king Jimeno Garcés, her husband's brother and successor, while Toda and Sancha were also aunts of Caliph Abd-ar-Rahman III, through their mother's first marriage to ʻAbdullah ibn Muhammad.
With the death of her brother-in-law Jimeno in 931, she became regent and guardian for her young son, Garcı́a Sánchez I. In 934 Toda signed a treaty pledging allegiance to her nephew Abd-ar-Rahman III, and released hostages of the Banu Di n-Nun clan, the caliph confirming the rule of her son Garcı́a (this has sometimes been interpreted as an act of the Caliph to liberate Garcı́a from his mother's direct control). This led to the rebellion in Falces by a count Fortún Garcés, an "irascible man who hated Muslims", the uprising being suppressed with Cordoban arms. Toda violated her treaty in 937, forcing a punitive campaign. She had been an energetic diplomat, arranging political marriages for her daughters among the competing royalty and nobility of Christian Iberia.
The Codex of Roda gives Sancho and Toda six children:
* Oneca (d. 931), married Alfonso IV the Monk of León in 926
* Sancha, married firstly Ordoño II of León, secondly Count Alvaro Herraméliz of Álava, and thirdly Fernán González, Count of Castile
* Urraca, married Ramiro II of León
* Velasquita (or Belasquita), married firstly Munio, count of Vizcaya, secondly Galindo, son of Bernard count of Ribagorza, and thirdly Fortún Galı́ndez, duke of Nájera.
* Orbita
* Garcı́a, king of Pamplona

Patrick P. NEE

[I040057]
Patrick P. Nee, age 84, died in his Walpole, Massachusetts home on Wednesday, June 10, 2015, surrounded by his loving family. Born in Boston, Massachusetts on September 10, 1930, he was the son of the late John P. and Bridget (Mulkerrin) Nee. He was raised in Dorchester and graduated from Roxbury Memorial High School with the class of 1949. His love of sports started at a young age, playing high school football before playing Semi Professional Football for the Chippawahs.
He married the love of his life, Dorothy V. Susan, on September 15, 1951, and together they made their home in South Boston. Shortly after their wedding, Patrick was inducted into the United States Army, where he proudly served his country during the Korean War. He was stationed in Germary for just over a year and was honorably discharged in 1954.
After his return from the Army, Patrick took a position with Bethlehem Steel in Quincy. It was during this time that Patrick and his wife Dorothy moved with their young family to 22 Bellflower Street in Dorchester and they settled in Walpole, Massachusetts in 1969.
In 1962, Patrick earned a position with the Boston Police Department as a patrolman. He later earned the position of Detective in 1985 and retired in 1995, after 33 years of dedicated service to the City of Boston. Patrick remained a member of the Boston Police Department Relief Association and enjoyed the Wednesday Morning Breakfasts with the other retirees.
As a member of the Walpole community, Patrick was a parishoner of Blessed Sacrement Church, the Kinghts of Columbus Council 1319, where he was a member of the 4th Degree, and the Walpole VFW Post 5188. His love of sports continued throughout his life and he enjoyed following them on TV. He also enjoyed traveling and taking trips to Foxwoods. Most of all, Patrick was a true family man, as a devoted husband of 64 years, and a devoted father and grandfather, he loved all the time that he spent with his family, especially watching his children and grandchildren compete in atheletic events.
