Jacob Robert TETRO

[I109103]
BEDFORD -- Jacob R. Tetro , 29, died Dec. 16, 2010.
He was born in Nashua, May 25, 1981, to Wayne and Trudy (Morrison) Tetro .
He was a graduate of Manchester West High School class of 2000. For many years Jake had been employed by Subaru of Manchester, first as a mechanic and most recently in the parts/Internet department.
For as far back as anyone can remember, Jake enjoyed finding out what makes things work. He was extremely smart and could fix just about anything.
He will be remembered as an easy going person who had a wonderful sense of humor.
People who met him loved being in his company.
He will be greatly missed, especially by his nephews and nieces that he adored.
Family members include, his parents of Bedford; three brothers, Phillip Tetro , of Weare, Brian and Scott Tetro , both of Bedford; his paternal grandmother, Virgina Tetro , of Merrimack; several nieces, nephews, aunts, uncles.
New Hampshire Union Leader, 18 December 2010

Adrienne Elizabeth THATCHER

[I039002]
Wife of former Huntingdon resident
After years of valiant struggles against multiple physical challenges and illnesses, Adrienne succumbed to the inevitable, Wednesday, May 14, 2014, at age 77. Surviving her are her daughter, Susan Koppenhaver of Poughkeepsie, New York, her son, Jim of Austin, Texas, her grandson, Cameron Utter of Poughkeepsie, New York, her daughter-in-law, Carin Petersen of Austin, Texas, and her granddaughter, Violet, also of Austin, Texas. After the untimely death of her first husband, James, in 1991, she was fortunate enough to meet and marry Bob Weaver of Huntingdon with whom she lived until his death in 2008. Bob's children also survive her, Cordy and Bob Henry, Cindy and Dean Kauffman, Kelly Shetrom and Melissa Kylor, all of Huntingdon, and Adam and Rae Weaver of Farnham, Virginia, and Rebecca and Roy Costo of Williamsport, Virginia. Also surviving are nieces, Betsy Valoret of Pennsylvania, Ginny Cahill of Saratoga Springs, New York, and John Fischer of Georgia. In addition to her two husbands, she was predeceased by her beloved sister, Carol, Carol's husband, Eddie, and Bob's oldest daughter, Jody Laird. Many stepgrandchildren, great-grandchildren and friends will miss her as well.
She enjoyed many things in life, including her beloved cats, her home, a dry wit, her books, her crafts, movies and her friends, both recent and the ones she made throughout her life. She supported the causes dear to her heart, especially the local libraries and the ASPCA. When she was able, she attended reading, knitting and game groups, and volunteered in the community. She earned both bachelor and master's degrees in history from Case Western Reserve University and was very supportive of education and learning of all types.
The family is planning a celebration of her life at 5 p.m. Saturday, June 28, at Germania on Overlook Road in the town of Poughkeepsie, New York, where dinner, music and memories will help us all say goodbye.
Following the June 28 celebration, she will be interred in the Arlington Cemetery with her first husband, James, at the convenience of the family.
The Daily News, Huntingdon, Mount Union, PA, 24 May 2014

Morris Frederick THELANDER

[I062589]
SINCLAIRVILLE-Morris F. Thelander, 44, of Middletown, Pa., son of Mr. and Mrs. George Thelander of this community, died unexpectedly Sunday in a Middletown hospital.
He was born November 5, 1909 in the Town of Gerry and was a former Sinclairville resident and graduate of Sinclairville High School.
For the past 14 years, Mr. Thelander had been employed by the U.S. Government as an electronic engineer.
Besides his parents, he is survived by his wife, Mrs. Fern Griswold Thelander; two daughters, Joyce Thelander and Marilyn Thelander, both at home; and a sister, Miss Margaret Thelander, Jamestown.
A prayer service will be held at 2 P.M. Tuesday from the Reber Funeral Home, Middletown.

Robert W. THIEL

[I004135]
A funeral for Robert W. Thiel will be at 10 a.m. Saturday, Dec. 23, 1995, in Memorial Gardens Funeral Chapel in Vancouver, Wash. Military honors will be given by the American Legion, Cape Horn Post No. 122, at the Evergreen Memorial Gardens Cemetary following the service.
Mr. Thiel died Dec. 19 at age 74.
Born Aug. 12, 1921, in Chicago, he had lived in the Vancouver area for the past 50 years. He worked as a carpenter for the Vancouver School District for 17 years. He was a World War II veteran, serving in the U.S. Army. Following the war, he joined the National Guard, where he was permanent personnel at the Vancouver Barracks until he retired in 1981.
Survivors include his wife, Margaret; daughters, Kathy Connolly and Barbara Turner, both of Washougal, Wash.; four grandchildren; and four great-grandchildren.
Interment will be in Evergreen Memorial Gardens Cemetery.
The family suggests remembrances be to the American Lung Association. Arrangements: Memorial Gardens Funeral Chapel.
