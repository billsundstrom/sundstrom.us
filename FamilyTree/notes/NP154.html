Funeral services for Fred A. Dunker, who died Sunday evening at his home in Rockville, will be conducted Wednesday at 3 p.m. in the Community Church in Rockville by the Rev. William Albright of the Loup City Methodist Church. Burial will be in Evergreen Cemetery in Loup City.
Mr. Dunker was born Aug. 5, 1874 in Omaha and came to Sherman County at the age of 3. The family settled near Ashton.
He and Miss Minnie W. Peterson were married Nov. 21, 1900, at St. Paul. In 1904, they moved to Schaupps, where he was manager of the Keystone Lumber Co. The family moved in 1930 to Rockville where he also was manager of the Keystone Lumber Co. He had been manager of the firm 45 years before his retirement in 1954.
Mr. Dunker was confirmed in the Lutheran faith and was a member of the IOOF Lodge. He had served three years as a Sherman County supervisor.
Surviving are his wife; two sons, Henry of Sioux City, IA and Floyd of Loup City; a daughter, Atlin Dunker of Ravenna; and three grandsons. He was preceded in death by his parents, a son Charles of St. Louis and a brother.

Edward Manlove Clayton DUNKLIN

[I024850]
He was a Baptist minister in Florida, who started more than 40 churches as a "home missionary." There's a church and recovery camp for alcoholics and drug addicts named for him in Okeechobee, FL. He was born in April 1863 in Alabama, possibly in Dothan.

Edward Manlove Clayton Dunklin (April 28, 1863-March 10, 1952) was born in Collirene, Lowndes County, Alabama. He received his early schooling at Barnes School in Strata, Florida, and graduated in 1897 from Georgetown College in Kentucky, and finished at The Southern Baptist Theological Seminary, in Louisville, Kentucky in 1898. Dunklin s family were slave owners, and when the slaves were set free, the family moved to Florida and bought orange groves. Freezing weather forced them out of the grove business. Dunklin was licensed to preach by the Ocala Church, and ordained on July 30, 1891, by the Oxford Church in the Alachua Association. He married Pauline Gomes on June 25, 1902. He served as pastor at Oxford, Peniel, Belleview, Apopka, Coleman, and Center Hill. He served as missionary of the State Board in the Tampa area from 1910-1917. From 1917-1921, he served as missionary in the St. Johns River Association. Then from 1921-1938, he entered his greatest service as missionary in the Indian River Association. He organized the Lemon Heights and Hollister Baptist Churches. He had a great and powerful influence in preaching to the Seminole Indians and received tribal and national recognition for his work.

Donald Norman DUNNING

[I064923]
Donald Dunning passed away on Sunday, April 28, 2013 at the age of 87.
Don was born in Brookfield, Illinois on April 3, 1926. He grew up in Brookfield during the Depression with his mother and brother Richard. Even though they had no money, he had nothing but fond memories of that time.
Don enlisted in the Army Air Force in January, 1944 and served until October, 1945.
Don's first professional job was with Argonne National Laboratory in 1949. While at Argonne, he attended the Illinois Technical Institute and received a Bachelorʼs of Science degree in Metallurgical Engineering in 1954. Also while working at Argonne, Don met Doris Hagemann and they married in June 1950. They had two sons, Craig in 1951 and Jeffrey in 1955.
Don moved to Los Alamos in April 1962 after working for firms including General Electric in the 1950s. With the exception of one year of working for Westinghouse in 1970, Don worked at LASL (and later LANL) until his retirement in 1991. Some of Donʼs notable engineering contributions were the design of fuel elements for various nuclear reactors, designing part of the Rover nuclear rocket in the 1960ʼs, developing the W-76 warhead in the 1970ʼs, and developing the W-88 warhead in the 1980ʼs. His one unique engineering skill was that he was able to visualize two dimensional drawings in three dimensions.
Many people in Los Alamos also knew Don from his work in real estate appraisal. Don performed thousands of residential real estate appraisals in and around Los Alamos starting in the mid-1970ʼs. In addition, Don performed commercial real estate appraisals and he continued to do this work up until the day he died. One recent job of his was to determine the damage to the ski area from the Las Conchas fire. Don was known for his high level of integrity in the real estate appraisal business. He refused to change his real estate valuations regardless of any pressure.
During this time, Don was able to find time to earn a Bachelorʼs in Law from LaSalle Extension University in 1971. He used that knowledge after he was elected as Los Alamos County probate judge in the 1970s.
Don had a passion for sports. He became a two-handicap golfer despite having to learn with cut-down, discarded clubs that had black tape for grips. In his younger years he could be frequently found on the golf course. Later, Don developed a passion for running. His last running event was running a marathon in 1982 and finishing second in his age group. Don was an avid skier before bad knees caught up to him, but he did get in three helicopter ski trips in during the late 1970ʼs. After running and skiing, Don took up biking and Masterʼs swimming. He won numerous awards in the 1993 Master's Swimming Championships. His favorite event was the 200 meter butterfly. Don enjoyed biking and a biking trip across Scotland in 1990 was his favorite. Other sports passions of Don's were fantasy baseball, which he participated in for close to 30 years and San Diego Chargers football, for which he had season tickets the last eight seasons.
Don was preceded in death by his mother Maude McCann (2003) and by his younger brother Richard (2002). He is survived by his son Craig of Boulder, Colorado, his son Jeffrey, daughter-in-law Gloria, and grandson Daniel of Los Alamos, NM.
Of all his varied interests, Donʼs greatest interest and joy was in supporting his sons, daughter-in-law, and grandson in all their activities.
Interment will be at Guaje Pines Cemetery in Los Alamos on Thursday, May 2 at 10:00 am.

John Austin Durfee of Malden, a former Malden traffic supervisor, died Tuesday at his home after a battle with cancer. He was 66.
Born in Foxboro, Mr. Durfee was raised in Roxbury and attended local schools.
He left high school early to enroll in the Air Force. He served during the Korean Conflict and attained the rank of airman third class.
Following the war, Mr. Durfee married and lived in Dorchester and Somerville before settling in Malden.
He was a member of the Teamsters Local 829, working out of the Stop & Shop distribution center in Readville. He later became a traffic supervisor for the city of Malden. He worked for the city for three years until disability caused him to retire.
Mr. Durfee was a member of VFW Post 19 in Somerville. He enjoyed fishing and playing softball.
He is survived by his wife, Margaret T. (Shinkwin); three sons, Patrick M. of Everett, Daniel A. of Ayer and Frederick J. of Somerville; a daughter, Marie H. of Malden; three sisters, Sandra of Quincy, Elizabeth Coburn of Hingham and Hazel Frazier of Florida; two brothers, Frederick of Maine and William of Braintree; five grandchildren; and many nieces and nephews.

A memorial service for George will be held at 2 p.m., Sunday, Nov. 22, at The Carriage House, 4680 Bellwether Lane, Oxford, Fla. George will be honored in a private military service at the Florida National Cemetery in Bushnell, Fla. on Monday, Nov. 23.
