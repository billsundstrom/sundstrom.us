Robert Bashford BOLT

[I072159]
Robert Bashford Bolt was born March 20, 1917, in Berkeley, California, the son of Dr. Richard A. Bolt and Beatrice French Bolt. He attended San Mateo Junior College in 1936- 1937 and graduated from Stanford University in 1940.
CAPT Bolt enlisted in the Navy V-7 program in September 1940, and was commissioned March 14, 1941, at Northwestern University. He served a year aboard the USS INDIANAPOLIS, then with the armed guard aboard merchant ships for 18 months and aboard several destroyer escorts until 1945. He was in the first group of Naval Reservists to attend the General Line Postgraduate School at Annapolis, Maryland, and later served one year in the USS DULUTH. During his initial Naval service, CAPT Bolt held various billets from junior division officer to temporary command of a destroyer escort. During this initial period of active duty, he was awarded the Silver Star for gallantry in action. Recalled to active duty in November 1950, CAPT Bolt served as OIC, Pacific Fleet Camera Party and Gunnery Assistant on the Staff, Commander, Training Command, Pacific until November 1952.
In his Naval Reserve participation (1947-1950 and 1952-1967) he commanded Reserve units on the division, battalion and group level and was a member of the 12th Naval District Public Affairs Board.
CAPT Bolt entered the insurance industry in 1947 with the Travelers Insurance Company. Since 1958, he had been affiliated with the Insurance Company of North America and was Executive Assistant in the Administration Division.
With the Naval Reserve Association, he had been Chapter and Naval District President and Vice President for Budget and Finance before being elected National President in September 1967. CAPT Bolt served as the National President of the Naval Reserve Association from September 1967 to October 1969, and received the Navy ºs Distinguished Service Award for his many contributions to the Navy and Naval Reserve. His memberships included the Navy League of the U.S., Naval Order of the U.S., Stanford Alumni Association, Commonwealth Club of California and Life Underwriters Association.
CAPT Bolt died on March 6, 2001. He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret (Winks) Kingsland Hewitt, step-daughter of RADM Colin Campbell, USN (Ret), USNA class of 1917, and daughter of COL Leland Hazelton Hewitt, USMA 1919. CAPT Bolt had one daughter, Mrs. Leslie Bolt Dennis and two grandchildren Jeffrey Mercer Dennis (29) and Brian Bolt Dennis (25).
A memorial service was held on 10 March 2001, St. Clements Pro Cathedral Church, El Paso, Texas, with a private interment, which followed. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the Naval Reserve Association.
NRA News, May 2001

Charles Meyer BONURA

[I069083]
Charles "Chuck" Meyer Bonura , M.D., 71, of Gallatin Gateway passed away on Sept. 5, 2001, after a courageous battle with cancer. Chuck was born June 13, 1930, to Charles and Grace (Perino) Bonura in Lodi, N.J.
Chuck grew up in Fort Lauderdale, Fla., graduating from Fort Lauderdale High School. He went on to graduate from Tulane University and the Tulane University School of Medicine. He served his internship at Jackson Memorial Hospital in Miami, Fla., before entering the United States Air Force as a flight surgeon. He was a general surgery resident at the Veteran's Administration Hospital in Coral Gables, Fla.
In 1960 he began his plastic surgery preceptorship with Dr. Neal Owens at the Owens Clinic in New Orleans. He began working in private practice of plastic and reconstructive surgery in Fort Lauderdale in 1963, continuing his practice until 1994. In 1965 he became certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery. He was a member of the American Society of Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery and the Aesthetic Society. He had been coming to Bozeman for 25 years and loved the area and its people, so in 1994 he moved his practice to Bozeman.
He loved the Boys and Girls Club and was a recent past president. He was also active in Ducks Unlimited and Pheasants Forever. He was a longtime member of the Coral Ridge Country Club in Fort Lauderdale, the Fort Lauderdale Yacht Club, Riverside Country Club, and Cottonwood Golf Club in Bozeman. He was an avid hunter and fly fisherman, who was environmentally sensitive, caring deeply for our land and the conservation of the land and wildlife.
Chuck married Althea Woods in 1976. He was a loving husband, father, grandfather, friend, physician and citizen. Chuck was caring, nurturing, funny, proud and private.
Chuck is survived by his wife, Althea; daughters, Ellen (Craig) Watson of North Salem, N.Y., and Patricia (Mark) Peters of Cortez, Colo.; son, Michael Bonura of Lakeland, Fla.; grandchildren, Craig Jr., Colin, Kelly and Kasey Watson, and Maggie, Cameron and Erica Peters; brother, Dr. Joseph (Jo) Bonura DDS of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; and sister, Jacqueline Davis of Deerfield, Fla.
Chuck goes now to be with his mother and father.
A memorial service will be at noon Saturday, Sept. 8, at their home at 2429 Cottontail Road.
Bozeman Daily Chronicle, 5 September 2001
