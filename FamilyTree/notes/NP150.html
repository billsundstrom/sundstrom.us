Helena Kozyra June 1, 1924 - December 12, 2010
It is with great sadness that we announce the sudden passing of Helena on December 12, 2010, longtime resident of Grande Prairie, AB. Helena was the oldest of seven children to Jan and Rozalia Dorocicz born in Borki, Poland on June 1, 1924. Helena and her children immigrated to Canada on December 7, 1971 to be near her parents and siblings. One of her proudest moments in life was when she became a Canadian citizen. In her spare time, she loved to read, sew, do needle point and being outdoors working in her flower garden. Helena was family oriented; enjoyed spending time with her family and watching her grandchildren grow up. She is predeceased by her husband Wladyslaw Kozyra, her daughter Teresa Platkowska, her parents Jan and Rozalia Dorocicz, sister Ania Dorocicz. GEORGE D. DOUBLEDAY, farmer, and grower of blooded horses, sheep and cattle. Sec. 17; P. O. Whitewater; has 400 acres; was born in Scott, Cortland Co, N. Y, Sept. 12, 1836; is the son of Charles D. and Mary J. Doubleday; came to Wisconsin with his parents in 1848, and settled in the town of Whitewater, Walworth Co. He was married, Jan. 14, 1867, in Chicago, to Miss Carrie Rockfellow, daughter of Rufus R. Rockfellow. Mrs. Doubleday was born in New York City. They have four children: Emma L., George D., Everette and Lewis. With the exception of eight years spent in the mining region of Colorado and in New York City, Mr. Doubleday has been a resident of Whitewater since coming here, in 1848. He has long been a successful grower of blooded horses, and his stables turn out some of the best steppers in the West.

Joseph J. DOUGHERTY

[I108816]
Joseph J. Dougherty, 77, of Springfield, the owner of Dougherty 's Wallpaper Decorating Co., and a World War II combat veteran, died Jan. 1 at Lankenau Hospital in Wynnewood, Lower Merion.
Born in Philadelphia, Mr. Dougherty resided in Springfield for 49 years. He served as a sergeant in the 106th Infantry Division, 591st Field Artillery Battalion during World War II in Europe. He fought in the Battle of the Bulge and received three medals for his service.
Mr. Dougherty enjoyed spending summer days with his family at their shore house in Wildwood Crest, N.J. He was a loving husband, father and grandfather.
He was the son of the late Joseph and Gertrude Gensheimer Dougherty .
Survivors: Wife of 53 years, Adeline Del Corpo Dougherty sons, Dennis Dougherty and Joseph T. Dougherty , both of Springfield , Kevin M. Dougherty of Highland, N.Y. daughters, Adele Ressler of Media, Patricia L. Dougherty of Springfield brother, Francis Dougherty of Prospect Park six grandchildren.
