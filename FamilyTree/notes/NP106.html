Joyce Elaine CRANDALL

[I036279]
Joyce Elaine Pick - 77, of White Rock, Kings County, passed away Friday, February 5, 2010 in Orchard Hall, Kentville. Born in Vernon Mines, Mountain Front, Kings County, she was a daughter of the late Levi and Annie May (Keizer) Crandall. In her younger years, she had been employed as a housekeeper in Cape Breton, Halifax and the Annapolis Valley. She was primarily a homemaker, painter and crafter. In addition to painting, she enjoyed other arts such as knitting, crocheting, pottery and quilting. She even taught her late husband to quilt when he was 80. She was an avid gardener and enjoyed going for evening drives where she would deposit lupin seeds along the roadside during her travels. She is survived by a daughter, Nancy Pick, White Rock; a son, Phillip (Tracey), Sunken Lake, Kings County; five grandchildren, Ian, William, Levi, Clayton and Lorna; two sisters, Hazel Melanson, Woodville and Muriel Young, Waterville; two brothers, Raymond (Lois) Crandall and Earl (Jean) Crandall, both of New Minas; several nieces and nephews. She was predeceased by her husband of 55 years, Elmer; a son, Kenneth; a sister, Patricia "Pat" Roy; two brothers, Willis and Howard. Cremation has taken place and there will be no visitation by family request. The funeral service, followed by a reception, will be held at 2:00 p.m. Tuesday, February 9, 2010 in the White Family Funeral Home, Kentville, Reverend Shelley Pick officiating. Burial will take place at a later date in the White Rock Cemetery, Kings County. Donations in memory may be made to the Parkinson Foundation of Canada or the Alzheimer Society. Funeral arrangements have been entrusted to the White Family Funeral Home and Cremation Services, Kentville.

William E. CRANE

[I092801]
William E. Crane , 89, of Oshkosh died on Thursday, Nov. 21, 2013 in Oshkosh.
He was born on Oct. 20, 1924 in Chippewa Falls, the son of Eugene and Stella Johnson Crane . He married Jeanne Vincent on May 26, 1946 in California.
He was a graduate of Chippewa Falls High School and Carleton College He practiced law in the Oshkosh area with Keefe, Petri, Stillman, Nolan, Crane and Engler, and then became a Winnebago County circuit court judge.
William served his country as a World War II naval aviator and was on the Military Honor Flight in 2012. He was a member of the American Legion Post 70, and Romeo's Club. He was a Rotarian and Free Mason and volunteered at the EAA.
Survivors include: his wife, Jeanne; sons Steven, Larry (Kyoko) and Brian Crane ; daughter Christine Crane ; granddaughters Jennifer ( William ) Baier and Ashley (Kurt) Klaske; and great grandson Nick. Also surviving are: a brother, Robert (Verene) Crane ; and a sister, Julie Stafford.
He was preceded in death by his parents and brother-in law, Richard Stafford.
A memorial service will be held on Tuesday, Nov. 26 at 2 p.m. at the Konrad-Behlman Funeral Home (Eastside), with military honors to follow. Family and friends may visit at the funeral home on Tuesday from 1 p.m. to 2 p.m.
Memorials to the EAA or the Oshkosh Area Community Foundation would be appreciated.
Konrad-Behlman Funeral Homes is assisting the family with arrangements.
The Chippewa Herald, 23 November 2013

Nan Louise CRAWFORD

[I104503]
Nan Crawford Van Buren died peacefully on Saturday, January 1, 2011, surrounded by family.
She was born on October 15, 1946 in Framingham MA, the daughter of the late Leonard and Dorothy Crawford. Nan worked in her father's florist shop while growing up, an experience the helped nurture her remarkable artistic talent. After earning a BS Ed degree from State College at Framingham, she taught elementary school, first in Massachusetts and then in Oklahoma. When she returned to Massachusetts, Nan entered Federal service and eventually became Personnel Officer for a Navy lab in Orlando FL. After her marriage to Arnie Lee Van Buren , she decided, with her husband's encouragement, to stay at home and apply her artistic talent as a professional quilter. The walls of Nan and Lee's home are covered with many of the beautiful quilts that she designed, made, and exhibited in shows. When living in Rhode Island after Lee was transferred there, Nan was Artist in Residence at the Newport Museum of Art for one summer. After Lee's retirement they moved to their present home in Cary.
Nan 's presence in any gathering was a brightness that emanated from her loving and caring nature. Acquaintances quickly became friends as they got to know her. When appropriate, she was always willing to offer constructive advice in a loving manner. Even as her emphysema advanced, Nan remained a positive, happy person. She was an inspiration to all.
Besides her parents, Nan was preceded in death be an infant son, Stephen and by her stepfather, Eddie Peters.
Nan is survived by her husband, Lee; daughter, Jessica Lindsey Wood and husband, Jonathan Jay of Cary; brother, Stephen Crawford and wife, Alice, of Raleigh; brother, James Crawford and wife, Donna of Framingham MA; and her uncle, Roland Avery of Port St Luce FL. She is also survived by a stepson, David Van Buren and wife, Retina of Orlando FL: and a stepdaughter, Rebecca Van Buren of Orlando FL. Also surviving are numerous nieces, nephews, and cousins.
Nan was a member of Good Shepherd United Church of Christ, 1050 NW Maynard Road, in Cary. A memorial service to celebrate her life will be held there Saturday, January 8, 2 pm.
The News & Observer, Raleigh, NC, 5 January 2011
