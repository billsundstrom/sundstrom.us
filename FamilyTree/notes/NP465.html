Donald Ingham RUDDER

[I085763]
There was a new star in the sky April 8, 2009, Donald Ingham Rudder (83) entered into eternal rest that day. Donald was born June 18, 1925, in Torrington, to the late Samuel and Bertha (Ingham) Rudder of Goshen. He was one of three sons, the late Sydney and Roger Rudder. He was a second class Radio Man in our U.S. Navy during World War II. A member of the Sphinx Temple and Order of the Shrine, Hartford. Belonged to Wyllys Lodge No. 99 of Masons, he became a 32nd degree mason. He was a stellar father to his four daughters: Donna Lynn Rudder of New Britain, Dale Rudder Hadeka of Castleton, VT, Darcy Griswold of Raleigh, NC, and Dawn Rudder-Zeffiro of Canton. Being a grandfather was one of his great joys, he adored his eight grandchildren: Andy, Kristy, Kate and Will Hadeka, Emily and Harris Griswold and Donald and Renee Zeffiro. He is survived by the mother of his children, Doris (Way) Rudder "Dolly" of Canton. Don dedicated many years of service to the town of Canton, he found "true democracy in the town meetings". He served as Assistant to the Prosecutor, before C.P.D, was the first President of the Canton Chamber of Commerce and served as Chairman of the Canton Board of Finance for years. He also owned and operated Rudder Building Service, R.&R. Cleaning and The Sunshine Class Co., for over 35 yrs. He was also member of Y.P.O. He was a partner in the Hearthstone restaurant, in Hartford and the LaTrottoria in Avon. His partners, patrons and associates were a huge part of his days and happy memories. It would be impossible to leave out the multitude of friends he made in his favorite retirement pastimes...Analyzing, Breeding and Racing thoroughbred race horses... He was dedicated to The National Assoc. of Thoroughbred Owners (NATO), Thoroughbred Owners of Florida (TOF) and EQUIX Biomechanics/Data Track International. His horses won the Travers, the Whitney, the Belmont, the Oaklawn handicap and the Gulfstream Park Handicap. All his life he enjoyed watching his ponies run. He leaves to cherish his memories of his beloved wife, best friend and partner, Anne H. Rudder of Florida; and all the others he loved, as was loved by. He will be missed, Anchors away... Services will be private. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to The American Lung Assoc. or The First Congregational Church of Canton Center, Canton Center, CT.

Vaughn S. RUDY

[I064498]
Vaughn S. Rudy , 88, a retired labor leader and senior citizens advocate, died Wednesday (Sept. 30, 1998) in Chautauqua County Home, Dunkirk.
He was born in Mayburgh, Pa., and graduated from Jamestown High School in 1928.
For 36 years, Rudy worked for MRC Bearings, where he organized United Auto Workers Local 338 and served as its president. In 1962, UAW President Walter Reuther appointed him to organize retirees in the Eastern United States.
After retiring in 1975, Rudy served as the UAW's regional retirees chairman for 16 years and as a member of the Local 338 executive board.
Rudy was presented the George Ritzer Labor Award in 1989 after 57 years in the union and was inducted into the UAW Retirees Hall of Fame.
He was a founder of the National Council of Senior Citizens in Washington, D.C., serving on its executive board for 34 years. He also helped start the first senior center in Jamestown in 1960 and received a grant to start the Meals on Wheels program in Chautauqua County.
Rudy also was a past president of the CIO Labor Council and a member of the Jamestown Area Labor Council; the Jamestown Labor-Management Council; the Loyal Order of Moose; Falconer Rod and Gun Club; Lakewood Rod and Gun Club; Chautauqua Coon Hunters; Chautauqua Lake Fishing Association; Post 53, Veterans of Foreign Wars War; Vets Recreation Club; Sinclairville 76ers; and the Southern Chautauqua County Democratic Club.
His wife of 23 years, Margaret A. Baker Rudy , died in 1996.
Survivors include a son, Jack C.; a sister, Mary A. Anderson of Lakewood; and five grandchildren.
A memorial service will be held at 2 p.m. Sunday in Park United Methodist Church
The Buffalo News, 1 October 1998

Charles M. Ruggles, 80, Passes Away at Home
Onieda, Sept. 14 - Death caused Charles M. Ruggles, 80, of 466 Elisabeth St., local insurance agent to miss the first communion service at First M. E. Church in 60 years. The head usher at the church and member of the Board of Trustees, ill for 10 days, died early Sunday morning, a few hours before communion.
Ruggles was active on the building committee when the present edifice was erected. He was city clerk under the Clark Frost administration. He retired a few years ago as second ward election inspector after serving nearly a half-century.
Mr. Ruggles was born in the town of Stockbridge May 16. 1856, a son of Royal and Eliza Ruggles. His family moved here when he was three years old. He was In the retail grocery business with the firm of Ruggles and Carter in what is now the Eldred Bros, store, Main St.
Surviving are his wife, the former Lacy Adams; one brother, Dr. Emory W. Ruggles, Rochester, and one nephew, Allen M. Ruggles, Norman, Okla.
