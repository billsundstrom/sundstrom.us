Richard Ward MCFARLANE

[I099985]
Richard Ward McFarlane, 80, Fargo, passed away Monday, August 1, 2005, in MeritCare Palliative Care Unit, Fargo, North Dakota.
Mr. McFarlane was born April 9, 1925, to Robert Theodore McFarlane and Gladys (Harris) McFarlane in Royal, Iowa. Richard attended school in Peterson and Spencer, Iowa. He served in the Army Air Corps as electrical technician, servicing B-29s on the island of Tinian during World War II.
Richard married Genieve "Bobby" Angeline Nelson of Galesburg, North Dakota, on February 5, 1946, in Hastings, Nebraska. During their 29 year marriage, they had eight children.
Upon his discharge from the military, he began his career as a horologist (watchmaker) and jeweler in Minneapolis and Los Angeles, returning to Fargo in 1975.
He is survived by his devoted companion of 30 years, Joan A. Moen, Fargo, ND; his five daughters, Michael (Gary) Arter, Baldwin City, KS; Lesli (Gary) Bergstrom, Orting, WA; Tracy McFarlane, Fargo, ND; Taji (Dan) Bahe, Moorhead, MN; Jadi (Sean Miller) McFarlane, Roseville, MN; his three sons, Koby (Linda) McFarlane, Clinton, IA; Dana McFarlane, Fargo, ND; Rory (Robert Becker) McFarlane, Long Beach, CA; Joan's three sons, Richard Moen, Fargo, ND; Scott Moen, West Fargo, ND; Kirk Moen, Chandler, AZ; his 18 grandchildren and 13 great-grandchildren; as well as a sister, Donna McFarlane, Ireland.
He was preceded in death by his wife, Genieve, in 1975; his father and mother in 1988; his sister, Barbara, in 1997; and a brother, Robert, in 2000.
Forum of Fargo Moorhead

Thomas Vance MCFARLANE

[I090354]
EX-SHERIFF J. V. McFARLAND DIES Was Sheriff of Rutherford County in 1894-1898 Funeral Held Christmas Day.
Rutherfordton, Rt 2 - James Vance McFarland, aged 72, who served Rutherford county as sheriff from 1894 to 1898. Died at his home here Wednesday afternoon, December 24, after an illness of three year. His son, J. Edgar McFarland, is now Rutherford county's sheriff while his father, the late John E. McFarland, was sheriff of the county from 1872 to 1878. Funeral services were held Christmas day, at three o'clock, at Brittain Presbyterian church, with Rev. T. S. McElroy, pastor of the Rutherfordton Presbyterian church In charge, assisted by Rev. J. W. Hoyle, pastor of the Rutherfordton Methodist church. Active pall bearers were ex-shtrii Eel Beam, ex-sheriff J. W. Beason, ex-sheriff C. E. Tanner, C. B. Justice, H.H. Harton and J. M. Hodge.
Ex-sheriff W. C. Hardin was prevented by illness from attending as an active pallbearer. A large number of Rutherfordton's leading business professional men attended as honorary pall bearers. Mr. McFarland was Born May 28, 1858, and at the time of his death was 72 years and six months of age. He was first married to Miss Lucy Hampton, who died in 1916. His second marriage was to Miss Belle Halmer, of Shelby, who survives. He was one of the county's leading citizens, and during his lifetime took an active part in the civic and commercial activities of the county. Mr. McFarland leaves five daughters, Mrs. W. F. Green, of Greenwood, S. C. Mrs. J.S. McFarland of Forest City; Mrs. Maurice Hendrick of Cliffside; Mrs. C. D. McEwen, of Charlotte, and Mrs. Percy Helms of Bessemer City. Four sons, Augus A. McFarland, Charles L. McFarland and J. Ed. McFarland of Rutherfordton and John McFarland of Hickory. Two sister . Mrs. Addie Lewis of Gainesville, Ga. and Mrs. W. J. Alexander of Arilmore, Okla., also survive, with one half sister, Mrs. Belle Duckett, of Forest City and a half brother, J. E. McFarland, of Hollis.
Forest City Courier, 1 January 1931

Rutherford Nelson MCGIFFERT

[I072344]
Rutherford N. McGiffert , 89, of East Second Street, died Wednesday, Feb. 21, 1996, in his home.
He was born in Duluth, graduated from Central High School and received a civil engineering degree at Virginia Military Academy. He was the commanding Officer for three Duluth area Civilian Conservation Corps camps before working with Harrison McGiffert Realty Co. and McGiffert Construction. In 1941, he began a career with Hallet Co., retiring in 1992 as an owner, chief executive officer and chairman of the board of many Hallet companies.
An active member of St. Paul's Episcopal Church, he was a founder of its educational scholarship and House of Prayer in Collegeville, Minn. He served on the board of directors of Northern City National Bank, was a founding member of the Junior Achievement Board and the Museum of Transportation, and belonged to the Propeller Club, receiving its Harbormaster of the Year award. He was also a member and chairman of the board of St. Luke's Hospital, a founder of St. Luke's Hospital Foundation and the president of Kitchi Gammi Club.
His son, John R., a member and chairman of the board of St. Luke's Hospital and III, died in 1965. He is survived by his wife, Frances Harrison McGiffert ; a daughter, Mary "Terry" Roberts of Duluth; a sister, Mary McGiffert Maggard of Raleigh, N.C.; and two grandsons.
Visitation: 5 to 7 p.m. Friday, with a 6 p.m. vigil service, in Jarvi-Dowd Chapel in Duluth. Services will be at 1 p.m. Saturday in St. Paul's Episcopal Church, with burial in Forest Hill Cemetery. The family prefers memorials to a charity of the donor's choice.
Duluth News Tribune, 22 February 1996

Malcolm Cushing MCGRATH

[I026185]
Malcolm Cushing McGrath, 83, Sarasota, died Sept. 3, 1997.
He was born Dec. 7, 1913, in Westfield, Mass., and came to Sarasota 17 years ago from Leawood, Kan. He was an engineer and a manager at Allied Signal in Missouri and a primary contractor for the Atomic Energy Commission. A graduate of the U.S. Naval Academy and Command and General Staff School, he served in the Navy for 17 years. During World War II, he was a fighter pilot on the USS Saratoga and the commanding officer of Anti-Submarine Warfare-Patrol Squadrons 214 and 205. He was a volunteer for the American Red Cross and was a member of the Retired Officers Club of Sarasota, the U.S. Naval Academy Alumni Association, and Bird Key Yacht Club.
Survivors include his wife of 58 years, Jayne; two daughters, Cynthia M. Secker of Kinnelon, N.J., and Ellen M. Peterson of Littleton, Mass.; a stepdaughter, Marea J. Mickley of New Richmond, Ohio; five grandchildren; and several great-grandchildren.
A memorial service will be at 2 p.m. Saturday at Toale Brothers Funeral Home, Colonial Chapel. Inurnment will be in Annapolis, Md.
Sarasota Herald-Tribune, 5 September 1997

Gardner Francis MCGREGOR

[I085170]
Mr. Gardner Francis McGregor , 87, longtime resident of Boxford, passed away on Monday, April 10 at Masconomet Healthcare Center in Topsfield.
Born June 13, 1929 in Topsfield, he was the son of the late James Frank and Anne (McGoff) McGregor . Gardner was raised in Topsfield and graduated from Topsfield High School in 1947. Gardner was a United States Army veteran. He had an extensive career as a licensed electrician and was a longstanding member of the International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers since 1953.
Gardner was the husband of Louise (Eldridge) McGregor for more than 60 wonderful years. They were avid sports fans and especially enjoyed auto racing. Gardner and Louise enjoyed traveling throughout the United States in their camper.
Gardner was a loving husband, father and grandfather.
Gardner is survived by his loving wife, Louise (Eldridge) McGregor ; his children, Deborah Wan and her husband, Samuel, Nancy Eldred and her husband, Brad and Valerie Foster and her husband, Kevin; his stepson, Roger Friend and his companion, Gail Boutilier; and nine grandchildren; one great-grandson; and several nieces and nephews.
Relatives and friends are invited to his funeral service which will be held graveside in the Pine Grove Cemetery, Haverhill Rd., Topsfield, Wednesday, April 12 at 11 a.m. Assisting the family with the arrangements is the Peterson-O'Donnell Funeral Home, 167 Maple St. (Route 62), Danvers.
The Salem News, Beverly, MA, 11 April 2017
