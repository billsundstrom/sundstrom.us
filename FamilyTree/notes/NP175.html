George Gilbert FERREN

[I097987]
Newbury, NH -- George G. Ferren, 99, died Monday, Aug. 22, 2005, at Pine Rock Manor in Warner after a period of failing health.
George was born Aug. 7, 1906, in Alston, Mass.
He ran and completed two Boston Marathons before the age of 20. In 1929, during the Depression, he went to Maine to earn money picking potatoes. There, he met his wife, Dorothy Sinclaire. George and Dorothy were married in Maine on September 20, 1931.
George and Dorothy moved to Alston, Mass., in 1936, when George took a full time job for Harvard University working on refrigeration and air conditioning maintenance. He worked for Harvard from 1936 to 1969.
In 1937 they moved to North Reading, Mass., where they lived until 1969. Their house was a four-bedroom cape with seven acres and was purchased for $1200. Their monthly mortgage payment was $25, and George's weekly salary at the time was $24.50.
In the 1940s, George and Dorothy founded the North Reading Rifle Club, an NRA-sanctioned club that was organized for indoor target shooting. He won many medals for his sharp-shooting, and went on to head the Massachusetts Rifle Association.
In the early 1950s, the Ferrens purchased land in Newbury and built a cottage that they used every weekend for skiing. Both avid skiers, George competed regularly in Nastar-sanctioned slalom races up until he was in his late 80s.
He received numerous medals and awards for racing, and was ranked second in the country for his age group. George and Dorothy could be found skiing all winter on the slopes from the New London rope tow, through the start and closing of King Ridge, and finally at Mount Sunapee.
George passed away one month short of his and Dorothy's 74th wedding anniversary.
George was predeceased by his eldest son Robert, and is survived by his loving wife, Dorothy of Newbury; and his children, Janet Capaldi of Sun City West, Ariz., Nick Ferren of Boston, George Jr. of Newbury and Dwight of Basalt, Colo.
Concord Monitor, 24 August 2005
