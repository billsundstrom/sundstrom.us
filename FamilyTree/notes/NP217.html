Elvin Harold GUZINSKI

[I047041]
RAVENNA - Elvin Harold Guzinski, 86, of Ravenna died on Monday, Feb. 17, 2014, at Good Samaritan Center in Ravenna.
Services will be at 2 p.m. Thursday at Our Lady of Lourdes Catholic Church in Ravenna. The Rev. Martin Egging will officiate. Burial will be in Highland Park Cemetery in Ravenna. Rasmussen Funeral Home in Ravenna is in charge of arrangements.
Mr. Guzinski was born on March 6, 1927, in Sherman County to Leo and Gertrude (Celmer) Guzinski.
He married Lois Bartunek on June 29, 1948. She preceded him in death.
He married Barbara Grier Schmidt in 1989.
Survivors of the immediate family include his wife, Barbara; two sons and one daughter-in-law, David and Susan Guzinski of Cairo and Timothy Guzinski of Tampa, Fla.; one daughter and son-in-law, Susan and Kent Zeller of Ravenna; two stepsons and stepdaughters-in-law, Steve and Carol Schmidt of Lincoln and Scott and Colleen Schmidt of Omaha; one stepdaughter and stepson-in-law, Sherri and Randy Esterling of Omaha; 13 grandchildren and eight great-grandchildren.
He attended District 55 and Rockville High School for two years. He graduated from Loup City High School.
In August 1945, Elvin was drafted into the U.S. Army. He served in Korea for 18 months. After his military service, he farmed with his dad and on his own.
He and Lois farmed in Sherman County until they purchased the International Harvester dealership in Ravenna in 1965. In 1979, he sold the business and retired. They moved to Ravenna in 1971. Elvin and Lois enjoyed camping, fishing, traveling and golf. He was also an avid Husker fan.
Elvin was a member of Our Lady of Lourdes Catholic Church. He was a lifetime member of the American Legion and the VFW. He was a former member and president of the Ravenna Chamber of Commerce. He served on the Good Samaritan Advisory Board and was a member of the Elks and Eagles.
He played baseball for many years for Rockville, Ravenna and Boelus. After his retirement, he spent many hours doing woodworking projects.
In addition to his wife Lois, he was preceded in death by his parents; brother, Ray Guzinski; and sister, Barbara Guzinski Tyma.

Mårten Andersson HACKZELL

[I011046]
Mårten Hackzell worked as a commander and the chief enforcement officer (Swedish: kronofogde) for the Swedish crown. Mårten was the only child of the Uppland clergyman Andreas Hackzelius (1630-1681) and Brita Mårtensdotter Hörling (1649-1710) from Stockholm, in Uppland.
The Hackzell family name derives from the Hacksta family estate, located in Hacksta, Uppland in Sweden. Through Mårten Hackzell - the only child of the Uppland clergyman Andreas Hackzelius[1][2] - and through Mårten's children, including Anders Hackzell, the Hackzell family spread to Norrland and Finland. Among the best-known 20th century members of the Hackzell family is the World-War-II-period Prime Minister of Finland Antti Hackzell (1881-1946).
Both Mårten and his son Anders Hackzell married to women from the culturally important and well-documented Scandinavian Bure kinship (Swedish: Bureätten) in Norrland. Accordingly, not only Anders but even more his son Zacharias Hackzell was an epitome of the Bure kinship genotype in the 18th century.

Clifford Eugene HADLEY

DAVENPORT - Funeral services for Clifford Hadley , 80, of Davenport, will be at 10:30 a.m. on Saturday at Cunnick-Collins Mortuary, Davenport. Family will greet friends on Saturday from 9:30 a.m. until service time at the mortuary. Burial will be at Davenport Memorial Park Cemetery.
After a courageous two month battle, Cliff died on Tuesday, July 31, 2007, at Mercy Hospital, Dubuque, Iowa, as a result of injuries suffered in a May 14th automobile accident.
Clifford Eugene Hadley was born October 17, 1926, in rural Mercer County, the son of Harry and Meril (Fischer) Hadley . He married Kathryn Marie Fraser in 1948 in Aledo. She preceded him in death on October 1, 2006.
Cliff graduated from Aledo High School in 1944 and he then served his country as a Marine during World War II in the South Pacific and at Pearl Harbor.
Cliff took pride in his work at Northwestern Bell/Qwest where he worked for 44 years before retiring in 1990. In those years he rarely missed a day, a lesson not lost upon his children.
Public service was always important to Cliff. He was past president of the Communication Workers of America (CWA), Secretary/Treasurer of the Quad Cities Federation of Labor, and a member of the Telephone Pioneers of America. He was also one of the Founding Fathers of the Handicapped Development Center in Davenport.
Cliff was an avid stamp and coin collector. He also enjoyed collecting genealogical information and spending time with his grandchildren.
Nothing showed the depths of his love better than his devotion to Kathryn in the last months of her life. Because of Cliff's determination and his love, Kathryn was able to spend her last days in the home which they had built together.
Survivors include his sons, Ted (Claire) Hadley , of Princeville, Hawaii, Bill Hadley , of Moline, and David (Carla) Hadley , of Emmetsburg, Iowa; daughters, Margaret Mickelson, of Lake Forest, Calif., and Cindy Hadley , and her husband, Mike Schwenker, of Dubuque; 13 grandchildren; two great-grandchildren; a brother, Roy Hadley , of Glens Falls, N.Y.; and sister, Jean Finch, of Aledo, Ill.
He was preceded in death by his parents, his wife, Kathryn, three brothers, and three sisters.
The family would like to extend special thanks to the medical staff in Iowa City and Dubuque who helped Cliff from the time of the accident until the time of his passing.
Memorials may be made to Beacon of Hope Hospice.
