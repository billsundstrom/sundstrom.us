PARKHURST---ARCHIBALD Delightful Autumnal Wedding at the First Universalist Church With a Prominent Medford Young Lady as the Bride.
On Saturday afternoon [October 12, 1907] at 3 oʼclock in the First Universalist church, Miss Anna Belle Archibald, daughter of the late James H. and Lucy L. Archibald and Rev Harry Adams Parkhurst were united in marriage. Miss Archibald is well known throughout the city having been clerk of the board of assessors for six years. She is a graduate of the Medford High School and has been a prominent worker in the Universalist church. Mr. Parkhurst is pastor of the Universalist church in Pigeon Cove.
The church was beautifully decorated with asparagus festoons tied with bunches of salvia, potted plants and autumn foliage, and was crowded with relatives and friends.
To the strains of the Lohengrin wedding march, played by Mrs. Noyes, the bridal party entered the church. They were unattended. The ceremony was performed by Rev. Clarence L. Eaton, pastor of the First Universalist church. [Anna Belleʼs older sister, Sarah L. Archibald, was married by Rev. Eaton a few weeks earlier, on September 19, 1907.] The ushers were Norman H. Archibald, of South Braintree, George H. Archibald of Medford, brothers of the bride, Sidney J. Willis, of South Acton, and Roger E. [actually F. for Frederick] Etz, of Cleveland, Ohio.
The brideʼs gown was white muslin trimmed with insertions. She carried white chrysanthemums.
An informal reception was held in the vestry followed by a reception at the residence of the brideʼs brother, George H. Archibald, 15 Pleasant street, where a collation was served. Many beautiful and useful gifts were received. After a wedding trip Rev. and Mrs. Parkhurst will reside at Pigeon Cove.
Medford newspaper
