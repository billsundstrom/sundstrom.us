Edwin W. CLARK

[I068801]
E. W. Clark Ends Life by Shootin
His life, made unbearable by stomach pains from which ha had long been a sufferer, Edwin W. Clark, sixty-four years old, a cotton broker, living In the Hotel Marseille, at Broadway and 1034 street, committed suicide in his apartment yesterday morning by shooting himself behind the left ear. Death was Instantaneous. He left a widow.
Through night end early morning Mr. Clark was in agony. His physician being out at town, his wife telephoned to Dr. William P. Stone, of No. 340 West Fifty-seventh street, and to Mr. Bogert, the hotel physician, to ask what could be done quickly to give him temporary relief. Many warm applications and plasters were tried, but Mr. Clark's pain could not be alleviated.
When his wife left his room for a few moments Mr. Clark took his revolver from a bureau drawer, seated himself on a 'couch and fired a single bullet into his brain. Mrs. Clark hurried to his side, but he was already dead.
Coroner Harburger, after learning details of Mr. Clark's death, gave a permit for the removal of the body.
Mrs. Clark said that husband had no reason for ending his life save the malady from which he suffered. She was Miss Laura Isabel Fiske of Shelwin, Mass.., when he married her ten years ago. His father was Joseph L. Clark, a former broker, and his mother, Mary Elisabeth Thorne a member of a well-known local family and a relative of the Vanderbilt ºs. Mr. Clark had been a member of the local Cotton Exchange for thirty years.
New York Herald, 7 May 1906

Loma CLARK

[I085991]
Loma Clarke Lavell 91, died May 8, 2010. Loma was born in McAlester, Oklahoma, to Denman Clarke and Katherine Leverenz. In her immediate family, she leaves her daughters, Linda and Judy, 4 grandchildren; 7 greatgrandchildren. Music was prominent in Loma's life; she played violin in a string quarted and high school orchestra, and sang in sextet and choirs for most of her life. She participated in the school debate team, backyard drama productions, numerous plays at McAlester High School(MHS) and Little Theater. At MHS she met Walter Lavell. After he was appointed to West Point, their four-year romance continued by letter. In October 1940, they married; Linda was born during the war and Judy, four years later. As a military wife, Loma maintained the warmth of a family home at each new base in the US and overseas. When Walter retired from the air force in 1961, they moved to Tulsa where Loma became involved in many activities: Hyechka Chorus, Tuesday Book Club, History & Music Club, PEO, bridge and line dancing groups. She spent over 20 years taping books for the blind for the Tulsa Public Library. After 40 years of commitment to the church, Loma was named honorary life member of the Presbyterian Women of the Kirk in 2005, having served as its first president, and elder, in the choir, and been a founder of the Kirk Apartment Committee, providing a place for parents while their children were at St. Francis Hospital. A Memorial service will be held at 2:00 p.m. on Monday, May 17, at Kirk of the Hills Presbyterian Church, following a graveside service at Floral Haven Cemetery at 1:00 p.m. Visitation will be at Floral Haven, on Saturday from 1 p.m. to 9 p.m. and Sunday 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.
Tulsa World, 14 May 2010

MRS. GUY SMITH DIES SUDDENLY
All Gladwin today mourned the death of one of its most respected townsfolk---Mrs. Guy E. Smith. Mrs. Smith died early this morning (Wednesday) in the Saginaw General Hospital, where she had undergone an operation Monday. She had been in poor health for some time, but her condition was not thought serious until last Sunday, when she was taken to the hospital.
A woman who was active in community affairs, Mrs. Smith's loss is keenly felt by Gladwin. Last year she was president of the Woman's Club. She was also a member of the A. C. E., woman's bridge club. She belonged to the Episcopal church.
She was married August 9, 1922 to Judge Guy E. Smith at Saginaw, who survives here with one daughter, Guinevere. There are three sons by a former marriage, Don C. Black and William H. Black of Gladwin; and George Harold Black of Midland.
