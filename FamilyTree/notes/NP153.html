Robert B. DUGAN

[I071986]
Robert Bannon Dugan of Lexington, died at the age of 79, on Oct. 23, 2010, after a long illness of Frontotemporal Dementia. Beloved husband of Constance Beaman Dugan, loving father of Carol E. Barker, of Saco, Maine, Amy L. Dugan, of Shirley Mills, Maine, and Jen Dugan-Agne, of Lexington, and husbands, Kenneth, John, and Carl, as well as two grandchildren, Tommy and Amy Agne. Also survived by one brother-in-law and uncle to two nephews and grandnephews, all of Pennsylvania. Bob was born on April 21, 1931, in Rochester, Pa. Graduated from Westminster College with a BBAd degree in 1953, followed by serving in the U.S. Army from 1953 - 1955. Graduated from Syracuse University Law School (LLB) in 1958. Bob was a tax attorney, employed by the U.S. Treasury for 29 years, ending those years as the District Council. During, and followed by his work with the Treasury, Bob obtained his Masters degree in taxation from Boston University School of Law, where he taught part time, and then full time after he retired from the U.S. Treasury, He also felt a great need to help and share his legal knowledge with people as a pro-bono attorney. His selfless help for those in need was a very very important part of who he was. Bob also shared himself and his knowledge at Park Avenue Congregational Church in many ways, always with grace, dignity, and love. He was active in the Charles River Power Squadron for several years, teaching navigation in the Basic Boating Course, and, was an active sailor on the sailboat he shared with his brother-in-law, as the navigator, sailing "down Maine," enjoying the many islands and surrounding area. He loved navigating the many camping trips we took, and the trips we took to England and Scotland. He was a dear man. A Memorial Service is planned for Nov. 6 at 11 a.m. in the Park Avenue Congregational Church, 50 Paul Revere Rd., Arlington Heights. In lieu of flowers, donations can be sent in his honor to either Park Avenue Congregational Church or Compassionate Care Hospice, 800 West Cummings Park, suite 3400, Woburn, MA 01801, The Saville Chapel of DeVito Funeral Home assisting with arrangements.
The Lexington Minuteman, 2 November 2010

William Joseph DUKE

William Joseph Duke passed away on Tues- day, July 24, 2012, at the age of 91. "Bill" was born in Seattle, Wa. on February 21, 1921. He entered the University of Washington in 1938; however, his studies were interrupted by service in the U.S. Civilian Army Corps of Engineers. He graduated from the University of Washington in 1947 with a degree in Business Ad- ministration. He worked for United Airlines in Seattle before moving to Fresno in 1950. He joined Daugherty Travel Service in 1952 and with Norm Beck started Beck and Duke Travel in 1960. He became sole owner of the company and retired in 1986. Bill was on the Board of the Fresno Chamber of Commerce, 1979- 1982. He was past President of the San Joaquin Valley Association of Travel Agents; member of the Institute of Certified Travel Agents; and a 52 year member and past President of North Fresno Rotary. It was during his employment with United Airlines in Seattle that he met his true love, Beth Wiley of Mason City, Iowa. They were married for 66 years. Bill is survived by his wife, Beth of Fresno; his daughter, Ann Delfino of Roseville, his daughter, Mary of Fresno; his son, David and his wife Vicky of Salinas; two grandsons, Austin and Bryan Duke of Salinas. LaVera Isabelle DULITZ

[I000239]
Mrs. LaVera Sundstrom, 76, of Ocean Springs,, passed away on the morning of Monday, 2 October 2006, after a brave battle against gall bladder cancer. She was diagnosed in late June, 2006.
LaVera was born 19 January 1930 in Brownsdale Township, Minnesota. She lived in southeastern Minnesota until she met and married her husband, Sidney in 1951. During their marriage they enjoyed travel to the Far East and Europe. They lived in various states and settled in Ocean Springs in 1969.
She was the bridal consultant in the china and gifts department for Gayfers Department Store (now Dillards) for 18 years. She retired from this job in 1993.
LaVera was an active member of Christus Victor Lutheran Church, Ocean Springs, since 1972, where she served on the Alter Guild, preparing for the communion services for 18 years. She was an active member of Women of the ELCA and recently had served as Treasurer of her local chapter. She had also worked as the co-coordinator for the congregation's work at the Lord Is My Help.
LaVera was a volunteer in the Ocean Springs Hospital Auxiliary since 1997, where she worked regularly in the hospital gift shop. She was also an active member in the Day Lily Garden Club, Ocean Springs, and had held several offices in that organization.
She was preceded in death by her parents, Alma and Emil Dulitz, her sister, Anelda Blanchard and her brother, Norman Dulitz, all of Minnesota.
Survivors include her husband of 54 years, M. Sidney Sundstrom, her children, Katherine Sundstrom, M.D., of Brooklyn, NY, Steven Sundstrom and his wife, Ellen of Appleton, Wisconsin, Ann Teitel and her husband Alan of Port Washington, NY, and Dawn Sundstrom and her partner Mitzi Griffin of Greenboro, NC, her stepsons, Bill and Brian Sundstrom, both of Minnesota, eight grandchildren (Birgitta Sundstrom, Dain Sundstrom, Aricka Sundstrom, Bjorn Sundstrom, Shelby Sundstrom, Eddy Baggenstoss, Belinda Brody, and Clara Tietel) and three great-grandchildren.
Visitation will be on Wednesday, October 4, 2006, from 7-9 PM, at the Ocean Springs Chapel of Bradford O'Keefe Funeral Home. The funeral service will be held on Thursday, October 5, 2006, at 10:30 A.M. at Christus Victor Lutheran Church in Ocean Springs. Burial will follow at the Biloxi National Cemetery.

Jeanette Duncan Johnston, age 86 of Dennison, died on Thursday morning, July 4, 2013 at Three Links Care Center in Northfield, surrounded by her children.
Jeanette was born on April 25, 1927 in Cannon Falls, the daughter of William E. and Agnes J. (Johnson) Duncan. She attended Cannon Falls High School, and married Ralph L. Johnston on May 6, 1944 in Farmington. They moved to their farm in Dennison in 1946. Ralph died on July 7, 2002 and she continued to reside there. As a younger woman, she taught Sunday school and sang with the Randolph Baptist Church choir and was part of the "Ladies Trio" for many years.
Jeanette had been a receptionist at Mineral Springs Sanatorium from 1972-82, and later worked at Sheldahl in Northfield and Midwest Importers of Cannon Falls until retiring in 2002. Jeanette enjoyed the times she was able to travel and particularly loved the mountains and other scenic areas. She loved spending time with her children, grandchildren and great-grandchildren. She always looked forward to Sunday afternoons when family would gather at the farm to visit and eat Betty's baked goods. As she became more home-bound, she enjoyed feeding her birds, sharing her home with her "special" cats, and listening to both local sports broadcasts and, of course, the Minnesota Twins. Almost every Saturday morning, she shared a phone conversation with her sister Beverly and always appreciated the calls she received from her friends. Jeanette was an avid reader, and was a lifelong learner.
She is survived by her six children, Ralph Jr. (Mary) of Cleveland, MN, Betty (Bob) Lee of Randolph, Marjorie Berendt of Cannon Falls, John of Dennison, Janet (Calvin) Thielbar of Randolph and David (Margaret) of Dennison; 11 grandchildren and 20 great-grandchildren, with 2 expected shortly; sister, Beverly (Roger) Anderson of Sun City West, AZ; sisters-in-law, Jane Duncan, Annette Duncan and Myong Su Johnston; and several nieces and nephews.

Marvin Hughes DUNCAN

[I089053]
Lt. Cmdr. MARVIN HUGHES DUNCAN "Dunc" Passed away on Saturday, July 16, 2011. Born in 1924, Dunc led an active life. During World War II, he served as a navy pilot and Tailhooker in the American Theatre, Asiatic Pacific Theatre, and European occupation. He received 23 flying medals and five distinguished flying crosses. He retired from the Navy in 1965 after 23 years of service. Dunc then continued to serve his country as a civil servant working for the Army, Navy, and The Justice Department. Following his retirement, he moved to Kansas City to be closer to his daughter, Susan, and her family. In addition to watching his grandchildren and great grandchildren grow, Duncan was an avid gardener, grape grower, and a great handyman. He was involved with the Association of Naval Aviators, The Fist of the Fleet, and enjoyed many good times with his buddy, Ozzie, of Oz's MAQ Doughnut House. He most recently returned to Washington, D.C. to rejoin his other daughter, Rebecca, and her family. Dunc had a great passion for life and a humor that warmed all hearts. He always had an adventure to seize and a great story to share, complete with a twinkle in his eye. Dunc now joins his beloved wife, Mickie, whom he was married to for 59 years. He is survived by his sister, Lillian Wohlgenant, two daughters, Susan Ziemianski and Rebecca Faeder, six grandchildren, three great grandchildren, and many nieces and nephews. A memorial service will be held in Kansas City in early September. Contributions in his memory may be made to the Fist of the Fleet Association* and to the Wings of Gold Program at Blue Valley North High School.
