Mary MCALEER

[I086110]
MERRITT, Mary M. Mary (McAleer) Merritt, 85, of Glastonbury, beloved wife of Robert Merritt, died on Tuesday (January 17, 2006). She was born in Brookline, MA on September 13, 1920, the daughter of Charles L. and Martha E. (Quinn) McAleer. She attended Brookline High School and Wellesley College, majoring in music. After Wellesley she served 18 months in the American Red Cross in London, England and Occupied Germany. She married Bob in 1948 and moved to Hartford. They settled in Glastonbury in 1951 and raised their family there. Mary was involved in a large number of community activities throughout her time in Connecticut, she was a co-founder and first president in 1955-56 of Glastonbury Cooperative Kindergartens, Inc., which led to the introduction of kindergartens in the Glastonbury public schools. She sang for some 20 years in the Hartford Symphony Chorale. A lifelong Democrat, Mary was active in Glastonbury Democratic politics for over 25 years, including 12 years as a member of the Democratic Town Committee, and was extensively involved in the writing of the well-known Democratic Skits, which ran from 1958-83. She served as Secretary of the Glastonbury Redevelopment Agency from 1967-69. In 1968 she helped found Glastonbury Summer Camps Association, Inc., which established an interracial summer camp. She worked with North Hartford parents from 1970-73 to raise funds for a day care center project, which became Northwest Family Center, Inc. From 1983-90, Mary served on the Board of Trustees of the Hartford Conservatory of Music. She was a board member and president of the Friends of Welles Turner Library in Glastonbury from 1986-96, and from 1994-2001 was a member of the board of Welles Turner's Second Century Fund, of which she was co-chair and organizer of the Individual Gifts Campaign. Most recently, she was active with the citizen group Glastonbury Coalition for Sensible Growth. Besides her volunteer activities, Mary was associated with the Counseling Center for Women of Hartford College for Women, first as a co-founder in 1968 and continuing until her retirement in 1983. She was its Director from 1976-1983. Both before and after retirement, she and Bob traveled extensively, especially to Italy, for which they shared a special fondness. They attended many musical performances, including numerous trips to the Metropolitan Opera and Glimmerglass Opera, and studied Italian language and literature. She never stopped learning, and was always keenly interested in current events. In addition to her husband, she is survived by two daughters, Priscilla Ann and her husband Louis Montalvo of Colchester and Cynthia Elizabeth of Glastonbury, and a son, Robert Nicholas and his wife, Susan Lennon of Hartford. She also leaves her grandchildren, Katherine Wang of St. Louis, MO and Stephen Wang of Haddam. She was predeceased by a son, Peter Charles, and her three brothers, Charles E. McAleer, Robert H. McAleer and A. Gordon McAleer. She leaves also three sisters-in-law, Barbara Nason of Concord, NH, Verda McAleer of Manchester, MA and Nancy McAleer of Sunderland, VT and 12 nieces and nephews. In lieu of flowers, contributions in Mary's name may be made to The Second Century Fund of Welles Turner Library, The Helen and Harry Gray Cancer Center, or the Visiting Nurse Association. Mary and her family want especially to thank the staffs of the Gray Center and the VNA for their help during her illness. A Mass of Resurrection will be celebrated on Saturday, January 21, 10 a.m., at St. Patrick and St. Anthony Church, Hartford. Glastonbury Funeral Home has charge of arrangements.
The Hartford Courant, 18 January 2006

Lloyd E. MCALLEN

Lloyd R. McAllen , 92, formerly of Lantz Corners, then Central Towers in Kane, died Thursday afternoon, Dec. 15, 2011, at the Bradford Manor.
Born June 17, 1919 in Kane, he was the son of Daniel W. and Ruth N. Becker McAllen . On Feb. 28, 1948, he married Dolores A. "Dee" Mitsch, who survives.
After graduating from Kane Area Schools in 1938, Mr. McAllen served in the U. S. Navy during WWII in the Asiatic-Pacific-Philippine Liberation, and was awarded five medals and the Purple Heart. He was a machinist at PESCO Products in Cleveland for over 27 years before returning to Kane in 1980, then worked in maintenance for The Lutheran Home at Kane until his retirement in 1982. He had been a member of the B. P. O. Elks #329 in Kane and the Kane Country Club.
Surviving, in addition to his wife of Kane, are two daughters, Kathy (Chester) Todd of Kane and Suger (Adrian Eckert) McAllen of White Pine, Tenn.; a son, David (Donna) McAllen of Chardon, Ohio; plus five grandchildren and eight great-grandchildren.

Jan. 22,1923 - Feb. 26, 2018
Marion Lorraine Paulsen passed away peacefully with her son Rick by her side at the age of 95. Marion was born in 1923 to William and Emma (LaBarge) McCabe in Lake Andes, South Dakota. She attended Southern State Teachers College in Springfield, South Dakota and she spent three years teaching in a one room school house. Marion married Max James Paulsen in 1943 while he was in the service, and they were married for 72 years, before his death in 2015. Marion , Max, Jim and Rick moved to Stockton from Davenport, Iowa in 1962.
Marion loved her children and grandchildren's activities, traveling, bridge, golf, Lunch Bunch groups, and volunteering with many community organizations. She was a tireless supporter of Presentation, St. Mary's High School, and UOP, and she particularly enjoyed preparing Sunday night meals for Rick's UOP team mates, fraternity brothers and friends. Marion and Max retired young and enjoyed 10 years of living in Mesa, AZ (Leisure World) with their New York Life friends. They moved back to Stockton after their grandson Max was born. Marion is survived by her son Rick Paulsen , and his wife Nancy; grandson's Max and Grant, her sister's Lois Donovan of Roseville and Jean Mueller of Spokane, Washington, and many nieces and nephews. Marion is preceded in death by her husband Max, her son Jim Paulsen and his wife Nancy, and her sister Mildred McCabe.
